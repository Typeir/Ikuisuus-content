# Mind Paths

Mind Paths are psionic techniques that allow you to manipulate the world around you using mental energy.

**Mind Paths are simple, reactive synaptic maneuvers.**  
They trigger in response to specific events and can be used **once per round**, as a **reaction**.
**Mind Paths** have a range of **60 feet** and require **no components**.

---

> ### **Feedback**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range deals damage  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice and add **Psychic damage** equal to the total rolled to the damage dealt by that creature.

---

> ### **Overload**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range fails a saving throw  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice and **add the total rolled** to their saving throw, potentially causing it to succeed.

---

> ### **Interference**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range succeeds on a saving throw or skill check
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice and **subtract the total rolled** from the roll, potentially causing it to fail.

---

> ### **Reinforcement**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range regains hit points  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice and **increase the hit points regained** by twice the total rolled.

---

> ### **Buffer**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range takes damage  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice; that creature gains **Temporary Hit Points** equal to twice the total rolled.

---

> ### **Vectoring**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range is successfully moved against its will  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice and **increase the distance moved** by **5 feet per total rolled**.

---

> ### **Waste**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range takes damage  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice; the creature’s **Speed is reduced** by **5 feet per total rolled** until the end of its next turn.

---

> ### **Focus**
>
> **Trigger:** A creature you can see within range makes an ability check  
> **Effect:** Roll your Psionic Dice and **add the total rolled** to the result of that check.
