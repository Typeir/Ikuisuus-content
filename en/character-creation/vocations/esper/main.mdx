# Esper

Espers are mind masters, wielding immense will with thought alone. Their psionic abilities allow espers to reactively manipulate the outcomes of events around them, bending reality to their mental commands.
Espers are deceptively fragile, relying on their intellect and mental fortitude to outthink and outmaneuver their foes rather than brute strength or physical resilience.

## Becoming an Esper

### As a Level 1 Character

- Gain all the traits in the Core Esper Traits table.
- Gain the Esper’s level 1 features, which are listed in the Esper Features table.

### As a mixed Character

- Gain the Hit Point Die from the Core Esper Traits table.
- See [mixing rules](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/class:multiclassing) for spell slot progression.
- Gain the Esper's level 1 features.

## 1st Level – Esper Spellcasting

Beginning at 1st level, your heightened cognition allows you to cast spells through psionic exertion.

### Casting Spells

The Esper Features table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your Esper spells of 1st level and higher.  
To cast one of these spells, you must expend a spell slot of the spell’s level or higher.

You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a Long Rest.

### Spells Known

You know a number of Esper spells as shown in the **Spells Known** column of the Esper Features table.
You pick your spells from the [_Esper spell list_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/esper/spells).

You can cast any spell you know, provided you have an available spell slot of the appropriate level.

Whenever you gain a level in this vocation, you may replace one Esper spell you know with another Esper spell of a level for which you have spell slots.

### Cantrips

You know two Esper cantrips of your choice.

At Esper levels 4 and 10, you learn one additional Esper cantrip of your choice, as shown in the Esper Features table.

### Spellcasting Ability

Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your Esper spells.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier  
Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

### Psionic Spellcasting

When you cast an Esper spell, it does not require Verbal or Material components, unless the spell consumes a material component or specifies a material cost.

You must still provide Somatic components as normal.

## 1st Level – Psionic Dice

You possess Psionic Dice that represent raw cognitive output.

- At 1st level, you have one Psionic Die (d4).
- At 9th level, you have two Psionic Dice.
- At 17th level, you have three Psionic Dice.

Whenever you roll your Psionic Dice, roll all of them together and add the results.

Different features will call for you to roll your Psionic Dice, often as a reaction.

</Collapsible>

## 1st Level – Mind Paths

You know a number of [_Mind Paths_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/esper/mind-paths), as shown in the Esper Features table.

All [_Mind Paths_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/esper/mind-paths) you know are always active.

Each [_Mind Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/esper/mind-paths) can be triggered once per round, as a reaction. You can use only one [_Mind Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/esper/mind-paths) in response to a given triggering event.

After a Short Rest, you may replace one [_Mind Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/esper/mind-paths) you know with another.

</Collapsible>

## 2nd Level – Psionic Link

A number of times per Long Rest equal to your Proficiency Bonus, you can establish telepathy with a single willing creature as a bonus action.

Roll your Psionic Dice; the telepathy range is 20 feet × the total rolled and lasts for 10 minutes.

## 5th Level – Mind Worm

When you make a Charisma ability check, or take the Influence or Parlay actions, you can use your Intelligence modifier in place of your Charisma modifier for that roll.

In addition, once per Short Rest, you can choose one type of tool. You gain proficiency with that tool until you finish your next Short Rest.

While you have proficiency with a tool gained from this feature, you can make ability checks using that tool even if you do not physically possess it, projecting a psionic facsimile sufficient to perform the check.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 7th Level – Psionic Surge

When you roll your Psionic Dice, you may expend one Hit Die per dice rolled, up to a maximum equal to the number of Psionic Dice you roll.
If you do, treat one roll of 1 as 4 per Hit Die expended.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 18th Level – Cognitive Overhang

While **bloodied**, you gain an additional reaction each round, that you can only use to roll your Psionic Dice.
You can roll your Psionic Dice twice per round while **bloodied**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 20th Level – Perfected Mind

Your Intelligence score increases by 4, to a maximum of 24.

While you are **incapacitated, unconscious, or sleeping**, you can project your mind to take **actions** independently of your body.

Choose a point you can see within **60 feet** of you. A **spectral projection** of yourself appears at that point and acts on **your initiative**.

This projection lasts for **1 minute**, until you regain consciousness, or until you end it early (no action required).

The projection is an **exact copy of you** in all respects, except as noted below:

- The projection is **invulnerable and intangible**.
- It cannot be grappled, restrained, damaged, or moved by ordinary means.
- Creatures can move through the projection as if it were **difficult terrain**.
- The projection can **move, take actions, bonus actions, and reactions**, use vocation features, interact with objects, and cast spells **as normal**, treating itself as corporeal for these purposes.
- If you were concentrating on a spell when this feature activates, you **continue concentrating through the projection**, ignoring the normal effects of being incapacitated.

The projection **disappears immediately** if it moves more than **300 feet** away from your body.

The projection **cannot pass through solid objects**. If it would end its turn inside a solid object, the projection instead ends immediately.

If the projection disappears, you can summon it again on your turn.

When the projection ends, you immediately lose any benefits that require its presence.

</Collapsible>
