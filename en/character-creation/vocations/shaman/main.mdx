# Shaman

Some magics demand devotion and study. Others demand lineage and sacrifice — or a contract sealed in blood under broken moons.

True magic asks only for presence. And the knowledge of what to say.

Through rites sung over carved effigies, shamans call upon Väkis: fragments of primordial Ideas born from the imperfections in the Demiurge's shaping of Arkhé.
Väkis are shards of the true shape of the world — shards of the Idea of Flame, the Idea of Blood, the Idea of Stairs, the Idea of Sorrow.
Lured to an effigy made of will and fervor, crafted in packed clay, whittled bone, or twisted wire, a Väki becomes a conduit.
For a brief duration, the shamans may dance with the Ideas themselves, and the Ideas dance back in turn.
Beyond reach of the lodgment, the conduit breaks.

The price of this method is visible.
Where another caster commands a vast repertoire of spells, the shaman carries only what they have lured and secured through rite.
Specialize deeply — building one chain of rites from first to last — and the shaman reaches further into a single Idea than most casters ever dream.
Scatter across many Idea-fragments, and each link in the chain remains short.

## 1st Level – Spellcasting

Shaman spellcasting is channeled exclusively through the domain of held rites. Rather than drawing from a broad class spell list, the shaman may only cast spells attached to their currently held rites.

- **Spellcasting Ability**: Wisdom.
- **Spellcasting Focus**: An effigy prepared as a focus, or a component pouch.
- **Spell Slots**: See the Shaman Features table.

---

### Casting Spells

You can only cast spells from rites for which you have an **active placed effigy** within **60 feet**. Without an active effigy bound to a rite, that rite's spells remain inaccessible.

</Collapsible>

## 1st Level – Effigy & Rites

Through ritual incantation, you call Väkis from the borderlands and lure them into physical lodgments. Your power flows through these temporary anchors.

---

### Rites

A rite is a formalized incantation tradition that calls to a Väki fragment and grants access to spells tied to that Idea's domain. Rites draw power from primordial Ideas — the Idea of Flame, the Idea of Blood, the Idea of Decay, the Idea of Standing, the Idea of Hunger, and countless others.

- **Rites Known**: Equal to your Proficiency Bonus. This is the total number of rites you may hold at one time.
- **Preparing Rites**: During a **Long Rest**, you prepare your rites. You may select any rites you have learned.
- **Replacing Rites**: During a **Short Rest**, you may replace **one** of your prepared rites with another you know.

---

### Rite Tiers

Rites exist in five tiers of increasing potency. Higher tiers are unlocked as the shaman grows in power.

| Name         | Unlocked At          | Spell Levels |
| ------------ | -------------------- | ------------ |
| Rite         | Level 1              | 1–4          |
| Major Rite   | Level 9              | 5–7          |
| Ancient Rite | Level 15             | 8–9          |
| Supreme Rite | Level 21             | 10–11        |
| True Rite    | Level 29 (epic play) | 12           |

Each tier of a thematic rite is a separate rite occupying its own slot. A shaman who holds the full chain of a single domain — **Rite of Flames**, **Major Rite of Flames**, **Ancient Rite of Flames**, **Supreme Rite of Flames**, **True Rite of Flames** — has committed five of their available rite slots to that domain.

**Rite Hierarchy**: To prepare a Major Rite, you must also prepare its base Rite. To prepare an Ancient Rite, you must prepare both its Rite and Major Rite.
You cannot hold a higher tier without committing all lower tiers of that same domain.

> **The cost of mastery**: At level 13, a shaman has 5 rite slots. A fully committed fire specialist who holds **Rite of Flames** and **Major Rite of Flames** occupies 2 slots, leaving 3 for other domains. At level 29, that same specialist can hold all five tiers of the flame chain, committing five slots to that single domain. Breadth and depth are always in tension.

---

### Effigies

An effigy is a crude physical object — carved wood, packed clay, bundled wire — that serves as a temporary lodging for a Väki called by a rite. Effigies are fragile: they have **1 hit point** and can be destroyed by enemies who reach them.

- **Creating Effigies**: You regain a number of effigy charges equal to your **Wisdom modifier** after each **Short Rest** or **Long Rest**.
- **Placing an Effigy**: As a **Bonus Action**, expend one effigy charge to place an effigy for one of your held rites in your current space or an adjacent unoccupied space. The effigy calls to the Väki associated with that rite.
- **Duration**: A placed effigy lasts for **10 minutes**, unless destroyed earlier.
- **Effigy Range**: While within **60 feet** of an active effigy, you may cast spells from the rite for which it calls.
- **Multiple Effigies**: You may have multiple effigies active simultaneously, each calling to a different held rite (or the same rite).
- **Placement is the constraint**: A rite whose effigy has not been placed, has expired, or has been destroyed is inaccessible until a new effigy calls to it again.

## 2nd Level – Minor Rite

At 2nd level, the shaman masters a Minor Rite — a performed ritual and vocal pattern that becomes woven into their body and voice, requiring no effigy and consuming no spell slot.

A Minor Rite provides two components:

---

### Emanation

A trained result of the Minor Rite performance — a persistent effect tied to the shaman's body or weapon. Minor Rite emanations fall into two forms:

- **Reflected Damage**: When you take a specific damage type, a small amount is reflected back to the attacker.
- **Added Weapon Damage**: Once per turn when you hit with a weapon attack, add a small die of a specific damage type.

The die used by the emanation begins at **d4** or **d6** (depending on the Minor Rite selected) and increases according to the following schedule:

| Level | Emanation Die |
| ----- | ------------- |
| 2     | d4 or d6      |
| 5     | One step up   |
| 13    | One step up   |
| 21    | One step up   |
| 29    | One step up   |

Die steps follow the standard [dice progression](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapons-and-dice-levels): d4 → d6 → d8 → d10 → d12.

---

### Cornerstone Incantation

You know a single 1st-level spell that you can cast any number of times without expending a spell slot. This spell requires no active effigy to cast.

The cornerstone is automatically upcast at higher effective spell levels on the following schedule:

| Level | Effective Spell Level |
| ----- | --------------------- |
| 2     | 1st                   |
| 9     | 2nd                   |
| 17    | 3rd                   |
| 25    | 4th                   |
| 30    | 5th                   |
