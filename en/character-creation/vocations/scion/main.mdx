# Scion

Scions wield innate magic that is stamped into their being. Some Scions can’t name the origin of their power, while others trace it to strange events in their personal or family history. The blessing of a dragon or a dryad at a baby’s birth or the strike of lightning from a clear sky might spark a Scion’s gift. So too might the gift of a deity, exposure to the strange magic of another plane of existence, or a glimpse into the inner workings of reality.

Scions don’t learn magic; the raw, roiling power of magic is part of them. The essential art of a Scion is learning to harness and channel that innate magic, allowing the Scion to discover new and staggering ways to unleash their power.

## Becoming a Scion

### As a Level 1 Character

- Gain all the traits in the Core Scion Traits table.
- Gain the Scion’s level 1 features, which are listed in the Scion Features table.

### As a mixed Character

- Gain the Hit Point Die from the Core Scion Traits table.
- See [Mixing Spellcasting](/en/library/rules/arcana-and-the-fold/mixing-spellcasting) for spell slot progression.
- Gain the Scion's level 1 features.

## 1st Level – Spellcasting

You know four Scion cantrips of your choice. When you gain a Scion level, you can replace one cantrip with another from the Scion spell list.
You prepare Scion spells as shown in the Features table. Your spellcasting ability is Charisma, and you use an Arcane Focus.

##### Spell Points

Instead of spell slots, you have a pool of Spell Points. Your Scion level determines your maximum Spell Points and your maximum Spell Level, as shown in the Features table (Spell Points column). You regain all spent Spell Points when you finish a Recovery.

##### Casting a Spell

To cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you expend a number of Spell Points equal to the spell’s level cost from the table below. Cantrips cost nothing.

| Spell Level | Point Cost |
| ----------- | ---------- |
| 1st         | 1          |
| 2nd         | 2          |
| 3rd         | 3          |
| 4th         | 5          |
| 5th         | 8          |
| 6th         | 13         |
| 7th         | 21         |
| 8th         | 34         |
| 9th         | 55         |

Each spell costs as much as the sum of the two previous levels, rounded down. For example, a 4th-level spell costs 5 Spell Points (2 + 3 = 5), and a 9th-level spell costs 55 Spell Points (21 + 34 = 55).

</Collapsible>

## 2nd Level – Font of Magic

You gain **Sorcery Points** to fuel various magical effects.

Your sorcery points equal your level.

Once per Repose, you can perform the following actions:

**Convert Spell points**: Convert spell points into Sorcery Points.

**Create Spell points**: Use Sorcery Points to create spell points.

##### Creating Spell points

<Collapsible>
## 3rd Level – Innate Sorcery

As a **Minor Action**, unleash your inner magic for **1 minute**:

- Your **spell save DC** increases by **+1**.
- You have **Advantage** on attack rolls of Scion spells.

You can use this feature **twice per Recovery**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## Level 4, 8, 12, 16: Ability Score Improvement

You gain an [Ability Score Improvement feat](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/feat:ability-score-improvement) or another feat for which you qualify.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## Level 5: Sorcerous Restoration

Once per Repose, regain Sorcery Points equal to **half your Scion level**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 7th Level – Sorcery Incarnate

- If you have no uses of **Innate Sorcery**, you can spend **2 Sorcery Points** to activate it.
- While **Innate Sorcery** is active, you can use **two Metamagic options** on each spell you cast.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 19th Level – Epic Boon

You gain an [Epic Boon feat](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/feat:all).

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 20th Level – Arcane Apotheosis

While **Innate Sorcery** is active, you can use one **Metamagic option** per turn **without spending Sorcery Points**.

</Collapsible>
