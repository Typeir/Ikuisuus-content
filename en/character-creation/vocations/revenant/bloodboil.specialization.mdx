# Bloodboil

A knight of Bloodboil holds dominion over blood, both their own and that of others, causing it to writhe, burn, stab, harden and erupt in a multitude of ways to keep
their foes within their grasp. To flee from a Bloodboil is to try and stop your own heart from beating, to avoid moonlight in the clearest of nights. 

## How one becomes a Revenant

A lunar curse — Or blessing, fell upon you. Kuutar’s blood, a mother’s curse, a moon-fiend’s blessing, an ooze or parasite seeking a host—anything with hunger and memory slipped into the wounds you should have died from. It keeps your heart moving, and it burns when you ignore it.

## 1st Level - Blood Ward
When a creature hits you with an attack, you may use your **reaction** to roll **one of your Hit Dice**.  
Add the result to your **Armor Class** against that attack. If this causes the attack to miss, the hit is negated.

You must use this feature **after the hit is declared but before damage is rolled**.  
You cannot use this feature if you have **no Hit Dice remaining**.

## 3rd Level - Heartburn
### Blood Sublimation
As a **bonus action**, choose a creature within **30 feet** that you can see. The creature must make a **Constitution saving throw**.

- On a failed save, the creature takes **2d8 + your Intelligence modifier fire damage** and has **disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks** until the end of your next turn.
- On a success, the creature suffers no additional effect.

A creature damaged by this feature gains **1 stack of [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding)**.  
Creatures immune to bleeding instead take **1d4 additional piercing damage**.

---

### Blood Deposition
As a **bonus action**, choose a creature within **30 feet** that you can see. The creature must make a **Constitution saving throw**.

- On a failed save, the creature takes **cold damage equal to your Intelligence modifier**, its **speed is halved**, and it can make **only one attack** on its turn, regardless of features or Extra Attack.
- If the creature casts a spell before the end of your next turn, it must succeed on a **Concentration check** (even if the spell is not normally concentration) or the spell fails and the slot is expended.
- On a success, the creature suffers no additional effect.

A creature damaged by this feature gains **1 stack of [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding)**.  
Creatures immune to bleeding instead take **1d4 additional piercing damage**.

---

You may use **Blood Sublimation** or **Blood Deposition** a total number of times equal to your **Constitution modifier** per long rest.  
A creature cannot be affected by both effects simultaneously.

## 6th Level -Bloodspire 
You may attack creatures within your **Abundance and Decay** with a Bloodspire instead of your weapon.

When you take the Attack action, you can replace one attack with a Bloodspire targeting one creature inside your Abundance and Decay.

- Make a **melee spell attack** using your **CON** modifier.
- On a hit, the target takes **2d8 magical piercing damage**.  
  This damage increases to **3d8 at 14th level** and **4d8 at 18th level**.

After taking damage, the creature must succeed on a **Dexterity saving throw** against your spell save DC or become **grappled** by the spire. 
Creatures two or more sizes larger than you save at advantage. 
A creature grappled by the spire has a **speed of 0** and remains grappled while inside your Abundance and Decay and while you are not incapacitated.

The grapple ends early if you use Bloodspire on a different target or if the creature escapes by using its action to attempt a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against your spell save DC.

Bloodspire counts as a **melee weapon attack** for the purposes of **Execution**, but does not benefit from weapon properties.

Hitting a [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding) creature with **Execution** or **Bloodspire** gives them **1 stack of [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding)**.  
Creatures immune to bleeding instead take **1d4 piercing damage**.

</Collapsible>

## 14th Level - Crimson Undergrowth 
Your **Abundance and Decay** becomes a hazardous field of lesser bloodspires.

- The area of your Abundance and Decay is **difficult terrain** for all creatures **other than you and your summons**.
- A creature other than you and your summons that **moves** within the area gains **one stack of [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding)** for every 5 feet it travels, or takes **1d4 piercing damage** per 5 feet if it is immune to bleeding.

You may **suppress** Crimson Undergrowth until the start of your next turn as a **bonus action**.

</Collapsible>

## 18th Level - Heartbreak 
When you hit a creature **grappled by your Bloodspires** with either **Execution** or **Bloodspire**, you may choose to **detonate** the spire that is holding it.

The target must make a **Dexterity saving throw** against your spell save DC.

On a failed save:

- It takes **8d6 magical piercing damage**.
- It gains **[_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding)** stacks equal to **your CON modifier + 1d4**.
- Creatures immune to bleeding instead take **12d6 piercing damage**.
- The detonation ends the grapple immediately.

On a successful save:

- It takes **half damage**.
- It gains **[_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#bleeding)** (**1 stack**).
- Creatures immune to bleeding instead take **1d4 piercing damage**.

You may use **Heartbreak** **twice**, regaining **one use on a short rest**, and **all uses on a long rest**.

</Collapsible>
