# Revenant

Revenants are warriors of the end itself, their bodies altered into a liminal state between life and death, they are scholars of entropy and death,
clad in iron and stillness to enact its purpose. Revenants are often aligned with powers capable of altering one into such deathless state,
and, as such, they are often found participating in causes most foul. Even if this is the case, a non-ignorable minority of them
betray their masters and seek to use their newfound forms for good, be it for the benefit of all or merely themselves.

## Becoming a Revenant

### As a Level 1 Character

- Gain all traits listed in the **Core Revenant Traits** table.
- Gain the Revenant’s level 1 features.

---

### As a Mixed Character

- Gain the Revenant’s **Hit Point Die**.
- Gain the revenants weapon proficiencies.
- Gain the Revenant's level 1 features.
- See [mixing rules](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/class:multiclassing) for spell slot progression.

## 1st Level – Execution

You deliver a single, decisive strike to end your foes.

- You can use this feature when you hit a creature with a melee weapon with the [**two handed**](/en/library/rules/weapon-properties) property you are wielding (two-handed weapon, or a versatile weapon held in two hands).
- On a hit, the attack deals **extra flat damage of the weapon's damage type equal to your Revenant level**.
- You can benefit from **Execution** only **once per turn**.
- Execution's extra damage is doubled on a critical hit.
- If a creature is left with less HP than your Revenant level after taking this damage, you can choose to instantly slay it.

</Collapsible>

## 1st Level – Obituary

You choose your **Obituary** at 1st level. Your obituary represents the form of that which made you into a Revenant, forever altering
your anatomy into a being that flows and ebbs within the veil of life and death.

## 2nd Level – Revenant Spellcasting

Beginning at second level, your studies of death and decay allow you to cast certain spells.

### Preparing and Casting Spells

The Revenant table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your revenant spells of 1st level and higher.
To cast one of these spells, you must expend a slot of the spell's level or higher.
You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest.

You prepare spells from the [_list_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/revenant/spells.list) of revenant spells that are available for you to cast.
To do so, choose a number of revenant spells from your list equal to your Intelligence modifier + half your revenant level, rounded down (minimum of one spell).
The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

You can change your list of prepared spells when you finish a long rest.

### Spellcasting Ability

Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your revenant spells.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

### Ritual Casting

You can cast a revenant spell as a ritual if that spell has the ritual tag and you have the spell in your list.
You don't need to have the spell prepared.

### Spellcasting Focus

You can use an appropriate arcane focus as a spellcasting focus for your revenant spells.

## 2nd Level – Death’s Retinue

You are never alone; your very will echoes through death and decay.

- You always have **_Find Familiar_** and **_Speak with Dead_** prepared. They count as Revenant spells for you and **don’t count** against your prepared total.
- When you cast **_Find Familiar_**, the familiar is a **Construct (Undead)** whose appearance reflects your Obituary.
- You can **reanimate a corpse** as a temporary servitor:

### Reanimate Corpse

As an **action**, choose the body of a Small or Medium creature that died within the last 10 minutes. The corpse rises as your **Retinue** until destroyed.
It acts on your initiative, immediately after you, obeying your verbal commands. If no command is issued, the retinue acts on it's own to achieve your goals to the best of their ability.
You can have only one Retinue at a time; creating a new one destroys the previous. You can use this feature **once per short or long rest**.

> ### Retinue
>
> _Medium Construct (Undead), unaligned_
>
> | **Armor Class**             | **Hit Points**              | **Speed** |
> | --------------------------- | --------------------------- | --------- |
> | 12 + your proficiency bonus | 1d8 + your CON per DK level | 25 ft.    |
>
> | STR      | DEX | CON      | INT | WIS | CHA |
> | -------- | --- | -------- | --- | --- | --- |
> | Your INT | 12  | Your CON | 2   | 10  | 6   |
>
> - **Saving Throws:** Uses your proficiency bonus; add your **CON** modifier
> - **Damage Resistances:** necrotic, cold
> - **Condition Immunities:** Terrified, poisoned
> - **Senses:** darkvision 60 ft.
> - **Languages:** understands the languages you know but can’t speak
>
> **Gravebound**
> The retinue can be affected by spells and effects that target corpses, such as [_Speak with the Dead_](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:speak-with-dead).
>
> **Actions — Grave Swipe.**  
> _Melee Weapon Attack:_ your spell attack modifier to hit, reach 5 ft.; one target.  
> **Hit:** 1d4 + your INT modifier necrotic damage. Target's speed is reduced by 5 ft. until the end of their next turn.
>
> **Cold Hand of Death.**  
> When **you** hit with **Execution**, you may use **your bonus action** to have the Retinue immediately attempt to **grapple** the target, provided they are within their reach.
> The retinue uses **your Athletics score**. This does not use the Retinues action.
>
> **Corpse Consumption.**  
> As a **bonus action**, the Retinue can devour a corpse or destroyed construct within 5 ft. The body crumbles; the Retinue regains hit points equal to **your Revenant level + your INT modifier**.
> A consumed corpse is destroyed.
> The Retinue cannot use this feature again until after a short or long rest.

## 5th Level – Abundance and Decay

As an **action**, choose a point you can see within 30 feet. The ground in a **10-foot-radius circle** centered on that point becomes corrupted for **1 minute**.
Only one such area can exist at a time, and you can dismiss it at any time **with an action** .

While the area is active, creatures other than you and your summons within it have their **Armor Class reduced by your Intelligence modifier** (minimum reduction of 1).
A creature may only be affected by one such area at a time.
Once per turn, when you or your **Retinue** deal damage with _Execution_, _Grave Swipe_ or a _Revenant Spell_, to a creature inside the area, you regain hit points equal to your **Intelligence modifier** (minimum of 1).

As a **bonus action**, you can move the corrupted area up to **10 feet** along the ground.
Moving it farther in a single turn ends the effect until you create it again.

You can create, dismiss, and reposition this area **at will**.
if you are incapacitated the area dissipates.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 7th Level – Dark Magicks

Whenever you cast a **Revenant spell** using a **spell slot of up to 5th level**, you may spend **any number of Dark Magick charges** to increase the spell’s **effective slot level** by the same amount.  
This increase has **no upper limit**.

- You have **Dark Magick charges** equal to your **INT modifier** (minimum 1).
- You regain one expended charge on a **short rest** and all expended charges return on a **long rest**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 10th Level – Tools of the End

You gain proficiency with **smith’s tools** and **alchemist’s supplies**.  
If you are already proficient, you **double** your proficiency bonus when using them.  
Repairing or crafting metal, bone, or death-related objects takes **half the usual time**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 10th Level – On a Pale Horse

You can **ritually cast spells** that summon or create creatures (such as _find steed_, _find familiar_, or any similar effect you know) as an **action** instead of 10 minutes.
doing so provokes opportunity attacks.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 11th Level – Shared Entropy

Your link with your summoned creatures becomes more prevalent, you gain the following features:

- Whenever **you** gain **temporary hit points**, any Retinue or summoned creature you control within **30 feet** gains the same amount.
- Whenever **you** regain hit points, each of your summons within **30 feet** regains the same amount.
- Spell effects that apply to you also apply to your summons within **30 feet**.
- This sharing ends for any summon that moves farther than 30 ft. from you.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 15th Level – Deathless Will

When you are reduced to **0 hit points or fewer**, you do **not** fall unconscious.  
You continue taking turns normally but still make **death saving throws** each round.

- You stop making saves if you are healed above 0 HP or finish your death saving throws (Either successfully or not).
- If you succeed, you are stabilized at 1HP, and do not fall unconscious.
- Taking damage at 0 HP or less no longer causes a failed saving throw.  
  Instead, it forces a Constitution saving throw of half the damage of the damage taken, to a minimum of 10.  
  On a failed save, you suffer one failed death saving throw.

You can use this feature once per **short or long rest**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 20th Level – Funeral Apex

Your **Intelligence** and **Constitution** scores each increase by **2**, to a maximum of **24**.  
Execution deals an additional **10 flat damage**.  
You add your **INT modifier** to all **death saving throws**.

</Collapsible>
