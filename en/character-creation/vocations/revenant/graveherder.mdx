# Graveherder

Graveherders are Revenants that puppeteer armies of corpses, disposing of them and replacing them as needed. 
Using their retinues to extend their powers and feed their enemies to the rot-world of Kalmora, they are masters of attrition and tactics.

## How one becomes a Revenant

You died in a place of the dead. A grave you dug yourself, a battlefield full of comrades, a pit of old bones, a burrow of Nigredo’s puppets—somewhere beneath the soil, the dead pulled you back up; they refused to let you join them.

## 1st Level — Death Coil

Once per round, when you cast a **spell** or use **Execution**, you may choose one of your **summoned creatures** (including your Retinue) as the **point of origin** for that spell or attack.

- The chosen creature must be within **30 feet** of you.
- The spell or attack can target or affect only creatures or areas within **30 feet** of the chosen creature, even if its normal range is greater.
- Line of sight and line of effect are measured from the chosen creature.
- The spell or attack is still made by **you**, using your own attack rolls, modifiers, and save DC.
- If the casting includes a weapon attack, that attack can also originate from the chosen creature, but you must use your own attack rolls and modifiers.
- You may project **only one** spell or Execution per round.
- The chosen creature does not cast the spell and does not expend its actions.

## 3rd Level — Children of the Grave

You can command multiple corpse-shells simultaneously.

While your Retinue is active, you may use a **bonus action** to animate a **second Retinue**, provided there is a **Medium or Small humanoid corpse** within **5 feet** of you or one of your Retinues.  
The corpse is consumed.  
You can control up to two retinues, this increases when you reach certain levels in this specialization.

Your Retinues share a **single hit point pool**, equal to the Retinue’s normal hit points.  
If the pool is reduced to 0, all Retinues collapse.

If two or more Retinues would take damage from the **same area effect**, or would be affected by the same spell, it takes effect **only once**.

Your Retinue’s **Grave Swipe** damage die becomes **1d6**, and it becomes **1d10** at 14th level, and **1d12** at 18th level.

## 6th Level — Explode Corpse

### Explode Corpse

As an **action**, you cause one **corpse** —or one of your **active Retinues**— within **10 feet** of you or one of your Retinues to explode.  
Each creature within **5 feet** of the exploding corpse or Retinue must make a **Dexterity saving throw** against your spell save DC.

- On a failed save, a creature takes **necrotic damage equal to your Execution’s flat damage**.
- On a success, it takes **half** as much.

The destroyed corpse or Retinue cannot be used to create a new Retinue.

## 6th Level — Embalmer's Knowledge

You gain proficiency in **Medicine** or **Nature** (your choice).  
If you are already proficient in both, you may gain expertise in one of these skills.

---

## 14th Level — Gravelink

You may now control up to **three** Retinues simultaneously.  
They follow the same corpse-consumption and shared hit point rules as before.

Once per round, when one of your Retinues hits a creature with **Grave Swipe**, each of your other Retinues may move up to **10 feet** toward that creature.  
This movement does **not** provoke opportunity attacks.

When a Retinue or any summoned creature you control within **30 feet** would take damage, you may use your **reaction** to take that damage **instead**.  
The creature takes **0** damage.  
You take the **full amount as true damage**. 
You cannot use this feature while unconscious.

---

## 18th Level — Kalmora’s Grasp

As an **action**, choose one creature within **30 feet** of you or one of your Retinues.  
The target must make a **Charisma saving throw** against your spell save DC.

On a failed save:

- The creature is **banished** to the Rot Pits of Kalmora for **1 minute**.
- While banished, it is **incapacitated** and cannot take actions or reactions.
- It automatically **fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws**.
- At the **start of each of its turns**, it takes **5d12 necrotic damage**.
- If reduced to 0 hit points while banished, it dies insatantly and it's body dissolves into the rot-pits of Kalmora.
- A creature may repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.

On a successful save:

- The creature takes **half** of 5d12 necrotic damage and is not banished.
- You may use this feature **once per short or long rest**.
