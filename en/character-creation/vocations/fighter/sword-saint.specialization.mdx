# Sword Saint

To be a Sword Saint is to wield blades in impossible ways, use miraculous techniques, and perform deeds of strength that'd be praised in song if t'were witnessed by trovadeurs or bards. Like the heroes of yore, the Sword Saint is a force of legend.

## 3rd Level – Twin Paths of Mastery
At 3rd level, choose one of the following paths. This choice is permanent.

### Colossal Grip

- You may wield two weapons with the **two-handed** property, one in each hand.
- The secondary two handed melee weapon is treated as an **off-hand weapon** for the purposes of two-weapon fighting.
- You gain access to **Cross Slash**.

#### Cross Slash

As an **action**, you unleash a sweeping **15-foot cone** of strikes.

Each creature in the area must make a **Dexterity saving throw** (DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Strength modifier).

- On a **failed save**, the creature **takes damage as if hit by both of your melee weapon attacks**, including all applicable weapon damage dice.
- On a **successful save**, the creature takes **half** as much damage.

You can use this technique a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus** per short rest.

---

### Perfected Grip

- You may wield a **two-handed melee weapon in one hand** and a **one-handed melee weapon in the other**. You cannot use shields while doing so.
- The secondary, one-handed melee weapon is treated as an **off-hand weapon** for the purposes of two-weapon fighting.
- It follows standard rules for two-weapon fighting, including the need for a **light** weapon or the use of the **Two-Weapon Fighting** style.
- You gain the following abilities:

#### Surgical Lunge

You may replace **one** of your attacks with this technique.

As part of the attack, you move in a **20-foot line**. This movement does **not** provoke opportunity attacks. If you encounter a wall or obstacle, you stop at the point of impact.

Each creature within a **5-foot wide, 20-foot long line** originating from your starting point must make a **Dexterity saving throw** (DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Strength modifier).

- On a **failed save**, the creature **takes damage as if hit by one of your off-hand weapon attacks**, using only the weapon’s damage dice (no modifiers).
- On a **successful save**, the creature takes **no damage**.

You can use this technique a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus** per short rest.

---

#### Iron Reserve

- Once per round, you may **replace a two-handed melee weapon attack with an off-hand weapon attack**.
- When you do, you gain **temporary hit points equal to half the damage rolled**, to a minimum of one.

## 7th Level – Saint’s Reversal
- When a creature makes a melee attack against you, you may use your **reaction** to initiate a **contested attack roll**.
- If your roll equals or exceeds theirs, negate the attack and make a **main-hand weapon attack** as part of the same reaction.
- Your attack uses your **contested roll** result as the attack roll.
- If you fail, resolve the attack as normal.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 10th Level – Saint’s Footwork
- After you make a melee weapon attack (hit or miss), you may move **5 feet**.
- This movement **provokes opportunity attacks** if you leave a creature’s reach.
- You cannot use this movement to break from grapples or similar movement impairing effects.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 15th Level – Martial Domination
- When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you may force them to make a **Charisma saving throw** (DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Strength modifier).
- On a **failure**, they are affected as if by the **Bane** spell for **1 minute**.
- Affected creatures may repeat the save at the **end of each of their turns**.
- This feature may be used **once per creature per minute**. If a creature succeeds, it is **immune to this effect for 24 hours**.
- This is a **non-magical effect**.

</Collapsible>

## 18th Level – Final Divergence
Your chosen path manifests its final technique.

### Colossal Grip – To Die a Thousand Deaths

- Your **reach with all two-handed melee weapons increases by 5 feet**.

You gain access to the following technique:

**To Die a Thousand Deaths.**  
As an **action**, you activate this technique. You **Disengage** as part of the same action.

Until the end of the current turn, whenever you **enter melee range** of a creature (your increased reach), that creature must make a **Dexterity saving throw** (DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Strength modifier).

- On a **failed save**, the creature **takes damage as if hit by one of your melee weapon attacks**, including all weapon damage dice and any features or effects that trigger when you hit with a melee weapon attack.
- On a **successful save**, the creature takes **half** as much damage.

You can use this technique a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus** per long rest.

---

### Perfected Grip – Hesitation is Defeat

- When you succeed on a **Saint’s Reversal**, you **immediately regain your reaction**.
- There is **no limit per creature**—you may continue to parry and counter as long as you win the contested rolls.
- Each subsequent successful **Saint’s Reversal** gains a -1 penalty to the contested roll.
- You may **replace any number of your attacks with Surgical Lunges**.

</Collapsible>
