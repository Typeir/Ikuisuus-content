# Paladin

Paladins are united by their oaths to stand against annihilation and corruption. Whether sworn before a god’s altar, in a sacred grove, or in solitude with the dead as witness, a Paladin’s oath is a powerful bond—turning a devout warrior into a blessed champion.

Paladins master weapons and armor but rely even more on divine magic to heal the injured, smite foes, and protect the helpless. Their life is one of adventure and cosmic struggle, lived on the front lines against corruption and ruin.

| **Starting Equipment**         | (A) Chain Mail, Shield, Longsword, 6 Javelins, Holy Symbol, Priest's Pack, 9 GP; or (B) 150 GP |

## Becoming a Paladin

### As a Level 1 Character

- Gain all Core Paladin Traits.
- Gain the Paladin’s 1st-level vocation features, listed in the Paladin Features table.

### As a Mixed Character

- Gain the Hit Point Die.
- Gain proficiency with Martial weapons.
- Gain training with Light and Medium armor and Shields.
- Gain the Paladin’s 1st-level vocation features.
- See [mixing rules](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/class:multiclassing) for spell slot progression.

---

## Breaking Your Oath

If you unrepentantly violate your oath, speak with your DM about appropriate consequences, such as changing your specialization or retraining into another vocation.

## Vocation Features

## 1st Level – Lay On Hands

Your blessed touch can heal wounds. You have a pool of healing power that replenishes when you finish a Long Rest. With that pool, you can restore a total number of Hit Points equal to **five times your Paladin level**.

As a **Bonus Action**, you can touch a creature (which could be yourself) and draw power from the pool to restore a number of Hit Points to that creature, up to the amount remaining in the pool.

You can also expend **5 Hit Points** from the pool to remove the **Poisoned** condition from the creature. Those points don’t also restore Hit Points.

---

## 1st Level – Spellcasting

You have learned to cast spells through prayer and meditation. You choose your spells from the [_Paladin spell list_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/paladin/spells).

- **Spell Slots.** The Paladin Features table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your spells of 1st level or higher. You regain all expended slots when you finish a Long Rest.
- **Prepared Spells.** You prepare Paladin spells of 1st level or higher. The number you can prepare is shown in the Prepared Spells column of the table. The chosen spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.
- **Changing Spells.** Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can replace one prepared spell with another Paladin spell.
- **Spellcasting Ability.** Charisma is your spellcasting ability for Paladin spells.
- **Spellcasting Focus.** You can use a Holy Symbol as a spellcasting focus for Paladin spells.

---

## 1st Level – Weapon Mastery

Your training allows you to use the mastery properties of **two weapons of your choice** with which you have proficiency.

Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can change your chosen weapons.

---

## 2nd Level – Fighting Style

You gain a **Fighting Style feat** of your choice.

Instead of choosing a feat, you can choose the option below:

**Blessed Warrior.**  
You learn two Cleric cantrips of your choice. They count as Paladin spells for you, and Charisma is your spellcasting ability for them. Whenever you gain a Paladin level, you can replace one of these cantrips with another Cleric cantrip.

---

## 2nd Level – Paladin’s Smite

You always have **Divine Smite** prepared.

You can cast it **once without expending a spell slot**, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a Long Rest.

---

## 3rd Level – Channel Divinity

You can channel divine energy from the Outer Planes to fuel magical effects.

- You have **two uses** of Channel Divinity.
- You regain **one use on a Short Rest** and **all uses on a Long Rest**.
- You gain an additional use at **11th level**.

If an effect requires a saving throw, the DC equals your Paladin spell save DC.

**Divine Sense.**  
As a **Bonus Action**, you detect Celestials, Fiends, and Undead within **60 feet** for **10 minutes**, unless you become Incapacitated. You also detect consecrated or desecrated places or objects in that area.

---

## 4th Level – Ability Score Improvement

You gain the **[Ability Score Improvement feat](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/feat:ability-score-improvement)** or another **[feat of your choice](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/feat:all)**.

You gain this feature again at levels **8, 12 and 16**.

---

## 5th Level – Extra Attack

You can attack **twice**, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

---

## 5th Level – Faithful Steed

You can call on the aid of an otherworldly steed.

You always have **Find Steed** prepared. You can cast it once without expending a spell slot, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a Long Rest.

---

## 6th Level – Aura of Protection

You radiate a protective aura in a **10-foot aura**. The aura is inactive while you are Incapacitated.

You and your allies in the aura gain a bonus to saving throws equal to your **Charisma modifier** (minimum +1).

A creature can benefit from only one Aura of Protection at a time.

---

## 9th Level – Abjure Foes

As a **Magic action**, you can expend one use of Channel Divinity to overwhelm foes with awe.

Choose a number of creatures you can see within **60 feet** equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum one). Each target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be **Frightened** for 1 minute or until it takes damage. While Frightened this way, a creature can take only one of the following on its turn: move, take an action, or take a Bonus Action.

---

## 10th Level – Aura of Courage

You and your allies have **immunity to the Frightened condition** while within your Aura of Protection.

---

## 11th Level – Radiant Strikes

When you hit a target with a Melee weapon attack or an Unarmed Strike, the target takes an extra **1d8 Radiant damage**.

---

## 14th Level – Restoring Touch

When you use Lay On Hands on a creature, you can also remove one or more of the following conditions: **Blinded, Deafened, Frightened, Paralyzed, or Stunned**.

You must expend **5 Hit Points** from the Lay On Hands pool for each condition removed.

---

## 18th Level – Aura Expansion

Your Aura of Protection is now a **30-foot aura**.

---

## 19th Level – Epic Boon

You gain an **Epic Boon feat** or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

---

## 21st Level – Divine Will

When an ally affected by your Aura of Protection makes a saving throw and fails, you can expend one use of Channel Divinity as a reaction to allow the ally to succeed instead.
Additionally, you gain one additional use of Channel Divinity.
