# Fiend Patron

_Make a Deal with the Hidden Kingdom_

Your pact draws on the Hidden Kingdom, where contracts are the local physics. You might forge a bargain with one of the great Hiisi — a Silent One old enough to have a court and a grudge — or with something out of the Everdark that keeps its word precisely and its intentions to itself. Such a patron's aims run to the corruption or dismantling of things, ultimately including you, and your path is defined by how much of that you are willing to be party to.

<Collapsible>
## 1st Level – Dark One’s Blessing
When you reduce an enemy to 0 Hit Points, you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to your Charisma modifier plus your Villein level (minimum of 1 Temporary Hit Point). You also gain this benefit if someone else reduces an enemy within 10 feet of you to 0 Hit Points.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible open>

<Collapsible>
## 6th Level – Dark One’s Own Luck
You can call on your fiendish patron to alter fate in your favor. When you make an ability check or a saving throw, you can add [% 1d10 %] to your roll. You can do so after seeing the roll but before any of the roll’s effects occur.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once), but no more than once per roll. You regain all expended uses when you finish a Recovery.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 10th Level – Fiendish Resilience
Choose one damage type, other than Force, whenever you finish a Repose or Recovery. You have Resistance to that damage type until you choose a different one with this feature.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 14th Level – Hurl Through Hell
Once per turn when you hit a creature with an attack roll, you can try to drag the target briefly through the Hidden Kingdom. The target must succeed on a Charisma saving throw against your spell save DC, or it disappears and hurtles through a nightmare landscape.

- If the target isn’t a Fiend, it takes [% 8d10 Psychic %].
- The target has the Incapacitated condition until the end of your next turn, when it returns to the space it previously occupied or the nearest unoccupied space.

Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a Recovery unless you expend a Pact Magic spell slot (no action required) to restore your use of it.

</Collapsible>
