# Vocations

Vocations are core aspects of characters.
They define what the character is capable of and how they interact with the world.
Some vocations focus on physical prowess, while others emphasize mental acuity or mystical power, some mix and match these elements.
Some vocations are combat-oriented, while others excel in support or utility roles.
Some vocations are stumbled upon, while others are chosen through rigorous training or innate talent.
Each vocation provides a unique set of abilities, proficiencies, and features that shape the character's identity and playstyle.

## Choosing a Vocation

At 1st level, you choose a vocation for your character.
Your vocation grants you features at 1st level, and will continue to grant you features as you gain levels in that vocation.

## Mixing Vocations

You may choose to mix vocations as you level up, gaining features from multiple vocations.
When you gain a level in a vocation, you gain the features listed for that level in the vocation's description.
Some vocation features, such as "Extra Attack", do not stack if you gain them from multiple vocations, unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Some vocations provide spellcasting abilities.
If you gain spellcasting from multiple vocations, you can use the spells known and spell slots from all your vocations combined, and your spell slot progression is determined by the total combined levels in all your spellcasting vocations.

Some vocations gain spell slots slower than others, these are known as "Spellblades" spellblades".
When calculating your spell slots, each level in a spellblade vocation counts as half a level (rounded down).

Some specializations within vocations may also provide spellcasting abilities, which function similarly to vocation spellcasting, at different rates of progression,
for example, the Eldritch Knight specialization of the Fighter vocation gains spellcasting at a third of the normal rate, so each level in Eldritch Knight counts as one-third of a level when calculating spell slots.
