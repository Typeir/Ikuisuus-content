# Ranger

Far from bustling cities, amid the trees of trackless forests and across wide plains, Rangers keep their unending watch in the wilderness. Rangers learn to track their quarry as a predator does, moving stealthily through the wilds and hiding themselves in brush and rubble.

Thanks to their connection with nature, Rangers can also cast spells that harness primal powers of the wilderness. A Ranger's talents and magic are honed with deadly focus to protect the world from the ravages of monsters and tyrants.

## Becoming a Ranger

### As a Level 1 Character

- Gain all Core Ranger Traits.
- Gain the Ranger’s 1st-level vocation features, listed in the Ranger Features table.

### As a Mixed Character

- Gain the Hit Point Die.
- Gain proficiency with Martial weapons.
- Gain training with Light and Medium armor and Shields.
- Gain the Ranger’s 1st-level vocation features.
- See the mixing rules to determine your available spell slots.

## Vocation Features

<Collapsible>
## 1st Level – Spellcasting

You can channel the magical essence of nature to cast spells. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability.
You can use a **Druidic Focus** as a spellcasting focus. You choose your spells from the [_Ranger spell list_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/spells.list).

**Prepared Spells.** You prepare a number of spells equal to your **Prepared Spells** column.

**Spell Slots.** You regain all spell slots after a Long Rest.

**Changing Spells.** You can change one spell from your list of prepared spells after a Long Rest.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 1st Level – Lay of the Land

You understand the world as a hunter does, through prey and through paths.

You gain **three selections** from the [_Lay of the Land_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land) lists. Each selection must be either a [_Prey_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#prey) or a [Path](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#path).

- **Preys** represent creatures you study, stalk, and understand.
- **Paths** represent environments, routes, and methods of traversal you have mastered.

All **Preys** and **Paths** you know are always active.

You gain two additional **Lay of the Land** selections at levels **5, 9, 13, and 17**.

When you gain an additional selection, you can choose to replace one of your existing selections with a different one.

##### Prey

You have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track your prey, as well as on Intelligence checks to recall information about them.

When you gain this feature, you also learn one language of your choice that is spoken by your prey, or that would otherwise be beneficial to study or hunt them.

As a bonus action, you can mark a creature from your [_Prey_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#prey) selections that you can see within 120 feet of you.
This mark lasts for 1 hour or until the creature dies. You can have only one creature marked at a time.
When attacking your **Marked Prey**, you deal an additional 1d6 damage of the same type as the weapon you are using.
Damage you deal to prey is considered magical for the purpose of overcoming resistances and immunities.

As you gain levels, your choices should reflect the types of monsters you have encountered on your adventures.

##### Path

When you make an Intelligence or Wisdom check related to your path, you can treat a roll of 9 or lower on the d20 as a 10.
Your movement speed increases by five feet while you are on your path.

While traveling for an hour or more in your path, you gain the following benefits:

Difficult terrain doesn’t slow your group’s travel.
Your group can’t become lost except by magical means.
Even when you are engaged in another activity while traveling (such as foraging, navigating, or tracking), you remain alert to danger.
If you are traveling alone, you can move stealthily at a normal pace.
When you forage, you find twice as much food as you normally would.
While tracking other creatures, you also learn their exact number, their sizes, and how long ago they passed through the area.

</Collapsible>

## 1st Level – Weapon Mastery

You can use the **mastery properties of two weapons** you have proficiency with. You can change these weapons after a Long Rest.

- **Expertise.** Gain Expertise in one skill you're proficient with.

## 2nd Level – Fighting Style

Choose a [Fighting Style feat](/en/library/character-creation/feats/fighting-styles), or select:

When you gain a Fighting Style this way, you do not increase an ability score.

**Druidic Warrior.** Learn two Druid cantrips. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability.

## 5th Level – Extra Attack

You can attack twice when you take the Attack action.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 6th Level – Cunning Hunter

Whenever you take the **Magic**, **Dodge**, **Hide**, **Dash** or **Disengage** actions, you can use [_Trinkets_](/en/library/items/trinkets/main) as part of the same Action, provided the trinket has a usage time of 1 action or less.
If those trinkets would impose a saving throw, the DC equals your spell save DC, and you can use your spell attack bonus instead of the normal attack bonus.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 9th Level – To Take The Better Beast

Once per short rest, you can choose to gain the benefits of a [_Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#path) your current terrain qualifies for, and
the benefits of [_Prey_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#prey) against the _creature type_ of a creature you can see within 120 feet of you.

This effect lasts for an hour, and it ends early if you are incapacitated or die.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 10th Level – Tireless

- **Temporary Hit Points.** As a Magic Action, gain 1d8 + Wisdom modifier Temp HP.
- **Decrease Exhaustion.** When you finish a Short Rest, reduce your Exhaustion level by 1.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 13th Level – One With Land

While you are on your [_Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#path)
You can cast Rituals you know as a Magic Action, ignoring the normal casting time of the spell.

Additionally, your movement speed increases by an additional 5 feet while you are on your [_Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#path).

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 14th Level – Nature's Veil

As a Bonus Action, you can become Invisible until the end of your next turn.
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier, and regain all expended uses after a Long Rest.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 17th Level – Precise Hunter

Your [_Prey_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#prey) damage die becomes **2d6** instead of **1d6**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 18th Level – Feral Senses

You gain **Blindsight** to 30 feet.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 19th Level – Epic Boon

You gain an [Epic Boon feat](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/feat:all). **Boon of Dimensional Travel** is recommended.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 20th Level – Foe Slayer

Your [_Prey_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#prey) damage die becomes **3d6**, and you have advantage on attack rolls while you are on your [_Path_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/ranger/lay-of-the-land#path).

</Collapsible>
