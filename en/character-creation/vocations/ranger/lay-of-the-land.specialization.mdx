# Lay Of The Land

A lay of the land is a specific **Prey** or **Path** that you have studied and understand.  
Each lay of the land provides benefits according to your ranger levels and the specialization you choose.

## Prey

A **Prey** is a specific type of creature that you have studied and understand. You can choose a **Prey** from the following list:

- **Aberrations**
- **Beasts**
- **Monstrosities**
- **Constructs**
- **Celestials**
- **Plants**
- **Humanoids**
- **Elementals**

Some **Preys** have specific subtypes. If a creature has a subtype, you do not gain the benefits of the **Prey** against that creature unless you have chosen the specific subtype as your **Prey**.  
For example, if you choose **Humanoids (Dwarves)** as your **Prey**, you do not gain the benefits of that **Prey** against a human or a gnome.
If you choose **Humanoids** without a subtype, you will seldom gain the benefits of that **Prey** against any humanoid, as most humanoids have a subtype.

Whenever you pick a **Prey** with a subtype, you can pick a different subtype of the same or another **Prey** as part of that selection.  
For example, you could choose **Humanoids (Dwarves)** and **Humanoids (Humans)** as part of a single lay of the land selection.

A common subtype of creature is **X (Ooze)** or **X (Undead)**, some Oozes might be _Constructs_ at heart, while others might be simple _Plant_ creatures, or _Monstrosities_ made by magicks.

## Path

A **Path** is a specific type of terrain that you have studied and understand. You can choose a **Path** from the following list:

- **Frigid** (An environment with _extreme cold_, such as _arctic_ tundras or _icy_ mountains)
- **Mountainous** (An environment characterized by _high elevations_, _sheer slopes_, and _rugged, rocky terrain_)
- **Swampy** (An environment dominated by _wetlands_, _marshes_, and _bogs_, often with _dense vegetation_ and _standing water_)
- **Arid** (An environment with _very little rainfall_, such as _deserts_ or _dry plains_, or land that is otherwise _barren_ and _inhospitable_)
- **Plain** (An environment characterized by _flat, open land_ with _few trees_ or natural obstacles, such as _grasslands_ or _savannas_)
- **Forested** (An environment covered in _dense trees_ and _thick undergrowth_, such as _jungles_ or _temperate forests_)
- **Coastal** (An environment _adjacent to the ocean_, including _beaches_, _cliffs_, and _tidal zones_)
- **Cavernous** (An environment consisting of _caves_, _underground tunnels_, and _subterranean spaces_)
- **Urban** (An environment characterized by _human-made structures_, _streets_, _buildings_, and _cityscapes_)
- **Volcanic** (An environment dominated by _volcanic activity_, including _lava flows_, _ash fields_, and _geothermal features_)
- **Ethereal** (An environment defined by a _lack of stable features_, such as _outer space_, _derelict astral objects_, or _featureless worlds_)
- **Windswept** (An environment shaped by _strong, persistent winds_, often found at _high altitudes_ or on _floating landmasses_)
- **Aquatic** (An environment dominated by _water_, such as _oceans_, _lakes_, or _deep underwater regions_)
