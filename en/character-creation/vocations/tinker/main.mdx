# Tinker

Masters of invention, Tinkers use ingenuity and magic to unlock extraordinary capabilities in objects.
They see magic as a complex system waiting to be decoded and then harnessed with their spells and inventions.

---

## Core Tinker Traits

| Trait                          | Description                                                                                                                       |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary Ability**            | **Intelligence**                                                                                                                  |
| **Hit Point Die**              | **d8 per Tinker level**                                                                                                           |
| **Saving Throw Proficiencies** | **Constitution, Intelligence**                                                                                                    |
| **Skill Proficiencies**        | **Pick two from: Arcana, History, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Sleight of Hand**                                  |
| **Weapon Proficiencies**       | **Simple**                                                                                                                        |
| **Tool Proficiencies**         | **Thieves’ tools, Tinker’s tools, and one type of artisan’s tools of your choice**                                                |
| **Armor Training**             | **Light, Medium, Shields**                                                                                                        |
| **Starting Equipment**         | **Choose A or B: (A) studded leather armor, dagger, thieves’ tools, tinker’s tools, dungeoneer’s pack, and 16 GP; or (B) 150 GP** |

## Becoming a Tinker

### As a Level 1 Character

- Gain all traits listed in the **Core Tinker Traits** table.
- Gain the Tinker’s level 1 features.

---

### As a Mixed Character

- Gain the Tinker’s **Hit Point Die**.
- Gain proficiency with **Tinker’s Tools**.
- Pick one skill proficiency from the Core Tinker Traits table.
- Gain training with **Light and Medium armor and Shields**.
- Gain the Tinker’s level 1 features.
- See [mixing rules](http://dnd2024.wikidot.com/class:multiclassing) for spell slot progression, adding **half your Tinker levels (round up)**.

## 1st Level – Spellcasting

You have learned how to channel magical energy through objects.
See the Player’s Handbook for the rules on spellcasting.
The information below details how you use those rules with Tinker spells, which appear in the Tinker Spell List.

### Tools Required

You produce your Tinker spells through tools.
You can use **Thieves’ Tools**, **Tinker’s Tools**, or another kind of **artisan’s tools** with which you have proficiency as a Spellcasting Focus,
and you must have one of those focuses **in hand** when you cast a Tinker spell (meaning the spell has an **M** component when you cast it).

### Cantrips

You know **two** Tinker cantrips of your choice.
_Choose from the Tinker spell list_.

Whenever you finish a **Long Rest**, you can replace **one** of your cantrips from this feature with another Tinker cantrip of your choice.

When you reach Tinker levels **10** and **14**, you learn another Tinker cantrip of your choice, as shown in the **Cantrips** column of the Tinker Vocation Features table.

### Spell Slots

The Tinker Vocation Features table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your level 1+ spells.
You regain all expended slots when you finish a **Long Rest**.

### Prepared Spells of Level 1+

You prepare the list of level 1+ spells that are available for you to cast with this feature.
To start, choose **two** level 1 Tinker spells.

The number of spells on your list increases as you gain Tinker levels, as shown in the **Prepared Spells** column of the Tinker Vocation Features table.
Whenever that number increases, choose additional Tinker spells until the number of spells on your list matches the number on the table.
The chosen spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

If another Tinker feature gives you spells that you always have prepared, those don’t count against the number of spells you can prepare with this feature,
but those spells otherwise count as Tinker spells for you.

### Changing Your Prepared Spells

Whenever you finish a **Long Rest**, you can change your list of prepared spells, replacing any of the spells there with other Tinker spells for which you have spell slots.

### Spellcasting Ability

**Intelligence** is your spellcasting ability for your Tinker spells.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

## 2nd Level – Tinker’s Gadgetry

You have learned arcane plans that you use to create temporary magic items.

### Plans Known

When you gain this feature, choose **four** gadget plans to learn from the gadgets available to you.
Whenever you gain a Tinker level, you can replace one of the plans you know with a new plan for which you qualify.

You learn another plan of your choice when you reach certain Tinker levels, as shown in the **Plans Known** column of the Tinker Vocation Features table.

### Gadgets Available

At Tinker level **2**, the level 2 gadgets become available to you.
At higher levels, additional gadgets become available.

See [_Tinker Gadgets_](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/tinker/gadgets).

### Creating a Gadget

When you finish a **Long Rest**, you can create **one or two** different gadgets if you have **Tinker’s Tools** in hand.
Each gadget you create is a **magic item**, based on one of the gadget plans you know for this feature.

If a created gadget requires **Attunement**, you can attune yourself to it the instant you create it.
If you decide to attune to the gadget later, you must do so using the normal process for Attunement.

When you reach certain Tinker levels specified in the **Gadgets** column of the Tinker Vocation Features table,
the number of gadgets you can create at the end of a Long Rest increases.
Each gadget you create must be based on a different plan you know.

You can’t have more gadgets from this feature than the number shown in the **Gadgets** column of the Tinker Vocation Features table for your level.
If you try to exceed your maximum number of gadgets for this feature, the oldest gadget vanishes, and then the new gadget appears.

### Duration

A gadget created by this feature functions as the normal magic item, except its magic isn’t permanent.
When you die, the gadget vanishes after **1d4 days**.

If you replace a plan you know with a new plan, any gadget created with the replaced plan immediately vanishes.

If a gadget that you created with this feature is a container, such as a **Bag of Holding**, and it vanishes,
its contents harmlessly appear in and around its space.

### Spellcasting Focus

You can use any **wand** or **weapon** created by this feature as a Spellcasting Focus in lieu of using a set of artisan’s tools.

## 6th Level – Magic Item Tinker

Your **Tinker’s Gadgetry** feature gains the following options.

### Charge Magic Item

As a **Bonus Action**, you can touch a magic item within **5 feet** of yourself that you created with **Tinker’s Gadgetry** and that uses charges.
You expend a level 1+ spell slot and recharge the item.
The number of charges the item regains is equal to the level of spell slot expended.

### Drain Magic Item

As a **Bonus Action**, you can touch a magic item within **5 feet** of yourself that you created with **Tinker’s Gadgetry** and cause the item to vanish,
converting its magical energy into a spell slot.

The slot is level **1** if the item is **Common** or level **2** if the item is **Uncommon or Rare**.

Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a **Long Rest**.
Any spell slot you create with this feature vanishes when you finish a **Long Rest**.

### Transmute Magic Item

As a **Magic action**, you can touch one magic item within **5 feet** of yourself that you created with **Tinker’s Gadgetry** and transform it into a different magic item.
The resulting item must be based on a gadget plan you know.

Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a **Long Rest**.

</Collapsible>

## 7th Level – Flash of Genius

When you or a creature you can see within **30 feet** of you fails an ability check or a saving throw,
you can take a **Reaction** to add a bonus to the roll, potentially causing it to succeed.
The bonus equals your **Intelligence modifier** (minimum of **+1**).

You can take this Reaction a number of times equal to your **Intelligence modifier** (minimum of once).
You regain all expended uses when you finish a **Long Rest**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 10th Level – Gadget Adept

If an item would **impose a saving throw** or **require an attack roll** to use, you can use your own spellcasting ability modifier instead of the normal ability for that item.
Additionally, you gain a bonus to the save DC, ability checks, or attack rolls from item actions equal to **half your proficiency bonus**.
This only applies to rolls performed through the **Use an Item** action, and not to rolls performed through the **Magic** or **Attack** actions.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 11th Level – Spell-Storing Item

Whenever you finish a **Long Rest**, you can touch one **Simple or Martial weapon** or one item that you can use as a **Spellcasting Focus**,
and you store a spell in it, choosing a level **1**, **2**, or **3** Tinker spell that has a casting time of an action and doesn’t require a Material component
that is consumed by the spell (you needn’t have the spell prepared).

While holding the object, a creature can take a **Magic action** to produce the spell’s effect from it, using your spellcasting ability modifier.
If the spell requires **Concentration**, the creature must concentrate.

Once a creature has used the object to produce the spell’s effect, the object can’t be used this way again until the start of the creature’s next turn.

The spell stays in the object until it’s been used a number of times equal to **twice your Intelligence modifier** (minimum of twice)
or until you use this feature again to store a spell in an object.

</Collapsible>

## 14th Level – Advanced Artifice

You gain the following benefits.

### Magic Item Savant

You can ignore **all vocation, bloodline, attribute, proficiency or alignment restrictions imposed by attunement** on magic items, and ignore all penalties for failing to attune to them. If an item would require a check or roll to attune to, you roll with advantage.

### Refreshed Genius

When you finish a **Short Rest**, you regain **one** expended use of your **Flash of Genius** feature.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 18th Level – Magic Item Master

Whenever you use the **Use an Item** action to activate a magic item, you can choose to activate it without expending any charges or uses of the item.
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your **Intelligence modifier** (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a **Long Rest**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
## 20th Level – Soul of Artifice

You have developed a mystical connection to your magic items, which you can draw on for aid.
You gain the following benefits.

### Cheat Death

If you’re reduced to **0 Hit Points** but not killed outright, you can disintegrate any number of **Uncommon or Rare** magic items created by your **Tinker’s Gadgetry** feature.
If you do so, your Hit Points instead change to a number equal to **20 times** the number of magic items disintegrated.

### Magical Guidance

When you finish a **Short Rest**, you regain all expended uses of your **Flash of Genius** if you have **Attunement** to at least one magic item.

</Collapsible>
