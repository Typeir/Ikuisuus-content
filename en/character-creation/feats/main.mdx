# Feats

**Feats** represent special training, natural talents, or extraordinary abilities that set adventurers apart. Each feat grants unique capabilities that enhance your character's effectiveness in and out of combat.

In the world of Damocles, feats are gained through:
- **Vocation Features**: Many vocations grant feats at specific levels (such as Fighter's Fighting Style).
- **Feat Selections**: When your vocation grants you a feat or when your proficiency bonus increases, you choose from the available feats, including the repeatable Ability Score Improvement feat.
- **Epic Boons**: At high levels (19+), some classes grant access to legendary feats that transcend normal mortal capabilities.
- **Campaign Rewards**: Your GM may grant feats as rewards for completing major story arcs or accomplishing legendary deeds.

---

## Feat Categories

### General Feats
The most common type of feat, representing specialized training or developed talents. These include combat abilities, skill enhancements, and the repeatable **Ability Score Improvement** feat that increases your ability scores.
These feats are not usually flagged as "General" in their descriptions, as they are the default type of feat.

### Origin feats
These are feats that can only be taken at character creation.

### Fighting Style Feats
Combat-focused feats that define your martial approach. Classes like Fighter and Paladin grant these as vocation features, though some can be learned through mixing or training.

### Epic Boons
Reserved for characters of legendary status (usually 19th level and higher), Epic Boons represent world-altering powers and divine gifts. Each Epic Boon is unique to the world of Damocles and reflects the influence of its gods, monsters, and cosmic forces.

---

## Feat Rules

**Prerequisites**: Some feats have prerequisites such as minimum ability scores, vocation features, or other feats. You must meet all prerequisites to select a feat.

**Multiple Selection**: Unless stated otherwise, you can select a feat only once. Some feats (such as **Ability Score Improvement**) explicitly allow multiple selections, gaining additional benefits each time.

**Replacement**: When you gain a level in a vocation that allows you to replace a feat (such as Fighter's Fighting Style), you may do so according to that vocation's rules.
