# Epic Boon: Refusal of Fate

Some wills were never meant to persist.

They were meant to burn out, to surrender their burden, to dissolve back into the machinery of the world once their task was complete.
One did not.

The First Pilgrim reached the Engine because it refused to stop walking.
Not through prophecy or through grace.
Through continuance.

Fate remembers that journey.
And so does your body.

> “Defy your fate — at any cost.”

---

When you select this feat, increase your **Any ability score** by 1, up to a maximum of **30**.

When you take this feat, you gain the following features:

- **Will Override.**  
  When you fail an attack roll, saving throw, or ability check, you may choose to treat the result as a success instead.

- **The Cost of Defiance.**  
  When you use Will Override, you lose one hit die.  
  You immediately take **true damage** equal to the maximum roll of said hit die and reduce your **maximum hit points** by the same amount, plus your constitution modifier.

- **Recovery.**  
  Lost Hit Dice and reduced maximum hit points from this feature can only be restored through a **long rest**, you only recover one hit dice at a time.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

This is not luck.  
This is not mercy.  
This is the world yielding.
