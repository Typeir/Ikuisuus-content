# Dreadful Discourse

_Prerequisite: **Charisma 16**_

When the Dreadlord spoke, some froze. The citizens of the acheron quickly learnt, fear was a language they all understood, and in fear their bodies became stiff and brittle.
Easily crushed by a single blow. Thus, the Dreadlord's words were weapons as deadly as any blade, and his followers had to learn the same art.

> “If with fear alone your body crumbles to the chisel of violence, then you have already lost the battle.” — Nekarion, the Dreadlord.  

---

When you select this feat, you gain the following benefits:

Increase your Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.

When you **speak to a creature for at least 1 minute**, and the creature **can understand you**, you may attempt to seed paranoia into its mind.

At the end of the conversation, the target must succeed on a **Wisdom saving throw** against your **Spell Save DC** or become **Frightened of you or another creature of your choice** for **1 hour**.
The effect ends early if the target is **attacked or damaged** by you or your allies, or if it **witnesses its allies being attacked or damaged** by you or your allies.

While frightened in this way, the target:
- Can’t willingly move closer to the source of its fear.
- Has **disadvantage on Charisma ability checks** against the source of its fear.

If the target succeeds on the saving throw, it has **no hint** that you attempted to frighten it.
Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a **short or long rest**.
In addition, you gain the following benefits against **frightened** creatures:

- You have **advantage on attack rolls** against them.
- Your **critical hit range increases by 1** against them (for example, a 20 becomes 19–20).
