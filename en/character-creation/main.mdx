# Character Creation

This page describes the basic process for creating a character in Damocles.  
Character creation is front-loaded: most of your character’s identity is defined at level 1 through bloodline and vocation choices.
You will also determine your ability scores, select a feat, and calculate secondary values such as hit points and armor class (AC).

This serves as a lesser guide to character creation, read each referenced section for full details on each step.

---

#### Step 1 — Choose Ability Scores

Determine your six ability scores.

Damocles supports two standard methods:

##### Standard Array

Use the following values:  
**15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8**

Assign them freely to your abilities.

For higher-powered campaigns, you may use **Standard Array +2**:

- Start from the standard array.
- Add **+2 to one ability score of your choice**.

##### Rolling

Roll **4d6**, drop the lowest die, six times.  
Assign the results freely.

---

#### Step 2 — Choose a Bloodline

Your **Bloodline** defines what you are, biologically and metaphysically.  
It determines your creature type, innate traits, and the menu of special features you can purchase.

Each bloodline has:
- **Core Features**: Always active, free, and non-optional.
- **Boons (BP)**: A list of purchasable traits, customized per bloodline.

Start by selecting one bloodline from the available list.

→ See: [Bloodlines](/en/library/character-creation/bloodlines/main)

---

#### Step 3 — Spend Bloodline BP

After choosing a bloodline, you must spend its **Boon Points (BP)**.

Every bloodline has a fixed **BP Budget**.  

BP are used to:
- Buy traits from **Suites** (tables of options)
- Purchase standalone features
- Customize anatomy, abilities, resistances, senses, and special rules

Unspent BP are lost and have no further effect.

→ See: [Bloodlines — Using BP](/en/library/character-creation/bloodlines/main#using-bp)

---

#### Step 4 — Choose a Vocation

Your **Vocation** defines what you do.

It determines:
- Core combat and utility features
- Hit dice and proficiencies
- Spellcasting (if any)
- Your long-term progression

At level 1, you choose exactly one vocation.

→ See: [Vocations](/en/library/character-creation/vocations/main)

---

#### Step 5 — Choose a Feat

At character creation, you gain **one Feat**. Some feats are only available at this time, known as **Origin Feats**.

If you select a feat that is not an **Origin Feat** at this stage, you must meet the requisites. 
Yoy gain no benefits to your Ability Scores from feat selection at this time, even if the feat normally provides them.
Other features of the feat function normally.

Feats represent exceptional training, talents, or innate advantages.  
Some vocations may grant additional feats as vocation features.

→ See: [Feats](/en/library/character-creation/feats/main)

---

#### Step 6 — Secondary Values

Once you have chosen your vocation and feat, determine your starting hit points.
Your starting hit points are equal to the maximum value of your vocation’s hit die, plus your Constitution modifier.
Some features or feats may modify this value, **_read carefully_**.

Your starting **armor class (AC)** is determined by several factors, such as armor, dexterity and features from feats, bloodlines and vocations **_read carefully_**.
Your initiative bonus is equal to your Dexterity modifier, plus any bonuses from features, feats, or items.
Your speed is determined by a combination of your bloodline, vocation, and any features or items you possess **_read carefully_**.

Alignment represents your character's moral and ethical perspective, but has little impact in a character’s abilities, sparsely referenced by effects and features.

---

#### Step 7 — Filling out the Details

Finally, you may fill out the remaining details on your character sheet, such as their story, appearance, personality traits, bonds, ideals, and flaws.
These details help bring your character to life and provide depth to their role-playing experience.

---

##### Step 8 — Preparing for character progression

As you level up, you will gain new features from your vocation and bloodline.
You may also choose to mix vocations, gaining features from multiple vocations as you level up.

To get more acquainted with character progression, see the [character progression](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/character-progression) page.
