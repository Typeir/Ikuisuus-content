# Tammeni (Dwarves)

Dwarves, known to the Pieni as the "Tammen Pieni", or Tammeni, as a short hand are compact, dense, and deliberate Pieni lineages whose culture barely survived the ruin of the Hidden Kingdom.
Their name is said to be rooted in the old word for "oak", in reference to their tree-felling myths about the construction of the black spire of ikuisuus, and their prophesied return to the world of Taiva, to fell the red tree of the end and restore the balance of the Sun and Moon.
Alas, the trees they felled were not the ones they expected, and those in the capital came to be known as the "Olivvi Pieni", or "Olvvini", in reference to the ferreous Olivares that grew near the spire and all throughout the great Elû.

The Dwarves are a practical people that value craft, knowledge, and endurance above all. Their grand city was built surrounding a great archive, and since its destruction they have been scattered across Taiva, often working as mercenaries, laborers, and scholars in the service of other peoples.

In their Diaspora, Dwarves are often misread as slow because they never rush decisions; they study, collect, plan, and execute with crushing finality.
Most dwarven settlements preserve ledgers, field notes, and quarry archives as practical inheritance from the old scholastic era, in hopes to one day return to Elû and place them within its archives.

### Dense Frame

You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

### Venom-Hardened

You have resistance to poison damage, and you are immune to the [_poisoned_](/en/library/rules/conditions#poisoned) condition.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **one additional language of your choice**.
Many dwarven communities preserve **Hidden** script for archival use.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

<Collapsible>

###### Heir of Great Sampsa <span>5 BP</span>

You are of the Lineage of **Sampsa**, the **prophesied Copper Giant** who is said to have felled the great tree whose **stump** now forms the great spire of **Ikuisuus**.
You have inherited the legacy of his strength and endurance.

You gain the following benefits:

- Your carrying capacity is **doubled**.
- You gain **proficiency** with the following [_weapons_](/en/library/items/equipment/weapons):
  - Battleaxe
  - War pick
  - Great axe
  - Halberd
  - Hand axe

While you are **wielding** a weapon from the above list, and have **mastery** with it, you gain the following benefits:

- Tree-feller of legend: You can use the mastery [_Topple_](/en/library/rules/weapon-properties#weapon-masteries) with that weapon, in **addition** to that weapon's own mastery, on **every attack**. If the weapon **already** has the **Topple** mastery, its DC **increases** by 2.
- Heir of the all-sundering: When you use the [_Masterful Blow_](/en/library/rules/weapon-properties#masterful-blow) of a weapon from the above list, You **inflict** the [_Sundered_](/en/library/rules/conditions#sundered) condition on the target until the **end** of your next turn.
- You deal **double damage** to objects and structures that are **trees** or made of **wood** of any kind.

_Notes._

> The copper giant Sampsa was said to have felled the great tree of the Hidden Kingdom, whose stump now forms the great spire of Ikuisuus. The Dwarves are said to be his descendants, and many of them inherit his strength and endurance, and like Sampsa, they hope to one day fell a tree of the end and restore the balance of the Sun and Moon.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
  ###### Through the branches of the great tree <span>5 BP</span>

You have mastered the art of careful study and focused attention; you can sharpshoot through the eye of a needle.

You gain **proficiency** and **mastery** with one martial **ranged weapon** of your choice, provided it belongs to the **firearms** category.

When you make an attack with that weapon, you can choose to take a **-5 penalty** to the **attack roll**. If the attack hits, you **add +10** to the **attack's damage**.

When you do so you **cannot ignore the weapon's loading property**, even if you have a feature that would normally allow you to ignore it.

You can use your **intelligence** modifier, **instead** of your **dexterity** modifier, for the **attack and damage rolls** of attacks you make with that weapon.

_Notes._

> When the Hiisi invaded great Elû, many of them perched on the branches of the great olivares that grew around the spire, and rained down fire on the dwarven defenders. The dwarves learned to sharpshoot through the eye of a needle to pick off those Hiisi, and many of them inherited that skill.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Cold of the Cave <span>4 BP</span>

You learn one cantrip of your choice from the **abjuration** or **necromancy** schools.
Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for it.

If you have the Spellcasting or Pact Magic feature, choose one spell from the following list:

- [_Minor Ice Rings_](/en/library/spells/minor-ice-rings)
- [_Sanctuary_](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/sanctuary)
- [_False Life_](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/false-life)
- [_Ray of Sickness_](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/ray-of-sickness)

The chosen spell is added to your spell list and is always prepared for you.

You can also cast that spell **once without expending** a spell slot, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a **long rest**.

_Notes._

> Within the stone-carved halls of great Elû Many cave-dwelling scholars once learnt to draw on the cold of the halls they inhabit for self defense, preservation and utility.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Archivist of Ikuisuus <span>4 BP</span>

Choose one language you do not already know; you learn it.

Choose one skill from the following list; you gain proficiency with it:

- Arcana
- History
- Investigation
- Nature
- Religion

Choose one tool proficiency from the following list; you gain proficiency with it:

- Smith's tools
- Brewer's supplies
- Calligrapher's supplies
- Jeweler's tools
- Mason's tools

When you take the [_Deliberate_](/en/library/rules/rolling-and-special-rolls#deliberate-roll-[dr]) option on a check made with your chosen skill or tool, **the added time is halved** (minimum 1 minute).

_Notes._

> Dwarven field-archives are carried in travel folios, slate leaves, and stitched manuals. This practice predates the Interlocking and remained intact after the collapse.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Kinetic Leverage <span>3 BP</span>

Once per turn, when you deal **bludgeoning** damage to a creature, you can **push** that creature directly away from you in a straight line up to **5 feet** to an unoccupied space, provided it is Huge or smaller.

_Notes._

> Dwarven striking disciplines are taught around momentum transfer, footing, and angles of collapse.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Scholarly Contacts <span>2 BP</span>

You have advantage on **Charisma** checks made to locate learned experts, and on **Charisma** checks made to negotiate or interview those experts once contact is established.

_Notes._

> Dwarven correspondence circles are dense and practical. If there is a specialist in a city, someone in the archive chain has a name and a prior obligation.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Literary linguist <span>2 BP</span>

Choose **three languages**. You are able to read and copy them proficiently, but you **can't speak them or understand them when spoken** nor write anything other than the most barebones phrases in them.

_Notes._

> Dwarves value the written word as an immutable record of knowledge, and many have learned distant tongues without contact with their speakers, simply to read and copy texts in those languages.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Rhetoric and Dialectics <span>2 BP</span>

You are **proficient in persuasion**. When making a persuasion check you may use **Intelligence** instead of **Charisma**.

_Notes._

> Dwarven scholars often attend public debates and lectures, and many of them have honed their rhetorical skills to a fine edge, even if their nature remains mercurial and their social graces are often lacking.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Hermenautics <span>1 BP</span>

Once per long rest, you may immediately know the **name, origin, language, author, and one notable trait** of an engraving, book, papyrus or manuscript you touch.

_Notes._

> Dwarves understand the intricacies in the craftsmanship of writing as well as the content, and can often glean information about a text just by examining it.

</Collapsible>
