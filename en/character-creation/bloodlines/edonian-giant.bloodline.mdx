# Edonian Giant

Giants of the Edo are massive, ape-like creatures, standing between 12 and 15 feet tall and weighing upwards of 500 pounds.
Their fur is thick and silvery white, their faces a contrasting red with exaggerated, uncanny features, and their limbs are disproportionately long;
This combination of features gives them a lanky, ethereal appearance despite their immense size.

They are intelligent and social beings, living in tight-knit clans in the high mountains of the Silvery Peaks, in eastern Thule.
Despite their social structure, most Edonians are reclusive and wary of outlanders, preferring to keep to themselves and avoid contact with foreigners.

The Edonian culture is rich with musical and smithing traditions, and they are known for their skill in crafting intricate metalwork and musical instruments from ancient debris.
They are also deeply spiritual, with a strong connection to the natural world and a reverence for the vakis of the mountains and forests.

### Frostbound

You are **adapted to cold environments**.

You are **resistant to cold damage**, and you suffer **no penalties for cold weather or terrain**, such as **ice, deep snow, or frigid temperatures**.

### Polydactyl Limbs

You can manipulate objects with **greater dexterity** thanks to your **extra fingers and toes**.

As a **bonus action**, you can use your **feet** to manipulate an object, open or close a door or container, or pick up or set down a **Tiny object**.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **Edonian**.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

<Collapsible>

###### Erudite Of Sword and Song <span>6 BP</span>

You have studied the **ancient arts of Edonian smithing and music**.

- You gain **one fighting style** of your choice from the **Fighter class**.
- You gain **expertise** with **one musical instrument** of your choice.

_Notes._

> From a young age, Edonian Giants are tasked with finding their **soulblade and soulsong**, a weapon and instrument that **resonate with their spirit**.
> These items are often **heirlooms passed down through generations**, or **crafted by the Giant themselves** using traditional methods.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Galeforce <span>6 BP</span>

You trained to move swiftly through the **mountain winds**, using traditional Edonian **warkites** to glide from peak to peak.

When you fall at least **10 feet** above the ground, you can use your **reaction** to extend your **skin membranes** to glide horizontally a number of feet equal to your **walking speed**, and you take **0 damage** from the fall.
You determine the **direction of the glide**.

_Notes._

> Edonian Giants craft **warkites** from **sturdy wood and fabric**, attaching them to their arms and legs to **catch the wind**.
> These kites allow them to **glide gracefully** through the air, using the **mountain winds** to their advantage
> and **avoid injury from falls**.
> Some Edonian Giants become **skillful enough to tame these winds** without the aid of a warkite.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Resourceful Scavenger <span>5 BP</span>

You have learned to make use of the **ancient debris** scattered throughout the **Silvery Peaks**.

- You gain proficiency with **tinker's tools**.
- Once per **short rest**, you can craft a single [_Trinket_](/en/library/items/trinkets/main) of rarity **lesser than "very rare"**.
- This trinket **breaks after 24 hours or its first use**, whichever comes first.

_Notes._

> The Silvery Peaks are **littered with the remnants of ancient civilizations**, from broken machinery to discarded tools and weapons.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Thousand-folded Edge <span>5 BP</span>

You have mastered the art of **eliminating impurities through strenuous effort**, both in **blade and body**.

You **ignore resistance to slashing damage** with weapons you have **crafted, sharpened, modified, or maintained**.
You gain proficiency with **smith's tools**.

_Notes._

> The process of refining a blade to its **purest form** is a **sacred tradition** among Edonian Giants,
> and their sharpest tool is often **embedded on their person** as both **weapon and symbol** of their dedication.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Song of the Mountain <span>4 BP</span>

Whenever you roll a **Charisma (Performance)** check related to **singing or playing an instrument**,
you can add your **Wisdom modifier** to the roll.

_Notes._

> The Silvery Peaks often **hum when the winds pick up**.
> Ancient pipes from long-abandoned industrial settlements like **Orpholea** carry these sounds, and Edonian Giants learn to **mimic them in their music**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Boon of the Land Spirits <span>4 BP</span>

You are **uniquely attuned to the spirits of the lands**.

When you finish a **long rest** in a location without having moved more than **1 mile** from that location,
you gain the following benefits until you finish a **short rest**:

After you roll **initiative**, you can roll an additional **d4** and add it to **weapon attack rolls, ability checks, or saving throws** you make until the end of your **first turn**.
The **first weapon attack** you make on every turn deals an additional **1d4 damage** of a type associated with the [_terrain_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/movement#difficult-terrain).

_Notes._

> Edonian Giants often spend long periods of time in **meditation or solitude**, attempting to **commune with the Väki**, splinters of reality.
> Some, through **devotion and patience**, manage to gain their favor, **if only briefly**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Wise Warrior <span>3 BP</span>

Whenever you use a [_Trinket_](/en/library/items/trinkets/main), if it imposes a saving throw, the saving throw becomes **DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier**.
If the trinket requires an attack roll, you can add your **Wisdom modifier** to the roll, instead of **Strength or Dexterity**, and you are considered **proficient** with it.

_Notes._

> Edonian Giants are trained from a young age to **respect ancient artifacts** scattered throughout their homelands.
> They learn to use these items with **care and precision**, often relying on their **wisdom to unlock their secrets**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Cragstep <span>2 BP</span>

You have **advantage** on **Dexterity (Acrobatics)** and **Strength (Athletics)** ability checks when navigating **rocky or mountainous terrain**.

_Notes._

> Edonian Giants follow a **strict no-rescue rule** for travelers in the Silvery Peaks.
> Those who venture into the mountains must be prepared to **face the dangers alone**, just as they do.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Mountain's Endurance <span>2 BP</span>

Once per **short rest**, you may reduce your **exhaustion level by 1** as a **bonus action**.

_Notes._

> Edonian Giants are known for their **resilience and endurance**, able to withstand **harsh conditions and grueling journeys** through the mountains.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Words of the World <span>1 BP</span>

You learn **Primordial or Glyphic**.

_Notes._

> Through communion with the **Väki**, Edonian Giants gain insight into the **ancient languages of the world**,
> although sometimes they commune with **Voids mistakenly**, and learn **nothing at all**.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Smith's Intuition <span>1 BP</span>

Once per **long rest**, you can **take 10** on a **smith's tools check**, even if rushed or distracted.

_Notes._

> "**A rushed craft is a wasted craft. But better to wasteful than dead.**" — _Edonian Proverb_

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Soul of the Warrior <span>1 BP</span>

Once per **long rest**, you may immediately know the **name, origin, and one notable trait** of the owner of a **weapon you touch**.

_Notes._

> Edonian Giants believe that **swordsmen leave an imprint of their soul** on their blades.
> Though the existence of souls is **discarded by many**, Edonian Giants hold firm to the belief that a **weapon can tell its story** to those who listen.

</Collapsible>
