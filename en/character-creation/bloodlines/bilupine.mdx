# Bilupine

Bilupines are constructs of glass-like black stone and ancient magicks of yore, more a machine than a living being.
Their original purpose to toil ceaselessly in the worlds of the Black Cradle, to build infrastructure for the War of Shapes.
Uncountable ages later, they remain, forever exploited as laborers, guardians, and tools of war by those who can command them.

### Bilupine Slam

Your heavy stone limbs are natural weapons.

- You have a melee natural weapon "Bilupine Slam" that deals 1d4 + your Strength modifier bludgeoning damage.

### Construct Physiology

You are a living construct of stone and alchemical magic.

- You do not need to eat, drink, or breathe.
- You must consume small amounts of **alchemical precursor** and **oil** each day to maintain your body. If you do not, you gain no benefits from resting.
- Your body contains a circulating reservoir of liquefied precursor and oil. This reservoir functions as your "blood."
- You do not sleep. Instead, you enter an inert, aware state for half as much time to gain the benefits of a rest.
- You are immune to nonmagical disease.
- You have resistance to poison damage and advantage on saving throws against the [_poisoned_](/en/library/rules/conditions#poisoned) condition.
- You are immune to nonmagical [_exhaustion_](/en/library/rules/conditions#exhaustion) effects, except those resulting from limb loss or catastrophic structural damage.
- Restoring broken limbs or repairing major physical damage requires specialized alchemical treatment. Magic that restores missing body parts does not function on you.

### Stonework Sense

You have an intuitive understanding of stone and worked masonry.

- You are proficient with mason's tools.
- When you make an Intelligence (History) check related to stonework, architecture, or ruins, you have advantage on the roll.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write two languages, each belonging to one of the previous owners of your two heads. Common and Empyrean are recommended.

###### Aristophany <span>7 BP</span>

Your second head is fully awakened and engraved into a state of dual consciousness.

- You can **concentrate on two different spells at the same time**.
- If you are concentrating on two spells, when you make a saving throw to maintain concentration on any spell, you do so **with disadvantage**.
- If you fail, you lose concentration on **both** spells.
- You gain **proficiency in Perception**.

_Notes._

> Most bilupines possess Aristophany by default, but to produce offspring they tear one of their two heads.
> They usually restore their second head over time but do not regain Aristophany naturally; it can be restored only by master artisans or powerful wizards.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Monady <span>5 BP</span>

Your second head is dormant, a mere vestige of your former self.

- You reduce your known languages to **one language** of your choice.
- You have **resistance to psychic damage**.
- You have **advantage on saving throws against being [_stunned_](/en/library/rules/conditions#stunned), [_paralyzed_](/en/library/rules/conditions#paralyzed) or [_staggered_](/en/library/rules/conditions#staggered)**.

_Notes._

> Bilupines with perpetual Monady are rare, as most seek to restore dual cognition.
> Some choose Monady for focus in labor or combat; others cannot reawaken their second head after catastrophic injury.

</Collapsible>

###### Immovable Frame <span>4 BP</span>

Your body is reinforced with deep-core load-bearing engravings.

- When you **take damage from a weapon attack**, **or** when a creature **attempts to forcibly move you** (push, pull, drag, knockback, slam, shove), you can use your **reaction** to oppose said action.
- Until the start of your next turn:
  - You reduce all damage from **weapon attacks** by an amount equal to your **proficiency bonus**.
  - All **forced movement** you would suffer is **reduced by half**, except for magical teleportation.

- You can use this feature a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus** (minimum of once), regaining all uses after a **long rest**.

_Notes._

> This reinforcement is popular among laborers, guardians, and Bilupines whose lives demand raw physical certainty over dual cognition.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Monument Builder <span>3 BP</span>

You were carved for endurance and creation, built to raise walls, haul stone, and shape monuments that outlast generations.

- You gain **proficiency in athletics**.
- When making an Athletics check to lift, carry, drag, or push heavy objects, you treat a roll of **9 or lower as a 10**.

You can carve functional implements from raw stone with practiced precision.

- As part of a **short rest**, you can craft a rudimentary stone version of any **simple or martial melee weapon**, any **thrown weapon**, a **shield, greatshield, or buckler**, single use copy of any set of **artisan's tools**,
  or a simple item such as a **lockpick set, a key you possess, a crowbar, or manacles** using stone.
- These stone items weigh **twice as much** as their counterparts.
- They function identically to their standard versions, except they are considered **objects made of stone**.
- These crafted items can be **sold for twice their normal price**, as they are valued as sculptures or artistic carvings.

_Notes._

> Monument Builders are prized among Bilupine crews. Their bodies carry the implicit geometry of construction; to them, moving stone is as breathing.
> Bilupines who master this engraving can shape stone almost as easily as breathing.
> While their creations are crude compared to true blacksmithing, their rarity and craftsmanship make them prized artifacts outside their homeland.

</Collapsible>

###### Rock Grinder <span>2 BP</span>

Your inner channels churn with fine precursor grit, letting you abrade and shape stone with uncanny ease.

- You learn the **Mold Earth** cantrip.
- Constitution is your spellcasting ability for this cantrip.

_Notes._

> Bilupines with this engraving can churn stone into sand inside their palms or jaws, letting them reshape terrain or carve simple forms with a mere gesture.

</Collapsible>

##### Internal Precursor Reservoir <span>2 BP</span>

Your body houses a stable chamber of refined precursor, allowing you to substitute your own core for missing spellcasting components.

- When a spell requires **material components** without a cost, you may use your **own body** as a spellcasting focus instead.
- You can do this a number of times equal to your **Constitution modifier** (minimum of once), regaining all uses after a **short rest**.

_Notes._

> This reservoir is not infinite—its use drains precursor vapors through your engravings, producing faint light, heat, or sound depending on the spell.
> Excessive use can deeply harm the user, so Bilupines ration this ability carefully.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Mountain Breaker <span>1 BP</span>

Your strikes carry the force of quarrying tools and demolition glyphs.

- When you damage **objects**, **barriers**, or **structures**, you deal true damage instead of normal damage.
  If the target is vulnerable to the original damage type, it still takes double damage.

_Notes._

> This applies only to non-creature targets—walls, doors, fortifications, rubble, siegeworks, and similar constructions.
> Bilupines carved for demolition or tunneling are prized for their ability to break through ancient stone as though it were soft earth.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### See-Through Core <span>1 BP</span>

Your inner precursor channels are exposed through a translucent section of stone, glowing faintly.

- You emit **dim light** in a **5-foot radius**.

_Notes._

> The glow varies in color from Bilupine to Bilupine, depending on their precursor mixture and the age of their engravings, but they are usually either a pastel pink or a soft lavender.
