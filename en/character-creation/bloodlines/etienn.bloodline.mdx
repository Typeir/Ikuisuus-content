# Etienn (Dopplers)

The word "Etienn" is derived from the Hidden tongue word _Etiäinen_ — a premonitory apparition of another, a confabulation born of longing. A fetch. The shape grief gives the missing.

Recognized by most Empyreans as _Etiennus_, they are what few viable descendants a forgotten, unnamed Silent One lineage managed to produce: skinless, vaguely humanoid composites, assembled from lesser beings that merged rather than perished. Not one creature, but a patchwork colony that learned, eventually, to speak with one voice and walk on two legs. A legion reduced to a person, still searching.

That search ends — provisionally — in others. A child lost in the mists, returned a season too late, carrying her exact weight in the doorframe. A husband pressed into the trenches, surfacing six months after the grief had curdled, asking about the chickens by name. Many Etienn are nothing more than parasites of memory, wearing the dead like borrowed coats until the lining wears out and they move on. But some are something else: caretakers of the departed, keeping the dead warm and present for those who cannot let go. Whether that is kindness or cruelty is rarely their concern to answer.

What is rarely spoken of is what that return costs. Acquiring a face is not an inheritance or a power — it is a week, sometimes longer, cocooned in stillness while the new surface integrates: the composite self learning the weight of someone else's jaw, the particular give of their hands, the way skin settles over what is not quite bone. It does not stop hurting during that time. But the week ends. And the Etienn who emerges from it can make breakfast for the family of the person they now resemble, can hold someone through a long night, can raise a child who will never know the difference. The horror of the process does not disqualify the life that follows it.

In their true form — seen rarely, usually by accident — they are unsettling in a way that resists description. No skin. No singular coherence. A suggestion of features that never quite resolve. Creatures that have seen it report not horror so much as an overwhelming sense of _wrongness_, as though looking at something whose existence is a minor violation of the world's assumptions.

### Etiäinen

### The shape of longing, the longing for a shape

Acquiring a false skin requires the remains of a recently deceased humanoid in workable condition, or a skin constructed from materials — see **False Skin** below. Either way, the Etienn must enter a **cocooned state** lasting **one week**: largely helpless, the composite body integrating the new surface into itself, learning its weight and its seams. The process is painful. It ends.

When the week ends, the Etienn emerges wearing a face. Their coloration, proportions, and surface features match the skin's original. Voice and mannerisms are not inherited — they are practiced, built through deliberate study of the person whose form is now worn.

You can adjust your **voice, posture, and mannerisms** at will. You cannot change your physical appearance beyond what the current false skin permits without cocooning into a new one.

You can only wear a **specific individual's appearance** if you have made direct physical contact with their remains, or spent sufficient time observing them closely to understand how they move and hold themselves.

Your false skin and your clothing are separate. The skin does not conjure clothes.

### Necrophagy

To acquire a **False Skin**, you must **consume all** but the outermost tissue of a deceased creature. The process takes 10 minutes, during which you are **incapacitated** and can be targeted normally.
You can only consume a corpse that has been dead for **no more than 1 hour**, and that has not been damaged beyond recognition.

When you consume a corpse in this way, you can **remove a level of exhaustion** as part of the process. You also gain **temporary hit points equal to your character level**, which last until the end of your next long rest.

Once you consume a corpse, you do not need to consume sustenance for the next 24 hours. You can still eat and drink as normal, but you do not need to.

You only gain benefits from consuming a corpse once per long rest. You are free to consume as many corpses as you like, but only the first one provides these benefits.

### False Skin

An Etienn without a false skin is visible as what they are. Most maintain one at all times.

**False Skin HP.** Your false skin has hit points equal to **5 + your character level**, tracked separately from your own. It does not restore from resting — it must be repaired.

**Damage thresholds.** Each time you take damage your false skin takes damage from the strain, losing one hit point. If you are damaged below 0 HP, your false skin loses an additional 5 hit points for every instance of damage you take while below 0, as you cannot protect it effectively while unconscious or incapacitated.

When your false skin reaches **0 HP**, its seams split and features begin to collapse. Your true form becomes visible. You can still act, but cannot benefit from any feature that requires wearing a false skin until it is repaired or replaced.

<Collapsible>
  ###### Repairing a false skin.

A false skin can be repaired using:

- [Mending](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:mending) — restores 1 HP per casting
- 10 minutes of work with a **disguise kit**, **leatherworker's tools**, **weaver's tools**, or **cobbler's tools** — restores HP equal to your **proficiency bonus** per session,

The right tool depends on the skin's material. A disguise kit addresses surface features — coloration, texture, hair. Leatherworker's tools handle structural tears in preserved flesh or cured hide. Weaver's tools handle fiber-reinforced composite skins. Cobbler's tools address extremity wear — hands, feet, the parts that move and crack first.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
  ###### Constructing a false skin.

Over **3 days** of work using leatherworker's tools, weaver's tools, or similar, you can construct a generic false skin from worked materials without requiring a corpse. A constructed skin cannot replicate a specific individual's appearance and cannot benefit from boons requiring a real person's remains, but functions as a serviceable false skin in all other respects.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>
  ###### Living Without A False Skin

When not using a false skin, you are visible as what you are. You can still speak and interact normally, but cannot benefit from any feature that requires wearing a false skin.
Additionally, you suffer a -10 penalty to Charisma checks made to interact with others that are not accustomed to your true form, as your unsettling true form makes social interaction difficult.

This also applies to your passive scores for such interactions, meaning you might attract unwanted attention.

</Collapsible>

### Tailor of Selves

You are proficient with the **disguise kit**. Maintaining a false skin is not sorcery — it is labor, patience, and understanding what bodies are made of.

You also gain proficiency in **Medicine** and **Performance**.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Hidden**, and one language belonging to your False Skin. While not wearing a false skin, you can only use Hidden.

_Notes._

> Etienn speak Hidden not as a living cultural tongue but as something carried over in bloodline memory — fragments of the [_Silent One_](/en/library/character-creation/bloodlines/silent-one.bloodline) lineage surfacing in moments of hunger or terror. Many Etienn speak it without knowing what it is, or where the words come from.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

<Collapsible>

###### The Faces We Keep <span>6 BP</span>

You have learned to preserve and maintain multiple false skins outside your body, rather than discarding old ones when you move on. You can own a number of preserved skins equal to your **proficiency bonus**, each stored and maintained between uses.

Switching into a preserved skin you have **previously cocooned into** requires only **1 day** of adaptation rather than a full week. Switching into a completely new skin still requires the full week.

While wearing a skin you have inhabited for a **cumulative total of at least one month**:

- You become virtually indistinguishable from the original wearer. You can perfectly replicate their voice, mannerisms, and any physical idiosyncrasies.
- If you managed to consume their bodies within 10 minutes of their death, You additionally know every single piece of information that the original wearer would have shared freely with close acquaintances, but not their deeper secrets or memories.
- You can be distinguished from the original wearer only by someone who has known them intimately for a long time, and even then only with a successful DC 20 Wisdom (Insight) check.
- You can still be identified as an impostor by magical effects such as detect magic, which reveals all creatures hailing from the Silent One lineage as aberrations.

A preserved skin can be passed to another Etienn. The receiving Etienn must still cocoon for the full week on first adaptation — but inherits all recognition, contacts, and social history bound to that face.

_Notes._

> Some Etienn families have kept the same face alive for four generations — a healer, a merchant, a confessor. The individual behind it long gone, the skin carefully passed down, its relationships and debts inherited by whoever wears it next. When the confessor finally died for the fifth time, an entire district mourned someone they had never actually met.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Unfathomable Dread <span>5 BP</span>

While in your true form, or while confronting a creature that knows you are an impostor, you gain the following benefits against them:

Whenever you attack them, they must succeed on a DC _(10 + your charisma modifier + your proficiency bonus)_ **Wisdom** saving throw or become [_frightened_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#frightened) until the end of your next turn.
If they were already [_frightened_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#frightened), they instead become [_terrified_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#terrified) until the end of your next turn. A creature that succeeds on the saving throw is immune to this effect for the next 24 hours.

When attacking a creature that is already [_frightened_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#frightened) or [ _terrified_ ](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#terrified), you deal extra psychic damage equal to your proficiency bonus.

_Notes._

> The Etienn have not forgotten the rules of the Hiisi way of life; when you interact with them, you unwittingly sign a contract. The terms are simple, you pretend they are who they say they are, and they will be who they say they are. Break the contract, and you will meet the true Etienn under the skin.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Skin Deep <span>4 BP</span>

Flesh carries history. A skin worn long enough by someone skilled at something begins to hold the shape of that skill — calluses, reach, the angle of a practiced movement. While wearing a false skin made from or patterned after a **specific individual**, you gain proficiency in **one skill** that individual possessed, as determined by the GM based on what is known or discoverable about them.

If you already have proficiency in that skill, you gain **expertise** instead. If you already have expertise from another source, you gain a **+1 bonus** to checks made with it instead.

_Notes._

> The hands remembered before the mind did — the grip on a needle, the pressure needed to hold a seam. She had been a tailor for forty years. The Etienn had worn her for three months. It was not her talent. But it was theirs now.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Patchwork Persistence <span>4 BP</span>

Once per **short rest**, when you are reduced to or below **0 hit points** while wearing a false skin, you can use your **reaction** to shunt the killing force outward through the skin rather than inward through yourself. You fall prone but instead drop **to 1 HP**.

Your false skin immediately drops to **0 HP**. It cannot be worn again until repaired.

_Notes._

> The horror of peeling the layers away is often enough of a deterrent to would-be attackers.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### _When You Gaze..._ <span>4 BP</span>

When a creature first sees you in your true form, they must succeed on a **DC 10 + your level** Wisdom saving throw or be [_terrified_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#terrified) for 1 minute. A creature that succeeds on the saving throw is immune to this effect for the next 24 hours.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Seamstress of Selves <span>4 BP</span>

You gain proficiency in **Leatherworker's tools** and **Weaver's tools**, if you don't already have them.

When you spend 10 minutes repairing your false skin using tools or a disguise kit, you restore HP equal to **twice your proficiency bonus** instead of once. When you cast [Mending](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:mending) on your false skin, it restores **2 HP** instead of 1.

Additionally, you can construct a generic false skin in **1 day** rather than 3, and from half the required material.

_Notes._

> There is no glamour to it. It is needlework. Knowing which thread holds and which frays, and understanding that the difference between a convincing face and a failed one is usually just a seam you didn't check.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Echo Reading <span>3 BP</span>

After you **succeed** on a Charisma (Deception) check while wearing a false skin to impersonate a specific individual, you learn to architect your behavior and mannerisms around them.

You have **advantage** on Wisdom (Insight) and Charisma (Persuasion) checks made against any creature you have successfully deceived in this way. This benefit is permanent for those specific creatures unless they become aware of the deception.

_Notes._

> To be believed is to glimpse through someone's armor. The Etienn do not forget what they saw inside it.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### A Kindness of Echoes <span>2 BP</span>

When you choose to maintain the form of a deceased or absent person among those who knew them you can use **Charisma** in place of any other ability score on skill checks made for purely **social purposes** in that context.

At the GM's discretion, actions taken as such a persona may resolve personal, social, or communal consequences left unfinished by the original individual.

Creatures that see through your disguise after interacting with them in such a way will immediately turn hostile towards you, and will not be affected by this boon in the future.

_Notes._

> Not all of them are thieves. Some just stayed. And no one asked them to leave.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Shapeless Nature <span>1 BP</span>

Once per long rest, you may shed and break your False Skin as an action, and fit in a container or space no smaller than 1 cubic foot.
You can slither through such a space for 10 minutes, after which you will start suffering exhaustion per every minute you remain in that form.
You can end this effect early as a bonus action, provided you can exit the space you currently inhabit.

_Notes._

> The legion can disassemble if need be, suffering horrible consequences if not reassembled quickly.

</Collapsible>
