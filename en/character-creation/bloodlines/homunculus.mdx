# Homunculus

Homunculi are artificial creatures chiseled from primordial wax and awakened through arcane ritual.  
These creatures are not carefully sculpted so much as _discovered_ — wizards learned, through trial, error, and many failures, that only a handful of crude shapes would take, only a few would settle and persist.  
Most resemble shrew-like or bird-like critters of wax, smooth and earless, animated by a magical flame burning atop their heads.

A homunculus is a **living lamp**.  
Its endurance, clarity, and function are bound entirely to the flame it carries, and to its master—unless it harbors a will brighter than any mere light.


---

## Core Features

##### Ability Score Increase

Your Constitution score increases by **3**.

##### Movement

Your base walking speed is **35 ft**.

##### Sight

You have standard eyesight.

##### Creature Type

You are a **Humanoid**.

##### Age

Homunculi do not age and do not die of old age.  
Their wax bodies may melt, crack, or deform over time, but time alone does not destroy them.
Without contradicting prior facts, their average lifespan ranges from 50 to 60 years, their bodies mechanically deteriorating beyond repair at around that point.
Most wizards would rather scrap their creations than keep mending them ad infinitum; it gets more and more prone to failure every time.

##### Size & Mass

You are **Small**.  
Despite your size, you count as **Medium** for the purposes of carrying capacity, pushing, dragging, and lifting.

##### Wax Physiology

You are an artificial being animated by the will of the world.

- You need to **eat** and **drink** like any other being.
- You need to sleep. During this time, you **must extinguish your flame**.
- You have **advantage on saving throws** against effects that would reduce your movement speed or inflict exhaustion.
- This advantage does **not** apply against paralysis, petrification, or similar total incapacitation effects.

##### Living Flame

A homunculus carries a magical flame atop its body.

- You emit **bright light** in a **10-foot radius** and **dim light** for an additional **20 feet**.
- You automatically fail Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in darkness.
- You can hood the flame with your hand, special implements or a piece of cloth or similar, reducing the light to **dim light only (5 ft)**, no longer automatically failing these checks, but doing them at a disadvantage.
- If your flame is **extinguished**, you roll **all attack rolls, ability checks and saving throws at disadvantage**.
- Relighting your flame requires a **short rest** or magical assistance with a spell, item or similar that can produce magical flames, unless a feature states otherwise.
- You ignite flammable objects that come into **contact** with your flame.

_Notes._
> Forcefully extinguishing the flame is catastrophic. The wax cools and stiffens; the Homunculus enters a drowsy state, similar to sleepwalking.

###### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Common** and one language taught to you by your creator.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

## Core Flame (5 BP)

Choose **one flame color** when you select this boon.  
Your flame determines what rolls you may empower by **spending Hit Dice**.

| Flame Color | Empowered Rolls            | Conversion Ratio   | Aspect                    |
| ----------- | -------------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------- |
| Red         | Damage rolls (fire damage) | 1 HD → 2 HD damage | The Fires of Kultharja    |
| Blue        | Ability checks (non tool)  | 1 HD → 1 HD bonus  | Itähënki & Länsihënki     |
| Green       | Saving throws              | 2 HD → 1 HD bonus  | The Resilience of Kalmora |
| Purple      | Tool checks                | 1 HD → 2 HD bonus  | The Backbone of Selkara   |
| Black       | Attack rolls               | 2 HD → 1 HD bonus  | The Hatred of Mana        |

You may spend Hit Dice **after rolling but before knowing the outcome**, unless a feature states otherwise.
You can only spend Hit Dice you have remaining from a short or long rest, and only once per round.

_Notes._
> If you choose no flame, you will perpetually suffer the effects of having it extinguished. Do so at your own risk.

##### Prismatic Wick (4 BP)

Your inner flame is unstable, refracting will and memory each time you rest.

- When you finish a **short rest**, roll **1d6** to determine the color of your flame until your next short rest.
- The result replaces your normal **Core Flame** for the duration.
- If you already possess a fixed Core Flame, you cannot take this boon.

| d6  | Flame Color | Effect         |
| --- | ----------- | -------------- |
| 1   | Red         | Damage rolls   |
| 2   | Blue        | Ability checks |
| 3   | Green       | Saving throws  |
| 4   | Purple      | Tool checks    |
| 5   | Black       | Attack rolls   |
| 6   | **Golden**  | See below      |

_Notes._
> The flame's color shift is visible and immediate after resting, often accompanied by very mild mood shifts.

###### Golden Flame — _The Will of Täiva_

When you roll a **6** on Prismatic Wick, your flame burns gold, steady and silent.

- You may apply your Hit Dice bonus to **any roll** you make, regardless of type.
- The conversion ratio is **1 Hit Die → 1 HD bonus**.
- You may do this **once per turn**.

_Notes._
> The Golden Flame does not stack with any other Core Flame effects.
> It cannot be chosen deliberately, extended, or preserved beyond the current rest cycle.
> Among homunculi, seeing gold is considered an omen rather than a blessing.

##### Cold Wick Discipline (4 BP)

You have learned to function without flame, your will no longer bound entirely to the light.

- While your flame is **extinguished**, you do **not** suffer disadvantage on **ability checks** or **saving throws**.
- You still suffer disadvantage on **attack rolls** while your flame is extinguished.

_Notes._
> This boon has no effect while your flame is burning or hooded.
> Most homunculi who master this discipline never relight their flame unless absolutely necessary.

---

##### Everburning Wick (3 BP)

Your inner flame is shielded by perpetual enchantment.

- Your flame **cannot be extinguished by mundane means**.
- Environmental effects such as wind, water, vacuum, or suffocation do not affect it.
- Magical extinguishing effects require a Constitution saving throw against the caster's DC in order to take effect. 
- If the caster cannot feasibly be determined, or the origin of the spell is diffuse, the DC is 13.

_Notes._
> Wizards often gift this boon to homunculi intended for hazardous environments, such as underwater exploration or high-altitude observation.

---

##### Waxflow Stabilizers (3 BP)

Your wax body better tolerates internal heat fluctuations.

- When you spend Hit Dice with your **Core Flame**, you gain **temporary hit points** equal to your proficiency bonus.

_Notes._
> These stabilizers often manifest in the form of jewelry or body modifications on homunculi, such as earrings or pendants.

---

##### Oversized Armament Discipline (3 BP)

No tool is made to fit, as such, you quickly learnt that all tools can be made to fit.

- You may wield **Large melee weapons**, despite being a small creature.
- While wielding a Large weapon, you make attack rolls with it at **disadvantage**.
- When you make an attack roll with a Large weapon you are proficient with, you add **double your proficiency bonus** to the roll instead of your normal proficiency bonus.

_Notes._
> Ever since the dawn of the fifth star and the end of the first pilgrimage, homunculi seem to gravitate towards oversized tools, as if yearning for something grander than them.

---

##### Protective Lantern (2 BP)

You instinctively shield yourself with your flame.

- When a creature you can see targets you with an attack, you can use your **reaction** to flare your flame.
- The attacker has to succeed on a **Constitution saving throw** (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be blinded until the start of their next turn, taking fire damage equal to twice your Constitution modifier on a failed save.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus**, regaining all uses after a long rest.

_Notes._
> This reflex is quite common in abused homunculi.

---

##### Chromatic Refinement (2 BP)

Your flame burns with deliberate coloration.

- Choose a distinct hue within your flame’s color.
- When you spend Hit Dice using your **Core Flame**, you may reroll any roll of **1 or 2** on any spent Hit Die. You must use the new result.

_Notes._
> Quite common in pet or companion homunculi, this refinement makes their flames visually unique without altering their function.

---

##### Designated Courier (2 BP)

You were carved to run, not to fight.

- You gain **proficiency in Athletics**.
- When your **movement speed is reduced** for any reason (such as difficult terrain, magical and mundane slow effects, or environmental hindrances), you gain **5 feet of additional movement** until the end of the current turn.
- This additional movement **cannot be reduced in any way**, provided you are still capable of taking actions.
- This bonus does not apply if you are incapacitated.  

_Notes._
> Wizards often employ homunculi as tireless couriers, scouts, and message runners, trusting them to carry instructions, reagents, or sealed commands across terrain no living servant could reliably cross.

---

##### Dimmed Core (1 BP)

You can partially shutter your flame.

- You can suppress your light emission to **dim light only (5 ft)**.
- While suppressed, you only have disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made in darkness.
- If you hood your suppressed flame, you can roll Dexterity (Stealth) checks normally.

_Notes._
> Some homunculi learn to control their light early on, especially those used for espionage or stealth missions.

---

##### Dripping Wax (1 BP)

Your body sheds excess wax when stressed.

- When you are hit by a melee attack, the attacker’s movement speed is reduced by **5 ft** until the start of its next turn, and yours increases by another **5 ft** until the end of your next turn.

_Notes._
> One of the most common features of modern era homunculi is their degraded material integrity and ease of chipping. Extremely uncommon in ancient homunculi.

---

##### Surveyor’s Implements (1 BP)

Your maker equipped you with tools meant for long roads and careful mapping.

Choose **one** of the following tool proficiencies:

- **Cartographer’s Tools**
- **Navigator’s Tools**

Additionally, your flame always points North, regardless of weather conditions.

_Notes._
> These implements are rarely glamorous, but indispensable to expeditions, pilgrimages, and long-distance operations.
> Homunculi bearing these tools are often assigned to chart uncharted regions or recently altered areas.

---

##### Scroll-Bound Mind (1 BP)

You were a tool for testing or delivering spell scrolls, and eventually learnt to decipher them yourself.

You can read and attempt to use spell scrolls regardless of your class’s spellcasting features.

If the spell is not on your class’s spell list or you otherwise lack the ability to cast it, you must still make the appropriate Arcana check as normal to successfully use the scroll.

_Notes._
> Many homunculi were created specifically to carry, test, or dispose of unstable magical implements. Some survived long enough to learn how to use them.

---

##### Reduced Caloric Outtake (1 BP)

You do not bear the sin of gluttony, but you instinctively appreciate the value of a well-prepared meal.

You gain proficiency with **cook’s utensils**.

Once per long rest, when you consume a **fulfilling meal**, you regain one spent Hit Die.

_Notes._
> Wizards often underestimate how much care a homunculus requires to remain functional.

##### Heatless Flame (1 BP)

Your flame burns gently in a soft dance.

Your flame **no longer ignites** flammable objects, intentionally or otherwise.

You gain proficiency in **Animal Handling**, as beasts are no longer afraid of your flame, but rather curious.

_Notes._
> Beasts instinctively fear fire. A flame that does not burn is unsettling, but less terrifying.
> This modification is common among homunculi assigned to stables, caravans, or field expeditions.
