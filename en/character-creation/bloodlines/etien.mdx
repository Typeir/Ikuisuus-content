The word "Etien" is derived from the Hidden tongue word _Etiäinen_ — a premonitory apparition of another, a confabulation born of longing. A fetch. The shape grief gives the missing.

Recognized by most Empyreans as _Etienus_, they are what few viable descendants a forgotten, unnamed Silent One lineage managed to produce: skinless, vaguely humanoid composites, assembled from lesser beings that merged rather than perished. Not one creature, but a patchwork colony that learned, eventually, to speak with one voice and walk on two legs. A legion reduced to a person, still searching.

That search ends — provisionally — in others. A child lost in the mists, returned a season too late, carrying her exact weight in the doorframe. A husband pressed into the trenches, surfacing six months after the grief had curdled, asking about the chickens by name. Many Etien are nothing more than parasites of memory, wearing the dead like borrowed coats until the lining wears out and they move on. But some are something else: caretakers of the departed, keeping the dead warm and present for those who cannot let go. Whether that is kindness or cruelty is rarely their concern to answer.

In their true form — seen rarely, usually by accident — they are unsettling in a way that resists description. No skin. No singular coherence. A suggestion of features that never quite resolve. Creatures that have seen it report not horror so much as an overwhelming sense of _wrongness_, as though looking at something whose existence is a minor violation of the world's assumptions.

### Etiäinen

### The shape of longing, the longing for a shape

As an **action**, you can alter your physical appearance and voice. You determine the specifics, including coloration, proportions, apparent age, and sex. You can only adopt the exact appearance of a specific individual if you have **directly encountered** them — made physical contact, or observed them in close proximity for an extended period. A secondhand description is insufficient.

Your clothing and equipment are unchanged by this trait. You remain in the new form until you use an action to revert to your true form or until you die.

In your true form, your features are composite and unresolved — assembled from what you have been and what you have worn. Most creatures find this deeply unsettling.

### Composite Instincts

Your existence as a shifting, assembled self has sharpened your ability to read the performance of others. You gain proficiency in **two** of the following skills of your choice: **Deception**, **Insight**, **Intimidation**, **Performance**, or **Persuasion**.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **Hidden** (the script of the Hidden Kingdom).

_Notes._

> Etien speak Hidden not as a living cultural tongue but as something carried over in bloodline memory — fragments of the [_Silent One_](/en/library/character-creation/bloodlines/silent-one) lineage surfacing in moments of hunger or terror. Many Etien speak it without knowing what it is, or where the words come from.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

<Collapsible>

###### The Faces We Keep <span>6 BP</span>

You can develop persistent persona-identities — forms built not just as appearance but as character, with histories, speech patterns, and behavioral memory. You can maintain a number of such **deep personas** equal to your **proficiency bonus**.

While operating as a deep persona you have inhabited for at least **one week** of cumulative use:

- You have **advantage** on Charisma (Deception) checks made while in that persona.
- Creatures attempting to identify you as an impostor make Wisdom (Insight) checks at **disadvantage**.

A deep persona can be transferred to another Etien through a **ritual requiring 1 hour of direct contact**. The receiving Etien inherits the persona's established contacts, behavioral memory, and social history, but not the original occupant's personal memories.

_Notes._

> Some Etien families have kept the same persona alive for four generations — a healer, a merchant, a confessor. The individual behind the face long gone, the character persisting, its relationships and debts inherited by whoever wears it next. When the confessor finally died for the fifth time, an entire district mourned someone they had never actually met.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### The Borrowed Dead <span>6 BP</span>

If you have made **direct physical contact** with a humanoid's remains, or with a creature who knew the deceased intimately within the last year, you can use **Etiäinen** to assume the dead person's appearance.

While wearing a dead person's face among those who knew them:

- Wisdom (Insight) checks to identify you as an impostor are made at **disadvantage**.
- Once per **long rest**, you can ask the DM one question about the deceased's known relationships, habits, or circumstances at death. The answer surfaces as an absorbed sensory fragment — not words, but impression: the smell of a place, the weight of a habit, the emotional register of a recurring fear.

_Notes._

> They came back with the right eyes. They used her name for him the way she always did — like it had three syllables. I didn't ask them to stop. I should have asked them to stop.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Skin Deep <span>5 BP</span>

When you adopt a **specific individual's** form using **Etiäinen**, you absorb fragments of their instincts. For the duration of that assumed form, you gain proficiency in **one skill** the individual possessed, as determined by the DM based on what is known or discoverable about them.

If you already have proficiency in that skill, you gain **expertise** in it instead. If you already have expertise in that skill from another source, you gain a **+1 bonus** to checks made with it instead.

_Notes._

> It is not mimicry alone. Something passes in the borrowing — the set of the shoulders when they lied, the way the hands moved near a blade. Memory is stored in the body. The Etien inherit the body.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Patchwork Persistence <span>4 BP</span>

Once per **short rest**, when you are reduced to **0 hit points** while in an assumed form, you can use your **reaction** to collapse into your true form instead. You fall prone but are **stabilized at 1 HP**.

Your assumed form dissolves and cannot be readopted for **1 hour**.

_Notes._

> The face sloughs away and what remains is harder to read — and somehow harder to kill. Most attackers hesitate long enough for that to matter.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Echo Reading <span>3 BP</span>

After you **succeed** on a Charisma (Deception) check while using **Etiäinen** to impersonate a specific individual, you absorb an empathic trace of the creature you deceived.

You have **advantage** on Wisdom (Insight) checks made against any creature you have successfully deceived in this way. This benefit is permanent for those specific creatures unless they become aware of the deception.

_Notes._

> To be believed is to glimpse through someone's armor. The Etien do not forget what they saw inside it.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Voidskin <span>3 BP</span>

Your composite flesh redistributes impact across its assembled mass rather than concentrating it in a single point. While in your **true form** (not using **Etiäinen**), you have resistance to **bludgeoning damage from nonmagical weapons**.

Additionally, once per turn when you are hit by a **melee attack** while in any form, you can use your **reaction** to shed an outer layer of assumed flesh. Reduce the damage dealt by an amount equal to your **proficiency bonus**.

_Notes._

> There are no vital organs in a fixed arrangement. The lesion opens through something that used to be a shoulder and closes somewhere that was recently a knee. It is less anatomy than architecture.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### A Kindness of Echoes <span>2 BP</span>

When you choose to maintain the form of a deceased or absent person among those who knew them — not to deceive for personal gain, but to ease grief or carry on unfinished care — you can use **Charisma** in place of any other ability score on skill checks made for purely **social purposes** in that context.

At the DM's discretion, actions taken as such a persona may resolve personal, social, or communal consequences left unfinished by the original individual.

_Notes._

> Not all of them are thieves. Some just stayed. And no one asked them to leave.

</Collapsible>
