# Väärät (Gnomes)

Gnomes are volatile Pieni lineages known for misdirection, close-range audacity, and abrupt disappearance.
In Hidden Kingdom, Gnomes are known as Väärät, "The Wrong Ones" or "The Shifted": descendants of Pieni Lineages warped by the Interlocking, with wide and unstable anatomical variance and high mortality.
Those who survive infancy often join the ranks of the "Blood Gnomes", a loosely affiliated network of berserking pillagers, hell bent on a prophetic death wish at the hands of their
God-Empress the Red Queen. In the wider world, Gnomes are often found in small, tight-knit communities where their skills in subterfuge and improvisation are valued for survival and social standing.

### Shifted Anatomy

Your body may present atypical proportions or organ placement common to Interlocking-affected lineages.
Whenever you take a short rest, roll a d20. if the result is less than or equal to 10, your size is **Small** until your next short rest.
If the result is greater than 10, your size is **Medium** until your next short rest.

### Mimicry

You can accurately mimic sounds you have heard, including voices.
A creature that hears the sounds can tell they are imitations with a successful Wisdom (Insight) check contested by your Charisma (Deception) check.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **one additional language of your choice**.
Many väärät communities also preserve dialects of the **Hidden** tongues.

##### Whimsy and Wonder <span>6 BP</span>

You have advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks and Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) checks.

You have learned to peer into the world with a childlike sense of wonder, and to use that perspective to find joy and opportunity in the unexpected.
You gain two abilities from the following table:

| Ability       | Effect                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The pathways  | You have learned to find hidden paths and shortcuts, you can Wondrously find a hidden passage through terrain no thicker than 10ft across no one knew existed.                                                                                                               |
| The prophet   | When you fail a deception check, the event you confabulated comes to fruition in an unexpected way within the next 10 minutes.                                                                                                                                               |
| The prankster | When you fail a sleight of hand check, the item you tried to juggle or smuggle appears in the pockets of a creature within 30 feet of you. You know which creature it is. if no creature would be available, it simply appears under a nearby hidden stash only you know of. |
| The pioneer   | When you fail create an item with artisan's tools, you can choose to have instead created a thingamajig of your own design. The thingamajig is any item from the same category as the item you were trying to create.                                                        |

When you chose to use one of these abilities, the DM will roll 1d2 in secret, on a 1 the ability succeeds as described, on a 2 the ability fails.
The DM does not have to share the outcome of the roll, or narrate the results in a way that makes it clear whether the ability succeeded or failed.

_Notes._

> Did a väärät fetch that for you? Or you just pull it from thin air? — Old Pieni Saying.

###### BLOOD FOR THE BLOOD GOD <span>5 BP</span>

You have advantage on attack rolls against creatures with the [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/conditions#bleeding) condition.

Whenever you use a [_Masterful Blow_](/en/library/rules/weapon-properties#masterful-blows) against a creature, you also give the creature 1d4 stacks of the [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/conditions#bleeding) condition.

If a creature is immune to the [_Bleeding_](/en/library/rules/conditions#bleeding) condition, you deal additional damage to it equal to your proficiency bonus when you hit it with a masterful blow.

_Notes._

> The Red Queen's followers are known for their reckless, berserking fighting style. When drums are heard, they charge into battle with abandon, seeking gruesome glory in the spilling of blood; that of their victims as well as theirs.

  ###### Commune with the Monstruous and the Aberrant <span>4 BP</span>

You can use your Animal Handling skill to communicate simple ideas with creatures of the Monstrosity and Aberration types, provided their intelligence is lower than 8.

You may ride one of these creatures as a mount, even if it would normally be too large for you to ride, and you can control it normally while mounted.

You have advantage on dexterity saving throws against being thrown off or dismounted from such a creature.

_Notes._

> In the Hidden Kingdom, cattle and beasts of war are rare and hard to maintain, many Blood Gnomes have communed with the Silent Ones left behind and the Monstrosities spawned by the corpse-gods, out of a shared sense of retribution towards Elû.

###### Quiet Casting Discipline <span>3 BP</span>

You have trained to reduce your audible and visible casting profile.

When you cast a spell in combat, creatures more than 40 feet away cannot identify you as the source by sight or sound alone.
Creatures within 40 feet must succeed on an Intelligence (Arcana) check against a DC of 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Dexterity modifier to identify you as the source.

If the spell you cast lacks verbal components or lacks somatic components, those creatures make this check with disadvantage.

This feature does not prevent creatures from noticing visible projectile trajectories, impact points, or obvious magical effects.

_Notes._

> Väärät casters are taught concealment as a practical survival method, not as ritual doctrine.

</Collapsible>

and effects that don't impose a saving throw on you only move you for half their normal distance.

you can craft one temporary trinket weighing 1 pound or less during a short rest.
