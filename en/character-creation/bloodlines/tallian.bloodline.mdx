Tallians are ancient biomechanical constructs first forged in the Fourth Star, during the [War of Shapes](/en/library/world/events/war-of-shapes.lore), when the old Empyrean war-machine still had purpose and direction.
They were built as servitors, escorts, and battlefield engines for the imperial houses that would eventually root themselves in [Thealas](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/thealas.lore).
Most were hastily put into stasis after the collapse of that order, in vain hopes of a future palingenesis of the empire. In time, the memory of them would erode, their directives frozen for ages while dust and mineral salts hardened in their joints.

In the current age, rediscovered millenia after the fall of the Thealasian empire, a small fraction of these chassis roam the lands, the rebirth of the star-faring empire was short lived and catastrophic.
Many awoke with incomplete mission trees, obsolete command hierarchies, and no consensus for what duty means in a world that no longer resembles the one that made them - It did not matter, for the world was to suffer great change still.

After the scarring, whatever faint murmur of purpose and direction the Tallians could've grasped was snuffed out in the agonizing cries of Taiva, and with time even the memory of this faded into nothingness.

The Tallians now merely exist among the ruins, some refusing to interact with the world around them, others becoming an extant form of life among the new ecosystems. Yet most anchor themselves to whatever lucky or unlucky Empyrean they come across, hoping for a legacy no surviving bloodline can give them.

### Maneuverable

When hovering, you hover at 2.5 ft. x your proficiency bonus (minimum 5 ft.) from the ground, and can hover over liquids.

### Inertity

Your frame is not sustained by living tissue.

- You cannot regain hit points from spells, potions, features, or effects unless your Repair Window is active.
- Any temporary hit points you gain are doubled.
- This does not change your temporary hit point cap from any effect.

### Construct Nature

You are a biomechanical construct, not a living organism.

- You need to eat, drink and breathe, though you do so via different internal processes than a typical creature.
- You do not sleep. To gain the benefits of a short rest, you must spend 4 hours in an inert but aware maintenance state. Long rests take 7 days of uninterrupted resting with no heavy activity, as usual.
- You have advantage on saving throws against the [_poisoned_](/en/library/rules/conditions#poisoned) condition.

### Legacy Faultline

Like many old war-construct designs, your frame has resonance weaknesses.

- You are vulnerable to thunder damage.

### Repair Window

[Mending](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:mending) can briefly restore your healing interface.

- When Mending is cast on you, you gain a Repair Window for 1 minute.
- During this window, you can regain hit points as normal.
- Mending itself does not restore hit points unless another feature explicitly says it does.
- You can spend one hit die to regain hit points during your Repair Window. When you do so, the repair window immediately ends.

### Maintenance Recovery

Your spent Hit Dice are restored as normal when you finish a long rest.

### Programmed Excellence

Once per short rest, as a bonus action, you can re-wire your programming.
You gain all of the following benefits until your next Short Rest:

- Choose one condition: blinded, charmed, deafened, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, or terrified. You are immune to that condition.
- Choose one elemental damage type: acid, cold, fire, lightning, or psychic. You gain resistance to that damage type.
- Choose one skill and one tool. You gain proficiency in both.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write Common and Empyrean.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

<Collapsible>

###### Multi-Dimensional Maneuvers <span>7 BP</span>

At the start of each of your turns, if you are not incapacitated, you can forego 10ft of movement for that turn.
If you do so, movement does not provoke opportunity attacks until the end of that turn.

Once per short rest, when you move at least 10 feet before making a melee attack on your turn, you can add your proficiency bonus to the attack and damage rolls.

_Notes._

> This suite descends from old hunter-frame movement doctrine: break contact, cut angle, re-enter from a better line.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Waking Protocol <span>6 BP</span>

Your chassis is fitted with a combat-ready restart stack.

Once per long rest, when you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead.
When you do, you immediately gain temporary hit points equal to your level + your Constituion modifier.

If your **Repair Window** is active when this triggers, you can also move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

When this feature triggers, **after** you moved if your **Repair Window** was active, you can arm an **End-of-Life Cycle**.
If you do, a 6 second countdown begins. During this countdown, you are incapacitated and immune to all damage and forced movement effects.
At the end of the countdown, or if you are reduced to 0 hit points again before it ends, each creature within 10 feet of you must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier), taking force damage equal to five times your level on a failed save, or half as much on a success.
After this detonation, you immediately drop to 0 hit points.

_Notes._

> Most Tallians destroyed after the Fourth Star were not truly beyond repair, they were merely discarded.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Multi-Purpose Tool <span>5 BP</span>

Choose one skill and two tools.
You gain proficiency in all three.

_Notes._

> In older design notes, this chassis category was expected to switch from intimidation, escort, and labor tasks without changing body architecture.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Disambiguator Subroutine <span>4 BP</span>

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can force it to make a Charisma saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier).
On a failed save, one magical effect currently affecting it ends (your choice).

You can use this feature once per turn, and a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

_Notes._

> This is a degraded cousin of old disambiguation hardware. It cannot collapse fate or nullity, but it can still collapse weaker active enchantments.

</Collapsible>
<Collapsible>

###### Strappy <span>4 BP</span>

Your frame is exceptionally modular, you can easily attach and detach modules and equipment without the usual limitations.

You instantly attune to items whose attunement process is shorter than an hour, and you cannot be disarmed or otherwise separated from items you are attuned to, save by total annihilation of the item or yourself.
When attaching **monstrous grafts**, all checks are made at advantage, and the process takes half the normal time.

You gain extra hit points equal to half your proficiency bonus for each attuned item.

_Notes._

> Tallians were not machines in the traditional sense. The empyreans could never shrink their engineering down to a truly microscopic level, so they twisted the designs of the world itself to their will - necrobiotics, grafting, cultivation...

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Gravitic Skirt <span>4 BP</span>

Your hover system is upgraded.

- Your hover height becomes 5 ft. x your proficiency bonus.
- While hovering, difficult terrain on solid ground does not reduce your movement.
- Falling damage you take is reduced by an amount equal to twice your proficiency bonus.

_Notes._

> These units were designed for broken causeways and anti-formation terrain where wheels, hooves, and feet failed.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Primeval Plating <span>3 BP</span>

You gain a +1 bonus to Armor Class.

Additionally, when a creature scores a critical hit against you, you can use your reaction to turn it into a normal hit.
You can do this once per short rest.

_Notes._

> Heavy frontal plating was common among Tallians assigned to shield observatory personnel during evacuation phases.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Innate Utility Suite <span>3 BP</span>

Choose one spell from the following list:

- [_Shield_](https://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:shield)
- [_Misty Step_](https://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:misty-step)
- [_Invisibility_](https://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:invisibility)
- [_Pass Without Trace_](https://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:pass-without-trace)

You can cast the chosen spell once per long rest without expending a spell slot.
Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.

_Notes._

> Hunter and commander frames often carried minimalist utility suites so they could operate away from support logistics.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Threatening Pressence <span>2 BP</span>

You gain proficiency in Intimidation. If you gaian another source of proficiency in Intimidation, you are considered to have expertise in it instead.

> The threat of violence the Tallians posed was often more effective than the act itself.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Static Anchor Jets <span>2 BP</span>

When a creature attempts to push, pull, or knock you prone, you can use your reaction to stabilize.

If you do, reduce the forced movement by a number of feet equal to 5 x your proficiency bonus, and you have advantage on the triggering saving throw or ability check.

You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

_Notes._

> Anchor jets were originally built for hull work along the outer shells of high-altitude Empyrean machinery.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Bound Equipment <span>1 BP</span>

As part of a long rest, you can bind your equipment to your frame.
If you do so, the equipment becomes **integrated** with you, and you **cannot be disarmed** or otherwise separated from it in a normal way.
The equipment rules are otherwise respected, no two items can occupy the same slots, and you can only bind to one weapon or similar per usable limb as normal. **Consumable** equipment is consumed as normal if bound to.

In order to separate you from your equipment, the creature attempting to do so needs to pass a DC 10 + your level **smiths** or **tinkers** tools check,
during an hour long procedure.

You can **choose** to separate yourself from the equipment at the end of a short rest.

> Ancient war machines would sooner break entirely than lose access to their weaponry.

</Collapsible>
