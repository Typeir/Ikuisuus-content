# Pellervi

Pellervis are small Pieni folk shaped by comfort culture, practical generosity, and a refusal to grovel before the world.
They are often underestimated until endurance, luck, and domestic craft turn a bad road into a survivable one.
Many Pellervi enclaves grew from post-Interlocking displacement routes, where shelter, food, and trust became survival infrastructure.

### Lucky

When you roll a 1 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll.

### Naturally Stealthy

You can attempt to Hide even when you are obscured only by a creature that is at least one size larger than you.

### Languages

You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **one additional language of your choice**.

## Boons

You have a budget of **10 Boon Points**.

<Collapsible>

###### Back on Your Feet <span>5 BP</span>

Whenever you fail a saving throw, you get advantage on the next saving throw you make before the end of your next turn.

Once per round, when you succeed on a saving throw while bloodied, you regain hit points equal to your level.

These features cannot be used if the saving throw is imposed by a creature that is not hostile to you, or is deliberately caused by an environmental effect by you or your allies.

_Notes._

> Was it sheer luck I wonder, or do their wills harbor resilience above that of mine kin? — Ruminations of Xanthous the Sulphurous, after their imprisonment at the hands of a nameless Pellervi.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Dependable <span>6 BP</span>

When making a attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, treat rolls of 4 or lower as 5.

_Notes._

> Pellervi are known for their reliability, in eons of history not a single soul has filed a complaint about a Pellervi failing to uphold their word, or so the saying goes.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Practical Magic <span>4 BP</span>

You learn one cantrip of your choice from the following list:

- [Mending](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:mending)
- [Create Bonfire](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:create-bonfire)
- [Druidcraft](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:druidcraft)

Choose one 1st-level spell from the following list:

- [Cure Wounds](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:cure-wounds)
- [Goodberry](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:goodberry)
- [Detect Poison and Disease](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:detect-poison-and-disease)

You can cast the chosen 1st-level spell once without expending a spell slot, and you regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for both spells learned through this boon.

_Notes._

> Pellervi magic trends toward maintenance, food security, and immediate care rather than spectacle.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Luck on Loan <span>4 BP</span>

When an allied creature you can see within 30 feet of you rolls a 1 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can use your **reaction** to allow that creature to reroll the die. The creature must use the new roll.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus**, and you regain all uses after a **long rest**.

_Notes._

> During the diaspora after the Interlocking, a single ill choice at the wrong moment could have ended the group. Pellervi learned to watch their companions' actions as closely as their own.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Exactly the Right Thing <span>3 BP</span>

You carry a closed bag in your backpack. It weighs 5 lbs.
When you open it for the first time, you can choose what nonmagical item you had put inside when you last stocked up. The item must cost 5gp at most, and weigh 5lb at most.
You can't use this boon again until you stock up again at a town, and spend an amount of gold equivalent to the cost of the item retrieved.

_Notes._

> Pellervi are known for resourcefulness and planning, and understanding the minutia of how everyday life intertwines with their adventures and misadventures. Sometimes a fork is just what you need. Sometimes you just so happen to need a gore hook.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Reciprocal Hospitality <span>3 BP</span>

When you enter a settled community where your identity is not openly hostile to local law or custom, you can usually secure modest food and lodging for yourself and your companions through polite request and social reciprocity.

At the DM's discretion, this benefit can be denied in settlements under siege, famine, plague lockdown, active purge, or comparable exceptional conditions.

You have advantage on Charisma checks made to request non-monetary aid from civilians who are not currently hostile.

_Notes._

> Pellervi hospitality is transactional only in memory: people remember being hosted well, then return the favor years later.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### No Open Ground <span>3 BP</span>

You can move through the space of any creature that is at least one size larger than you, treating that space as difficult terrain.

While you are within 5 feet of a creature at least one size larger than you, you gain **half cover** from that creature, regardless of whether it is allied or hostile.

Creatures two or more sizes larger than you count as **full cover** for you.

_Notes._

> Surviving accounts from the Interlocking corridors share one pattern: the Pellervi did not clear the road. They moved at the edges of things too large or too preoccupied to pursue them.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Second Breakfast <span>2 BP</span>

As part of a short rest, you can spend 20 uninterrupted minutes sitting down and eating.
At the end of those 20 minutes, you can spend one or more Hit Dice to regain hit points as normal, and you regain additional hit points equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1).

You can gain this additional healing up to three times per short rest.

_Notes._

> Pellervi do not treat meals as flavor or ritual alone. Eating is maintenance, morale, and medicine.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Hard to Kill <span>2 BP</span>

You can mark no more than one failed death saving throw per turn.
This limit applies even if multiple effects would cause additional failures during the same turn.

You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would kill you outright when reducing your hit points to 0 or lower.

_Notes._

> Many Pellervi survival accounts end with a line equivalent to: "should have died there, did not."

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Table Manners <span>2 BP</span>

You gain **proficiency** in the Persuasion skill.

Additionally, when you cook and distribute a hot meal to companions during a **short** or **long rest**, a number of creatures equal to your **proficiency bonus** who eat from it gain **temporary hit points** equal to your **Wisdom modifier** (minimum 1) at the end of the rest.

This benefit requires access to Cook's utensils, a fire, and sufficient rations or fresh ingredients.

_Notes._

> Pellervi hospitality carries a long memory. Those who have eaten at a Pellervi fire tend to say yes when the question arrives three months later.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### General Knowledge <span>1 BP</span>

You gain proficiency in one of the following skills:

- Animal Handling
- Insight
- Medicine
- Perception
- Survival

_Notes._

> Households that trade across roads and seasons accumulate practical, transferable knowledge.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Household Craftiness <span>1 BP</span>

You gain proficiency with one of the following tools:

- Carpenter's tools
- Cook's utensils
- Woodcarver's tools
- Leatherworker's tools
- Cobbler's tools
- Potter's tools

Additionally, when you make an ability check with your chosen tool for a simple task performed under time pressure, you do not suffer disadvantage from being rushed.

_Notes._

> Pellervis build for daily use, frequent repair, and low waste. Their craft priorities are comfort and reliability.

</Collapsible>

<Collapsible>

###### Rumor Mill <span>1 BP</span>

You have advantage when making wisdom or charisma saves related to local gossip, rumors or folk tales. You may be asked to help out on some menial tasks in return.

_Notes._

> Pellervi live in close knit agrarian communities, and are often the first to involve themselves in local businesses, and the first to learn of eecent events.

</Collapsible>
