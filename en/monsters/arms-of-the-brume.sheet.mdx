| --------------------- | ---------------- | --------- |
| 23 (Bonded Tombsteel) | 285 (30d8 + 150) | 30 ft.    |

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 22 (+6) | 18 (+4) | 20 (+5) | 14 (+2) | 16 (+3) | 18 (+4) |

- **Saving Throws**: Strength +14, Dexterity +12, Constitution +13, Wisdom +11
- **Skills**: Athletics +14, Perception +11, Intimidation +12
- **Damage Resistances**: Bludgeoning, Piercing, Slashing

- **Languages**: Common, Empyrean
- **Challenge**: 23 (50,000 XP)
- **Proficiency Bonus**: +7

---

## Traits

#### Legendary Deeds
Arms of the Brume has **3 legendary deeds per round**, and regains all expended deeds at the start of its turn.

#### Entropic Sarcophagus

Arms of the Brume is encased in a sealed tombsteel shell designed for forward momentum and impact over evasion.

- Arms of the Brume has disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks
- Arms of the Brume has advantage on saving throws made to resist being pushed, pulled, or knocked prone
- Arms of the Brume cannot benefit from bonuses to movement speed granted by nonmagical effects

---

#### Grave Chill

Arms of the Brume’s tombsteel armor holds up to 12 charges.  
When a creature hits Arms of the Brume with a melee weapon attack, the attacker immediately takes 15 cold damage as frozen rime shards erupt from the armor. This effect requires no action or reaction.

- Each activation consumes 1 charge
- Charges regenerate at midnight, recovering 6 + 1d6 charges
- This cold damage ignores resistance from nonmagical or mundane sources

---

#### Ferocious Rider

Arms of the Brume rides an ethereal construct in the shape of a great wolf. The wolf is not a creature.

- The wolf cannot be targeted, damaged, moved, restrained, incapacitated, or affected by any effect
- The wolf does not occupy a creature space and does not count as a creature for any purpose
- For all other purposes, the wolf uses the statistics of a standard War Wolf. The wolf cannot take actions other than Dodge, Dash and Disengage.
- Arms of the Brume cannot be dismounted against it's will.

While mounted, Arms of the Brume gains the following benefits:

- Cannot be critically hit
- Opportunity attacks against Arms of the Brume are made at disadvantage

---

#### Doctrine of the Brume

Arms of the Brume projects a disciplined doctrinal aura in a 20-foot radius while conscious.

For each other rider of the Brume within range, all riders gain the following benefits (bonuses stack):

- +3 bonus to all saving throws
- +2 bonus to Armor Class

**Formation Thresholds**

- Three active auras:

  - Resistance to all damage
  - Immunity to forced movement
  - Cannot be knocked prone
  - Riders automatically Disengage at the start of their turns

- Two active auras:

  - Resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, slashing, fire, cold, lightning, and necrotic damage
  - Advantage on saving throws against being pushed, pulled, or knocked prone

- One or fewer active auras:
  - No resistances or movement immunities from this feature

A rider’s aura is suppressed only while that rider is incapacitated.  
Paralyzed, stunned, or unconscious creatures do not contribute to the doctrine.

## Actions

#### Multiattack

Arms of the Brume makes three attacks with **Paivämies Javelin**.

---

- **Paivämies Javelin**  
  _Weapon (Spear), Heavy, Thrown (60/240), Magical_  
  Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +16 to hit, reach 10 ft. or range 60/240 ft., one target.  
  Hit: 26 (3d10 + 10) piercing damage plus 18 (4d8) radiant damage.
  The spear reforms in Arms of the Brume’s hand immediately after being thrown.

---

## Bonus Actions

#### Binding Strike (recharge 6)

Whenever Arms of the Brume hits a creature with Paivämies Javelin, it can use a bonus action to attempt to restrain the target with illusory chains of tombsteel.
- The target must succeed on a DC 25 charisma saving throw or be restrained for 1 minute.
- The restrained target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.
- The attack deals an additional 18 (4d8) radiant damage if the target is restrained when hit.

---

## Legendary Deed: Stratagem

#### Blinding Cascade

**Declare:**  
Arms of the Brume hurls the spear in a precise doctrinal strike, drawing a 5-foot-wide, 120-foot-long line extending from its position.
When this line appears, each creature in it must make a DC 22 Dexterity saving throw.
On a failed save, a creature takes 22 (5d8) radiant damage, and their speed is halved until the end of their next turn.
On a successful save, it takes half as much damage.

**Resolve (start of Arms of the Brume’s next turn):**  
The initial line becomes the axis of execution.
Two 15-foot-wide, 120-foot-long lines erupt simultaneously, one on each side of the original line, perfectly parallel to it and not overlapping.
When these secondary lines appear, each creature in either line must make a DC 22 Dexterity saving throw.
On a failed save, a creature takes 45 (10d8) radiant damage and is blinded for a minute.
On a successful save, it takes half as much damage.
Any creature that was in the original line automatically fails this saving throw.

After the effect resolves, the spear reforms in Arms of the Brume’s hand.

---

## Legendary Deed: Act

Arms of the Brume can expend legendary deeds to use the options below. Only one legendary deed can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn.

- **Punitive Strike.** (Costs 1 Deed)  
  Arms of the Brume makes one attack with Paivämies Javelin.

- **Relocation.** (Costs 1 Deed)  
  Arms of the Brume moves up to 30 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

- **Judgment Advance.** (Costs 3 Deeds)  
  Arms of the Brume immediately declares Blinding Cascade, if available, or recharges it.

---

## Legendary Deed: Phase

#### Bloodied (50% HP)

While Arms of the Brume is bloodied and not incapacitated:

- Paivämies Javelin deals an additional 2d8 radiant damage
- Binding Strike recharges on 4–6
- Whenever Arms of the Brume reduces a creature to 0 HP, it may immediately make one additional attack

If Arms of the Brume becomes incapacitated, these benefits end immediately.
