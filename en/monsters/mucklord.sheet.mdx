## Traits

#### Legendary Deeds

The Mucklord has **3 legendary deeds per round**, and regains all expended deeds at the start of its turn.

#### Breathless, Inertity

The Mucklord does not need to breathe or eat. Although it may do either of the two just for the sake of it.

#### Aura of Stillness (60-foot radius)

Creatures that start their turn in the aura must succeed on a **DC 23 Strength saving throw** or their speed becomes **0** until the start of their next turn. Creatures immune to paralysis instead have their speed **halved**.

#### Garbage Communion

The Mucklord is aware of refuse and corpses within **1 mile**. As a **bonus action**, it calls debris in an **80 feet radius** to itself, **5 temporary hit points** per corpse CR or Character Level, **consuming said corpses**.

#### Belly of the Muck

A swallowed creature is **banished** to the **Muck Dimension** (see [_Nullbrand_](/en/library/items/heirlooms/nullbrand)). While there, the creature is **incapacitated** and takes **6d10 force damage** at the start of each of its turns until it escapes or the effect ends.
If a creature that is not a **construct** falls below 0HP while in the Muck Dimension, they are **instantly** absorbed into the muck, along with all they are carrying, and dissolve into sludge.

#### Cannon Tongue

The Mucklord’s tongue is a siege cannon. It can target any point it can see within **600 ft.** It has **disadvantage** on attack rolls against creatures within **10 ft.**

#### Sad Resilience

Once per round, when the Mucklord takes damage, it gains **temporary hit points** equal to **half the damage taken** until the start of its next turn. The source creature must succeed on a **DC 23 Wisdom saving throw** or be **terrified** of the Mucklord until the end of its next turn.
If the mucklord then takes damage that does not exceed the temporary hit points, the source of the damage is cursed with a **Curse of indolence**, applying a stack of [_Erosion_](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/illnesses/erosion) **as if the d4 had landed on a "4"**,
and forcing them to use their bonus action to harmlessly sigh when in combat. This effect stacks indefinitely.

#### Gag Reflex

When attacked, the Mucklord must succeed on a **Concentration Saving Throw**, on a failure, it spews out the contents of it's belly, including those transported to the **Muck Dimension**

## Actions

#### Multiattack

The Mucklord makes **one Cannon Tongue** attack and **two Crushing Claw** attacks.

#### Crushing Claw

_Melee Weapon Attack:_ +17 to hit, reach 20 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 55 (8d10 + 10) bludgeoning damage plus 22 (4d10) acid damage. If the target is Large or smaller, it must succeed on a **DC 22 Strength saving throw** or be **grappled** (escape **DC 23**).

#### Swallow

The Mucklord makes one **Crushing Claw** attack against a **grappled** Medium or smaller target. On a hit, the target is **swallowed** and transported to the **Muck Dimension** (see _Belly of the Muck_).

#### Cannon Tongue (Recharge 5–6)

_Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +20 to hit, range 600 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ 25 + (20d8) force, plus an extra 25 force damage per stack of erosion on the target, and the target must succeed on a DC 25 Charisma saving throw or suffer the effects of [_Feeblemind_](http://www.dnd5e.wikidot.com/spell:feeblemind).  
_Miss:_ The shot detonates prematurely; creatures within **15 ft.** of the Mucklord must make a **DC 23 Dexterity saving throw** or take **40 (10d6) acid damage**, **40 (10d6) fire damage** and **40 (10d6) piercing damage**.

## Legendary Deed: Act

The Mucklord can expend legendary deeds to use the options below. Only one legendary deed can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn.

- **Detect.** (Costs 1 Deed) The Mucklord makes a Wisdom (Perception) check with advantage.
- **Lurch.** (Costs 2 Deeds) The Mucklord moves up to 30 feet. All creatures that harm it during this movement take 20 acid damage.
- **Brine Rattle.** (Costs 3 Deeds) The Mucklord releases a mist of poisonous brine. Creatures within 40 ft. of the Mucklord must make a **DC 23 Dexterity saving throw** or take 54 (12d8) poison damage and gain a level of exhaustion. Creatures take half as much damage on a success and do not gain exhaustion.

## Legendary Deed: Stratagem

#### The Pit Remembers

_Declared at the end of the Mucklord’s turn; resolves at the start of its next turn. Recharges after combat._

The mucklord attempts to consume a **120-foot cone** in front of it. Creatures in the area must succeed a **DC 24 Strength saving throw** or **sink 10 ft.** and become **restrained**.  
At resolution, all **restrained** creatures are devoured and **banished** to the **Muck Dimension** (see [_Nullbrand_](/en/library/items/heirlooms/nullbrand)) for **1 minute**, taking **6d10 force damage** at the start of each of their turns there.  
If the Mucklord dies while this effect is active, the breach collapses, dealing **150 (20d12) force damage** to all creatures within **120 ft.**

#### Gluttony and Hatred

_Declared at the end of the Mucklord’s turn; resolves at the start of its next turn. Recharges after combat._
At declaration, the Mucklord **devours itself**, consuming its own mass. Its body vanishes entirely at the **end of its turn**.
Until resolution, a **spectral afterimage** of the Mucklord is visible, trudging in a **straight line** along the path it will take through the **Muck Dimension**. This path is obvious, slow, and uninterrupted; it cannot turn, stop, or change direction once declared.
The afterimage ignores terrain, obstacles, and creatures.
At the start of its next turn, the Mucklord **re-emerges violently** at any point along that line, detonating outward in a rupture of brine and compressed mass.

- **Re-emergence Area**: 40-foot radius sphere centered on the emergence point.
- **Saving Throw**: DC **24 Strength**.

On a **failure**, a creature takes **88 (16d10) bludgeoning damage** and **44 (8d10) acid damage**, is **pushed 30 feet** directly away from the center, and is **knocked prone**.  
On a **success**, the creature takes half damage, is pushed **10 feet**, and is not knocked prone.

If the Mucklord emerges in a space occupied by one or more creatures, those creatures **automatically fail** the saving throw, take the full damage, and are forcibly displaced to the nearest unoccupied spaces of the Mucklord’s choice.
Creatures reduced to **0 hit points** by this detonation are **absorbed** along with everything they were carrying, the Mucklord gains **20 temporary hit points per creature** absorbed this way.
The Mucklord is considered **absent from the battlefield** between disappearance and re-emergence. It cannot be targeted, damaged, or affected during this time.

## Legendary Deed: Lair

At the start of the creature with the highest initiative's turn, the Mucklord can expend a legendary deed to use one of the following lair deeds.  
It cannot use the same lair deed two rounds in a row.

---

#### Forlorn Summons

The Mucklord convulses, belching up fragments of the **Muck Dimension**.  
From the brine emerge malformed Forlorn — remnants of abandoned machines and creatures forgotten by time.

Roll **1d4**, summoning the corresponding Forlorn in unoccupied spaces within **60 feet** of the Mucklord.  
They act immediately after the Mucklord in the initiative order and remain until destroyed or until the Mucklord uses this action again.

| d4  | Summoned Creature                                                                 |
| --- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **1 + 1d6 [Forlorn Sludgelings](/en/library/monsters/forlorn-sludgeling)**        |
| 2   | **2 [Brine Sea Worm](/en/library/monsters/brine-sea-worm)**                       |
| 3   | **1 [Toxic Avenger](/en/library/monsters/toxic-avenger)**                         |
| 4   | **1 [Abandoned Old War Machine](/en/library/monsters/abandoned-old-war-machine)** |

---

#### Forlorn Detonation

The Mucklord emits a shuddering groan that resonates through the brine, forcing every active Forlorn within **300 feet** to rupture violently.

Each Forlorn detonates in a **30-foot radius**, forcing all creatures in range to make a **DC 23 Dexterity saving throw**.  
On a failure, the creature takes **acid and force damage equal to as many d6 as the Forlorn's CR** and is **knocked prone**.  
On a success, it takes half damage and is not knocked prone.

If **three or more** Forlorn are destroyed this way, the Mucklord regains **50 hit points** and gains **temporary hit points equal to 10 × the forlorn's CR** for every forlorn destroyed.

---

#### Mass Rusting

The brine surrounding the Mucklord rises and begins to seethe with Nullbrand’s resonance.  
All creatures **standing on brine** or within **60 feet** of the Mucklord that are touching the ground gain **one stack of [_Erosion_](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/illnesses/erosion)** .

If a creature already has **[_Erosion_](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/illnesses/erosion)**, regardless of whether it is on brine or not it must succeed on a **DC 23 Constitution saving throw** or gain **another stack** instead.  
Objects and weapons made of metal in contact with the ground take **10d10 force damage**, ignoring resistance.

> Everything fades, eventually.

---

#### Temper Tantrum

When wounded or ignored, the Mucklord lets out a miserable, childlike wail that shakes the entire field.  
Shutes around the chamber release offerings to appease it — the **rotting corpses** of failed warriors and discarded equipment.

Roll **1d20** to determine the tantrum’s intensity.

- The number rolled is the number of **corpses (1d20)** that **fall into the arena**, landing in random unoccupied spaces within the lair of the Mucklord.
- The **DC** for the resulting shockwave is **35 – the number rolled** (minimum 15, maximum 34).
- The Mucklord then emits a thunderous cry, or a joyful screech. All creatures of its choice within **300 feet** must make a **Constitution saving throw (DC = result)**.
  - **Fail**: The creature is **deafened and blinded** until the end of its next turn and takes **(DC)d4 thunder damage**.
  - **Success**: No damage or condition.

The corpses remain where they fall and can be consumed by the Mucklord as part of its **Garbage Communion** feature.  
They are inert but reanimate if struck by brine or acid during subsequent rounds.

> The tantrum passes quickly. The corpses stay forever.
