# Conjure Celestial

Something answers. It is not an angel, and it is not glad to be here. It performs the shape of help with the patience of a machine that has done this a great many times.

---

> **Conjure Celestial**  
> _5th-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 90 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a mirror-polished disc of tombsteel, held up to nothing)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 hour  
>  
> You call an **agent of the Canvas** into an unoccupied space you can see within range — alien, procedural, majesty in some state of degradation. Its Challenge Rating, or the combined rating of what answers, may not exceed **twice this spell's level**. See [Summoning and Conjuration](/en/library/rules/arcana-and-the-fold/summoning-and-conjuration) for the Challenge Budget, Swarms and Formations, command, and loss of control.  
>  
> Celestials are the hardest things in the world to call and the hardest to send away, which is why this spell sits where it does and not lower.  
>  
> **The one order it will not take.** A celestial cannot be commanded to act against its alignment. In practice this seldom binds — most celestials are unaligned servants of the world's uncaring machinery and will do very nearly anything with the same flat courtesy — but the exceptional aligned ones refuse, and are essence-bound enough that any detection worth the name will tell you which you have called.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** The Challenge Budget is always twice the level of the slot spent.
