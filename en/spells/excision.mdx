# Excision

You excise life from the shells of your enemies, leaving but husks behind.

---

> **Excision**  
> _4th-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 150 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (the powder of a crushed black pearl worth at least 500 gp, required for the 6th-level mode and above)  
> **Duration**: Instantaneous  
>  
> Rot wells up through a creature of your choice that you can see within range, draining moisture and vitality from it. The target must make a Constitution saving throw, taking [% 8d8 dark %] on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. This spell has no effect on the undead or constructs.  
>  
> If you target a plant creature or a magical plant, it makes the saving throw with disadvantage and the spell deals maximum damage to it. A mundane plant that isn't a creature simply withers and dies.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.**  
>  
> - **6th level or higher:** the excision instead fills a **60-foot-radius sphere** centered on a point within range. Each creature in the area saves against [% 8d6 dark %]. The corpses of creatures slain in the area are scoured — they cannot be raised as undead.  
> - **8th level or higher:** the excision instead fills a **30-foot cube** of momentary, impenetrable darkness centered on a point within range. Each creature in the area saves against [% 12d8 dark %]. Aberrations, Monstrosities, and Celestials take maximum damage from it.  
> - For each slot level above a mode's minimum, its damage increases by [% 1d8 %].
