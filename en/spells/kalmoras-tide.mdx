# Kalmora's Tide

The grave-current runs early. It moves through the living the way it moves through the drowned, and it does not much distinguish between the two.

---

> **Kalmora's Tide**  
> _5th-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 60 feet  
> **Components**: V, M (a mouthful of grave-silt)  
> **Duration**: Instantaneous
>
> You open Kalmora's pull beneath one creature you can see within range. The target makes a Constitution saving throw, taking [% 5d12 dark %] on a failure or half as much on a success. A construct is unaffected — the current has nothing in it to take hold of.
>
> **The Tide Rises.** At the start of your next turn, corpses within 20 feet of the target stand up as constructs (undead), each with a Challenge Rating no higher than the creature it was made from. Together they cannot exceed the **Challenge Budget** for this spell's slot — twice the slot level, as laid out in [Summoning and Conjuration](/en/library/rules/arcana-and-the-fold/summoning-and-conjuration). Corpses past the budget stay where they fell.
>
> None of them are yours. Each pursues the closest creature it can see, and shows you no preference.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 6th level or higher, the damage increases by [% 1d12 %] for each slot level above 5th. The Challenge Budget rises with the slot, so a higher casting raises more of the field, or worse things from it.
