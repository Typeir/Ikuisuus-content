# Despairing Darkness

You pull down a patch of the dark that lies beneath the eternal stairs, from the tunnels under the Brume where the Everdark has been sitting undisturbed for a very long time. The orb it makes is deathly quiet. Nothing comes out of it, sound included, and everyone inside it learns what the tunnels are like.

> **Despairing Darkness**  
> _8th-level Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 150 feet  
> **Components**: V, M  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 10 minutes  
>  
> Magical darkness spreads from a point you choose within range to fill a 60-foot-radius sphere until the spell ends. The darkness spreads around corners. A creature with darkvision can't see through this darkness. Mundane light, as well as light created by spells of 8th level or lower, can't illuminate the area.  
>  
> **No sound enters the sphere and none leaves it.** Inside, there is nothing to hear at all — not your own breathing, not the creature beside you, not the thing you are certain is also in here. Whenever a creature starts its turn in the sphere it must make a Wisdom saving throw, taking [% 8d8 psychic %] on a failed save or half as much on a success. The damage is the pressure of the place, not anything it says to you.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 9th level, the damage increases by [% 1d8 %] for each slot level above 8th.
