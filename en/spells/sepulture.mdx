# Sepulture

Perform an attack akin to a funeral rite on a creature within range, marking them as about to die or deceased.

---

> **Sepulture**  
> _Necromancy cantrip_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Action  
> **Range**: 120 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M*
> **Duration**: 1 round
> 
> 
>  Make a ranged attack against a target, a ghastly burial overtaking them. On a hit, the target takes 1d8 necrotic damage. 
>  
>  If the attack hits, the target will be marked for death, thus it can't regain hit points until the start of your next turn. If this damages a creature that was bloodied or undead, it also has disadvantage on attack rolls against you until the end of your next turn.  
> 
> The spell's damage increases by 1d8 when you reach 5th level (2d8), 11th level (3d8), and 17th level (4d8).  
> 
> Additionally, you may cast this spell on a corpse after performing a simple ceremony that lasts at least 30 minutes. If you do, it can't be reanimated through nigromancy.

###### Burial traditions in damocles

Choose a rite from one of the cultures you belong to. that will be the material component the spell demands, and the aspect the spell will take.

| Rite         | Material                          | Effect                                                     | Practicing Cultures      |
| ------------ | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------ |
| Cremation    | a pinch of ash  | ghastly flames swirling around their body | Brume empire, homunculi, silent ones|
| Earth burial | a pinch of dirt from a graveyard  | the suffocating pressure of the grave                      | Pellervi           |
| Sea burial   | a vial of saltwater     | the cold embrace of the ocean       | Borossa             |
| Sky burial   | a vulture's feather    | vulltures pecking at their innards     | Edo        |
| Display      | a small black gauze veil          | spectral gazes looking at them as if they had already died | Tammeni, tallian, väärät |
| Embalming    | a drop of oil and a grain of salt | bandages cover their body and salt mummifies tissue             | Tammeni archivists       |

_Notes._

> Bilupine slaves do not receive burial of any kind in the brume empire.
> Silent ones' corpses are often abandoned on the outskirts of town, which constitutes a sky burial only on the broadest sense of the term. When given the choice they usually prefer cremation, as they feel it is a method of returning to paivamies.
