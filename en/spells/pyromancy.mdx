# Pyromancy

You harness the swirling embers into a speck of pure flame, the thought of incineration.

> **Pyromancy**  
> _Conjuration cantrip_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: Self  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: 10 minutes
>
> A flickering flame appears in your hand. The flame remains there for the duration and harms neither you nor your equipment.
> The flame sheds bright light in a 10-foot radius and dim light for an additional 10 feet. The spell ends if you dismiss it as a Major Action or if you cast it again.
>
> As a Major Action while holding the flame, you can set it upon a 5-foot square of ground within 20 feet to create a bonfire.
> Until the spell ends, the bonfire fills a 5-foot cube in that space. A creature that enters the bonfire's space or ends its turn there must make a Dexterity saving throw or suffer the [_Burning_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#burning) condition.
> The bonfire ignites flammable objects in its space that aren't being worn or carried. You can have only one bonfire at a time; creating another extinguishes the first. As a Minor Action, you can recall the flame to your hand.
>
> When you cast this spell, or as a Major Action on a later turn, you can hurl the flame at a creature within 20 feet of you. Make a ranged spell attack against the target.
> On a hit, the target must make a Dexterity saving throw or suffer the [_Burning_](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#burning) condition.
>
> From **5th level**, a hurled flame that hits also deals [% 1d8 fire %] on top of the Burning condition. This increases to [% 2d8 fire %] at 11th level and [% 3d8 fire %] at 17th. The bonfire never deals damage directly — it only sets things alight, which is usually enough.
