# Prophecy

This spell is not innate to any vocation, but rather, granted as a boon or a blessing by a **Heirloom** or a **higher power**.  
This spell can be attained by those who have lived long enough to see the birth of fate.

> “Destiny is written in the canvas of the world, but it's ink is not yet dry.”

---

> **Prophecy**  
> _11th-Level Legendary Divination_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 action  
> **Range**: Touch  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a Sphere of Annihilation, which the spell consumes)  
> **Duration**: Special  
> **Ritual**
>
> You imbue a Sphere of Annihilation with the weight of fate itself, transforming it into a **Star of Prophecy**. As you complete the spell, you speak aloud a prophecy—a specific outcome you wish to manifest in reality. So long as the star remains intact, the prophecy will **inevitably become true**.
>
> Once the prophecy is fulfilled, the star is destroyed and ceases to exist.
>
> ## Time and Scope
>
> The time required for a prophecy to manifest depends on the **magnitude, complexity, and causal distance** of the effect. The DM determines the appropriate timeframe using the following framework:
>
> ### Base Manifestation Time
>
> | **Scope**      | **Base Time**                       | **Description**                                           |
> | -------------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
> | Immediate      | 1 round                             | Direct, modest physical manipulation within line of sight |
> | Trivial        | 1d6 rounds                          | Simple events involving one creature or object            |
> | Minor          | 2d6 rounds                          | Events requiring multiple coincidences                    |
> | Moderate       | 4d6 rounds                          | Events affecting a small group or location                |
> | Significant    | 1d6 days                            | Events requiring shifts in behavior or circumstance       |
> | Major          | 1d6 years                           | Events affecting organizations or regions                 |
> | World-Altering | 1d6 decades                         | Events reshaping nations or civilizations                 |
> | Godlike        | 1d6 centuries                       | Events transcending mortal understanding                  |
> | Cosmic         | Longer than the universe's timespan | Stopping the end of the world, etc                        |
>
> ### Complexity Modifiers
>
> The DM may adjust the timeframe based on these factors:
>
> - **Resistance to Fate**: Prophecies affecting creatures with high Wisdom or legendary resistance may take longer as fate must work around their agency.
> - **Distance**: Events occurring far from the star's location may require additional time for causal chains to propagate.
> - **Probability**: Events that would naturally occur anyway manifest faster; highly improbable events take longer.
>
> ### Examples
>
- **"The chieftain's axe shatters"** (Immediate): 1 round—direct physical effect, visible target
- **"The guard trips and drops his keys"** (Trivial): 1d6 rounds—simple event, single agent
- **"A fire breaks out in the warehouse district"** (Minor): 2d6 rounds—requires specific circumstances to align
- **"The merchant caravan arrives safely despite the ambush"** (Moderate): 4d6 rounds—multiple factors must align, several agents involved
- **"The duke's advisor betrays him to his enemies"** (Significant): 1d6 days—requires shift in loyalty, opportunity, and motivation
- **"The siege ends with the defenders victorious"** (Major): 1d6 years—requires countless small events to cascade toward outcome
- **"The Kingdom collapses into civil war"** (World-Altering): 1d6 decades—requires systemic failure across institutions
- **"The return of the Black One is delayed by a millennium"** (Godlike): 1d6 centuries—requires fundamental alteration of cosmic forces
>
>
> ## Rules and Restrictions
>
> - The prophecy must be **specific and achievable**. Vague or impossible prophecies fail, and the spell is wasted.
> - The Star of Prophecy appears as a luminous point of light. It remains stationary in the location where it was created.
> - If the star is **destroyed or dispelled** before the prophecy is fulfilled, the prophecy fails and the effect never occurs.
> - The star can be dispelled with **Dispel Magic** cast with a spell slot of level 11 or higher, or destroyed by dealing **1000 hit points** of damage to it (AC 20, immune to all conditions).
> - You cannot have more than **one Star of Prophecy** active at a time. Casting this spell again causes the previous star to wink out harmlessly.
