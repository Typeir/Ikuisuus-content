> **Summon Construct**
> _4th-level Conjuration_
> **Casting Time**: 1 Action
> **Range**: 90 feet
> **Components**: V, S, M (an ornate stone and metal lockbox worth at least 400 gp)
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 hour
>
> You call forth a construct spirit. It manifests in an unoccupied space that you can see within range. This corporeal form uses the Summoned Construct stat block. When you cast the spell, choose clay, metal, or stone. The creature resembles a golem or other construct of the chosen material, which determines certain traits in its stat block. The creature disappears when it drops to 0 hit points or when the spell ends.
>
> The summoned creature is an ally to you and your companions. In combat, the creature shares your initiative count, but it takes its turn immediately after yours. It obeys your verbal commands (no action required by you). If you don’t issue any, it takes the Dodge action and uses its move to avoid danger.

> **Constructed Resilience.** The construct has advantage on saving throws against being poisoned, and it has resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from nonmagical attacks.

> **Material Nature.** The construct has one of the following traits, depending on the material you chose:
>
> *Clay.* The construct’s speed increases by 10 feet, and it can move across difficult terrain made of earth or stone without expending extra movement.
>
> *Metal.* The construct has resistance to acid damage, and its attacks are considered magical for the purpose of overcoming immunity and resistance to nonmagical attacks and damage.
>
> *Stone.* The construct has advantage on saving throws against effects that would move it or knock it prone.
>
> **Multiattack.** The construct makes a number of attacks equal to half this spell’s level (rounded down).

> **Slam.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* your spell attack modifier to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 1d8 + PB + the spell’s level bludgeoning damage.
