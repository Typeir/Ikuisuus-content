# Crown of Madness

A jagged circlet of iron closes over the brow — a dead king's regalia, from a kingdom that appears on no map — and something wrong gets into the eyes beneath it.

---

> **Crown of Madness**  
> _2nd-level Enchantment_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 120 feet  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute
>
> One humanoid of your choice that you can see within range must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become **charmed** by you for the duration. While charmed this way, the crown sits on its head and a madness glows in its eyes.
>
> A crowned creature cannot tell ally from enemy. Once on each of its turns it must make one melee attack against a creature of your choosing — at whatever point in its turn you direct, not only before it moves. It can act normally otherwise, and acts entirely normally if you choose no creature or if the one you choose is out of its reach.
>
> Holding the crown in place costs you a **Minor Action** on each of your turns; if you do not spend it, the spell ends. The target repeats the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the spell on itself on a success.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, you can crown one additional humanoid for each slot level above 2nd. Each saves separately and breaks free separately, and one Minor Action holds every crown you are maintaining.
