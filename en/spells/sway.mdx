# Sway

Nobody's mind is changed. Their footing is — one step along a slope they were already standing on, and the rest follows on its own.

---

> **Sway**  
> _2nd-level Enchantment_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 60 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a lump of alum soaked in vinegar, or a drop of honey, depending which way you mean it)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute
>
> Choose a direction along the [attitude](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/disposition-reputation-and-attitude) track — **toward hostile**, **toward neutral**, or **toward friendly** — and name whom the shift concerns.
>
> Each humanoid in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point you choose within range must make a Charisma saving throw, and any creature may choose to fail it. On a failure, that creature's attitude toward the named subject moves **one step** in your chosen direction for the duration.
>
> **Toward neutral** additionally suppresses any effect currently making the target charmed or frightened. A suppressed effect resumes when this spell ends, if its own duration has not run out in the meantime.
>
> A shifted creature snaps back the instant it is attacked or harmed, or sees one of its own harmed. Attitude is cheap to move and expensive to keep.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, the radius increases by 10 feet for each slot level above 2nd.
>
> **From 8th level**, you may instead spend **1 hour** to fix an attitude into a place or an object rather than a crowd. Name one kind of intelligent creature — sludgelings, platonian hunters, hounds of Tindalos — and invest the target with an aura that holds for **10 days**:
>
> **Repulsion.** A named creature that sees the target or comes within 60 feet of it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be **frightened** while it remains in sight or in range, using its movement to reach the nearest spot from which the target cannot be seen.
>
> **Attraction.** A named creature that sees the target or comes within 60 feet of it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or spend its movement each turn approaching the target, and cannot willingly move away once it arrives. If the target harms it, it may save again immediately.
>
> In either case, a creature that ends its turn more than 60 feet from the target and cannot see it may make a Wisdom saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success.
