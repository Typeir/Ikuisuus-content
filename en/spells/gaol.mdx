# Gaol

This spell is not innate to any vocation, but rather, granted as a boon or a blessing by a **Heirloom** or a **higher power**.  
This spell is born to those who carry a burden most gruesome: that of past sins.

> “Given enough time even demons become saints.” — Plato the Last

---

> **Gaol**  
> _12th-Level Legendary Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 action  
> **Range**: 1 mile  
> **Components**: S  
>
> You designate an area of reality you can see, centered on a point within range, with a radius up to **1 mile** (your choice).  
> The selected space must be a continuous region and may include structures, terrain, and creatures.
>
> Reality is **excised**: the designated space is torn free and **shunted into the Folds**, becoming a sealed prison-space whose interior geometry is identical to the area as it was at the instant of casting.
>
> The excised space is no longer part of the world. In its place remains a **concave, glassy crater** that precisely matches the removed volume. The crater is inert, reflective, and cannot be dispelled by magic of **11th level or lower**.
>
> **Creatures and objects** wholly or partially within the area when the spell is cast are pulled into the Gaol.  
> Creatures pulled into the Gaol must succeed on a **Strength saving throw** or be **knocked prone** as the world lurches beneath them.
>
> ---
>
> ## Inside the Gaol
>
> The Gaol is not a demiplane with new terrain—it is the **literal space that was removed**, preserved and sealed. While inside:
>
> - The boundary of the Gaol is a perfectly rigid limit, secured by glowing stakes. A creature cannot pass through it by nonmagical means.
> - A creature that attempts to cross the boundary through magical means is forcibly thrust into the Folds and suffers **Fold Exposure** (below).
>
> ---
>
> ## Fold Exposure (Attempted Escape)
>
> If a creature is ever forced into the boundary, attempts to exit, or otherwise makes contact with the prison’s limit, it is briefly shunted into the Folds.
> While exposed (immediately, and again at the start of each of its turns until it returns fully inside the Gaol), the creature takes:
>
> - **40d10 radiant damage**
> - **40d10 force damage**
> - **40d10 necrotic damage**
>
> This damage treats immunities as resistances.
> A creature reduced to 0 hit points by Fold Exposure is **annihilated**, leaving no remains.
>
> A creature returns fully inside the Gaol at the end of its turn if it is not in contact with the boundary.
> While within the folds, a creature may exit the Gaol by moving one mile away from the boundary in any direction.
>
> ---
>
> ## Ending the Spell
>
> When the spell ends, the excised space is restored to reality exactly where it was removed from, returning all surviving creatures and objects to their original positions (or the nearest unoccupied spaces if overlap would occur).
>
> The glassy crater remains under the restored area, inert and undisturbed.
>
> If the spell ends early due to loss of concentration, the return is violent: each creature in the Gaol must succeed on a **Constitution saving throw** or be **stunned** until the end of its next turn as the world “snaps” back into continuity, and all creatures, unsecured objects and structures take **15d12 force damage**.
