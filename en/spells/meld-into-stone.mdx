# Meld into Stone

Your body sinks into adjacent stone, flesh flattening and sealing as you become embedded in the rock.

---

> **Meld into Stone**  
> _3rd-level Transmutation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: Touch  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: 8 hours  
>  
> You step into a stone object or surface large enough to fully contain your body, melding yourself and all the equipment you carry with the stone for the duration.  
>  
> While merged with the stone, you can’t see what occurs outside it, and any Wisdom (Perception) check you make to hear sounds outside it is made with disadvantage. **Vibration is the exception** — footsteps, digging, a wagon on the road above — which you feel through the rock plainly, and without that disadvantage.  
>  
> You remain aware of the passage of time and can cast spells on yourself. You can use your movement to leave the stone where you entered it, which ends the spell; otherwise you are unable to move.  
>  
> Stone keeps what it is given. Time spent merged **counts as safe rest**: nothing finds you in there, and a Recovery taken inside the rock is a Recovery taken.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, you can take **one additional willing creature** into the stone with you for each slot level above 3rd, provided the stone is large enough to hold you all.  
>  
> Minor physical damage to the stone doesn’t harm you, but its partial destruction or a change in its shape (to the extent that you no longer fit within it) expels you and deals [% 6d6 bludgeoning %] to you. The stone’s complete destruction (or transmutation into a different substance) expels you and deals 50 bludgeoning damage to you. If expelled, you fall prone in an unoccupied space closest to where you first entered.
