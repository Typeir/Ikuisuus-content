# Sigil of Ruin

The sigil does not guard the gate. It watches the heart of whoever comes to the door, and it has never once been wrong about what it finds waiting there.

---

> **Sigil of Ruin**  
> _3rd-level Abjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Minute  
> **Range**: Touch  
> **Components**: V, S, M (powdered lodestone and a drop of your own blood, worked into the mark)  
> **Duration**: Until dispelled or triggered
>
> You inscribe a ruinous sigil on a surface no larger than 10 feet across, or within an object that can be closed to hide it — a book, a chest, a doorframe. If that object is moved more than 10 feet from where you inscribed it, the sigil breaks and the spell ends without triggering. The mark is nearly invisible, and finding it takes a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC.
>
> **The sigil reads intent.** Rather than watching for a touch or a footfall, the sigil **reads the true disposition** of every creature that comes within 30 feet of it, as defined in [Disposition, Reputation, and Attitude](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/disposition-reputation-and-attitude). It triggers on the first creature whose disposition toward you — or toward whatever the sigil guards — sits in the **Hostile** range. Because it reads intent and not allegiance, a disguise, an invisibility, a false uniform, or a kind word cannot fool it; only a creature that genuinely means no harm passes it by. When you inscribe the sigil you may name a password, or a specific creature, that it always lets through.
>
> When you inscribe the sigil, choose one of the following effects.
>
> **Ruin.** When triggered, the sigil bursts in a 20-foot-radius sphere that spreads around corners. Each creature in the area makes a Dexterity saving throw, taking [% 5d8 %] chemical, frost, fire, lightning, or bludgeoning damage — your choice at inscription — on a failed save, or half as much on a success.
>
> **Stored Casting.** As part of inscribing the sigil, you cast a prepared spell of a level no higher than this one into it; that spell takes no effect until the sigil triggers, at which point it goes off, targeting or centred on the triggering creature. A stored spell that requires concentration lasts its full duration without your attention.
>
> Once triggered, the sigil is spent and the spell ends.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, a **Ruin** sigil's damage increases by [% 1d8 %] for each slot level above 3rd, and a **Stored Casting** sigil can hold a spell of up to the slot's level.
>
> When you cast this spell using a spell slot of **7th level or higher**, you may instead inscribe a **greater sigil**. On triggering, it flares to fill a 60-foot-radius sphere with dim light for 10 minutes. Each creature caught in the sphere when it flares — and any that enters or ends its turn there before the light fades — is subject to an effect you chose at inscription:
>
> - **Death.** Constitution saving throw, taking [% 10d10 dark %] on a failed save, or half as much on a success.
> - **Pain.** Constitution saving throw, or be **incapacitated** with agony for 1 minute.
> - **Stunning.** Wisdom saving throw, or be **stunned** for 1 minute.
> - **Fear.** Wisdom saving throw, or be **frightened** for 1 minute, dropping whatever it holds and moving at least 30 feet from the sigil on each of its turns if able.
> - **Sleep.** Wisdom saving throw, or fall **unconscious** for 10 minutes, waking early if it takes damage or a creature uses a Major Action to rouse it.
> - **Discord.** Constitution saving throw, or be **incapacitated** with uncontrollable laughter for 1 minute.
>
> Except for **Death**, a creature affected by a greater sigil repeats the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 8th level or higher, the **Death** effect's damage increases by [% 1d10 %] for each slot level above 7th.
