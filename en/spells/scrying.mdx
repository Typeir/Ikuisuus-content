# Scrying

A pale, glassy aperture opens in the air, revealing a live view of a distant scene.

---

> **Scrying**  
> _5th-level Divination_  
> **Casting Time**: 10 Minutes  
> **Range**: Self  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a basin of still water, or a mirror backed with lead)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 30 minutes
>
> You can see and hear a particular creature you choose that is **within the same Fold of the world** as you. The target must make a Wisdom saving throw, modified by how well you know it and what you hold of it. A target that knows you are casting this spell can fail the save deliberately if it wants to be seen.
>
> | Knowledge                                 | Save Modifier |
> | ----------------------------------------- | ------------- |
> | Secondhand (you have heard of the target) | +5            |
> | Firsthand (you have met the target)       | +0            |
> | Familiar (you know the target well)       | −5            |
>
> | Connection                                        | Save Modifier |
> | ------------------------------------------------- | ------------- |
> | Likeness or picture                               | −2            |
> | Possession or garment                             | −4            |
> | Body part, lock of hair, bit of nail, or the like | −10           |
>
> On a successful save the target is unaffected, and you cannot target it with this spell again for 24 hours.
>
> On a failed save, an invisible sensor forms within 10 feet of the target. You see and hear through it as though you stood there. It moves with the target, staying within 10 feet of it for the duration. A creature that can see invisible objects sees a luminous orb about the size of your fist.
>
> Instead of a creature, you can choose a location you have seen before. The sensor appears there and does not move.
>
> The sensor is a small aperture held open in the Fold, and the Fold is watched. Those who scry often, and from the same place, are found eventually by the things that follow such openings home.
