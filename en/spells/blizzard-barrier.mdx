# Blizzard Barrier

A wall of solid ice rockets up from the ground, forming a tall barrier that frosts the air.

---

> **Blizzard Barrier**
> _6th-level Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Action  
> **Range**: 120 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 10 minutes
>  
>  You create a barrier of ice on a solid surface within range, it is enveloped by a blizzard on each side. The wall is made of up to ten *permafrost panes*, which you may choose to make all transparent or opaque. Each panel must be contiguous with another panel, and they last for the duration.
>  
> If the wall cuts through a creature’s space when it appears, the creature is pushed to one side of the wall (you choose which side) and makes a Dexterity saving throw, taking 5d6 Cold damage on a failed save or half as much damage on a successful one. 
---
> **Permafrost Pane**
> 10'x10'x1' Solid ice panel
>  
> **Armor Class** 12  
> **Hit Points** 30 + 10 for each spell level above 6th  
>  
> **Damage Immunities** Psychic, cold, poison
> **Damage Resistances** Piercing, dark
> **Damage Vulnerabilities** Bludgeoning, fire
>  
> **Polar Atmosphere.** The pane is covered in a sheet of glacial air, which covers any squares cut by it. This air makes them difficult terrain. A creature in the glacial air when it appears, starting their turn in one, or entering one for the first time in a turn must make a Constitution saving throw. That creature takes 4d6 cold damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.  Once the pane is destroyed, the glacial air remains for a minute, but strong wind can disperse it.
>  
> **Brittle Ice.** A creature can try to breach through and break a section the barrier by entering the polar atmosphere and then making strength saving throw against your spell save DC. On a successful save, the creature destroys the pane. If it fails, the creature takes 1d6 cold damage and falls prone.
---
> If you maintain your concentration on this spell for its whole duration, the ice barrier becomes nonmagical and can’t be dispelled, melting over eight hours under temperate weather. Otherwise, the wall disappears when the spell ends.  
> 
> **Using a Higher-Level Spell Slot.** The damage the wall deals increases by 1d6 for each spell slot level above 6.. and you can create an additional pane
