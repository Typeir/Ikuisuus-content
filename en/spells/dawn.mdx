# Dawn

A column of light stands where you put it and does not go out. Whether it merely lights the room or scours it depends entirely on how much you were willing to spend.

---

> **Dawn**  
> _3rd-level Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 60 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a sunburst of beaten gold worth at least 100 gp)  
> **Duration**: 4 hours
>
> A 60-foot-radius sphere of light spreads from a point you choose within range. The sphere is bright light and sheds dim light for a further 60 feet. **This light is sunlight** — see [Vision, Light, and Environment](/en/library/rules/the-long-road/vision-light-and-environment).
>
> The light spreads around corners. If you choose a point on an object you are holding, or one not being worn or carried, the light emanates from that object and travels with it. Covering the source completely with something opaque blocks the light.
>
> **Where this light overlaps darkness created by a spell of this spell's level or lower, that darkness is dispelled.**
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, the radius increases by 60 feet for each slot level above 3rd, and the light dispels magical darkness created by a spell of the slot's level or lower.
>
> **From 5th level**, you may instead call the light down as a burning column: a 30-foot-radius, 40-foot-high cylinder, the spell becoming **Concentration, up to 1 minute**. Each creature in the cylinder when it appears, and each that ends its turn there, must make a Constitution saving throw, taking [% 4d10 holy %] on a failure or half as much on a success. While within 60 feet of the column you can move it up to 60 feet as a **Minor Action**. In this form the damage increases by [% 1d10 %] for each slot level above 5th.
