# Lithomancy

You give an object a life it never asked for. It wakes, and it understands at once that it has no mouth, and it must scream.

---

> **Lithomancy**  
> _4th-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Minute  
> **Range**: Touch  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a drop of your own blood worked into the object's surface)  
> **Duration**: 8 hours
>
> You wake a will inside a nonmagical object no larger than Medium — a chair, a blade, a shackle, a doorframe. It becomes **The Wretch**, a Construct for the duration: animate and aware, knowing what it is and that it did not choose to be, unable to speak, weep, or die on its own. It obeys you, because obeying you is the only thing its brief, screaming existence permits.
>
> The Wretch acts on your turn, in the space it occupies. You direct it with a Minor Action; left alone, it repeats its last order, or, given none, claws mutely at whatever stands nearest. Its statistics derive from you and the slot you spent:
>
> | The Wretch  |                                                                |
> | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
> | **AC**      | 11 + the spell's level                                         |
> | **HP**      | 5 × the spell's level + your spellcasting modifier             |
> | **Speed**   | 20 ft., dragging itself (a woken door or window does not move) |
> | **Attacks** | uses your spell attack modifier and save DC                    |
>
> **Flail.** _Melee, reach 5 ft._ On a hit, [% 1d6 %] bludgeoning or slashing (by the object's nature) for every two full slot levels spent — [% 2d6 %] at 4th, [% 3d6 %] at 6th, [% 4d6 %] at 8th.
> The damage of **Flail.** Increases with size category as normal.
>
> **It Must Scream.** Once on each of its turns, in place of the Flail, the Wretch screams — the sound of a thing that has just learned it is alive and cannot stop being. One creature of your choice within 10 feet that can hear it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of the Wretch until the end of its next turn. The first time each Wretch screams, you take [% 1d4 psychic %] — a sliver of what you have made it feel returns to you.
>
> When the spell ends, the will goes out. Whether anything lingers in the object afterward is not known, and those who cast this spell twice upon the same object report that the second waking is worse.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 5th level or higher, you may wake one additional Wretch, **or** raise the size cap by one category, for each slot level above 4th.
