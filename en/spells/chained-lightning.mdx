# Chained Lightning

A tether of lightning leaps to a throat and holds there, humming, yours to feed — and when you pour enough into it, it stops holding to one throat and starts to leap between them.

---

> **Chained Lightning**  
> _1st-level Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 30 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a twig from a tree struck by lightning)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute  
>  
> A crackling arc of lightning lances toward a creature within range. Make a ranged spell attack against it. On a hit, the target takes [% 1d12 lightning %], and an arc of lightning links you to it.  
>  
> On each of your later turns, you can use your **Major Action** to feed the tether, dealing [% 1d12 lightning %] to the linked target automatically, with no attack roll. The spell ends if you use your Major Action for anything else, if the target leaves the spell's range, or if it gains total cover from you.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher, both the initial hit and the sustained damage increase by [% 1d12 %] for each slot level above 1st.  
>  
> When you cast this spell using a spell slot of **3rd level or higher**, the tether **chains**: each time it deals damage, the lightning leaps from the linked target to one other creature within 30 feet of it, dealing the same damage (no attack roll — the leap simply strikes). The arc gains one additional leap for every two slot levels above 3rd, and no creature is struck by more than one leap in a turn.
