# Distill Ambrosia

A warm, viscous elixir wells from your palm and pours over wounds, sealing and knitting torn flesh.

---

> **Distill Ambrosia**  
> _1st-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Minute  
> **Range**: Self  
> **Components**: V, S, M (two empty flasks, which the spell fills)  
> **Duration**: 7 days
>
> You distil ambrosia into **two empty flasks** you are touching. Drinking one restores **8 + [% 1d8 %]** hit points — the same as a [Lesser Potion](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/potions-of-healing), which is what it is, whatever the name suggests.
>
> Ambrosia is a potion in every way that matters. It is drunk as a **Minor Action**, administered to another creature as a **Major Action**, and **it counts against the potion limit** like anything else that heals out of a flask. The body does not care that it was conjured.
>
> The flasks keep for **7 days** and then go inert — one Recovery's worth of potency, and no more.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** Casting this spell with a higher slot raises the **rank** of what you distil rather than adding dice:
>
> | Slot Level | Ambrosia Rank   | Healing        |
> | ---------- | --------------- | -------------- |
> | 1st        | Lesser          | 8 + [% 1d8 %]  |
> | 3rd        | Standard        | 16 + [% 2d8 %] |
> | 5th        | Greater         | 32 + [% 4d8 %] |
> | 7th        | Superior        | 64 + [% 8d8 %] |
> | 9th        | Ancient Draught | 128 + [% 16d8 %] |
