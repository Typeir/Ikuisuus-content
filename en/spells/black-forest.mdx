# Black Forest

A sphere of cold darkness forms, swallowing light and sound as writhing tendrils move within.

---

> **Black Forest**  
> _3rd-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 150 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute  
>  
> You open a gateway to the dark between the stars, a region infested with unknown horrors. A 20-foot-radius sphere of blackness and bitter cold appears, centered on a point with range and lasting for the duration. This void is filled with a cacophony of soft whispers and slurping noises that can be heard up to 30 feet away. No light, magical or otherwise, can illuminate the area, and creatures fully within the area are blinded.  
>  
> The dark is a thicket, and the thicket is grasping: the area is difficult terrain. Any creature that starts its turn in the area takes [% 2d6 frost %]. Any creature that ends its turn in the area must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or take [% 2d6 psychic %] as something with starlight-reflecting eyes regards it through the undergrowth.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 5th level or higher, the undergrowth seizes: a creature that fails its end-of-turn saving throw is also restrained until it escapes (a Strength or Dexterity check against your spell save DC, made as a Major Action). The damage of each kind increases by [% 1d6 %] for every two slot levels above 3rd.
