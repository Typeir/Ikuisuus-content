# Invoke Death

Thought of Oblivion says to one throat that what exists can be unmade. Invoke Death says it to a field, and the field agrees — all at once, in the space between two heartbeats, for everything on it that still had them.

---

> **Invoke Death**  
> _12th-level Enchantment_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 120 feet  
> **Components**: V (_Let there be an end — and there was_)  
> **Duration**: Instantaneous  
>  
> You utter the unmaking word not at a creature but at a place. Each creature of your choice that you can see within range with **100 hit points or fewer** dies instantly. A creature with more hit points than that is unaffected, though every creature within range knows the word was spoken and knows, for a moment, exactly how close it came.  
>  
> This is the field-scale twin of [_Thought of Oblivion_](/en/library/spells/thought-of-oblivion); like it, the word offers no saving throw and no resistance — only the threshold decides.
