# Earthmove  

This spell is not innate to any vocation, but rather, attained by those who peer beyond the everdark, and into the true shape of this world.  
To cast it is to invite the memory of an **Earthmover** — one of the things that consumed all beyond the Everdark.  

> “Taking a close look at what is around us, there is some sort of a harmony.
>  It is the harmony of overwhelming and collective murder.
>  And we in comparison to the articulate vileness and baseness and obscenity of all this, we in comparison to that enormous articulation, 
>  we only sound and look like badly pronounced and half-finished sentences out of a vain tale, a cheap, diminished story.
>  And we have to become humble in front of this overwhelming misery and overwhelming proliferation, overwhelming growth, and overwhelming lack of order.
>  Even the stars up here in the sky look crooked beyond repair.
>  There is no harmony left in the universe.
>  It’s like a curse weighing on an entire planet. 
>  And whoever goes too deep into these lands has his share of that curse. 
>  It’s a land that our Gods, if they exist, have created in anger. It’s as if creation was unfinished yet…
>  We have to get acquainted to this idea that there is no harmony as we have conceived it.
>  But I say this all full of admiration for this world.
>  It is not that I hate it, I love it, I love it very much, but I love it against my better judgment.” 
>
> — Tarcus, the Lion.

---

> **Earthmove**  
> _12th-Level Legendary Transmutation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 action  
> **Range**: 5 feet.
> **Components**: V, S, M (a chunk of derelict opal from beyond the Belt worth at least 5,000 gp, and a bottled flame of the Everdark, both consumed)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 hour  
>
> You summon the **Shade of an Earthmover**, a 75-foot-tall, 5-foot-thick, and up to 50-foot-long section of eternal silence.  
> The wall appears within range on a surface you can see and advances **5 feet forward** at the end of each of your turns in the direction you chose when casting the spell.  
>
> When the wall moves into a creature’s space, or a creature into the wall, that creature takes **150 + 20d10 true damage** and is **pushed 5 feet forward** by the wall’s movement.
> This effect allows **no saving throw** to avoid or reduce the damage.  
> When magical or nonmagical teleportation is attempted through it, the creature or creatures teleporting have to succeed on a Charisma save, or be stopped inside the wall and take the damage.  
> A creature reduced to 0 hit points by this spell is returned to oblivion, all memory of it dragged along with it. The body, along with everything it was carrying dissapear. 
> Casting this spell permanently reduces your maximum hit points by 10. If they are reduced beyond 1, you are instantly obliterated, suffering the same fate as those killed by the spell.
>
> The wall erases terrain, structures, and matter in its path, leaving behind burnt-black matter that's freezingly cold to the touch.  
> It emits no sound or light and cannot be dispelled, countered, or damaged.  
> When the spell ends, the effect vanishes instantly, in a thunderous clap that **can be heard from 100000000 ft. away**.  
> For several moments afterward, the air and soil where the Earthmover crossed collapse inward as if the world is closing a wound.
