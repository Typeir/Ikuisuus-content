# Threaded Blade

A spell that suspends execution above the battlefield, its fall delayed only by chance, pain, and failing resolve.

> “When the blade finally fell, some felt relief, for the torture was finally over.”

---

> **Threaded Blade**  
> _7th-level Transmutation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 action  
> **Range**: 30 feet  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute
>
> You conjure a colossal, razor-thin blade of force suspended directly above a creature you can see within range. The blade hangs motionless, bound by invisible threads of will.
>
> When you cast this spell, choose one creature. The blade remains suspended above that creature for the duration.
>
> **Thread of Failure.**  
> Each time the target **takes damage**, it must make a **Charisma saving throw**.  
> The first saving throw is made against a DC equal to **your spell save DC − 10**.
>
> Each time the target is forced to make this saving throw, the DC **increases by 1**, cumulative for the duration of the spell.
>
> If the target **fails** one of these saving throws, the blade immediately falls.
>
> **Falling Blade.**  
> When the blade falls, it strikes the target, dealing **80 force damage + 20d6 force damage**.
>
> After resolving this damage, you may immediately **move the blade** to a different creature you can see within **60 feet**, suspending it above the new target.
>
> - The spell does **not** end when the blade falls.
> - The **saving throw DC resets** to **your spell save DC − 10** for the new target.
>
> The blade can fall and be reassigned multiple times during the spell’s duration.
>
> If the spell ends early due to loss of concentration, any suspended blade dissipates harmlessly.
>
> The blade occupies no space, cannot be targeted, and ignores cover.
>
> A creature can be affected by only one **Threaded Blade** at a time.
