# Animate Dead

The corpse shudders, joints creaking as sinew tightens and it drags itself upright in jerking, unnatural motion.

---

> **Animate Dead**  
> _3rd-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Minute  
> **Range**: 10 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a drop of blood, a piece of flesh, and a pinch of bone dust)  
> **Duration**: Instantaneous  
>  
> Cast only at night. You animate a **construct (undead)** from a corpse or pile of bones of a Medium or Small humanoid within range. An undead is not a soul, nor a spirit, nor a secret third thing between life and death — it is an animated corpse, a thing you have wound up and set moving. It has no more of the person it was than a puppet has of the hand.  
>  
> A pile of bones becomes a skeleton; a corpse becomes a zombie (the bestiary holds their statistics). On each of your turns you can use a Minor Action to command any construct you have animated this way within 60 feet, issuing the same order to any or all of them. If you issue no command, a construct only defends itself. Once ordered, it follows the order until the task is complete.  
>  
> The construct obeys you for 24 hours, then stops. To hold it another 24 hours, cast this spell again before the period ends; that reassertion covers up to four of your constructs rather than animating a new one.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** Higher slots let you animate stronger corpses and hold more of them:  
>  
> - **4th level or higher:** animate or reassert control over two additional constructs for each slot level above 3rd, each from a different corpse or pile of bones.  
> - **6th level or higher:** you may instead raise **ghouls** (and, at 7th, ghasts or wights; at 8th, mummies), by supplying grave-dirt, brackish water, and a black onyx worth at least 150 gp per corpse.
