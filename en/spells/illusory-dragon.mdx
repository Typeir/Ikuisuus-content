> **Illusory Dragon**
> _8th-level Illusion_
> **Casting Time**: 1 Action  
> **Range**: 120 feet  
> **Components**: S  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute
>
> By waving your hand, you create the image of a huge, threatening dragon in a space you can see within range. The illusion lasts for the spell’s duration and occupies a 10-foot cube. The illusion is tangible to all senses, but it can’t deal damage or otherwise directly affect the world.
>
> The dragon appears in an unoccupied space of your choice that you can see within range. It can be any color you choose. The illusion lasts for the spell’s duration and occupies a 10-foot cube. The illusion is tangible to all senses, but it can’t deal damage or otherwise directly affect the world.
>
> As a bonus action on your turn, you can move the illusion up to 60 feet to a space you can see, but it must remain within range. At any point during its movement, you can cause it to exhale a blast of energy in a 60-foot cone originating from its mouth.
>
> Each creature in that area must make an Intelligence saving throw, taking 7d6 psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. The illusion then vanishes if it deals any damage.
>
> The illusion is so convincing that an Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC is required to discern that it is an illusion.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 9th level, the damage increases to 8d6.
