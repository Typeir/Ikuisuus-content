# Wall of Ice

A wall of ice springs into existence at a point you choose within range. You can make the wall up to 10 feet long, 10 feet high, and 1 foot thick per level of the spell. The wall lasts for the duration. If the wall cuts through a creature’s space when it appears, the creature is pushed to one side of the wall (your choice which side).

---

> **Wall of Ice**
> _6th-level Evocation_
> **Casting Time**: 1 Action
> **Range**: 120 feet
> **Components**: V, S, M
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 10 minutes
>
> The wall is an object that can be damaged and thus breached. It has AC 12 and 30 hit points per 10-foot section. Reducing a section to 0 hit points destroys it and leaves behind a sheet of frigid air in the space the wall occupied. A creature moving through the sheet of frigid air for the first time on a turn must make a Constitution saving throw. That creature takes 5d6 cold damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. The frigid air lasts for 1 minute, though a strong wind can disperse it.
>
> You can shape the wall into a hemispherical dome or a sphere with a radius of up to 10 feet per level of the spell. The wall is 1 foot thick. You can also shape the wall into a flat surface made up of ten 10-foot-square panels. Each panel must be contiguous with another panel. In any form, the wall is 1 foot thick.
>
> If a creature tries to break through the wall, it must make a Strength saving throw. On a successful save, the creature can move through the wall as if it were difficult terrain, taking 5d6 cold damage. On a failed save, the creature can’t move through and takes 10d6 cold damage.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 7th level or higher, increase the length of the wall by 10 feet, or the radius by 10 feet, for each slot level above 6th.
