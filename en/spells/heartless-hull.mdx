# Heartless Hull

The beat of your heart slows to a crawl, and so does that of those surrounding you, life fleeing from all in terror.

---

> **Heartless Hull**  
> _5th-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: Self (10-foot radius)  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 hour  
>  
> A hull of grave-cold spreads 10 feet around you and moves with you, remaining centered on you for the duration. Undead and constructs have no heart to still and are unaffected.  
>  
> **The heart falters.** At the start of each of your turns, every other creature inside the hull takes [% 2d6 dark %] as its pulse drags against the cold.  
>  
> **Conversion.** While a creature is inside the hull, any **dark** damage it takes — from this spell or any other source — also inflicts an equal amount of [Grievous Wounds](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/grievous-wounds) upon it. Grievous Wounds reduce a creature's maximum and current hit points and can be undone only by spending Hit Dice; no magic mends them.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 6th level or higher, the dark damage at the start of your turns increases by [% 1d6 %] for each slot level above 5th.
