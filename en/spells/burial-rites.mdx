# Burial Rites

Souls are a contrived topic, but identity is not. Some have learned to thieve the identity of the departed by capturing their shadow at the moment of death.
This spell allows a caster to assume the appearance and some knowledge of a recently deceased humanoid.

---

> **Burial Rites**  
> _2nd-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 reaction, which you take when a humanoid dies within 30 feet of you  
> **Range**: 30 feet  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: 1 hour  
>
> You bind the departing shadow of a dying humanoid to yourself.
>
> Until the spell ends, you can use an **Action** to consume the bound shadow. When you do, the shadow vanishes and you magically transform into a **disguise** that makes you look like the dead humanoid as it appeared in life, but healthy and uninjured. This disguise lasts for the spell’s duration or until you end it early as a **Bonus Action**.
>
> While disguised, you gain access to all information the creature would **freely share with a casual acquaintance**. This includes general details about its background and personal life but **does not include secrets**.
>
> Another creature can see through this disguise by succeeding on a **Wisdom (Insight) check** with disadvantage against your passive **Charisma (Deception)**. You gain a **+5 bonus** to your Deception checks while you remain disguised by this spell.
