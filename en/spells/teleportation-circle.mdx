# Teleportation Circle

Chalked lines complete a circuit that has been sitting there, finished and waiting, since long before anyone alive was born.

---

> **Teleportation Circle**  
> _5th-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Minute  
> **Range**: Touch  
> **Components**: V, M (rare chalks and inks infused with precious gems worth 50 gp, which the spell consumes)  
> **Duration**: 1 round
>
> **This spell does not make a road. It starts an engine somebody else built.**
>
> You must be standing on one of the **ancient circles** — the fixed plates set into the floors of the monolithic travel machines. A known and finite number of them still work. Tracing your chalk across the plate closes a circuit that has been waiting to be closed, and for one round a seam opens onto another such circle, provided you know its **sigil sequence** and it lies within the same Fold of the world.
>
> Any creature that enters the seam appears within 5 feet of the destination plate, or in the nearest unoccupied space if that one is taken. The seam shuts at the end of your next turn whether or not anyone used it.
>
> **Sigil sequences** are not invented. They are read off the machines themselves. You begin knowing two, and you can commit another to memory by studying a working circle for 1 minute — assuming you can reach it, and assuming it still runs.
>
> **You cannot create a circle.** Neither can anyone else. The plates are precursor work and nobody has made a new one in living memory; where a machine has failed, that destination is simply gone, and the sigil sequence for it is a dead word people still write down.
