# Whirlwind

A towering vortex rips the air into a maelstrom, shredding anything in its path and hurling debris outward.

---

> **Whirlwind**  
> _7th-level Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 300 feet  
> **Components**: V, M  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute  
>  
>  
> A whirlwind howls down to a point on the ground you specify within range. The whirlwind is a 10-foot-radius, 30-foot-high cylinder centered on that point. Until the spell ends, you can use your Major Action to move the whirlwind up to 30 feet in any direction along the ground.  
>  
> Each creature in the whirlwind’s space must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes [% 10d6 bludgeoning %] and is flung up 20 feet away from the center of the whirlwind in a random direction and knocked prone. On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn’t flung away or knocked prone. If the thrown creature strikes an object, such as a wall or floor, the creature takes [% 3d6 bludgeoning %] from the impact. If the creature is thrown at another creature, that creature must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or take the same damage and be knocked prone.  
>  
> The whirlwind sucks up any Medium or smaller object that is not secured and is not worn or carried. Such an object takes [% 10d6 bludgeoning %] if it strikes something. Objects too large or too well anchored are simply not lifted.
