# Lost in the Muck

There is a rot-brine microchamber folded under the world, a bad copy of the drowned layer beneath Plato Station. People who come back out of it can draw you a map, and no two maps agree.

---

> **Lost in the Muck**  
> _8th-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 60 feet  
> **Components**: V, S  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 10 minutes
>
> You fold a creature you can see within range into the **Muck Dimension**. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be banished there, waist-deep in warm brine, for the duration or until it finds its own way out.
>
> At the end of each of its turns, a banished creature can make an Intelligence check using **Cartographer's Tools** or **Navigator's Tools** against your spell save DC. On a success it reads the chamber correctly, walks out, and the spell ends.
>
> When the spell ends, the target reappears in the space it left, or in the nearest unoccupied space if that one is taken.
