# Prismatic Wall

A towering curtain of refracted light materializes, each color fracturing and consuming matter that touches its surface.

---

> **Prismatic Wall**
> _9th-level Abjuration_
> **Casting Time**: 1 Action
> **Range**: 60 feet
> **Components**: V, S
> **Duration**: 10 minutes
>
>
> A shimmering, multicolored plane of light forms a vertical opaque wall—up to 90 feet long, 30 feet high, and 1 inch thick—centered on a point you can see within range. The wall remains for the duration. If you position the wall so that it passes through a creature’s space, the spell fails, and your action and spell slot are wasted. The wall blocks line of sight. When an area would be affected by more than one wall of force or similar effect, only the strongest one applies.
>
> The wall is immobile and of a color chosen by the caster. The wall consists of seven layers, each with a different color. Each layer has a different power and purpose. Each layer can be destroyed in order from red to violet by a specific means, but only one layer can be destroyed at a time. If the wall is struck by a spell of a particular school or effect, the corresponding layer is destroyed, but the others remain. The wall can be passed through, but each layer has a different effect on creatures passing through it. The effects are as follows:
>
> **Red.** The first layer is red. It deals 10d6 fire damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 cold damage to it.
>
> **Orange.** The second layer is orange. It deals 10d6 acid damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 lightning damage to it.
>
> **Yellow.** The third layer is yellow. It deals 10d6 lightning damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 force damage to it.
>
> **Green.** The fourth layer is green. It deals 10d6 poison damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 acid damage to it.
>
> **Blue.** The fifth layer is blue. It deals 10d6 cold damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 fire damage to it.
>
> **Indigo.** The sixth layer is indigo. It deals 10d6 psychic damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 radiant damage to it.
>
> **Violet.** The seventh layer is violet. It deals 10d6 radiant damage to any creature passing through it. A creature that succeeds on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw takes half damage. The layer can be destroyed by dealing at least 25 necrotic damage to it.
>
> **Passing Through the Wall.** A creature can attempt to pass through the wall, but must make a saving throw for each layer. On a failed save, the creature suffers the effect of that layer and can’t pass through. On a successful save, the creature can continue to the next layer. If a creature fails a saving throw against any layer, it is stopped and can’t pass through the wall.
>
> **Destroying the Wall.** Each layer can be destroyed by a specific type of damage, as described above. Once a layer is destroyed, it no longer has any effect. If all seven layers are destroyed, the wall disappears.
