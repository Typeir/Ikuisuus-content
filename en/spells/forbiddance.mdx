# Forbiddance

A lattice of pale wards blooms outward, seams in stone and air sealing with a hard, resonant snap.

---

> **Forbiddance**  
> _6th-level Abjuration (Ritual)_  
> **Casting Time**: 10 Minutes  
> **Range**: Touch  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a line of salt and powdered bone worth 1,000 gp, consumed on the thirtieth casting)  
> **Duration**: 1 day  
>  
>  
> You create a ward against magical travel that protects up to 40,000 square feet of floor space to a height of 30 feet above the floor. For the duration, no creature can enter the area by folding space, by any aperture opened through the Folds, or by any passage between Clone Worlds. Whatever route a thing might take that is not simply walking, the ward closes it.  
>  
> When you cast forbiddance, you may designate individuals who can pass through the warded area freely. You can also specify a password that, when spoken aloud, allows a creature to enter the area unimpeded. Otherwise, a creature that attempts to enter the protected area by mundane means takes [% 5d10 holy %] or dark damage (your choice when you cast this spell). When you cast this spell, you can designate whether the ward damages those who enter, those who exit, or both. A creature can take this damage only once per day, regardless of how many times it enters or exits the area.  
>  
> The spell’s area can’t overlap with another forbiddance spell. If you cast forbiddance every day for 30 days in the same location, the spell lasts until it is dispelled, and the material component is consumed on the last casting.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 7th level or higher, the duration increases to 10 days for each slot level above 6th.
