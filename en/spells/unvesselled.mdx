# Unvesselled

You step out of the vessel and leave it standing. What walks away is everything about you except the part that needed feeding.

---

> **Unvesselled**  
> _9th-level Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Hour  
> **Range**: 10 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (for each creature affected, one jacinth worth at least 1,000 gp and one silver bar worth at least 100 gp, all of which the spell consumes)  
> **Duration**: Special
>
> You and up to eight willing creatures within range come out of your bodies. Each body left behind stands empty and insensible, needing neither food nor air, and does not age while it is untenanted.
>
> What steps out has your shape and your statistics and your possessions, because those were never really the body's. The one difference is the **cord**: a silver thread leaving you between the shoulder blades and running back to the vessel you left, however far that has become.
>
> **The cord is conceptual, but real, a thing in place, made of naught.** It is not a formality and not a metaphor. Anything with **Fold-Sense**, truesight, or any other means of seeing into the Folds can see it plainly, follow it in either direction, and swing at it.
>
> | The Cord                 |                                                                          |
> | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
> | **AC**                   | 15                                                                       |
> | **Hit Points**           | 8 × your caster level                                                    |
> | **Can take damage from** | **Akashic** damage (holy, dark, force) and **True** damage. Nothing else |
>
> The cord is not made of anything. Blades pass through it, fire does not catch on it, and poison finds nothing to work on.
>
> **You feel every hit.** The moment the cord takes damage you know it, and you know roughly where along its length the blow landed. You may then use your **Reaction** to end the spell and be drawn back into your vessel at once, from any distance.
>
> If the cord is reduced to 0 hit points, vessel and identity are parted for good and you die where you stand, irrecoverable.
>
> Unvesselled, you move through the **Folds of the world** at the pace of thought rather than of legs, unbothered by walls and unable to affect them. Where the Folds run close enough to allow it you can cross into another **Clone World**, and if you do, your vessel and possessions are drawn along the cord to meet you there.
>
> What you are while unvesselled is a separate incarnation. Damage and effects that land on it do not touch the body you left, and do not persist when you return to it.
>
> The spell ends when you use a Major Action to dismiss it, and everyone affected wakes in their own body. It also ends early for any individual whose vessel is damaged or moved, or against whom an [_Objection_](/en/library/spells/objection) is successfully cast.
