# Anaximander's Gift

When Anaximander opened the first stable rift to the Hidden Kingdom, he was rewarded with a boon from the lord of flies. A spell of ruin and rot; may his name remain forever in infamy.

> **Anaximander's Gift**  
> _9th-level Necromancy_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 90 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a fly, dead and dry, carried on your person for at least a year)  
> **Duration**: Instantaneous
>
> You target a creature you can see within range and run its body forward through years it has not lived. The target must make a Constitution saving throw, taking [% 10d12 dark %] on a failure or half as much on a success. If the damage reduces it to 0 hit points, it dies and collapses into dust. On a successful save it takes no years.
>
> **The Years.** A creature that fails the save and survives is aged **[% 5d20 %] years** where it stands. What that costs depends entirely on what it is; measure it against that lineage's lifespan. For most mortals it is a maiming, and for the old it is simply the end.
>
> **Things That Do Not Age.** A creature outside of time — a Silent One, an Empyrean, anything genuinely immortal — takes the damage and nothing more. The years arrive and find nothing in it to spend.
>
> A creature running on borrowed material is the opposite case. A homunculus has perhaps sixty good years in its wax; a construct has however long its body holds. The years are taken out of that material directly, and such creatures age exactly as a mortal would, which makes this spell one of the very few things that can murder a homunculus by simply waiting, faster.
>
> **At Higher Levels.** When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 10th level or higher, the damage increases by [% 1d12 %] and the years taken by [% 1d20 %] for each slot level above 9th.
