# Conjure Null

You reach past the burning barrier and take hold of something that never managed to exist. It comes back with you because coming back is not a thing it knows how to refuse.

---

> **Conjure Null**  
> _6th-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 90 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a thing destroyed and never replaced, held in mind as you speak)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 hour  
>  
> You draw a **Null** — an absence, a fault where meaning failed — into an unoccupied space you can see within range. Their combined Challenge Rating may not exceed **twice this spell's level**. See [Summoning and Conjuration](/en/library/rules/arcana-and-the-fold/summoning-and-conjuration) for the Challenge Budget, Swarms and Formations, and command.  
>  
> **It takes no orders.** A Null has nothing to bargain with and nothing to want. It cannot be commanded, only aimed: you choose the space it arrives in, and thereafter it moves toward whatever is nearest and unmakes it. Choose that space carefully, and stand well away from it.  
>  
> A Null is immune to any effect that would charm, frighten, or compel it. There is no mind there to reach.  
>  
> **If the hold slips.** Should your concentration break, the Null remains for the spell's full remaining duration and cannot be dismissed, banished, or sent back by any working of yours. The Everdark was built to keep these out, and you have just carried one through it.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** The Challenge Budget is always twice the level of the slot spent.
