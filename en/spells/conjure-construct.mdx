# Conjure Construct

A made thing assembles out of borrowed geometry, stands where you point, and waits with the terrible patience of something that was never asked whether it wanted to.

---

> **Conjure Construct**  
> _4th-level Conjuration_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 Major Action  
> **Range**: 90 feet  
> **Components**: V, S, M (a working part of something else — a cog, a hinge, a length of good wire)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 hour  
>  
> You assemble a **construct** in an unoccupied space you can see within range, drawn together out of the idea of a mechanism rather than any particular one. Their combined Challenge Rating may not exceed **twice this spell's level**. See [Summoning and Conjuration](/en/library/rules/arcana-and-the-fold/summoning-and-conjuration) for the Challenge Budget, Swarms and Formations, command, and loss of control.  
>  
> A conjured construct follows orders exactly, including the parts of an order you did not intend to give. It has no judgement to fall back on and will not improvise a kindness.  
>  
> **At Higher Levels.** The Challenge Budget is always twice the level of the slot spent.
