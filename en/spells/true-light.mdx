# True Light  

This spell is not innate to any vocation, but granted to those who have collected a tear from the **Twin Eyes of Truth**.  
The first burns cold, the other burns bright, locked in eternal conflict.  

> “The sun we know is a lie. The moon we long for a fraud. 
> The mad god of creation shunned their children and now we play the part of both their jailor and their plaything”  
>
> — Aeridas, heir to nothing.  

---

> **True Light**  
> _11th-Level Legendary Evocation_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 action  
> **Range**: 120 feet  
> **Components**: V, M (a scale of sunstone and a lunar pearl worth at least 2,500 gp each, both consumed)  
> **Duration**: Concentration, up to 1 minute  
>
> You conjure **two celestial orbs** — one of radiant sunlight, the other of freezing moonlight — each 10 feet in diameter.  
> Both appear within range and hover at points you can see.  
> You can move one orb up to **30 feet** as a bonus action on your turn.  
>
> Any creature that starts its turn within **20 feet** of an orb, or enters the orb's space for the first time this round, takes **10 + 40 (8d10)** (radiant or fire) damage and must make a **Constitution saving throw**.  
>
> - **Sun Orb**: On a failed save, a creature takes **radiant damage** and is **blinded** until the start of its next turn.  
> - **Moon Orb**: On a failed save, a creature takes **cold damage** and is **slowed**, reducing its movement speed by half and preventing reactions until the start of its next turn.
>  
> On a success, they take half damage instead, and suffer no other adverse effects.
> If the two orbs come within **10 feet** of each other, they detonate in a pulse of creation.  
> All creatures within **60 feet** of the point between them must make a Dexterity Saving throw or take **50 + 100 (20d10)** radiant and cold damage, split evenly.
> Creatures take half as much damage on a success.  
> The orbs vanish after this detonation or when the spell ends.  
> The orbs can be pushed by effects from other creatures.
>
> You are blinded for the duration of the spell.
