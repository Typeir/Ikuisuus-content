# Eternal Return

This spell is not innate to any vocation, but rather, granted as a boon or a blessing by a **Heirloom** or a **higher power**.  
This spell can be attained by those who have wandered the sands of time.

> “The falling leaves and swirling sands tell a story, one must merely learn to witness.”

---

> **Eternal Return**  
> _10th-Level Legendary Divination_  
> **Casting Time**: 1 hour  
> **Range**: Self (300-foot radius sphere)  
> **Components**: V, S, M (an hourglass filled with the sands of time, and soil or debris from the location, which the spell consumes)  
> **Duration**: Special  
> **Ritual**
>
> You anchor yourself to a location and peer into its past, experiencing the history of everything that occurred within a **300-foot radius sphere** centered on you. The spell allows you to witness events as though you were present, observing them unfold in real time.
>
> ## Time Investment and Historical Depth
>
> The amount of history you can **access** depends on how long you maintain the ritual. The relationship follows a **quadratic progression**: the longer you spend in the ritual, the exponentially deeper into the past you can reach.
>
> **Formula**: For every **n seconds** spent in the ritual, you can reach **n² minutes** into the past.
>
> | **Time Invested**          | **History Witnessed** | **Approximate Time Span**           |
> | -------------------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
> | 10 seconds                 | 100 minutes           | ~1.7 hours                          |
> | 30 seconds                 | 900 minutes           | 15 hours                            |
> | 1 minute (60 seconds)      | 3,600 minutes         | 60 hours (~2.5 days)                |
> | 2 minutes (120 seconds)    | 14,400 minutes        | 240 hours (10 days)                 |
> | 5 minutes (300 seconds)    | 90,000 minutes        | 1,500 hours (~62.5 days, ~2 months) |
> | 10 minutes (600 seconds)   | 360,000 minutes       | 6,000 hours (~250 days, ~8 months)  |
> | 30 minutes (1,800 seconds) | 3,240,000 minutes     | 54,000 hours (~6.2 years)           |
> | 1 hour (3,600 seconds)     | 12,960,000 minutes    | 216,000 hours (~24.7 years)         |
> | 2 hours (7,200 seconds)    | 51,840,000 minutes    | 864,000 hours (~98.6 years)         |
> | 5 hours (18,000 seconds)   | 324,000,000 minutes   | 5,400,000 hours (~616 years)        |
> | 10 hours (36,000 seconds)  | 1,296,000,000 minutes | 21,600,000 hours (~2,465 years)     |
>
> ### Extended Example
>
> A caster who spends **30 seconds** performing this ritual can reach back **900 minutes** (15 hours) into the past within the 300-foot sphere. They must then spend time observing events—they experience history in real time, so watching 15 hours of events takes 15 hours.
>
> A dedicated scholar who spends **5 minutes** in ritual can reach back approximately **2 months** into the past. To witness all of that history would require 2 months of observation, but the caster can strategically jump to specific moments or periods of interest.
>
> A caster seeking to investigate the **last 230 years** of a fortress's history would need to perform the ritual for approximately **2.5 hours** to reach back that far.
> However, actually watching 230 years of events would be impossible—instead, the caster must be clever, using their knowledge to jump to key moments (construction dates, known battles, important ceremonies) like performing a binary search through time.
>
> ## Rules and Restrictions
>
> - **Observation Only**: You experience history as an invisible, intangible observer. You cannot interact with the past or change events in any way.
> - **Real-Time Observation**: You experience history at normal speed—1 minute of observed events takes 1 minute of your time. You cannot fast-forward or accelerate playback.
> - **Temporal Navigation**: You can choose to jump forward or backward to different moments within the accessible time range, but once you begin observing, time flows normally.
> - **Perception Matters**: While you witness events as they unfold, you still rely on your own senses to notice details, understand context, and interpret what you see. Subtle clues may be missed without careful observation.
> - **Strategic Viewing**: Intelligent casters use their knowledge to jump to specific dates, events, or moments of interest rather than watching centuries of mundane daily life. This makes historical knowledge and deductive reasoning essential for effective use of the spell.
> - **Physical Vulnerability**: While performing this ritual, you are vulnerable to all damage, and you cannot take actions other than dodge, dash, hide and disengage.
> - **Location-Bound**: The spell reveals only what occurred within the 300-foot sphere. Events outside this radius are invisible to you.
> - **Interruption**: If the ritual is interrupted (by damage, being moved, or similar), the spell ends immediately and you retain only the history witnessed up to that point.
