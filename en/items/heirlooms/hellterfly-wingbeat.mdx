# Hellterfly Wingbeat

_Rare (requires attunement)_  
_Wondrous item (Spellcasting Focus)_

A pair of gloves that shimmer with a fiery glow of their own — a fire that leaves no trace. Their delicate lacework is woven with threads singed by the Vaki of fire: animistic, nameless spirits born from splinters of the idea of flame itself.

The fire Vaki are neither kind nor cruel. They are fire, and fire does not choose sides. They gravitate toward those who wield flame regardless of purpose — drawn not by intent but by resonance. To earn a fire Vaki's favour is to perform for it: to sing, to dance, to burn with enough passion that the spirit deems your fire worthy of its attention.

These gloves are a stage for fire Vaki. When the wearer casts flame with enough conviction, the spirits gather — flickering, joyful presences drawn to the warmth.

---

## Special Features

- **Born of the Flames**:  
  Fire Vaki gather around both gloves whenever the bearer casts a spell that deals fire damage or receives fire damage. The number of Vaki drawn equals the level of the spell cast or the number of fire damage dice received. They linger for one minute, flickering around the bearer during that time.

- **Vaki Resonance**:  
  The bearer receives a bonus to their magical attack rolls and spell save DC when casting spells that deal fire damage, based on the number of Vaki present before casting:

  | Vaki | Bonus |
  | ---- | ----- |
  | 0–2  | +0    |
  | 3–5  | +1    |
  | 6–8  | +2    |
  | 9+   | +3    |

- **Consuming Fire**:  
  If at the start of your turn you have more than 9 Vaki gathered, you take 1d4 fire damage for each Vaki above 9. The spirits do not mean harm — they simply burn, and too many burn too brightly.

---

## Effects

- **Summer's Embrace**:  
  If the bearer receives cold or fire damage while one or more fire Vaki linger around them, they can use their **reaction** to release one or more Vaki, reducing the damage taken by **1d4 per Vaki released**.

- **Scorching Heat**:  
  When the bearer deals fire damage to a creature, they can release one Vaki to inflict the **Scorched** condition on that creature until the end of the bearer's next turn.

- **Final Blaze**:  
  The bearer can use their **bonus action** to release all Vaki they possess, increasing the damage of the next fire spell damage roll they make this turn by an amount equal to the number of Vaki released.

---

## Scorched Condition

While a creature is **Scorched**, it has **disadvantage on concentration saving throws**. Additionally, if the Scorched condition is applied again to an already Scorched creature, that creature takes **1d6 fire damage**.

A creature ceases to be Scorched if it is doused in water, takes cold damage, or is otherwise meaningfully cooled.

---

## Spellcasting Order

When casting a spell that deals fire damage:

1. Apply the bonus from **Vaki Resonance**
2. Cast the spell with that bonus applied
3. On dealing damage, decide whether to use **Scorching Heat**
4. Gain Vaki drawn by the spell itself

Vaki drawn by a spell cannot be used for that same spell.
