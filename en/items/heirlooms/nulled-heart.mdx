# Nulled Heart

_Mythic Artifact_  
_Consumable_

---

This is the end of all things, neither predestined nor fated, but utterly inevitable.

> “... And that heart will, in time, hollow thine own.”

## Effect

As an action, you forego your life, to let the null consume you.

Upon doing so, you immediately enter the **Nulled Heart** state. This effect cannot be interrupted, suppressed, countered, or reversed by any means.

This transformation is permanent.

This item is destroyed upon use.

While in this state, you gain the following effects:

- You regain all of your hit points.
- Whenever you would roll a d20, you instead treat the result as **20**.
- Any saving throw you impose has its DC increased to **35**.
- **All damage you deal is True damage.**
- You are immune to all damage except **True** and **Force** damage.
- You are immune to all conditions.
- You automatically fail saving throws against effects that would remove you from the battlefield (such as banishment, maze, or similar effects).
- Your spell slots are immediately refilled.
- Every spell you cast is considered to be cast at the highest slot possible.
- You regain all your spell slots every day at midnight.
- You gain **one additional action each round**. This action may only be used to make an attack, cast a spell, or activate an effect that deals damage.

This state lasts for **1d4 + your Charisma modifier rounds**.

At the end of this duration, the null finally devours your entire being.

Afterwards, from your body emerges a **Nascent Earthmover, servant to the New Null**.

- The Earthmover will seek those who knew of you, and try to erase them from existence. 
- When the Earthmover falls, it will reawaken the next midnight, exactly at the spot where you let the null consume you, ready to continue its mission.
- The earthmover shares your statistics, and is permanently affected by the **Nulled Heart** state.
- The Earthmover can travel through any kind of planar impedement at the rate of one planar jump per decade.
- The Earthmover is an Elemental.
