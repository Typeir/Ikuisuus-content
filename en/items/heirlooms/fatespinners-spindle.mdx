# Fatespinner’s Spindle

_Legendary (requires attunement)_  
_Staff (Focus, Versatile, Special, Mastery: Topple)_  
_Can be used as a spellcasting focus when attuned_

Once wielded by **Yskeia**, the Spear of Resolution, this indestructible staff was molded from **primordial wax** — the same divine substance from which the **White Homunculus** was formed.  
It was created to **subjugate the God of Fate**, and for a brief instant, it succeeded. When Yskeia fell, the staff was lost among the ruins of her final battle.

Uncountable aeons later, it was recovered by the starcallers, who discovered its strange affinity for the wax-born.  
The staff was used by one of them to **bind three existing homunculi** to it: companions of profound emotional importance, each with their own mind and purpose.  
The Spindle does not command them; it simply **anchors them**, preserving their forms and consciousness through the bond.

> “Never will it bind gods again. It shall only protect the memories of our friends.”

---

## Weapon Properties

- **Type**: Staff (Versatile, Special)
- **Damage**: 1d6(1d8) bludgeoning
- **Weight**: 4 lbs

---

## Attunement

Attuning to the Spindle requires an **Arcana or Insight check (DC 20)** to align the wielder’s will to the residual homuncular echoes within. On a failure, the staff remains inert until dawn.

Once attuned, the wielder senses the living presence of **Cierto**, **Plinio**, and **Adastria** at all times.  
When one dies, a faint tremor passes through the staff — cold, brief, and impossibly sad.

## Special Features

- **Primordial Wax**  
  The staff is **indestructible**. It cannot melt, crack, warp, or be destroyed by any means short of conceptual erasure. It cannot be affected by antimagic, and appears mundane to detection spells.

- **Spellcasting Focus**  
  May be used as a focus for casting spells and cantrips.

- **Bound Companions**  
  The staff is attuned to three homunculi dear to Dreidre. Each can be **summoned or dismissed** as a **bonus action**, and commanded as a **bonus action** on subsequent turns.  
  They remain until dismissed or destroyed. If slain, reconstructing one requires **50,000 gp in materials** and **one month of dedicated work**.
  - **Cierto, Poet of Oblivion**  
    A Wax Rat with a theatrical streak and an infernal glimmer. Upon summoning, Cierto immediately performs a **Black Quill Strike** (+13 to hit) dealing 15 + 2d8 bludgeoning damage, plus 14d8 psychic damage, and stunning the target without save,
    Cierto then vanishes back to his own adventure.  
    His voice lingers for this round, whispering poetry and folk tales that grant a single **d12 bardic inspiration** to allies within 30 feet.
    Cierto can only be summoned once every short rest, as he is busy hunting demons.

  - **Plinio**  
    Dutiful, gluttonous, lethargic and quiet. When summoned, Plinio manifests a **20-foot-radius dome of translucent wax**.

  - **Adastria**  
    Gentle, anxious and pragmatic. When summoned, Adastria releases a slow, radiant pulse of warmth: all allies within 30 feet regain **30 hit points** and end one condition (blinded, deafened, frightened, or poisoned).

- **Auxiliary Motion**  
  While attuned to the Spindle, the wielder gains **one additional bonus action per turn**.

- **Bound Will**  
  If the wielder dies, the staff liquefies and cocoons their body in wax.  
  The body becomes irrecoverable, along with everything it was carrying, as it turns into primordial wax.

## Weapon Mastery

- **Topple**  
  When you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force it to make a **Strength saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier)**.  
  On a failed save, the creature is **knocked prone**.

- **Mastery: Deconstruct (1/Short Rest, or 3/Short Rest if the wielder has mastery with staves) **  
  As an **action**, you may target a **construct** you can see within your weapon’s reach.  
  The construct must make a **Charisma saving throw (DC = 12 + your proficiency bonus + your spellcasting modifier)**.  
  On a failure, the construct is **stunned until the start of your next turn**.
