# Dreamcatcher

_Mythic Artifact, Fiend (requires attunement)_  
_Weapon +5 (Two-Handed)_

---

_In the dawn of time, before the world was shaped, there was only the Will of the World._
_This Will rode upon a golden chariot, drawn by two mighty steeds—one dark as night, the other pale as the stars._

_For an age uncounted, the steeds strained against their master. They sought to steer the chariot themselves, wrestling for dominion over its path. Seeing their wills were unbreakable, the Will released the reins and left them to their own design._

_Thus unbound, the steeds entered the Primordial Engine as one. There they endured the Trial of Selection and emerged as a single, twinned god: the God of Chaos._

_Yet the Third Star—the Era of Chaos—was not to last. The twins quarreled, and the God of Chaos tore itself apart. Its halves became two gods:_

_The pale steed, the One Primordial Terror, became the Dragon, God of Creation._

_The dark steed, ambition incarnate, became the Dreamcatcher, God of Destruction._

_Their battle raged for aeons—yet in the scale of eternity, it was but a breath. At last the Dragon triumphed._
_The Dreamcatcher was cast from the Derelict Paradise, Damocles, into the reachless Everdark._
_With the Dreamcatcher’s fall, and with the Golden One’s quorum restored, the Dragon claimed the Fourth Star: the golden age of the world, which seemed as though it would last forever._

_But even an eternity is but a moment to ambition. When the empyreans first crossed the Everdark, the fiend became lodged in one of their ships._
_And when the fourth star finally started unfurling at it's seams, the blade finally emerged in the brine pools beneath one of the Seven Empyrean Observatories._
_There it'd forge a new identity; hare of a thousand legs, never ending snake, scion of ambition and destruction._
_It came into the hands of a pitiful, nameless homunculus. It took up the blade so it would become the One True Fiend,_
_hoping that the end of all things be written by the Black Plume of Oblivion, and the Dragon’s heart stayed._

> “Little one, let our waltz be eternal, and our voices be last song unsung.” - The Dreamcatcher, to the white Homunculus.

## Attunement

To attune to this weapon, you must make **six [RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls) saving throws** (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma) in any order you choose.
The Dreamcatcher automatically removes any magical effects from the would-be-wielder when touched, even if it is not done with the intention of attuning, and suppresses any aura emanating from or affecting the wielder.

- **DC**: Equal to the Attunement Save DC for the weapon’s current level.
- **Failure**: On a failed save, reduce that ability score by the number specified on the table (to a minimum of 1).
- **Maximum Failures**: If you fail more saves than the maximum allowed for that weapon level, you die instantly.
- **Partial Attunement**: You may stop the attunement process early. You keep any ability score penalties suffered but are not attuned. You may try to attune again after a long rest.

### Evolution

This weapon increases in level through major narrative milestones, at the GM’s discretion.

When the weapon’s level increases, you must repeat the attunement process using the new DC. Previously failed saves must also be re-rolled.

### The shape of Ambition

When you successfully attune to the weapon, depending on the weapon's level, you must roll 4d8's, discarding the lowest.
You then replace one ability score of your choice with the result. Each weapon level increases the number of scores you can change by 1, up to all 6 at +5.
Your maximum for that score is removed.

### The Shape of Freedom

The Dreamcatcher does not bind to you — it **unstrings you**.  
Every thread that once moved your body and mind is cut away, leaving you to animate yourself by sheer ambition.  
For each saving throw, you must endure the loss of what was once automatic:

- **Strength**: You feel every muscle go slack — you must now clench each fiber of your body manually, flexing with thought alone.
- **Dexterity**: Your sense of timing collapses. You must command each joint one by one to move, forcing coordination where instinct once handled it.
- **Constitution**: Your heartbeat stops until you tell it to beat. Your lungs no longer breathe unless you will them to. Your gut churns only when you focus.
- **Intelligence**: Your memory unravels into loose threads. You must recall facts deliberately, consciously forcing each synapse to fire in their specific order, one detail at a time.
- **Wisdom**: Your reflexes die. Pain, hunger, thirst, balance — all are silent until you actively search for them. You are blind to the world unless you pay attention.
- **Charisma**: Your sense of self is gone. Smiles, laughs, even the sound of your voice must now be conjured consciously. Others see you as you truly are — or not at all.

You will never regain what was cut away. That part of you remains forever manual, forever heavy, dragging at you with every motion.
If you succeed, you eventually learn to navigate the world by ambition alone.  
If you fail the throws, you never adequate to this new reality.
Should you fail more saves than allowed, your body is crushed under the weight of your own ambition, it simply forgets how to live — and you die instantly.

#### Unstrung potential

Passing a saving throw means you have learned to seize control cleanly, without losing capacity.
Each passed score grants the following feature, depending on the score:
You may only choose as many features as **the weapon's level +1**.

- **Strength**: You may strain past normal carrying capacity, hauling impossible loads, up to twice as much as your maximum load.
  You do so by incurring severe penalties, equal to **one level of exhaustion per minute hauling the load** and **Vulnerability to all damage**.
- **Dexterity**: You may perform sleight-of-hand and precision stunts that would otherwise be impossible, even if they dislocate or contort your own body.
  You may spend two hit die to add a **+5 bonus to any Dexterity check or saving throw**. You can do so after the dice is rolled, but before the result is known.
- **Constitution**: You may ignore the effects of starvation, dehydration, or suffocation so long as you remain conscious.
  You will still die of starvation, dehydration, or suffocation, the system simply turns binary, with no symptoms. you're either conscious, or dead.
- **Intelligence**: You may willingly erase facts or memories from your mind with perfect precision.
  As a bonus action, you may cast [Modify memory](/en/library/spells/modify-memory) on yourself, with the sole intention of removing a memory.
  If cast by this feature, [Modify memory](/en/library/spells/modify-memory) is cast as a **9th level spell**.
- **Wisdom**: You may excise your own consciousness at will, letting you rest under any conditions that are not actively lethal.
  You may rest while wearing armor, standing, in extreme but non lethal weather...
- **Charisma**: You may willingly end any charm, fear, or mind-altering effect by **concentrating for a full round**, as if snapping its thread.
  As an action, you may end a charm, fear or mind-altering effect, stunning yourself until the start of your next turn in the process.

### The shape of Destruction

When you successfully attune to the weapon, you may choose the shape of the blade.

- You may choose the shape of any two-handed melee weapon.
- The weapon does not count as magical.
- The weapon gains a modifier to attack and damage rolls equal to the weapon's level.

## Features

### Just a tool

The Dreamcatcher does not count as magical for the purposes of overcoming resistances or immunities.
The Dreamcatcher can be affected by spells and effects that alter this behavior.

### Fate Arisen

When you hit a creature with the Dreamcatcher, it must succeed on a **Charisma saving throw** or become **Laid Bare** against the weapon’s strikes for an hour.

- The saving throw DC is equal to the **Attunement Save DC** for the weapon’s current level.
- A **Laid Bare** creature has all magical effects affecting it immediately end.
- A **Laid Bare** creature cannot reduce, negate, or redirect the Dreamcatcher’s damage in any way.
  - Resistance, immunity, temporary hit points, damage reduction effects, or features like _Uncanny Dodge_, evasion or _Stoneskin_ provide no benefit.
  - Magical effects that increase AC, such as _Shield_, _Absorb Elements_, or _Blade Ward_ cannot be used to prevent its damage. Persistent effects from **heirlooms**, **equipment** or **features** still work.
- Whenever the Dreamcatcher deals damage to a Laid Bare creature, their maximum hit points are reduced by the same amount.
  - This reduction does not recover naturally.
  - It can only be undone by Remove Curse or Greater Restoration, cast at a level equal to or higher than the Dreamcatcher’s weapon level +3, as shown in the table below.
  - For example, if struck by Dreamcatcher +3, Remove Curse or Greater Restoration must be cast at 6th level or higher.
  - If struck by Dreamcatcher +5, only Wish or equivalent effects can restore the reduction.

| Weapon Level | Upcast Requirement | Curable By                            |
| ------------ | ------------------ | ------------------------------------- |
| 0            | 3nd-level slot     | _Remove Curse_, _Greater Restoration_ |
| 1            | 4rd-level slot     | _Remove Curse_, _Greater Restoration_ |
| 2            | 5th-level slot     | _Remove Curse_, _Greater Restoration_ |
| 3            | 6th-level slot     | _Greater Restoration_                 |
| 4            | 7th-level slot     | _Greater Restoration_                 |
| 5            | —                  | Only _Wish_ or equivalent             |

After **Laid Bare** ends, or if the creature succeeds on the saving throw they become immune to this effect until the next dawn.

### Weapon Mastery

- **Graze** _(Only available if the wielder has mastery with The Dreamcatcher's shape)_: If you miss a creature with your weapon, you deal damage equal to the ability modifier you used to make the roll.
- **Enhanced Graze**: **Graze** deals additional damage equal to the Dreamcatcher’s level.
- **Fate Rend** _(1/Short rest, or 3/Short rest if the wielder has mastery with the Dreamcatcher’s shape)_:  
  You may replace an attack with a precise slice to a creature's fate string. Target a creature you can see within **30 feet**. The target must immediately make the **six Dreamcatcher saves** — Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma — in any order it chooses.
  - **For each failed save**: The creature takes **3d10 true damage** and suffers **1 level of exhaustion**.  
    The creature instantly dies if it fails all six saves, unable to keep existing with it's unstrung identity.
