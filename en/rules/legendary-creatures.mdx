# Legendary Creatures

## Overview
Legendary creatures are exceptional monsters that can take special actions outside their turns, possess lairs with automatic effects, and create regional effects in the world around them. This rule block also covers brief guidance on monster equipment and material components for spellcasting monsters.

## Mechanics

### Monster Equipment and Material Components
- A DM can equip monsters with additional gear and trinkets as desired. Decide how much of a monster's equipment is recoverable after the creature is slain and whether any of that equipment is still usable. A battered suit of armor made for a monster is rarely usable by someone else.
- If a spellcasting monster requires material components to cast spells, assume the monster has the material components necessary to cast the spells listed in its stat block.

### Legendary Creatures (General)
- A legendary creature can do things ordinary creatures cannot: it can take special actions outside its turn, and it might exert magical influence for miles around.
- If a creature assumes the form of a legendary creature (for example, via a spell), that creature does not gain the legendary actions, lair actions, or regional effects of the form.

### Legendary Actions
- A legendary creature can take a certain number of special actions called legendary actions outside its turn.
- Only one legendary action option can be used at a time, and only at the end of another creature's turn.
- A creature regains its spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.
- A legendary creature can forgo using legendary actions.
- A legendary creature cannot use legendary actions while incapacitated or otherwise unable to take actions.
- If the creature is surprised, it cannot use legendary actions until after its first turn in the combat.

### A Legendary Creature's Lair
- A legendary creature might have a lair section describing its lair and the special effects it can create while there, either by act of will or simply by being present.
- A lair section applies only to a legendary creature that spends a great deal of time in its lair.

### Lair Actions
- If a legendary creature has lair actions, it can use them to harness the ambient magic of its lair.
- On initiative count 20 (losing all initiative ties), the creature can use one of its lair action options.
- The creature cannot use lair actions while incapacitated or otherwise unable to take actions.
- If the creature is surprised, it cannot use a lair action until after its first turn in the combat.

### Regional Effects
- The mere presence of a legendary creature can have strange and wondrous effects on its environment.
- Regional effects are tied to the creature's existence and proximity; when the legendary creature dies, those regional effects might end abruptly or slowly dissipate, at the DM's discretion.

## Interactions
- Polymorphing or otherwise assuming the form of a legendary creature does not grant legendary actions, lair actions, or regional effects to the creature assuming the form.
- Legendary actions and lair actions are constrained by the creature's capacity to take actions—conditions that prevent actions (incapacitated, surprised before first turn, etc.) also prevent use of these options.
- Decisions about recoverable equipment and lingering regional effects are adjudicated by the DM and can affect treasure recovery, encounter aftermath, and story continuity.
