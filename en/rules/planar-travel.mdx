# Planar Travel

## Overview
Planar travel covers travel between the Material Plane and the other planes of existence that make up the multiverse. These journeys are accomplished either by magic (spells and magic items) or by traversing preexisting or created portals. The rules here describe the planes relevant to travel, the means of transit, and the principal behaviours of portals and the transitive planes.

## Mechanics

- Scope of the planes
  - The planes of existence encompass every world where adventures occur, and also realms of raw elemental matter, pure thought and ethos, the homes of demons and angels, and the dominions of deities. They are not merely other worlds but different qualities of being.

- Methods of planar travel
  - By Spell: A number of spells allow direct or indirect access to other planes. Examples named in tradition include plane shift and gate, which can transport adventurers directly to other planes (with differing degrees of precision), etherealness, which allows entry to the Ethereal Plane and travel from there to planes it touches (such as the Elemental Planes), and astral projection, which projects adventurers into the Astral Plane to travel to the Outer Planes. Many spells and magic items can draw energy from other planes, summon creatures that dwell there, communicate with their denizens, or allow characters to travel to them.
  - By Portal: A portal is a stationary interplanar connection that links a specific location on one plane to a specific location on another. Portals can take many physical forms and have varied behaviours:
    - Doorways, clear windows, or fog-shrouded passages that transport those who step through them directly to the paired location on another plane.
    - Fixed-location portals that are manifested as circles of standing stones, towers, sailing ships, or even whole towns that exist on multiple planes at once or that flicker from one plane to another in turn.
    - Vortices that typically join an Elemental Plane to an analogous location on the Material Plane (for example, the heart of a volcano joining to the Plane of Fire, or the ocean depths joining to the Plane of Water).
  - travel can be effected either by casting an appropriate spell that grants planar movement or by locating and using a portal with the required access conditions.

- The Material Plane
  - The Material Plane is the nexus where the philosophical and elemental forces that define the other planes collide with mundane matter. It is the usual starting point for campaigns and adventures. Worlds within the Material Plane reflect the imagination of the GM and the players and include wildly diverse settings (deserts, water worlds, magic-ruled lands, advanced-technology realms, stagnant Stone Age worlds, places where gods walk, and places they have abandoned).
  - Domains on other planes are defined in relation to the Material Plane; travel to those planes may be literal traversal (stepping into an Elemental Plane at a corresponding location) or accomplished via spells or portals.

- The Transitive Planes
  - The Ethereal Plane and the Astral Plane are called the Transitive Planes; they are largely featureless realms that serve primarily as ways to travel from one plane to another.
  - Ethereal Plane: a misty, fog-bound dimension whose Border Ethereal overlaps the Material Plane and the Inner Planes so that every location on those planes has a corresponding location on the Ethereal Plane. Certain creatures can perceive into the Border Ethereal, and spells or effects that grant similar perception (for example, see invisibility) allow characters to detect or interact with Border Ethereal phenomena.
  - Astral Plane: used as a medium to reach the Outer Planes; the astral projection spell allows travelers to project themselves into the Astral Plane and journey to the Outer Planes from there.

- Portals — further details
  - Some portals function like simple passages: stepping through effects immediate planar travel.
  - Other portals are complex locations that require specific conditions, rituals, keys, or precise coordinates to activate or traverse. Some portals are semi-permanent; others flicker open only at particular times or under particular conditions.
  - Portals vary in precision: some deposit travelers at a precise location on the destination plane, others place travelers in a broad region and require additional navigation.

## Interactions

- Spells, magic items, and portals interact: many spells and items draw energy from the planes or temporarily open planar pathways; portals may be created, stabilized, or discovered by magical means.
- Travel consequences and narrative effects:
  - Planar travel often changes the tone and potential hazards of an adventure — the environment, available resources, and the nature of denizens encountered differ from those of the Material Plane.
  - The GM should treat journeys between planes as significant events: travel to the realms of the dead, bargaining with extraplanar servants, or visiting a deity's dominion are moments of legend that can have long-term campaign consequences.
- Usage notes for GMs:
  - When designing portals, specify form, activation method, precision of destination, permanence, any required ritual or key, and whether the portal connects to a single destination or cycles among multiple planes or locations.
  - When allowing spells that grant planar movement, determine whether the spell's precision and limitations apply (for instance, whether plane shift or gate reaches the exact intended locus or merely a general area) and adjudicate travel time and risk on the destination plane.

## References and examples

- Typical spells and effects referenced in this context include plane shift, gate, etherealness, and astral projection; these are examples of magical means to access or traverse the planes.
- Typical portal forms include doorways, windows of clear air, fog-shrouded passages, circles of standing stones, towers, sailing ships, and vortices connecting analogous elemental locations.

## Summary
Planar travel is accomplished primarily by spells or by portals. The Material Plane serves as the familiar starting point; the Transitive Planes (Ethereal and Astral) function as travel conduits. Portals and spells vary widely in form, activation, and precision, and GMs should treat interplanar journeys as significant, mechanically and narratively consequential events.
