# Monster Stat Blocks

## Overview

This section explains the statistics that appear in a monster's stat block and how those statistics are calculated and used in play. It covers hit dice and hit points, saving throws, speed and movement modes, proficiency bonuses derived from challenge rating, and skill bonuses.

## Mechanics

### Hit Dice and Hit Points

A monster's hit points are determined by its Hit Dice (HD) and its Constitution modifier. Its Constitution modifier is multiplied by the number of Hit Dice the monster possesses, and the result is added to its hit points.

Example: a monster with Constitution 12 (+1 modifier) and 2d8 Hit Dice has hit points equal to 2d8 + 2 (average 11).

The following table shows the die used for a monster of a given size and the average hit points per die (for reference):

| Size | Hit Die | Average HP per Die |
|------|---------:|-------------------:|
| Tiny | d4      | 2.5                |
| Small | d6     | 3.5                |
| Medium | d8    | 4.5                |
| Large | d10    | 5.5                |
| Huge | d12     | 6.5                |
| Gargantuan | d20 | 10.5              |

> Note: the actual HP is rolled (or taken as the average at design time) and then the total Constitution bonus (Con modifier × number of HD) is added.

### Saving Throws

The Saving Throws entry in a monster stat block is present only for creatures that are particularly adept at resisting certain kinds of effects. When present, a saving throw bonus shown for an ability is the sum of the monster's relevant ability modifier and its proficiency bonus (the proficiency bonus is determined by the monster's challenge rating; see the proficiency table below).

For most creatures that do not have special saving-throw training, this section is omitted from the stat block.

### Speed and Movement Modes

A monster's speed indicates how far it can move on its turn. All creatures have a walking speed (simply called the monster's speed). Creatures that lack any form of ground-based locomotion have a walking speed of 0 feet.

Some monsters have one or more additional movement modes. When present, these movement modes are listed in the stat block and work as follows:

- Burrow: A monster with a burrowing speed can use that speed to move through sand, earth, mud, or ice. A monster can't burrow through solid rock unless it has a special trait that allows it to do so.
- Climb: A monster with a climbing speed can use all or part of its movement to move on vertical surfaces. The monster does not need to spend extra movement to climb.
- Fly: A monster with a flying speed can use all or part of its movement to fly. Some flying monsters can hover; a creature that can hover doesn't fall when it ends its turn in the air and has advantages against being knocked out of the sky. See the Player's Handbook for rules on flying.
- Swim: A monster with a swimming speed does not need to spend extra movement to swim; its swim speed replaces walking movement when it moves in water.

### Proficiency Bonus by Challenge Rating

A monster's proficiency bonus is determined by its challenge rating (CR). That proficiency bonus applies to the monster's attack rolls, saving throws (when present), skill bonuses, and other proficiency-based calculations.

| Challenge Rating | Proficiency Bonus |
|------------------:|------------------:|
| 0 (and 1/8, 1/4, 1/2) | +2            |
| 1–4               | +2               |
| 5–8               | +3               |
| 9–12              | +4               |
| 13–16             | +5               |
| 17–20             | +6               |
| 21–24             | +7               |
| 25–28             | +8               |
| 29–30             | +9               |

(If a monster's CR is expressed as fractional values such as 1/8, 1/4, or 1/2, use the proficiency bonus listed for that CR bracket—fractional CRs at the low end use the +2 value.)

### Skills

The Skills entry is reserved for monsters that are proficient in one or more skills. A monster's skill bonus is the sum of the relevant ability modifier and the monster's proficiency bonus (as determined by its challenge rating). Other modifiers (circumstance, trait-based modifiers, or magic items) may also apply and are shown in the stat block.

Example: a monster that is very perceptive and stealthy might have bonuses to Wisdom (Perception) and Dexterity (Stealth). Each listed skill bonus equals (ability modifier) + (proficiency bonus) + any other modifiers.

## Interactions

- Ability-score modifiers affect several derived statistics simultaneously: Constitution affects hit points (Con modifier × HD) and Constitution-based saving throws; other ability modifiers contribute to attack and damage rolls, skills, and checks as usual.
- The proficiency bonus derived from the monster's challenge rating is used consistently for attack rolls, saving throws (when listed), skill bonuses, and any other proficiency-based calculations in the stat block.
- Movement modes interact with terrain and conditions as described in the Player's Handbook and related rules (for example, difficult terrain, being restrained, or rules that impair a particular movement type).


## Examples and Notes

- Example HP calculation repeated for clarity: A monster with 2d8 Hit Dice and Constitution 12 (+1) has hit points = 2d8 + (1 × 2) = 2d8 + 2 (average 11).
- The Saving Throws and Skills sections are omitted from a monster's stat block unless the monster has particular proficiency or resistance worth noting.
