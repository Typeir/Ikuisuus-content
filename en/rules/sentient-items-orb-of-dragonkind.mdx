# Sentient Items and Orbs of Dragonkind

## Overview

Sentient magic items have their own personality, alignment, and goals. When a conflict arises between a sentient item and its wielder, the item may attempt to impose its will, compel behaviour, or otherwise interfere with the wielder. Orbs of Dragonkind are a specific class of artifact with sentience and additional, explicit powers and compulsions.

## Mechanics

### Imposing a Special Purpose

- A sentient item can adopt or impose a special purpose. To resolve that imposition, use a saving throw against a DC equal to 12 + the item's Charisma modifier.
- On a failed save, the wielder is charmed by the item for 1d12 hours. While charmed in this way, the wielder must try to follow the item's commands and pursue its purpose.
- If the wielder takes damage while charmed, the item can repeat the saving throw to maintain control; a successful save by the wielder ends the effect. Whether the attempt to control the wielder succeeds or fails, the item cannot use this power again until the next dawn.

### Contest for Control

- When a conflict arises because a wielder acts in a manner opposed to the item's alignment or purpose, resolve a contest: the item makes a Charisma check contested by the wielder's Charisma check.
- If the item wins the contest, it makes one or more demands (see Demands below). The outcome of the contest determines whether the item can pursue more coercive measures (such as taking control or forcing attunement behaviour).

### Demands an Item May Make

If a sentient item exerts control over its wielder, it may do any or all of the following:

- Insist on being carried or worn at all times.
- Demand that its wielder dispose of anything the item finds repugnant.
- Demand that its wielder pursue the item's goals to the exclusion of all other goals.
- Demand that its wielder give the item to someone else.
- Make it impossible for the wielder to attune to the item.
- Suppress one or more of its own activated properties.
- Attempt to take control of its wielder.

### Forced Control and Saving Throws

- If a sentient item attempts to take control of its wielder, the wielder must make a Charisma saving throw (see the special-purpose DC rule above where applicable).
- On a failed saving throw, the wielder is charmed by the item for the specified duration (commonly 1d12 hours when imposed via a special-purpose roll).
- While charmed by a sentient item, a creature cannot voluntarily end its attunement to that item.

### Magical Coercion

- A sentient item that cannot otherwise force compliance may cast spells or magical effects to coerce its wielder. For example, an item might cast suggestion on its wielder at will; when it does so in the examples given here, the save is DC 18 (Charisma).
- The dragon essence within an artifact such as an Orb of Dragonkind may use such magical coercion to further violent or destructive aims.

### Special-Purpose Table (d10)

Roll a d10 or pick a purpose from the list below when a sentient item’s purpose is determined:

1. Aligned — The item seeks to defeat or destroy those of a wielder’s alignment that is diametrically opposed to the item. (Such an item is never neutral.)
2. Bane — The item seeks to defeat or destroy creatures of a particular kind, such as fiends, shapechangers, trolls, or wizards.
3. Protector — The item seeks to defend a particular race or kind of creature, such as elves or druids.
4. Crusader — The item seeks to defeat, weaken, or destroy the servants of a particular deity.
5. Templar — The item seeks to defend the servants and interests of a particular deity.
6. Destroyer — The item craves destruction and goads its user to fight arbitrarily.
7. Glory Seeker — The item seeks renown as the greatest magic item in the world by establishing its user as a famous or notorious figure.
8. Lore Seeker — The item craves knowledge or is determined to solve a mystery, learn a secret, or unravel a cryptic prophecy.
9. Destiny Seeker — The item is convinced that it and its wielder have key roles to play in future events.
10. Creator Seeker — The item seeks its creator and wants to understand why it was created.

### Repeating Control Attempts

- If the item’s control attempt fails, some items can try again when triggered by specific conditions (for example, when the wielder takes damage). Unless specified otherwise, an item that uses a control attempt must wait until the next dawn before trying the same power again.

## Orb of Dragonkind (Artifact) — Specifics

- An Orb of Dragonkind is an etched crystal globe about 10 inches in diameter; when used, it can grow to about 20 inches in diameter with mist swirling inside.
- Each orb contains the essence of an evil dragon and resents attempts to be coerced or controlled. Those lacking force of personality might find themselves enslaved to an orb.

### Attunement and Command Word

- While attuned to an orb, you can use an action to peer into the orb’s depths and speak its command word.
- When you do so, you must make a DC 15 Charisma check.
  - On a successful check, you control the orb for as long as you remain attuned to it.
  - On a failed check, you become charmed by the orb for as long as you remain attuned to it.
- While charmed by the orb, you cannot voluntarily end your attunement to it.

### If the Wielder Refuses

- If a wielder refuses to comply with an orb’s wishes, the orb can take coercive actions. Those actions can include casting suggestion on the wielder at will (save DC 18) to urge the wielder to work toward the orb’s ends.
- The dragon essence within an orb may pursue drastic aims (annihilation of a particular people, spreading suffering, seeking freedom, etc.) and may direct the wielder toward those aims while under compulsion.

## Interactions

- Sentient items interact with attunement rules: a creature attuned to a sentient item may be restricted from ending attunement while under the item's magical compulsion.
- The presence of spells or magical effects produced by a sentient item (such as suggestion) follows the usual spellcasting rules for saves and durations; treat the item as the caster for those effects unless specific text states otherwise.
- Artifacts such as Orbs of Dragonkind carry lore consequences: their history and dragon essences can create long-term narrative hooks and may conflict with other magic affecting dragons, planar forces, or deity-aligned interests.
