# Magic Items

## Overview

Magic items are objects discovered in hoards or vaults that grant capabilities a creature could rarely have otherwise. They may be worn or wielded and sometimes require a bond called attunement before their magical properties can be used. This rule describes how items are worn and wielded, how attunement works, limits on wearing and attuning multiple items, paired-item behaviour, and how items are activated.

## Mechanics

### Wearing and Wielding Items

- A magic item meant to be worn must be donned in the intended fashion: boots on the feet, gloves on the hands, hats and helmets on the head, rings on the finger.
- Magic armor must be donned, a shield must be strapped to the arm, a cloak fastened about the shoulders, and a weapon must be held to be used. Many magic garments are made adjustable or magically adjust themselves to the wearer.
- Use common sense when a nonhumanoid tries to wear an item: decide whether the item functions as intended. For example, a ring placed on a tentacle might work, but a yuan-ti with a snakelike tail instead of legs can’t wear boots.
- Without becoming attuned to an item that requires attunement, a creature gains only the item’s nonmagical benefits, unless the item’s description states otherwise.
- If story or setting reasons suggest an item fits only certain creatures (for example, drow-made armor that fits elves only, or items made only for dwarves), the DM may rule the item doesn’t adjust to other body shapes.

### Attunement

- Some magic items require a creature to form a bond with them before their magical properties can be used; this bond is called attunement.
- If an item has a prerequisite for attunement (for example, membership in a class), a creature must meet that prerequisite to attune to the item.
  - If the prerequisite is a class, the creature must be a member of that class to attune to the item. If the class is a spellcasting class, a monster qualifies if it has spell slots and uses that class’s spell list. If the prerequisite is to be a spellcaster, a creature qualifies if it can cast at least one spell using its traits or features.
- Attuning to an item requires the creature to spend a short rest focused on only that item while in physical contact with it. This focus can take the form of weapon practice (for a weapon), meditation (for a wondrous item), or some other appropriate activity.
  - The short rest used to attune cannot be the same short rest used to learn the item’s properties.
  - If the short rest is interrupted, the attunement attempt fails. Otherwise, at the end of the short rest, the creature gains an intuitive understanding of how to activate the item’s magical properties, including any necessary command words.
- An item can be attuned to only one creature at a time.
- A creature can be attuned to no more than three magic items at a time. Any attempt to attune to a fourth item fails; the creature must end its attunement to an item first.
- A creature can’t attune to more than one copy of the same item (for example, more than one ring of protection).
- A creature’s attunement to an item ends if:
  - the creature no longer satisfies the prerequisites for attunement to the item;
  - the item has been more than 100 feet away from the creature for at least 24 hours;
  - the creature dies; or
  - another creature attunes to the item.

### Multiple Items of the Same Kind

- Use common sense to determine whether more than one of a given kind of magic item can be worn at once.
- Normally, a character can’t wear more than one of the following at a time:
  - more than one pair of footwear;
  - more than one pair of gloves or gauntlets;
  - more than one pair of bracers;
  - more than one suit of armor;
  - more than one item of headwear; and
  - more than one cloak.
- Exceptions are possible. For example, a character might be able to wear a circlet under a helmet, or to layer two cloaks. The DM adjudicates such cases.

### Paired Items

- Items that come in pairs—such as boots, bracers, gauntlets, and gloves—impart their benefits only if both items of the pair are worn.
- Example: If a character wears a Boot of Striding and Springing on one foot and a Boot of Elvenkind on the other, neither boot provides its benefit because the paired effect requires both items of the pair.

### Activating an Item

- Activating some magic items requires a creature to do something special, such as holding the item and uttering a command word or performing a specified action.
- The description of each magic item states how it is activated and what actions, bonus actions, or other conditions (for example, attunement) are required for its magic to function.

## Interactions

- Attunement interacts with class prerequisites and spellcasting capabilities: if an item requires membership in a class or that the wearer be a spellcaster, verify the creature’s class membership or spellcasting trait before allowing attunement.
- Limits on attunement (maximum of three items, one creature per item, and not more than one copy of the same item) affect inventory decisions in play and prevent stacking of multiple copies of identical magical effects through attunement.
- Paired items and multiple-item rules can negate expected benefits if items of the same slot or pair are mixed or improperly combined; the DM should apply common-sense adjudication when layering items.
- When an item’s physical fit is in question (nonhumanoid anatomy, unusual limbs, or creature size and shape), the DM decides whether the item functions as intended. If an item does not fit or cannot be worn as intended, its magical properties typically cannot be used.
