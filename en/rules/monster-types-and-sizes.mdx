# Monster Types and Sizes

## Overview

This section explains the information found in a monster's stat block and defines the creature size categories and monster types used by the game. A monster's stat block provides the essential information you need to run the monster in play. A monster's type speaks to its fundamental nature and can cause specific spells, magic items, class features, and other effects to interact with that creature in special ways.

## Mechanics

### Monster Stat Block

- A monster's statistics (its stat block) present the mechanical data required to run the creature: size, type, alignment, armor class, hit points, speeds, ability scores, saving throws, resistances and immunities, senses, languages, challenge rating, actions, traits, and legendary or lair features when present.

### Size Categories

Size indicates how much space a creature controls in combat and affects reach and other tactical rules.

| Size | Space | Examples |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Tiny | 2½ by 2½ ft. | Imp, sprite |
| Small | 5 by 5 ft. | Giant rat, goblin |
| Medium | 5 by 5 ft. | Orc, werewolf |
| Large | 10 by 10 ft. | Hippogriff, ogre |
| Huge | 15 by 15 ft. | Fire giant, treant |
| Gargantuan | 20 by 20 ft. or larger | Kraken, purple worm |

See the Player’s Handbook for expanded rules on creature size and space.

### Monster Types

The game includes several monster types. Each type describes a fundamental nature that may interact with spells and abilities.

- Aberration
  - Aberrations are utterly alien beings whose nature and motives are often incomprehensible. Many have innate magical abilities derived from an alien mindset rather than the world's natural forces. Typical examples: aboleths, beholders, mind flayers, slaadi.

- Beast
  - Beasts are nonhumanoid creatures that are a natural part of the fantasy ecology. They include ordinary animals, dinosaurs, and giant versions of animals.

- Celestial
  - Celestials are creatures native to the Upper Planes, often servants or agents of deities. Many celestials are good by nature; a good celestial that strays from that alignment is a horrifying rarity. Celestials commonly serve as messengers or champions: examples include angels, couatls, and pegasi.

- Construct
  - Constructs are made, not born. Some constructs follow simple programmed instructions, while others are imbued with sentience and independent thought. Golems are iconic constructs; modrons and similar clockwork beings are also constructs shaped from material by creators' will.

- Dragon
  - Dragons are large, reptilian creatures of ancient origin and tremendous power. True dragons (metallic and chromatic) are highly intelligent and possess innate magic. Related but less powerful and usually less intelligent creatures include wyverns and pseudodragons.

- Elemental
  - Elementals are native to the elemental planes. Some are little more than animate masses of their element; others have biological forms infused with elemental energy. The genie races (djinn, efreet) form important civilizations on the elemental planes. Other elemental creatures include azers and invisible stalkers.

- Fey
  - Fey are magical creatures closely tied to the forces of nature. They dwell in twilight groves and misty forests and are commonly associated with realms like the Feywild. Fey examples: dryads, pixies, satyrs.

- Fiend
  - Fiends are creatures of wickedness native to the Lower Planes. Many are servants of archdevils and demon lords; evil priests and mages sometimes summon fiends to do their bidding. Examples: demons, devils, hellhounds, rakshasas, yugoloths. A good fiend is almost inconceivable.

- (Other types)
  - The rules may reference additional or subtypes (such as humanoid, monstrosity, ooze, etc.) in other sections of the rules.

### Examples and Cross-Type Notes

- Some spells, magic items, and class features refer to a creature type. For example, an effect that deals extra damage to dragons affects true dragons and other creatures of the dragon type, such as dragon turtles and wyverns.
- Creature lists in bestiaries include many specific examples of each type; use those examples as guidance when adjudicating interactions.

### Modifying Creatures

- GMs are encouraged to modify creatures to suit adventure needs by tweaking statistics, borrowing a trait from another monster, or applying a variant or template. Such modifications can change a monster's challenge rating and intended role.
- When applying templates or variants, be aware of downstream mechanical effects: altered resistances, immunities, senses, action options, and challenge rating.
- Maintain clarity in the stat block after modification so that the monster's intended behavior, traits, and action economy remain explicit to the table team running it.

## Interactions

- A monster's type and size interact with many systems: area effects, targeting, reach, grappling and carrying rules, class abilities that target specific types, and spells that have special effects on particular types.
- When creating or modifying monsters, verify whether spells, magic items, or class features in the campaign reference a creature's type or size to avoid unintended power shifts.
