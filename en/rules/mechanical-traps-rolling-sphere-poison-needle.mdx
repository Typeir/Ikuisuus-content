# Mechanical Traps

## Overview

This page documents several mechanical and magical trap types encountered in dungeons: a ceiling-mounted pressure-plate rolling sphere, chest-mounted poison needles, and a stone-mouth housing a sphere of annihilation. Each entry reproduces the trap's detection, activation, and mechanical effects exactly as given in the source text.

## Mechanics

### Pressure-plate: Rolling Sphere

- Trigger: When 20 or more pounds of pressure are placed on this trap's pressure plate, a hidden trapdoor in the ceiling opens and releases a 10-foot-diameter rolling sphere of solid stone.
- Detection: A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check can spot the trapdoor and pressure plate. A search of the floor accompanied by a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check reveals variations in the mortar and stone that betray the pressure plate's presence. The same DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check made while inspecting the ceiling notes variations in the stonework that reveal the trapdoor.
- Prevention: Wedging an iron spike or other object under the pressure plate prevents the trap from activating.

Activation and initiative

1. Activation requires all creatures present to roll initiative.
2. The sphere rolls initiative with a +8 bonus.
3. On the sphere's turn, it moves 60 feet in a straight line.
4. The sphere stops when it hits a wall or similar barrier. It can't go around corners; it stops when obstructed by a barrier or wall.

Movement & interaction with creatures

- The sphere can move through creatures' spaces, and creatures can move through its space, treating the sphere's space as difficult terrain.
- Whenever the sphere enters a creature's space or a creature enters the sphere's space, resolve the sphere's effect for that interaction: a random target within 10 feet of the sphere's space while it is rolling must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take 55 (10d10) bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone. Vision is irrelevant to this attack roll.
- If the sphere encounters no valid targets in the area, that resolution has no effect (no targets are hit).

Slowing the sphere

- A poison-needle mechanism (see below) or a mechanical trap check can attempt to slow the sphere.
- A successful DC 20 Strength (Mechanical trap) check reduces the sphere's speed by 15 feet. If the sphere's speed drops to 0, it stops moving and is no longer a threat.

Notes on dungeon design

- Dungeon builders sometimes incorporate gentle, curving turns into nearby passages so the sphere can keep moving rather than stopping at a corner.

### Poison Needle (chest- or object-mounted)

- Description: A poisoned needle is hidden within a treasure chest's lock, or in another item that a creature might open. Opening the chest without the proper key causes the needle to spring out, delivering a dose of poison.
- Needle extension and immediate damage: When the trap is triggered, the needle extends 3 inches straight out from the lock. A creature within range takes 1 (1d4) piercing damage and 11 (2d10) poison damage, and must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 hour.
- Disarming and detection:
  - A successful DC 20 Intelligence (Investigation) check allows a character to deduce the trap's presence from alterations made to the lock to accommodate the needle.
  - A successful DC 15 Dexterity check using thieves' tools disables the trap, removing the needle from the lock.
  - Unsuccessful attempts to pick the lock can trigger the trap.

### Stone Face / Mouth: Sphere of Annihilation (magic trap)

- Description: Magical, impenetrable darkness fills the gaping mouth of a stone face carved into a wall. The mouth is 2 feet in diameter and roughly circular. No sound issues from it, no light can illuminate the inside of it, and any matter that enters it is instantly obliterated.
- Inside the mouth is a sphere of annihilation that can't be controlled or moved; it is otherwise identical to a normal sphere of annihilation.
- Some versions: Some versions of this trap include an enchantment placed on the stone face, such that specified creatures feel an overwhelming urge to approach it and crawl inside its mouth. This effect functions like the sympathy aspect of the antipathy/sympathy spell.
- Removing the enchantment: A successful DC 20 Intelligence (Arcana) check reveals that the mouth contains a sphere of annihilation that can't be controlled or moved. A successful dispel magic (DC 18) removes this enchantment.

### Mixed effects & duplicate poison wording

- Several poison-damage entries and saving throw phrasing appear for different traps. Where listed above, the relevant DCs and damage values are reproduced verbatim from the source:
  - DC 15 Constitution saving throws against poison for the needle and some poison-trap variants; 11 (2d10) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one where the wording indicates half damage.
  - The rolling sphere's bludgeoning effect: 55 (10d10) bludgeoning damage on a failed DC 15 Dexterity save and knocked prone.

## Interactions

- Disarming and spotting traps interacts with Investigation, Perception, and thieves' tools checks as noted above.
- A dispel magic (DC 18) effect can remove some enchantments placed on a stone face's mouth; a successful DC 20 Intelligence (Arcana) check can reveal the presence of a sphere of annihilation inside that mouth.
- Mechanical attempts to slow or stop the rolling sphere use a DC 20 Strength (Mechanical trap) check that subtracts 15 feet from the sphere's speed on success.
