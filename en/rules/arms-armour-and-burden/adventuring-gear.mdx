# Adventuring Gear

## Overview

This page collects the mechanical descriptions for common adventuring gear: lamps, lanterns, torches, oil and tinder, restraints, tools, and other mundane equipment. Each entry below reproduces the usable effects, durations, distances, interaction triggers, and any ability checks or saving throws required to use, supply, or overcome the item.

## Mechanics

### Lamp

- A lamp casts bright light in a 15-foot radius and dim light for an additional 30 feet.
- Once lit, a lamp burns for 6 hours on a flask (1 pint) of oil.
- As an action, you can lower the hood (where applicable), reducing the light to dim light in a 5-foot radius.

### Lanterns

- Bullseye Lantern
  - A bullseye lantern casts bright light in a 60-foot cone and dim light for an additional 60 feet.
  - Once lit, it burns for 6 hours on a flask (1 pint) of oil.

- Hooded Lantern
  - A hooded lantern casts bright light in a 30-foot radius and dim light for an additional 30 feet.
  - Once lit, it burns for 6 hours on a flask (1 pint) of oil.
  - As an action, you can lower the hood, reducing the light to dim light in a 5-foot radius.

### Torch

- A torch burns for 1 hour.
- A torch provides bright light in a 20-foot radius and dim light for an additional 20 feet.
- If you make a melee attack with a burning torch and hit, it deals 1 fire damage.

### Oil (flask of oil)

- A flask of oil usually holds 1 pint.
- As an action, you can:
  - Splash the oil onto a creature within 5 feet of you, or
  - Throw the flask up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact (make a ranged attack against a creature or object). Treat the thrown flask as an improvised weapon.
- On a hit when splashed or thrown, the target is covered in oil.
- If the covered target takes any fire damage before the oil dries (the oil dries after 1 minute), the target takes an additional 5 fire damage.
- You can pour a flask of oil on the ground to cover a 5-foot square area (the surface must be level). If the oil in that area is then set on fire:
  - The lit oil burns for 2 rounds and deals 5 fire damage to any creature that enters the area or ends its turn there.
  - A creature can take this damage only once per turn.
- Lighting a torch (or starting a similar fire with abundant, exposed fuel) using oil/tinder is described under Tinderbox; lighting other kinds of fires may take 1 minute.

### Tinderbox

- A tinderbox is a small container that holds flint, fire steel, and tinder (usually dry cloth soaked in oil).
- Using a tinderbox to light a torch—or anything else with abundant, exposed fuel—takes an action.
- Lighting other fires with a tinderbox (fires that require care to start) typically takes about 1 minute.

### Poison (basic, vial)

- A basic vial of poison can coat one slashing or piercing weapon or up to three pieces of ammunition.
- Once applied, the poison retains potency for 1 minute before drying (after that minute it is no longer effective for coating).
- A creature hit by a poisoned weapon or poisoned ammunition must make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or take 1d4 poison damage.
- Applying the poison to a weapon or ammunition takes an action.

### Potion of Healing

- A potion of healing contains a magical red fluid. Drinking or administering a potion of healing takes an action.
- Drinking a potion of healing restores 2d4 + 2 hit points.

### Pouch / Component Pouch

- A cloth or leather pouch can hold up to 20 sling bullets or 50 blowgun needles, among other small things.
- A compartmentalized pouch specifically intended for holding spell components is called a component pouch (see component rules earlier in the rules text).

### Quiver

- A quiver can hold up to 20 arrows.

### Magnifying Glass

- A magnifying glass allows closer inspection of small objects and grants advantage on any ability check made to appraise or inspect an item that is small or highly detailed.
- A magnifying glass can substitute for flint and steel when starting fires via focused sunlight, but it requires light as bright as sunlight, tinder to ignite, and about 5 minutes of focused time to start a fire.
- When using a magnifying glass as part of teamwork to use other tools (for example, helping someone use a ram), the magnifying glass can grant advantage on the related check as described below.

### Ram, Portable

- A portable ram is used to break down doors.
- When used to break down a door, you gain a +4 bonus on the Strength check to force the door.
- One other character can help you use the ram; when they do so they can grant you advantage on that Strength check.

### Rations

- Rations consist of dry foods suitable for extended travel, including jerky, dried fruit, hardtack, and nuts.

### Rope

- Rope (hemp or silk) has 2 hit points.
- Rope can be burst with a successful DC 17 Strength check.

### Manacles

- Manacles are metal restraints that can bind a Small or Medium creature.
- Escaping the manacles requires a successful DC 20 Dexterity check.
- Breaking the manacles requires a successful DC 20 Strength check.
- Each set of manacles comes with one key. Without the key, a creature proficient with thieves' tools can pick the manacles' lock with a successful DC 15 Dexterity check.
- Manacles have 15 hit points.

### Lock

- A lock is supplied with a key. Without the key, a creature proficient with thieves' tools can pick the lock with a successful DC 15 Dexterity check.
- Your GM may decide that better locks exist for higher prices; such locks would have different DCs or requirements.

### Scale, Merchant's

- A merchant's scale includes a small balance, pans, and a suitable assortment of weights up to 2 pounds.
- With a merchant's scale, you can measure the exact weight of small objects (for example, raw precious metals or trade goods) to help determine their worth.

### Mess Kit

- A mess kit is a tin box that contains a cup and simple cutlery.
- The box clamps together; one side can be used as a cooking pan and the other as a plate or shallow bowl.

### Spellbook

- A spellbook is essential for wizards: a leather-bound tome with 100 blank vellum pages suitable for recording spells.

### Spyglass

- A spyglass magnifies objects viewed through it to twice their apparent size.

### Tent

- A simple canvas tent is portable and sleeps two.

## Interactions

- Oil + Fire: creatures covered in oil take additional damage if they take fire damage before the oil dries (see Oil). Poured oil on the ground creates a temporary hazardous area that deals fire damage while burning.
- Magnifying Glass + Sunlight: a magnifying glass requires sunlight-level bright light to start fires by focusing; it grants advantage on inspection/appraisal checks for small items.
- Locks & Manacles + Thieves' Tools: without keys, locks and manacles can be picked by a creature proficient with thieves' tools using the DCs listed (typically DC 15 Dex for these items); stronger locks may have higher DCs as determined by the GM.
- Ram teamwork: another character can help with the ram to grant advantage; a magnifying glass or other tool that logically helps the action can also grant advantage at the GM's discretion.
- Potions: administering or drinking a potion takes an action; potions restore hit points per their listed effect (potion of healing: 2d4 + 2).
