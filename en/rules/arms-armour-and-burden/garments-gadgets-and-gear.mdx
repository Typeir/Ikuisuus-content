# Garments, Gadgets, and Gear

## Overview

Not everything a character carries is a weapon or a suit of armour. This section covers the categories of carried equipment: what they are, where their rules live, and where to find their item listings.

## What You Can Carry

### Armour

Worn protection that sets your base Armour Class. Rules for donning, doffing, and AC calculation are in [Using Weapons](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapons). Full armour statistics are in the [Equipment section](en/library/items/equipment/weapons).

### Weapons

Instruments of attack. Rules covering proficiency, properties, and the Damocles mastery system are spread across [Using Weapons](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapons), [Weapon Properties](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapon-properties), and [Weapons and Dice Levels](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapons-and-dice-levels). Full weapon statistics are in the [Equipment section](en/library/items/equipment/weapons).

### Trinkets

Consumables and tactical adventuring items with active mechanical uses — oil flasks, torches, caltrops, manacles, healing potions, and similar gear. Each trinket entry lists its effects, action cost, range, and any saving throw DCs. Browse the full catalogue in the [Trinkets section](en/library/items/trinkets).

### Heirlooms

Rare and named items of magical or exceptional quality. Browse them in the [Heirlooms section](en/library/items/heirlooms).

### Tools

Artisan's tools, kits, and musical instruments — the instruments of craft and trade rather than combat. Proficiency rules, the cost table, and starting equipment packs are in [Tools and Trades](en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/tools-and-trades). Rules for producing goods during downtime are in [Crafting and Creation](en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/crafting-and-creation).

### Objects

An object is any physical item that can be interacted with and is not included in the previous categories. You may or may not be able to carry it, but it is not a weapon, piece of armour, trinket, heirloom, or tool. Examples include doors, chests, furniture, and similar objects. Rules for interacting with items are in [Objects](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/objects).
