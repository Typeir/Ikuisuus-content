# Garments, Gadgets, and Gear

Not everything a character carries is a weapon or a suit of armour. This section covers the categories of carried equipment: what they are, where their rules live, and where to find their item listings.

## What You Can Carry

### Armour

Worn protection that sets your base Armour Class. Rules for donning and doffing times, and for losing your Dexterity bonus when incapacitated or unable to move, are in [Donning, Doffing, and Defence](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/donning-doffing-and-defense). Full armour statistics are in the [Equipment section](/en/library/items/equipment/weapons).

### Weapons

Instruments of attack. Rules covering proficiency, properties, dice levels, and the mastery system are in [Weapons](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapons-and-dice-levels) and [Weapon Properties](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapon-properties). Full weapon statistics are in the [Equipment section](/en/library/items/equipment/weapons).

### Trinkets

Consumables and tactical adventuring items with active mechanical uses — oil flasks, torches, caltrops, manacles, healing potions, and similar gear. Each trinket entry lists its effects, action cost, range, and any saving throw DCs. Browse the full catalogue in the [Trinkets section](/en/library/items/trinkets).

### Heirlooms

Rare and named items of magical or exceptional quality. Browse them in the [Heirlooms section](/en/library/items/heirlooms).

### Tools

Trades, kits, and musical instruments — the equipment of craft and professional life rather than combat. Trade proficiency rules, the cost table, and individual trade pages (including specialization tables) are in [Trades](/en/library/items/tools) and [Tools, Trades and Crafts](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/tools-trades-and-crafts). Rules for producing goods during downtime are in [Crafting and Creation](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/crafting-and-creation).

### Objects

An object is any physical item that can be interacted with and is not included in the previous categories. You may or may not be able to carry it, but it is not a weapon, piece of armour, trinket, heirloom, or tool. Examples include doors, chests, furniture, and similar objects. Rules for interacting with items are in [Objects](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/objects).
