# Using Weapons

## What Is a Weapon Attack

When you make a weapon attack, you roll your attack die against the target's Armour Class. On a hit, you roll the weapon's damage die and add your relevant ability modifier. Melee attacks use Strength by default; some weapons permit Dexterity through their property definitions (see [Weapon Properties](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapon-properties)).

Specific weapon damage dice and any special rules tied to individual weapons are in [Equipment: Weapons](en/library/items/equipment/weapons).

## Proficiency

You add your proficiency bonus to attack rolls only with weapons in which you have proficiency. If you lack proficiency with a weapon, you may still use it — you simply do not add the proficiency bonus to the attack roll. Damage rolls are unaffected.

## Two-Weapon Fighting

When you take the Attack action and attack with a Light melee weapon held in one hand, you may use a bonus action to make one additional attack with a different Light melee weapon held in your other hand. You do not add your ability modifier to the bonus attack's damage roll, unless that modifier is negative.

## Improvised Weapons

A character without a proper weapon can attack with any object they can hold — a broken chair leg, a heavy book, a loose brick. The GM adjudicates whether the object is similar enough to a real weapon to use that weapon's statistics. If it bears no useful resemblance, it deals **1d4 damage** of a type the GM deems appropriate.

Improvised thrown objects have a normal range of 20 feet and a long range of 60 feet.

## Silvered Weapons

Certain creatures are susceptible to silvered weapons where they would otherwise resist or ignore non-magical blows. A weapon can be silvered without altering its normal function; the process requires skilled metalwork and is not trivially inexpensive.

## Mastery and Masterful Blow

Every weapon belongs to a weapon group. Characters who qualify may perform a **Masterful Blow** — a specialized strike that exploits their weapon's Mastery property. Which groups a character can use, how many Masterful Blows are available per rest, and the mechanics of each Mastery type are detailed in [Weapon Properties](en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapon-properties).

The full list of weapons, their groups, damage dice, and Mastery assignments is in [Equipment: Weapons](en/library/items/equipment/weapons).
