# Encumbrance and Carrying Capacity

Carrying capacity represents how much weight a creature can bear before movement and effectiveness begin to suffer.

## Carrying Capacity

- Carrying capacity is **Strength score × 15** in pounds.
- You can push, drag, or lift a weight up to **twice your carrying capacity** (up to 30 × Strength score in pounds).
- While pushing, dragging, or lifting beyond your carrying capacity (but within the push/drag/lift limit), your speed drops to 5 feet.
- **Size** affects carrying capacity: for each size category above Medium, double the carrying capacity and push/drag/lift limit. For Tiny creatures, halve both values.

## Variant: Encumbrance

The variant encumbrance rules provide more granular penalties based on carried weight. When using this variant, ignore the Strength column of the Armor table.

**Encumbered** — carrying more than 5 × Strength score in pounds:

- Speed drops by 10 feet.

**Heavily Encumbered** — carrying more than 10 × Strength score in pounds (up to the carrying capacity maximum):

- Speed drops by 20 feet.
- Disadvantage on ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws that use Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution.

## Armor and Dexterity

Some armor types cap how much of your Dexterity modifier applies to Armor Class. Consult the armor tables for the maximum Dexterity bonus (if any) for a given suit.
