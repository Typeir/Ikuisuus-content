# Armor and Weapons

## Overview

This page collects the mechanical rules for armor, shields, and weapons: their costs, AC calculations, strength requirements, stealth interactions, donning and doffing times, weapon proficiency, and common weapon properties such as ammunition, improvised use, and finesse. All discrete cases and recovery rules are reproduced.

Notes:
- Light armor uses the character's full Dexterity modifier for AC.
- Medium armor uses Dexterity modifier up to a maximum of +2.
- Heavy armor provides a fixed AC and imposes disadvantage on Stealth checks unless otherwise noted.

### Getting Into and Out of Armor (Donning and Doffing)

The time required to don (put on) or doff (take off) armor depends on the armor's category. If you have help, reduce the don or doff time by half when specified.

| Category | Don (put on) | Doff (take off) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Light Armor | 1 minute | 1 minute |
| Medium Armor | 5 minutes | 1 minute |
| Heavy Armor | 10 minutes | 5 minutes |
| Shield | 1 action | 1 action |

Definitions and special cases:
- Don. The time it takes to put on armor. You benefit from the armor’s AC only if you take the full time to don the suit of armor.
- Doff. The time it takes to take off armor. If you have help, reduce this time by half.

### Weapon Proficiency and Weapons

- Your race, class, and feats can grant proficiency with certain weapons or categories of weapons. The two broad categories are simple and martial weapons.
- Proficiency with a weapon allows you to add your proficiency bonus to the attack roll for attacks you make with that weapon. If you make an attack with a weapon with which you lack proficiency, you do not add your proficiency bonus to the attack roll.
- Classes grant proficiency in weapons reflecting their training; martial weapons require more specialized training to use effectively.

#### Weapons and the Weapons Table

The Weapons table (not reproduced in full here) lists common weapons, their price, weight, damage, and any special properties. Every weapon is classified as melee or ranged. A melee weapon is used to attack a target within 5 feet of you; a ranged weapon is used to attack a target at a distance.

### Weapon Properties (selected, preserved rules)

- Ammunition. You can use a weapon with the ammunition property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition to fire from the weapon. Each time you attack with the weapon, you expend one piece of ammunition. Drawing the ammunition from a quiver, case, or other container is part of the attack. You need a free hand to load a one-handed weapon. At the end of a battle, you can recover half your expended ammunition by taking a minute to search the battlefield.

- Improvised Weapons. Sometimes an object not designed to be a weapon is used in combat. If you use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a melee attack, you treat the weapon as an improvised weapon (see improvised weapons rules). A sling must be loaded to deal damage when used as a melee improvised weapon.

- Finesse. When making an attack with a finesse weapon, you use your choice of your Strength or Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both rolls.

### Other Weapon Usage Notes

- Drawing ammunition from a quiver, case, or other container is part of the attack (you do not usually spend an action to ready ammunition separately for that attack).
- A single piece of ammunition is expended each time you attack with a weapon that has the ammunition property; recovering ammunition requires a deliberate search after combat and recovers half the expended pieces.

## Interactions

- Armor and Stealth: Wearing armor that imposes disadvantage on Stealth checks interacts directly with any effect that grants advantage or disadvantage on Stealth. That disadvantage from armor is not bypassed unless a feature explicitly removes armor's Stealth penalty.
- Don/Use timing: You gain an armor’s AC only after taking the full donning time. This interacts with short-rest/long-rest or surprise round timing and with effects that depend on instantaneous AC changes.
- Weapon proficiency and attacks: Features that let you make attacks while lacking proficiency still do not grant proficiency bonus unless the feature explicitly says so.
- Ammunition and improvised use: Using a ranged-ammunition weapon as an improvised melee weapon changes how the weapon is treated and may interact with features that care about weapon properties (for example, a feature requiring a ranged weapon will not apply when the weapon is improvised for melee use).
