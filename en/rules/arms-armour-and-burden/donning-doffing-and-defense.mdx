# Donning, Doffing, and Defence

Defense requires time spent — Whether it's the time it takes to strap on a breastplate, or the time it takes to react to an incoming attack, there are moments when a character is more vulnerable than others. This section covers the rules for putting on and taking off armour, and for losing your Dexterity bonus to Armour Class when incapacitated or unable to move.

## Putting Armour On and Taking It Off

Donning and doffing armour takes time. A creature in a hurry can try to rush the process — usually at the cost of buckles done up wrong, straps out of alignment, or plates left loose. The times below assume deliberate, correct fitting.

| Armour Category | Don        | Doff      |
| --------------- | ---------- | --------- |
| Light           | 1 minute   | 1 minute  |
| Medium          | 5 minutes  | 1 minute  |
| Heavy           | 10 minutes | 5 minutes |
| Shield          | 1 action   | 1 action  |

**Donning**: A creature must be familiar with the armour type to don it within these times. Donning armour of a type in which you have no proficiency takes twice as long.

**Doffing**: Doffing is always faster — buckles and straps are designed to release quickly in an emergency.

**Help**: If another willing creature assists, halve the time required to don or doff any armour category.

## Losing Your Dexterity Bonus

A creature loses its Dexterity modifier to Armour Class when either of the following conditions applies:

- The creature is **incapacitated**.
- The creature has a movement speed of **0** from any source (restrained, paralysed, magically held, or otherwise unable to move).

Those who rely on their reflexes must be keenly aware of this fact — active repositioning, weight transfer, and reactive guard — is unavailable to a creature that cannot move or act.

> **Note**: Armour that already excludes the Dexterity modifier (such as heavy armour with a Dex cap of +0) is unaffected by this rule, since no Dexterity bonus is applied in the first place.
