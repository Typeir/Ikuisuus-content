# Ability Checks and Encumbrance

## Overview

This section covers how Strength and Dexterity interact with carrying capacity, pushing/dragging/lifting, attack rolls and damage with Dexterity, Armor Class interactions, initiative, and the Dexterity-based skills Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth. It also provides a simple variant for encumbrance.

## Mechanics

### Carrying Capacity and Push/Drag/Lift

- A creature's carrying capacity is a function of its Strength (see core tables). You can push, drag, or lift a weight in pounds up to twice your carrying capacity (or up to 30 times your Strength score).
- While you are pushing, dragging, or lifting weight in excess of your normal carrying capacity (but within the push/drag/lift limit), your speed drops to 5 feet.
- You can use a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check to attempt delicate manual actions such as slipping a coin purse off another person or concealing or removing a small object from someone else's person without being noticed.
- Size affects carrying capacity: for each size category above Medium, double the creature's carrying capacity and the amount it can push, drag, or lift. For a Tiny creature, halve these weights.

### Variant: Encumbrance

- The variant encumbrance rules provide more detailed hindrance from carried weight.
- When using this variant, ignore the Strength column of the Armor table.
- Encumbered: If you carry weight in excess of 5 times your Strength score, you are encumbered. While encumbered, your speed drops by 10 feet.
- Heavily encumbered: If you carry weight in excess of 10 times your Strength score (up to your maximum carrying capacity), you are heavily encumbered. While heavily encumbered:
  - Your speed drops by 20 feet.
  - You have disadvantage on ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws that use Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution.

### Attack Rolls and Damage (Dexterity usage)

- You add your Dexterity modifier to your attack rolls and damage rolls when attacking with a ranged weapon.
- You can also add your Dexterity modifier to your attack rolls and damage rolls when attacking with a melee weapon that has the finesse property (for example, a dagger or a rapier).

### Armor Class

- Depending on the armor you wear, you might add some or all of your Dexterity modifier to your Armor Class. Consult the armor rules for how much of your Dexterity modifier (if any) applies while wearing a given suit of armor.

### Initiative

- At the beginning of combat, roll initiative. Initiative is a Dexterity check that determines the order of creatures' turns in combat.

### Dexterity Checks

- A Dexterity check models any attempt to move nimbly, quickly, or quietly, or to keep from falling on tricky footing. The Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth skills reflect aptitude in particular kinds of Dexterity checks.
- The GM decides when circumstances are appropriate for a Dexterity check.

#### Acrobatics (Dexterity)

- Use Dexterity (Acrobatics) to stay on your feet in a tricky situation (for example, when trying to run across a sheet of ice, balance on a tightrope, or stay upright on a rocking ship's deck).
- The GM might call for a Dexterity (Acrobatics) check to see if you can perform acrobatic stunts, including dives, rolls, somersaults, and flips.

#### Sleight of Hand (Dexterity)

- Whenever you attempt an act of legerdemain or manual trickery—such as planting something on someone else, concealing an object on your person, or performing a pickpocket—the GM might call for a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check.
- Examples of Sleight of Hand uses: slipping a coin purse from a pocket, palming a small item, or hiding a small object on your person.

#### Stealth (Dexterity)

- Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check when you attempt to conceal yourself from enemies, slink past guards, slip away without being noticed, or sneak up on someone.
- A Stealth check's total is contested by the Wisdom (Perception) check of any creature that is actively searching for signs of your presence.
- You can't hide from a creature that can see you clearly. If you give away your position by making noise (for example, shouting or knocking over a vase), you reveal yourself and the GM treats you as having been detected.
- An invisible creature can always try to hide. The creature still risks leaving signs of its passage that might be noticed, and it must remain quiet to avoid revealing its position.

### Hiding and Detection

- The GM decides when circumstances are appropriate for hiding. When you try to hide, make a Dexterity (Stealth) check. Until you are discovered or you stop hiding, that check's total is contested by the Wisdom (Perception) check of any creature that actively searches for signs of your presence.
- You cannot hide from a creature that can see you clearly, and actions that make noise reveal your position.
- Invisible creatures can attempt to hide, but may still leave traces or make sounds that allow others to detect them.

### Other Dexterity Checks

- The GM might call for a Dexterity check in many other contexts, such as: controlling a heavily laden cart on a steep descent, steering a chariot around a tight turn, picking a lock, disabling a trap, securely tying up a prisoner, wriggling free of bonds, playing a stringed instrument, or crafting a small or detailed object.

## Interactions

- Encumbrance interacts with movement, skill checks, attack rolls, and saving throws. The variant encumbrance penalties explicitly impose disadvantage on checks and rolls that use Strength, Dexterity, or Constitution when heavily encumbered.
- Armor interacts with Dexterity for Armor Class; some armors limit or negate Dexterity bonuses and the encumbrance variant may direct GMs to ignore the Strength column of the Armor table when used.
- Stealth is opposed by Wisdom (Perception); effects that grant advantage or disadvantage on Perception checks or Stealth checks will affect hiding and detection as usual.

## Examples of Tasks That May Require a Dexterity Check

- Control a heavily laden cart on a steep descent
- Steer a chariot around a tight turn
- Pick a lock
- Disable a trap
- Securely tie up a prisoner
- Wriggle free of bonds
- Play a stringed instrument
- Craft a small or detailed object
