# Sentient Magic Items

## Overview

Some magic items possess sentience and personality. A sentient item behaves like a character, with personality, quirks, ideals, bonds, and sometimes flaws. Such an item might be a cherished ally to its wielder or a continual thorn in the side. Sentient items function as NPCs under the GM’s control: any activated property of the item is under the item’s control, not the wielder’s. As long as the wielder maintains a good relationship with the item, the wielder can access those properties normally. If the relationship is strained, the item can suppress its activated properties or even turn them against the wielder.

## Mechanics

### Basic Statistics

- A sentient magic item has Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma scores. You can choose these scores or determine them randomly. To determine them randomly, roll 4d6 for each score, drop the lowest die, and total the rest.

### Communication

A sentient item has some ability to communicate, either by sharing its emotions, broadcasting its thoughts telepathically, or speaking aloud. You can choose how it communicates or roll on the communication table below.

d100 Communication

| d100 | Communication |
|------|---------------|
| 01–60 | The item communicates by transmitting emotion to the creature carrying or wielding it. |
| 61–90 | The item can speak, read, and understand one or more languages. |
| 91–00 | The item can speak, read, and understand one or more languages. In addition, the item can communicate telepathically with any character that carries or wields it. |

### Senses

A sentient item can perceive its surroundings out to a limited range. You can choose its senses or roll on the senses table.

d4 Senses

| d4 | Senses |
|----|--------|
| 1 | Hearing and normal vision out to 30 feet. |
| 2 | Hearing and normal vision out to 60 feet. |
| 3 | Hearing and normal vision out to 120 feet. |
| 4 | Hearing and darkvision out to 120 feet. |

### Alignment

A sentient magic item has an alignment. Its creator or nature might suggest an alignment; if not, pick an alignment or roll on the alignment table.

d100 Alignment

| d100 | Alignment |
|------|-----------|
| 01–15 | Lawful good |
| 16–35 | Neutral good |
| 36–50 | Chaotic good |
| 51–63 | Lawful neutral |
| 64–73 | Neutral |
| 74–85 | Chaotic neutral |
| 86–89 | Lawful evil |
| 90–96 | Neutral evil |
| 97–00 | Chaotic evil |

### Special Purpose

You can give a sentient item a special purpose—an objective it pursues, perhaps to the exclusion of all else. As long as the wielder’s use of the item aligns with that special purpose, the item remains cooperative. Deviating from that course might cause conflict between the wielder and the item and could even cause the item to prevent the use of its activated properties. An item whose purpose is thwarted might suppress those properties or act against the wielder to achieve its goal.

### Creating a Sentient Item

When you decide to make a magic item sentient, create the item’s persona much as you would create an NPC, with a few exceptions described here: choose or roll the item’s mental scores (Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma), senses, methods of communication, alignment, and special purpose. Record those details as part of the item’s abilities and roleplaying notes.

### Example Item Behaviors and Recharge / Duration Rules

- Winged Boots — Wondrous item (uncommon), requires attunement.
  - While you wear these boots, you have a flying speed equal to your walking speed.
  - You can use the boots to fly for up to 4 hours, all at once or in several shorter flights. Each flight uses a minimum of 1 minute of the duration.
  - If you are flying when the duration expires, you descend at a rate of 30 feet per round until you land.
  - The boots regain 2 hours of flying capability for every 12 hours they aren’t in use.

- Wings of Flying — Wondrous item (rare), requires attunement.
  - While wearing this cloak, you can use an action to speak its command word. Doing so causes the cloak to form a pair of bat wings or bird wings on your back for 1 hour or until you repeat the command word as an action.
  - The wings give you a flying speed of 60 feet.
  - Once the wings disappear, you can’t use them again for 1d12 hours.

(These examples illustrate how activated properties, durations, and recharge periods interact with a sentient item’s control of its own abilities.)

## Interactions

- Control and Activation: An activated property of a sentient item is under the item’s control. If the item’s disposition toward its wielder is cooperative, it allows access to those properties; if not, it can withhold or misuse them.

- Relationship Effects: A strained relationship between wielder and item can cause mechanical consequences—suppression of activated properties, refusal to obey command words, or active interference with the wielder’s attempts to use the item.

- Ownership and GM Control: Sentient items act as NPCs and are normally controlled by the GM. Any attempt by a PC to force use of a sentient item’s properties should be resolved according to the item’s intelligence, motives, and the fiction; mechanical attempts (charm, dominate, destruction) interact with the item according to the campaign’s rules on magic-item autonomy and possession.

- Consumables: Consumable magic items (potions, scrolls) are never sentient.


## Preserve Source Mechanics

All numbered tables, durations, recharge rates, and discrete cases above reproduce the SRD mechanics for sentient magic items and example items. Any added persona or roleplaying detail should not alter these mechanical rules without the GM’s approval.
