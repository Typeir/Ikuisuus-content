# Ability Checks

## Overview

This section describes ability-based checks and how certain ability scores serve as spellcasting abilities for classes. It explains Wisdom and Charisma checks and the common skills tied to those abilities. These rules govern when the GM calls for checks and what those checks represent.

## Mechanics

### Spellcasting Ability

- Wizards use Intelligence as their spellcasting ability. Intelligence determines how well a wizard can prepare and cast spells and helps determine the saving throw DCs of spells the wizard casts.

- Clerics, druids, and rangers use Wisdom as their spellcasting ability. Wisdom reflects how attuned a character is to the world around them; it represents perceptiveness and intuition and helps determine the saving throw DCs of spells those classes cast.

- Charisma measures a character's ability to interact effectively with others. It includes confidence and eloquence and can represent a charming or commanding personality.

### Wisdom Checks

Wisdom checks represent a character's general attunement to the world and cover several specific skills. The GM calls for a Wisdom check when the situation requires perception, intuition, or an understanding of living things and natural signs.

- Animal Handling. When there is any question whether you can calm a domesticated animal, keep a mount from getting spooked, or intuit an animal's intentions, the GM might call for a Wisdom (Animal Handling) check. You also make a Wisdom (Animal Handling) check to control your mount when you attempt a risky maneuver.

- Insight. A Wisdom (Insight) check decides whether you can determine the true intentions of a creature, such as when searching out a lie or predicting someone's next move. Doing so involves gleaning clues from body language, speech habits, and changes in mannerisms.

- Medicine. A Wisdom (Medicine) check lets you try to stabilize a dying companion or diagnose an illness.

- Perception. A Wisdom (Perception) check lets you spot, hear, or otherwise detect the presence of something. It measures your general awareness of your surroundings. For example, you might try to hear a conversation through a closed door, eavesdrop under an open window, or hear monsters moving stealthily in the forest. You might also try to spot things that are obscured or easy to miss, whether they are lying in ambush on a road, thugs hiding in the shadows of an alley, or candlelight under a closed secret door.

- Survival. The GM might ask you to make a Wisdom (Survival) check to follow tracks, hunt wild game, guide your group through frozen wastelands, identify signs that certain creatures live nearby, predict the weather, or avoid quicksand and other natural hazards.

- Other Wisdom Checks. The GM might call for a Wisdom check when you try to accomplish tasks similar to the above examples: reading body language, understanding someone's feelings, noticing things about the environment, or caring for an injured person.

### Charisma Checks

Charisma checks represent a character's force of personality and social effectiveness. The GM calls for a Charisma check when you attempt to influence, entertain, or otherwise affect the attitudes or behavior of others.

- Deception. A Charisma (Deception) check determines whether you can convincingly hide the truth, either verbally or through your actions. Deception can encompass everything from misleading others through ambiguity to telling outright lies. Typical situations include trying to fast-talk a guard, con a merchant, earn money through gambling, pass yourself off in a disguise, or maintain a straight face while telling a blatant lie.

- Intimidation. When you attempt to influence someone through overt threats, hostile actions, or physical violence, the GM might ask you to make a Charisma (Intimidation) check. Examples include trying to pry information out of a prisoner, convincing street thugs to back down from a confrontation, or using the edge of a broken bottle to convince a sneering vizier to reconsider a decision.

- Performance. A Charisma (Performance) check determines how well you can delight an audience with music, dance, acting, storytelling, or some other form of entertainment.

- Persuasion. When you attempt to influence someone or a group of people with tact, social graces, or good nature, the GM might ask you to make a Charisma (Persuasion) check. Typically, you use persuasion when acting in good faith, to foster friendships, make cordial requests, or exhibit proper etiquette.

## Interactions

- The GM decides which ability check applies to a situation. When a situation fits more than one skill or ability, the GM chooses the most appropriate check or may allow the player to choose with or without disadvantage, as applicable.

- Ability checks interact with other systems: spells, special class features, and environmental modifiers can grant advantage, impose disadvantage, or modify the DC. Any feature that specifies a bonus to a particular check or skill applies to that check when rolled.

- Spellcasting ability is used to determine spell save DCs and spell attack modifiers for classes that rely on that ability. For example, a wizard's spells use Intelligence for those calculations; a cleric's spells use Wisdom; a warlock or bard would use Charisma for their spellcasting calculations (see the class entries for exact formulas and modifiers).

- The GM may call for skill checks in social, exploration, or combat contexts; treat each prompted check as the authoritative mechanical resolution for the described task unless a rule or feature specifies otherwise.
