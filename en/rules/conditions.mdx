# Conditions

Conditions alter a creature’s capabilities in a variety of ways and can arise as a result of a spell, a vocation feature, a monster’s attack, or other effects.  
Most conditions, such as _blinded_, are impairments, but a few, such as _invisible_, can be advantageous.

A condition lasts either until it is countered (the _prone_ condition is countered by standing up, for example) or for a duration specified by the effect that imposed the condition.

---

## Bleeding

A bleeding creature loses hit points from accumulated open wounds.

- At the start of each of its turns, a bleeding creature takes **(Bleeding Counter) × d4** damage.
- After taking this damage, the **Bleeding Counter decreases by 1.**
- The Bleeding Counter **only increases when a weapon, effect, or feature specifically inflicts Bleeding.**
- The Bleeding Counter has no upper limit.
- A creature can use its **action** to attempt to **stanch the bleeding**, making a DC 10 + stacks **Wisdom (Medicine)** check.  
  On a success, it reduces the Bleeding Counter by the creature's Medicine modifier + **1d4.**
- **Magical healing** reduces the Bleeding Counter by the creature's spellcasting modifier (minimum 1), regardless of the amount healed.
- The **Bleeding condition ends** when the Bleeding Counter reaches 0.

---

## Blinded

- A blinded creature **can’t see** and automatically **fails any ability check that requires sight**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **advantage**, and the creature’s own **attack rolls have disadvantage**.

---

## Burning

A burning creature is on fire and takes ongoing heat damage.

- At the start of each of its turns, a burning creature takes **1d10 fire damage.**
- A creature can use its **action** to **extinguish the flames** by dropping prone, rolling, or smothering itself.  
  - Doing so **automatically ends the Burning condition**, and the creature ends its turn **prone.**
- Another creature within 5 feet may also use its **action** to extinguish the flames.
- While burning, a creature sheds **bright light in a 10-foot radius** and **dim light for another 10 feet.**

---

## Charmed

- A charmed creature **can’t attack the charmer** or target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects.
- The charmer has **advantage on ability checks to interact socially** with the creature.

---

## Deafened

- A deafened creature **can’t hear** and automatically **fails any ability check that requires hearing**.

---

## Exhaustion

Some special abilities and environmental hazards, such as starvation or extreme environments, can lead to **exhaustion**.  
Exhaustion is measured in six levels.

| Level | Effect                                         |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Disadvantage on ability checks                 |
| 2     | Speed halved                                   |
| 3     | Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws |
| 4     | Hit point maximum halved                       |
| 5     | Speed reduced to 0                             |
| 6     | Death                                          |

- A creature suffers the effects of its current level of exhaustion as well as all lower levels.
- Finishing a long rest reduces a creature’s exhaustion level by **1**, provided it has consumed food and drink.

---

## Frightened

- A frightened creature has **disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls** while the source of its fear is within line of sight.
- When the creature moves **toward the source of its fear**, each **1 foot of movement costs 5 feet** of its movement.

---

## Grappled

- A grappled creature’s **speed becomes 0**, and it **can’t benefit from bonuses to speed**.
- The condition **ends if the grappler is incapacitated**.
- The condition also ends if an effect **removes the grappled creature from the reach** of the grappler or grappling effect, such as when a creature is hurled away by the _thunderwave_ spell.

---

## Incapacitated

- An incapacitated creature **can’t take actions or reactions**.

---

## Invisible

- An invisible creature is **impossible to see** without the aid of magic or a special sense.  
  For the purpose of hiding, the creature is **heavily obscured**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **disadvantage**, and the creature’s **attack rolls have advantage**.

---

## Paralyzed

- A paralyzed creature is **incapacitated**, can’t move or speak, and **automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **advantage**.
- Any attack that hits the creature is a **critical hit** if the attacker is within 5 feet of it.

---

## Petrified

- A petrified creature is **transformed into an inanimate substance** (usually stone), along with any nonmagical objects it is wearing or carrying.
- The effect takes two rounds to complete.
- The creature is allowed to **repeat the saving throw** to avoid petrification at the end of its next turn. The creature is considered **restrained** while petrification is being applied.
- The creature is **incapacitated**, can’t move or speak, and is **unaware of its surroundings**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **advantage**, and it **automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws**.
- The creature has **resistance to all damage** and is **immune to poison and disease**, although a poison or disease already in its system is suspended.

---

## Poisoned

- A poisoned creature has **disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks**.

---

## Prone

- A prone creature’s only movement option is to **crawl**, unless it **stands up** and ends the condition.
- The creature has **disadvantage on attack rolls**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **advantage** if the attacker is within 5 feet; otherwise, those rolls have **disadvantage**.

---

## Restrained

- A restrained creature’s **speed becomes 0**, and it **can’t benefit from any bonus to its speed**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **advantage**, and the creature’s **attack rolls have disadvantage**.
- The creature has **disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws**.

---

## Slowed

A slowed creature’s body and perception are weighed down by temporal drag or magical interference.

- The creature’s **speed is halved**.
- It takes a **–2 penalty to AC and Dexterity saving throws**.
- The creature **can’t use reactions**.
- On its turn, it can **use either an action or a bonus action**, not both.
- Regardless of the creature’s abilities or magical items, it **can’t make more than one melee or ranged attack** during its turn.
- If the creature attempts to cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action, **roll a d20**. On an **11 or higher**, the spell doesn’t take effect until its next turn, and the creature must use its action on that turn to complete the spell. If it can’t, the spell is wasted.

---

## Staggered

- A staggered creature **can’t take reactions**.

---

## Stunned

- A stunned creature is **incapacitated**, can’t move, and can speak only falteringly.
- The creature **automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws**.
- **Attack rolls** against the creature have **advantage**.

---

## Suffocating

A suffocating creature is **deprived of air** and begins to **die slowly while conscious**.

- A creature can hold its breath for a number of minutes equal to **1 + its Constitution modifier (minimum 30 seconds)**.
- When that time expires, it begins **suffocating**.
- While suffocating, at the start of each of its turns, the creature must make a **death saving throw** (as if dying), even if still conscious.
- The condition **ends immediately** when the creature can breathe again.
- If a suffocating creature **fails three death saving throws**, it dies.

---

## Terrified

A terrified creature is overwhelmed by panic and survival instinct.

- The creature has **disadvantage on ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws** while it has the terrified condition.
- The creature must spend its movement to **actively move away from the source of its fear**, choosing the safest available path if possible.
- The creature **cannot willingly move closer** to the source of its fear by any means.

---

## Unconscious

- An unconscious creature is **incapacitated**, can’t move or speak, and is **unaware of its surroundings**.
- The creature **drops whatever it’s holding** and **falls prone**.
- It **automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws**.
- **Attack rolls** against it have **advantage**.
- Any attack that hits it is a **critical hit** if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.
