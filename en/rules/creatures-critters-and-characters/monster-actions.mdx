# Monster Actions and Combat Rules

## Overview

This section covers mechanics specific to monster action entries: the psionics tag, limited-use notations, automatic grapple on hit, Multiattack, and ammunition assumptions. For general combat actions and attack resolution, see [Actions in Combat](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat) and [Making an Attack](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/making-an-attack).

## Psionics

A monster that casts spells using only the power of its mind has the psionics tag added to its Spellcasting or Innate Spellcasting trait. The tag indicates the creature does not require material, somatic, or verbal components to cast those spells.

## Limited Usage Notation

Many special monster abilities carry a usage limit shown in the stat block:

- **X/Day.** The ability can be used X times. The monster must finish a long rest to regain expended uses.
- **Recharge X–Y.** The ability can be used once; at the start of each of the monster's subsequent turns, roll a d6. If the result falls within the listed range (e.g. 5–6), the ability is regained. It also recharges on a short or long rest.
- **Recharge after a Short or Long Rest.** The ability can be used once, then requires a short or long rest to regain.

## Grapple Rules for Monsters

Many monsters have attacks that grapple automatically on a hit. This differs from the standard rules at [Attack Alterations](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/attack-alterations):

- If a monster's attack text states it grapples the target on a hit, the grapple succeeds automatically — no additional ability check required, unless the attack text says otherwise.
- To escape, the grappled creature uses its action and succeeds on a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against the escape DC listed in the stat block.
- If no escape DC is listed, use 10 + the monster's Strength (Athletics) modifier.

## Multiattack

A creature that can make multiple attacks on its turn has the Multiattack action. It makes the combination of attacks specified by that entry.

- A creature cannot use Multiattack when making an opportunity attack; opportunity attacks are always a single melee attack.
- Follow the Multiattack entry text for which attacks are made and in what order.

## Ammunition

Assume a monster carries enough ammunition to make its ranged attacks. Default quantities when not stated: **2d4** pieces for thrown weapons, **2d10** for projectile weapons such as bows or crossbows.
