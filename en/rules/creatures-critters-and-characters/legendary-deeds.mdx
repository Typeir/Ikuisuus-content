# Legendary Deeds

Legendary creatures possess an innate capacity for extraordinary actions that set them apart from common foes. **Legendary Deeds** unifies all such abilities under a single resource pool.

---

## Core Mechanics

### Deed Pool

A legendary creature has a number of **legendary deeds per round**, specified in its stat block (typically 1-3). It regains all expended deeds **at the start of its turn**.

> **Example**: A creature with "**3 legendary deeds per round**" can spend up to 3 deeds between its turns, regaining all 3 when its next turn begins.

### Deed Cap

The total number of deeds that can be used per round, globally is **capped by the number of player characters** actively engaged in the encounter. If there are fewer PCs than the deed pool of all combatants, reduce the available deeds accordingly.

> **Example**: A creature with 3 deeds faces a party of 2 PCs. It can only use **2 deeds per round** until more combatants enter the fray.

### One Deed Per Turn

A legendary creature can use **at most one legendary deed per turn**. This applies across all deed types—if a creature uses a **Legendary Deed: Act** at the end of one creature's turn, it cannot use another deed until after a different creature's turn ends.
This limit is shared by all creatures, no two legendary deeds can occur within the same turn cycle.
In case of collision between multiple legendary deed users, the creature with the highest proficiency bonus or combat rating acts first.
If there is a further tie, the GM chooses the order, prioritizing player deeds over monster deeds where possible.
This limit does not take into account the cost of the deed used, some _act_ or _stratagem_ deeds may cost multiple deeds to use.

---

## Deed Types

Legendary Deeds are organized into four categories, each representing a different facet of the creature's legendary nature.

### Legendary Deed: Act

**Timing**: At the end of another creature's turn.

Acts represent quick, decisive actions the legendary creature can take outside its normal turn. These often mirror or enhance the creature's regular action economy—movement, attacks, or utility abilities.

- Listed with deed costs (e.g., "Costs 1 Deed", "Costs 2 Deeds")
- Can only be used once at a time
- Represent the creature's reflexive combat prowess

> **Example**: "**Lurch** (Costs 2 Deeds). The creature moves up to 30 feet."

### Legendary Deed: Resist

**Timing**: When the creature fails a saving throw, or at the end of another creature's turn to end all conditions on themselves.

This deed serves to create meaningful choices —does the creature save its deeds for offensive acts, or preserve them to shrug off debilitating effects?
These deeds, unlike others, are limited in usage by a set number of times per rest period (conventionally short), as specified in the creature's stat block.
These deeds conventionally do not exceed 3 uses per rest, to maintain balance and encourage strategic play.

- Typically costs 1 deed.
- Creature can choose to succeed on a failed saving throw
- Some creatures may have enhanced versions that reflect damage or apply conditions

> **Example**: When the creature fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead."

### Legendary Deed: Stratagem

**Timing**: Declared at the end of the creature's turn; resolves at the start of its next turn.

Stratagems are powerful, telegraphed abilities that give players a window to react. They represent the creature's tactical acumen and battlefield control, requiring forethought rather than reflexive action.

- **Declaration**: The creature announces the stratagem at the end of its turn
- **Resolution**: The effect occurs at the start of the creature's next turn
- **Recharge**: Many stratagems recharge only after combat ends
- **Counterplay**: Players have a full round to reposition, use countermeasures, or prepare defenses

> **Example**: "**Consuming Maw** (Stratagem). Declared at end of turn. At the start of next turn, creatures in a 60-foot cone must succeed on a DC 20 Strength save or be swallowed."

### Legendary Deed: Lair

**Timing**: At the start of a round, disregarding initiative.

Lair deeds represent the creature's connection to its environment—the very terrain rising to defend its master. These trigger at a predictable point in combat while still maintaining environmental pressure.

- Trigger at the start of the round, regardless of initiative order
- Cannot use the same lair deed two rounds in a row
- Represent environmental hazards, summoned reinforcements, or terrain manipulation
- Only function while the creature is within its lair

> **Example**: "At the start of the round, the lair floods with poisonous gas. All creatures must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution save or take 4d6 poison damage."

### Legendary Deed: Phase

**Timing**: When a creature drops below a certain threshold on their maximum HP, conventionally in quarters;

- **Wounded (_75%_)**
- **Bloodied (_50%_)**
- **Doomed (_25%_)**
- **Slain (_0%_)**

Phase deeds always cost one legendary deed to activate, and must be used when the creature meets the specified condition.
If the creature is out of legendary deeds when the condition is met, the phase deed must be activated at the start of its next turn when it regains its deeds.
Phase deeds represent the creature's transformation as it nears defeat, unlocking new abilities or altering existing ones.
These phases often reflect narrative beats in the encounter, signaling a shift in tactics or power level.

> “Example": "Bloodied (50% HP): the creature sprouts wings and gains a flying speed of 60 feet.”
