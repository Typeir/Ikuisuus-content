# Mounts and Vehicles

This page documents the rules for mounts, animals, and vehicles used to travel and carry gear. It covers mount statistics, pulling limits, barding, saddles, vehicle handling, and river travel.

## Mechanics

### Mounts and Other Animals

A good mount can help you move more quickly through the wilderness, but its primary purpose is to carry the gear that would otherwise slow you down. The Mounts and Other Animals table shows each animal's cost, speed, and base carrying capacity.

| Mount          |   Cost |  Speed |  Capacity |
| -------------- | -----: | -----: | --------: |
| Camel          |  50 gp | 50 ft. |   480 lb. |
| Donkey or mule |   8 gp | 40 ft. |   420 lb. |
| Elephant       | 200 gp | 40 ft. | 1,320 lb. |
| Horse, draft   |  50 gp | 40 ft. |   540 lb. |
| Horse, riding  |  75 gp | 60 ft. |   480 lb. |
| Mastiff        |  25 gp | 40 ft. |   195 lb. |
| Pony           |  30 gp | 40 ft. |   225 lb. |
| Warhorse       | 400 gp | 60 ft. |   540 lb. |

- An animal pulling a carriage, cart, chariot, sled, or wagon can move weight up to five times its base carrying capacity, including the weight of the vehicle.

- If multiple animals pull the same vehicle, they can add their carrying capacities together.

- Mounts other than those listed (flying animals such as pegasi, griffons, hippogriffs, and similar animals, and even aquatic mounts like giant seahorses) are available in fantasy worlds but are rare and not normally available for purchase. Acquiring such a mount often requires securing an egg and raising the creature yourself, making a bargain with a powerful entity, or negotiating with the mount itself.

### Feed and Stabling

- Feed (per day): 5 cp (provides 10 lb. of feed).
- Stabling (per day): 5 sp.

### Barding (Armor for Animals)

- Barding is armor designed to protect an animal's head, neck, chest, and body. Any armor shown on the Armor table can be purchased as barding.

- The cost of barding is four times the equivalent armor made for humanoids, and it weighs twice as much.

### Saddles

- Military saddle. A military saddle braces the rider, helping keep a seat on an active mount in battle. It gives advantage on any check you make to remain mounted.

- Exotic saddle. An exotic saddle is required for riding any aquatic or flying mount.

### Vehicle Proficiency

- If you have proficiency with a certain kind of vehicle (land or water), you can add your proficiency bonus to any check you make to control that kind of vehicle in difficult circumstances.

### Rowed Vessels and Boats

- Keelboats and rowboats are used on lakes and rivers.

- If going downstream, add the speed of the current (typically 3 miles per hour) to the speed of the vehicle.

- These vehicles can't be rowed against any significant current, but they can be pulled upstream by draft animals on the shores.

- A rowboat weighs 100 pounds, in case adventurers carry it over land.

## Interactions

- Mount carrying capacities and vehicle-pulling rules interact with encumbrance and item weights: when determining whether a mount or vehicle can carry gear, include the vehicle's own weight and any passengers or cargo; animals pulling vehicles may move up to five times their base carrying capacity.

- Multiple draft animals add their carrying capacity when pulling the same vehicle.

- Barding modifies armor cost and weight for animals only: when fitting armor to a mount, use the barding multipliers (cost ×4, weight ×2) and treat the barding as the mount's armor for damage mitigation where appropriate.

- Saddles and military saddles affect rider stability and checks to remain mounted; exotic saddles are required to ride aquatic or flying mounts and may be prerequisite for riding special mount types.

- Vehicle proficiency grants the proficiency bonus to checks to control a vehicle in difficult conditions (rough terrain, stormy seas, sudden maneuvers), and should be used where the GM calls for checks related to vehicle handling.

- Feed and stabling are recurring travel costs for overland journeys with mounts.
