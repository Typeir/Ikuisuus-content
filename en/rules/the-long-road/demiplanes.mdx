# Demiplanes

## Overview

This rule describes demiplanes — small pocket dimensions that exist apart from the primary planes — and the magical methods by which characters can enter them.

## Mechanics

A demiplane is typically a small pocket plane that has been pinched off from the rest of the multiverse or that exists as a nascent, "baby" universe growing in power. A given demiplane can be entered through a single point where it touches another plane.

- Entry point: A demiplane's connection to the wider multiverse is limited to a single contact point with another plane. Travel into the demiplane is possible only at that contact point.

- Plane Shift: In theory, the plane shift spell can carry travelers to a demiplane. Doing so requires tuning the spell to the demiplane's specific planar frequency (often described metaphorically as a "tuning fork"). Acquiring or reproducing the proper frequency is extremely difficult in practice.

- Gate: The gate spell is a more reliable method of travel to a demiplane, provided the caster knows of the demiplane. Gate creates a direct connection that does not require discovering the demiplane's tuning frequency.

## Interactions

- Spells and requirements: plane shift can reach a demiplane only if the caster can determine and reproduce the demiplane's planar frequency; without that frequency, plane shift cannot target the demiplane. Gate bypasses the frequency requirement but requires the caster to know the demiplane's identity or location.

- Knowledge and research: Locating or accessing a demiplane typically depends on planar lore, rituals, artifacts, or NPC knowledge. The rarity of reliable tuning information makes plane-shift-based access uncommon compared to the targeted use of gate or natural entry points.

- Practical considerations: Because a demiplane's access is restricted to a single contact point and because the tuning frequency is hard to acquire, adventuring groups should plan logistics (entry, exit, and contingency) around known gates or documented contact points whenever possible.
