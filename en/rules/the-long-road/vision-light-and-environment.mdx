# Vision, Light, and Environmental Hazards

This section defines how light, vision, and common environmental hazards affect perception and survival. It covers light categories (bright, dim, darkness), obscured areas, falling, blindsight, darkvision, truesight, suffocating and holding one’s breath, and basic food-and-water rules that cause exhaustion when neglected.

## Mechanics

### Light and Vision

- The environment’s illumination falls into three categories: bright light, dim light, and darkness.
- Bright light: most creatures see normally.
- Dim light (also called shadows): areas such as twilight, dawn, moonlight, the soft light of a torch, or patchy light from a distant light source. Dim light is lightly obscured for most creatures.
- Darkness: an absence of light; may be natural (an unlit dungeon) or magical. Darkness creates a heavily obscured area.

In a lightly obscured area (for example: dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage):

- Creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

In a heavily obscured area (for example: darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage):

- The area blocks vision entirely. A creature in that area effectively suffers the blinded condition when trying to see something in that area.

### Blindsight

- A creature with blindsight can perceive its surroundings without relying on sight, within a specific radius.
- Such a creature can perceive creatures and objects within that radius even if they are in darkness or are invisible, provided the effect that grants blindsight does not explicitly say otherwise.

### Darkvision

- Many creatures have darkvision. Within the specified range, a creature with darkvision can see in darkness as if the darkness were dim light.
- A creature with darkvision can’t discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

### Truesight

- A creature with truesight can, out to a specific range:
  - See in normal and magical darkness,
  - See invisible creatures and objects,
  - Automatically detect visual illusions and succeed on saves against them,
  - Perceive the true form of shapechangers or creatures transformed by magic,
  - Perceive the Fold and presences within it.

### Falling

- A fall from a great height deals bludgeoning damage. The creature takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet it fell, to a maximum of 20d6.
- When the creature lands, it lands prone unless it avoids taking damage from the fall.

### Suffocating and Holding Breath

- A creature can hold its breath for a number of minutes equal to 1 + its Constitution modifier (minimum of 30 seconds).
- If a creature runs out of breath or is choking, it can survive for a number of rounds equal to its Constitution modifier (minimum of 1 round) before it begins to suffer the consequences described below.
- At the start of its next turn after those rounds expire, the creature drops to 0 hit points and is dying. It can’t regain hit points or be stabilized until it can breathe again.

### Food and Water

- A character needs one pound of food per day.
- A character can subsist on half rations to make food last longer; doing so has mechanical consequences determined by the campaign (commonly slower healing or other penalties set by the GM).
- Characters who don’t eat or drink suffer the effects of exhaustion (see the exhaustion rules). Exhaustion caused by lack of food or water can’t be removed until the character eats and drinks the full required amount.

## Interactions

- Light categories and senses interact with targeting, Perception checks, stealth, and many spells and class features. For example:
  - Darkvision treats darkness as dim light (lightly obscured) for the creature with darkvision but does not restore color perception.
  - Blindsight bypasses many penalties that rely on sight, but it only works within its stated radius and modality.
  - Truesight overrides many concealment and illusion effects and can perceive ethereal creatures within range.
- Falling damage and landing prone interact with opportunity attacks, grapple checks, and reaction abilities that trigger on landing or on being knocked prone.
- Suffocation and exhaustion from lack of food or water are long-term hazards: they interact with resting rules, magical healing, and effects that remove exhaustion.
