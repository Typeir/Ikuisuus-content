# Pit Traps

## Overview

Pit traps are hidden holes or covered depressions that injure, trap, or expose intruders to secondary hazards. They are typically triggered by a hidden pressure plate and activate when more than 20 pounds of weight is placed on that plate. Pit traps appear in a variety of constructions and commonly include simple holes, covered pits, locked/springloaded covers, spikes, poison, flame effects from statues, and spring‑loaded poison darts.

## Mechanics

- Trigger: The trap activates when more than 20 pounds of weight is placed on the pressure plate.
- Detection: The DC to spot a simple pit is 10. Hidden or camouflaged pits are harder to spot (see variants).
- Cover behavior: Some pits have covers that swing open like a trapdoor; others have covers constructed of material identical to the surrounding floor. A cover can be wedged or otherwise prevented from opening by inserting an iron spike or similar object under the pressure plate or the cover. Wedging an iron spike or other object under the pressure plate prevents the trap from activating.
- Freeing / disabling: A successful DC 20 Strength check is necessary to pry a cover open in some locking pits. A character in the pit can attempt to disable a spring mechanism from inside with a successful DC 15 Dexterity check using thieves' tools, provided that the mechanism can be reached and the character can see it.
- Magical interference: A pit cover can be magically held shut (for example by arcane lock). In some springloaded statue traps, a successful dispel magic (DC 13) cast on the statue destroys the trap.
- Damage from falling: Creatures falling into a pit take falling damage appropriate to the pit's depth (pits are usually 10 or 20 feet deep, though some are deeper). Additional trap-specific damage applies (spikes, poison, fire, darts).

## Disarming and Improvised Mitigation

- Wedging: Wedging an iron spike or similar object under the pressure plate prevents activation.
- Stuffing holes: Stuffing the small nozzle holes that contain poison darts with cloth or wax prevents those darts from launching.
- Breaking the mechanism: Dealing damage to a trap's closure (nets, covers, or mechanisms) can render the trap harmless; example values are given in trap variants below when present.

## Variants

### Simple Pit

- Description: A hole dug in the ground and covered by a large cloth anchored on the pit's edge and camouflaged with dirt and debris.
- Detection: The DC to spot the pit is 10.
- Effect: Anyone stepping on the cover falls through and takes falling damage based on the pit's depth (usually 10 feet, sometimes 20 feet or deeper).

### Hidden Pit

- Description: A pit whose cover is constructed from material identical to the surrounding floor (stone, mortar) and hidden among bas‑reliefs, murals, or frescoes.
- Detection: The DC to spot a hidden pit is 15.
- Investigation: A successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check lets a character deduce the presence of the pressure plate from variations in the mortar and stone used to create the cover, compared to the surrounding floor. A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check discerns an absence of foot traffic over the section of floor that forms the pit's cover.
- Mitigation: Wedging an iron spike or other object under the pressure plate prevents the trap from activating. Stuffing the tiny holes with cloth or wax prevents poison darts from launching (where darts are present).

### Locking / Springloaded Pit (Statue or Mechanical Locking Pit)

- Description: A pit covered by a springloaded trapdoor or locking cover. In some designs a nearby statue (or similar device) is keyed to the pressure plate so that when the plate is triggered the statue breathes fire or the cover snaps shut.
- Activation: When an intruder steps on the hidden pressure plate, the mechanism releases; for statue variants the statue may emit a 30‑foot cone of flame.
- Detection: The DC to spot the pressure plate and faint scorch marks is 15. Detect Magic or similar effects that sense a presence of magic (such as detect magic) reveal an aura of evocation magic around the statue.
- Pry: A successful DC 20 Strength check is necessary to pry the cover open in some locking pits. A successful DC 15 Dexterity check using thieves' tools is necessary to disable the spring mechanism from the inside, provided the mechanism can be reached and seen.
- Magical counter: A successful dispel magic (DC 13) cast on the statue destroys that trap's magical effect.

### Fire‑Breathing Statue Locking Pit

- Activation: When the trap is activated, the linked statue releases a 30‑foot cone of flame.
- Save: Each creature in the cone must make a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw.
- Damage: On a failed save a creature takes 22 (4d10) fire damage, or half as much on a successful save. If the cover is springloaded and snaps shut after a creature falls in, the cover may trap the creature inside.

### Spiked Pit

- Description: A simple or hidden pit with sharpened wooden or iron spikes at the bottom.
- Effect: A creature falling into the pit takes piercing damage from the spikes in addition to any falling damage. Typical spike damage given: 11 (2d10) piercing.
- Poisoned spikes: Nastier versions have poison smeared on the spikes. When poison is present, anyone taking piercing damage from the spikes must make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw.
- Poison damage: On a failed save the creature takes 22 (4d10) poison damage, or half as much on a successful one.

### Poison Darts

- Description: Poison‑tipped darts shoot from springloaded or pressurized tubes cleverly embedded in the surrounding walls when a pressure plate is triggered. The tiny holes in the walls are often obscured by dust, cobwebs, or decoration.
- Prevention: Stuffing the holes with cloth or wax prevents the darts from launching.
- Attack: When the trap activates, an area might include multiple pressure plates, each rigged to its own set of darts. Each dart makes a ranged attack (text: "+8" appears in the source); the darts deal poison‑tipped damage as appropriate for the trap construction.

## Example Values (from source text)

- Spot simple pit: DC 10 to spot the pit.
- Spot hidden/camouflaged pit: DC 15 to spot.
- Investigate pressure plate / deduce mechanism: DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) or DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) to notice absence of foot traffic.
- Prise cover open (locking pit): DC 20 Strength check.
- Disable spring mechanism from inside: DC 15 Dexterity check with thieves' tools.
- Fire cone statue save: DC 13 Dexterity save; damage 22 (4d10) fire on failed save, half on success.
- Poison on spikes save: DC 13 Constitution save; damage 22 (4d10) poison on failed save, half on success.
- Dart attack example: each dart makes a ranged attack with a "+8" (source fragment).
- Activation weight: more than 20 pounds on the pressure plate triggers the trap.

## Interactions

- A character who detects a pit can wedge an iron spike or similar object to prevent activation or to hold a cover closed.
- Arcane effects such as arcane lock can hold a cover shut; conversely, spells that detect or suppress magic (detect magic, dispel magic) interact with statue‑linked or magically held traps (dispel magic DC 13 applied to statue in source).
- Traps that combine effects (spikes + poison, spikes + nets, springloaded cover + statue breath) impose all relevant damage and saves when triggered.

## Notes for the GM

- Pits are versatile: adjust depth, spike damage, poison potency, dart number, and detection DCs to suit the environment and challenge desired.
- When combining multiple effects, resolve falling damage first, then spike damage, then poison or fire effects and associated saving throws as described above.
