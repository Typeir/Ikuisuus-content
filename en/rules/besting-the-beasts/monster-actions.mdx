# Monster Actions and Combat Rules

## Overview
This section explains the rules used in monster stat blocks for special tags, limited-usage abilities, attacks, grappling, multiattack, ammunition assumptions, and the "recharge" notation. It collects the mechanical text that appears alongside monster actions and explains how to resolve those mechanics at the table.

## Psionics
A monster that casts spells using only the power of its mind has the psionics tag added to its Spellcasting or Innate Spellcasting special trait. This tag typically indicates the creature does not require material, somatic, or verbal components to cast those spells. If a creature has no special reaction related to psionics, the psionics subsection is simply absent from its stat block; the tag exists only where the game references it.

## Limited Usage Notation
Many special monster abilities have restrictions on how often they can be used. The stat block uses concise notations to show these limits:

- X/Day. The notation "X/Day" means the special ability can be used X times, and the monster must finish a long rest to regain expended uses. For example, "1/Day" means the ability can be used once, and the monster must finish a long rest to use it again.

- Recharge X–Y. The notation "Recharge X–Y" means a monster can use a listed special ability once and that ability then has a random chance to recharge during each subsequent round of combat. At the start of each of the monster's turns, roll a d6. If the roll is one of the numbers shown in the recharge notation, the monster regains the use of that special ability. For example, "Recharge 5–6" means the monster can use the special ability once; then at the start of each of its turns it regains the use of that ability if it rolls a 5 or 6 on a d6. The ability also recharges when the monster finishes a short or long rest.

- "Recharge after a Short or Long Rest." This notation means the monster can use the special ability once and then must finish a short or long rest to use it again.

## Actions
When a monster takes its action, it chooses from the options in the Actions section of its stat block or it may use one of the actions available to all creatures (for example, the Dash or Hide actions as described in the Player's Handbook). A monster's Actions section lists the attacks and special actions it can take on its turn.

### Melee and Ranged Attacks
Melee and ranged attacks are the most common actions a monster takes in combat. An attack entry may be a spell attack or a weapon attack. The term "weapon" in a stat block can refer to a manufactured item or a natural weapon such as a claw or tail spike.

- Creature vs. Target. The target of a melee or ranged attack is usually either one creature or one object. The difference is that a "target" can be a creature or an object.

### Hit
Any damage dealt or other effects that occur as a result of an attack hitting a target are described after the "Hit:" notation in the attack entry. The stat block typically presents both the average damage and the damage die expression. The user has the option of taking the average damage or rolling the damage dice.

### Miss
If an attack has an effect that occurs on a miss, that information is presented after the "Miss:" notation in the attack entry.

## Grapple Rules for Monsters
Many monsters have special attacks that allow them to grapple prey quickly. The rules for grappling in monster stat blocks are:

- If a monster hits with a special attack that indicates it grapples the target, the monster does not need to make an additional ability check to determine whether the grapple succeeds, unless the text of the attack explicitly says otherwise.

- A creature grappled by the monster can use its action to try to escape. To escape, the grappling creature must succeed on a Strength (Athletics) check or a Dexterity (Acrobatics) check contested by the escape DC listed in the monster's stat block.

- If no escape DC is given in the stat block, assume the escape DC is 10 + the monster's Strength (Athletics) modifier.

## Multiattack
A creature that can make multiple attacks on its turn has the Multiattack action. Multiattack lets a creature make a specific combination of attacks as described by that action. Rules and restrictions:

- A creature can't use Multiattack when making an opportunity attack; an opportunity attack must be a single melee attack.

- The Multiattack action is distinct from individual attack actions listed elsewhere in the Actions section; follow the text of the Multiattack entry to determine which attacks are made and in what order.

## Ammunition and Equipment
A stat block rarely lists a creature's equipment other than armor or weapons used by the monster. When ammunition or thrown weapons are implied, use the following assumptions:

- A monster carries enough ammunition to make its ranged attacks. Assume a monster has 2d4 pieces of ammunition for a thrown weapon attack and 2d10 pieces of ammunition for a projectile weapon such as a bow or crossbow.

- A creature that customarily wears clothes, such as a humanoid, is assumed to be dressed appropriately.

## Interactions
- Recharge rolls occur at the start of the monster's turn; if the recharge roll matches the recharge notation, the ability is regained immediately and may be used later in that turn if the action economy allows.

- Short and long rests restore abilities marked with X/Day when the stat block specifies that rest-based recovery.

- When an attack entry provides both average damage and a die expression, either may be used; choose consistently for simplicity.
