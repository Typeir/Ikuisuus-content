# Weapons and Dice Levels

Weapons are conduits for violence, and their damage is expressed through **dice levels**.  
The dice level of a weapon represents its **manufacture quality and killing potential**.

---

### Important Notes

There is **no separate tracker** for weapon dice levels.  
The **Game Director** is expected to decide what quality of weapon is present in the world.  
If a player acquires a superior weapon, they are expected to **replace** the old one rather than "upgrade" it.
This is a system entirely separate from weapon bonuses (e.g. +1, +2, etc.).

---

## Dice Progression (Quality)

Weapons exist at many different qualities.  
When the Game Director introduces a weapon, choose its place along the dice sequence.

| Dice Sequence |
| ------------- |
| 1d4           |
| 1d6           |
| 1d8           |
| 1d10          |
| 1d12          |
| 2d8           |
| 2d10          |
| 2d12          |
| 4d8           |
| …and so on    |

> **Note**: After reaching the largest single die (d12), the sequence adds more dice instead of increasing die size. This avoids extreme variance.

---

## Weapon Size (Dice Multiplication)

All weapons are assumed **Medium** by default.  
Weapons physically made for larger creatures **multiply the number of dice** they roll.

- Weapons more than one size larger than the wielder require **two hands** regardless of weapon type.
- A creature needs a Strength Score of 18 or higher to wield a weapon one size larger than itself. They do so at disadvantage.
- A creature cannot wield a weapon more than two sizes larger than itself unless it has a trait that explicitly allows it.

| Weapon Size | Dice Multiplier |
| ----------- | --------------- |
| Medium      | ×1              |
| Large       | ×2              |
| Huge        | ×3              |
| Gargantuan  | ×4              |
| Colossal    | ×5              |
| Titanic     | ×6              |
| …and so on  | …and so on      |

---

## Examples

- **Longsword (1d8, Medium)**

  - A superior version might roll **1d10** or **1d12**.
  - A Large version doubles its dice: **2d8** or **2d10**.

- **Greatsword (2d6, Medium)**
  - A superior version might roll **2d8**.
  - A Huge version triples its dice: **6d6**.

---

## Game Director Guidelines

- Weapons do **not** improve themselves. A poor weapon must be **replaced** with a better one if higher quality is desired.
- Apply **quality first, then size**: determine the weapon's dice on the sequence, then multiply for size.
