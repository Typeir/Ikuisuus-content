# Sight Rot

## Overview

Sight Rot is a progressive disease that impairs a victim's vision over time. It worsens with each long rest after symptoms appear and can lead to total blindness if untreated.

## Mechanics

- On each long rest after the symptoms appear, the penalty worsens by 1.
- When the penalty reaches −5, the victim is blinded until its sight is restored by magic such as lesser restoration or heal.

Sight rot can be cured using a rare flower called Eyebright, which grows in some swamps. Given an hour, a character who has proficiency with an herbalism kit can turn the flower into one dose of ointment.

- Preparing the ointment: 1 hour of work while wearing or using an herbalism kit, by a character with proficiency in that kit, converts one Eyebright flower into one dose of ointment.
- Preventing progression: If one dose of the ointment is applied to the eyes before a long rest, that single dose prevents the disease from worsening after that rest.
- Cure by repeated doses: After three doses of the ointment have been applied (on separate occasions as implied by long-rest timing), the ointment cures the disease entirely.

## Interactions

- Magical restoration: The blindness caused when the penalty reaches −5 is removable only by magic that restores sight or removes conditions (examples given: the spells lesser restoration or heal).
- Tool proficiency: Crafting the Eyebright ointment requires proficiency with an herbalism kit; without that proficiency the text provides no alternative crafting method.
- Rest timing: The ointment's preventative effect is time-sensitive—each dose must be applied before a long rest to prevent worsening for that rest.
