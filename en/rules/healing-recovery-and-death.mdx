# Healing, Recovery, and Death

Steel bites, bones break, and blood spills. These rules govern what happens when a combatant drops, how they can be brought back, and what it costs to cheat death.

---

## Being Downed

When a creature is below 1 HP, it falls to the ground and is considered **unconscious**, or "downed".

- The creature is **incapacitated**, cannot move, and can only speak falteringly.
- The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
- The creature drops whatever it is holding and falls **prone**.
- The creature cannot take actions or reactions.
- The creature automatically fails any skill check that requires a roll.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage**.
- The creature loses their Dexterity bonus to AC.

## Overkill

If a creature takes damage that reduces it below 1 HP and there is damage remaining, the creature dies if the remaining damage equals or exceeds half its maximum HP.

## Negative hit points

If a creature takes damage while downed it accrues negative hit points, and automatically fails a death saving throw.
A creature can be stabilized in negative health, but any damage they take thereafter returns them to an **unstable** state.
In order to regain consciousness, a creature must be healed to at least 1 HP.
If negative hit points ever exceed **a creature's maximum HP** they instantly die. 

## Death Saving Throws

When a creature is reduced below 1 HP, it collapses and begins making death saving throws at the start of its turn as normal.

- Three successes = stabilized, the creature rolls 1d4 and regains consciousness after that many hours, they are healed to 1HP thereafter.
- Three failures = death.
- On a roll of 20, the creature regains 1 HP and becomes conscious immediately.

---

## Taking Damage While Downed

When a creature at 0 or less HP takes damage:

- The creature gains **1 failed death saving throw**.
- Critical hits still count as **one failed save**.
- A creature only dies instantly if a single hit deals damage equal to or greater than **half its maximum HP**.

This makes every strike against a fallen foe feel dangerous, but not automatically fatal — leaving space for capture, rescue, or bitter choices.

---

## Attacks Against Downed Creatures

No rolls are needed to hit an unconscious or sleeping creature with a melee attack.
Melee attacks against an unconscious or sleeping creature are **automatic critical hits**.  
Critical damage still counts as only one failed save unless it is enough damage to kill outright (over half their total HP).

> A fallen body cannot guard itself — every blow bites deeper.

---

## Healing from 1 HP

Being dragged back from the brink is not painless.

When a creature is restored to positive HP from 0 or less:

- It **loses its action** until the end of its next turn.
- It may still **move**, take **bonus actions**, and use **reactions** to protect itself.
- If the creature was restored on its own turn, it immediately loses its action but will regain it at the start of their next turn as normal.

> Rising from the dirt is a desperate scramble, not a heroic second wind.

---

## Global Healing Adjustment

All healing spells from 5e heal **double the normal amount** unless otherwise stated.

- Example: _Cure Wounds_ heals 2d8 + spellcasting modifier when cast with a 1st-level slot.
- Fixed healing effects (such as _Lay on Hands_) are not doubled unless the Game Director rules otherwise.

> As the old saying goes, better to prevent than to heal.

---

## Resurrection

Resurrection is impossible in Damocles. Unless stated otherwise, spells such as _Raise Dead_, _Resurrection_, and _True Resurrection_ do not function.
Revivify is the only resurrection spell that works, and it must be cast within 10 minutes of death, on a body that could still operate.
Revivify works more like a defibrillator than a true resurrection spell, bringing the creature back with 1 HP and all the penalties it accrued while below 1 HP.
An arduous (DC 30) Medicine can also bring a creature back, for a minute after their death, and with the same restrictions as the revivify spell.
A creature brought back this way has their Exhaustion set to the fifth level.

> Death is a one-way street in Damocles. Choose your battles wisely.
