# Indefinite Madness

## Overview

This entry provides a d100 table of flaws associated with indefinite madness and the mechanical means to cure such madness. The flaws persist until cured and describe lasting behavioral or cognitive changes.

## Mechanics

The following d100 table lists flaws that an afflicted character may suffer. Each entry lasts until the madness is cured.

| d100 | Flaw |
|------|------|
| 01–15 | "Being drunk keeps me sane." |
| 16–25 | "I keep whatever I find." |
| 26–30 | "I try to become more like someone else I know—adopting his or her style of dress, mannerisms, and name." |
| 31–35 | "I must bend the truth, exaggerate, or outright lie to be interesting to other people." |
| 36–45 | "Achieving my goal is the only thing of interest to me, and I'll ignore everything else to pursue it." |
| 46–50 | "I find it hard to care about anything that goes on around me." |
| 51–55 | "I don't like the way people judge me all the time." |
| 56–70 | "I am the smartest, wisest, strongest, fastest, and most beautiful person I know." |
| 71–80 | "I am convinced that powerful enemies are hunting me, and their agents are everywhere I go. I am sure they're watching me all the time." |
| 81–85 | "There's only one person I can trust. And only I can see this special friend." |
| 86–95 | "I can't take anything seriously. The more serious the situation, the funnier I find it." |
| 96–100 | "I've discovered that I really like killing people." |


### Curing Madness

- A calm emotions spell can suppress the effects of madness temporarily. Use the spell's suppression to prevent the madness from causing its usual behavioural effects while the spell lasts.
- A lesser restoration spell can remove a short-term or long-term madness, returning the character to normal if the madness is of those types.
- Depending on the source of the madness, magic such as remove curse or dispel evil might prove effective; these are situational remedies tied to the madness's origin.
- A greater restoration spell or more powerful magic is required to rid a character of indefinite madness.

## Interactions

- Spells listed above interact with madness as remedies or suppressants; the exact effect depends on the spell description (duration, saving throws, etc.).
- If the madness has an external source (curse, possession, or divine/malign influence), resolving that source (for example with remove curse, dispel magic, or targeted ritual magic) may be required in addition to or instead of restorative spells.
- Suppression (calm emotions) does not cure the condition; it only temporarily prevents behavioural symptoms. Cure requires the spells specified above or more powerful magic in the case of indefinite madness.
