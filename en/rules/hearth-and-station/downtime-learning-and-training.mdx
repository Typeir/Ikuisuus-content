# Downtime: Learning and Training

Between expeditions and conflicts, characters may spend time training or studying. Skills and crafts are not often gained through sudden insight—they usually require time, dedication, and patronage.

---

### Important Notes

- _All rolls are **Intelligence checks**. Only the character's **Intelligence modifier** applies. No spells, items, or abilities may alter these rolls. This is known as a **[RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls)**_

### Learning a Tool, Language, or Weapon Proficiency

A character may train to gain proficiency with a **new set of tools, a language, or a specific weapon**.

- **Duration**: 250 days
- **Cost**: 1 gp per day (total: 250 gp)
- **Requirement**: Access to a competent instructor, tools, or documentation sufficient to teach the craft.

---

#### Exam and Mastery

At the end of training, the character must pass a test of capability.

- [RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls) **Int DC 20**.
- **On failure**: the character may retry after another 250 days of training. Each retry reduces the DC by **5 points**, to a minimum of **DC 5**.

---

### Learning a Skill Proficiency

Learning a new **skill proficiency** is possible but more difficult.

- **Duration**: 250 days
- **Cost**: 5 gp per day (total: 1250 gp)
- **Requirement**: Access to relevant instruction or practice opportunities.

---

#### Skill Training Stages

Skill proficiency is learned in stages:

- After the first 250 days:
  - [RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls) **Int DC 20**.
  - **On success**: gain **half proficiency** (rounded down).
  - **On failure**: the character may retry after another 250 days of training. Each retry reduces the DC by **5 points**, to a minimum of **DC 5**.
- To gain **full proficiency**, the character must train for **an additional 250 days** (total 500 days).
- After the second training period:
  - [RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls) **Int DC 25**.
  - **On success**: gain full proficiency.
  - **On failure**: the character retains half proficiency and may retry after another 250 days of training. Each retry reduces the DC by **5 points**, to a minimum of **DC 5**.

---

### Learning an Armor Proficiency

Armor is a deceitfully complex topic. Most military men take years to blend in with their plate and not exhaust themselves by merely walking around in a tin can. Learning to use one is a Sisyphean endeavor.

- **Duration**: 250 days
- **Cost**: 10 gp per day (total: 2500 gp)
- **Requirement**: Access to relevant instruction or practice opportunities.

---

#### Armor Training

Armor proficiency is scaffolded:

- One must first learn to use **light armor**, then **medium**, then **heavy**.
- After each 250-day period:
  - [RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls) **Int DC 30**.
  - **On failure**: the character may retry after another 250 days of training. Each retry reduces the DC by **5 points**, to a minimum of **DC 5**.

## Other Downtime Activities

Beyond structured learning, characters may engage in a range of downtime activities between adventures. Each activity requires at least **8 hours per day** of dedicated effort; days need not be consecutive.

---

### Crafting

A character with appropriate tool proficiency may craft non-magical objects — adventuring gear, artisan goods, or works of art.

- **Requirement**: Proficiency in the relevant artisan's tools, and access to the required facilities (e.g., a forge for metalwork, a workshop for leatherwork).
- **Output**: Each day of crafting produces progress toward items with a combined market value of **5 gp**. Raw materials equal to half the market value must be expended as you go.
- **Large items**: For items worth more than 5 gp, track progress in 5 gp increments. A suit of Plate (1,500 gp market value) takes 300 days of solo crafting and requires 750 gp in raw materials.
- **Collaborative work**: Multiple proficient characters may combine efforts. Each contributor adds 5 gp of daily progress, provided all have the required proficiency and share access to the same facilities. Three smiths can finish that suit of Plate in 100 days.

---

### Practicing a Profession

A character may work between adventures, drawing income from their trade or vocation.

- Practicing a profession covers the cost of a **modest lifestyle** for each day spent working (negating the normal 1 gp/day expense).
- A character with ties to an organization (temple, thieves' guild, artisan collective) may earn enough to cover a **comfortable lifestyle** instead.

### Recuperating

You can spend downtime recovering from debilitating injuries, disease, or poison.

- After three days of downtime spent recuperating, make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw.
- On a successful save, you can choose one of the following results:
  - End one effect on you that prevents you from regaining hit points.
  - For the next 24 hours, gain advantage on saving throws against one disease or poison currently affecting you.
- If the save fails, you do not gain those benefits; continue to work with the GM on further recovery options.

---

### Other Activities

If a desired downtime activity is not listed here, discuss it with the Game Director. Downtime is intentionally open-ended — researching a forbidden ritual, building a reputation in a city, or commissioning a craftsman all fall within this framework.
