# Trade Goods, Vehicles, and Lifestyle Expenses

## Overview

This section lists trade goods, mounts, tack, drawn and waterborne vehicles, feed and stabling costs, and the lifestyle expense rules used to model basic living costs in the world. It governs prices, item weights, vehicle speeds where provided, and the rules for selecting and paying for a lifestyle over a period.

### Lifestyle Expenses — Rules and Prices

Lifestyle expenses represent the day-to-day cost of living: accommodations, food and drink, and maintenance of equipment so characters remain ready for adventure. At the start of each chosen period (week or month, as determined by the GM or campaign), a character chooses a lifestyle and pays the listed price per day for the duration of that period. The prices below are per day; to compute a 30-day period multiply the daily price by 30.

### Trade and Wealth

- Most wealth in the world is measured in tangible goods: livestock, grain, land, rights to collect taxes, or rights to resources (such as a mine or forest).
- Guilds, nobles, and royalty regulate trade and set prices for goods and services in many regions.
- Chartered companies can be granted rights to conduct trade along specific routes or to operate merchant shipping.
- Merchants commonly exchange trade goods without using currency; the Trade Goods table provides typical barter equivalencies.

### Practical Notes for Play

- When buying mounts, vehicles, or tack, consult the tables for listed cost and weight. Apply local modifiers for rarity, season, or regional scarcity as the GM sees fit.
- Feed and stabling are recurring daily costs that must be paid to avoid penalties (e.g., lost readiness, weakened mounts) as adjudicated by the GM.
- For drawn vehicles and ships, the listed speeds are representative; actual overland or sea travel rates should be resolved using the campaign's travel rules and navigation mechanics.

## Interactions

- Lifestyle choice can affect social interactions and story outcomes: living lavishly might attract attention (positive or dangerous), while living frugally can reduce exposure to thieves and social opportunity.
- The Trade Goods table interacts with bargaining, merchant encounters, and downtime activity rules: goods can be used as currency in regions where guilds permit barter.
- Vehicle costs and weights interact with encumbrance and overland travel rules. A GM should reconcile vehicle carrying capacities and crew requirements with party resources.
- Stabling and feed interact with animal handling, mount fatigue, and long overland journeys — failing to provide feed/stabling can result in reduced mount performance.
