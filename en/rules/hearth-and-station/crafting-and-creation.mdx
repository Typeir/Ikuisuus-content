# Crafting and Creation

## Overview

Characters with artisan's tool proficiencies can produce nonmagical goods during downtime. Crafting requires time, materials, access to appropriate facilities, and the right tool proficiency. These rules cover individual crafting, combined effort, and the relationship between crafting pace and lifestyle expenses.

For the list of artisan's tools and their costs, see [Tools and Trades](/library/rules/hearth-and-station/tools-and-trades).

## Mechanics

### Crafting

You can craft nonmagical objects, including adventuring equipment and works of art.

- **Requirement**: You must be proficient with the appropriate artisan's tools (or other tool proficiency relevant to the object). You might also need access to special materials or facilities (for example, a forge to create a sword or suit of armor).
- **Output per day**: For every day of downtime you spend crafting, you can craft one or more items with a total market value not exceeding 5 gp, and you must expend raw materials worth half the total market value.
- **Progress for expensive items**: If the item's market value exceeds 5 gp, you make progress in 5 gp increments each day until completion.

Examples:

- A suit of plate armor (1,500 gp market value) takes 300 days to craft alone. Raw materials cost 750 gp, paid out over the course of crafting.
- Facilities matter: a character proficient with smith's tools needs access to a forge; lacking proper facilities may prevent crafting or increase the time and cost at the GM's discretion.

### Combining efforts

Multiple characters can combine their efforts toward a single item, provided:

- Each contributing character is proficient with the requisite tools, and
- All characters are working together in the same place with access to the required facilities.

Each contributing character adds 5 gp worth of progress per day.

Example: Three characters with the requisite proficiency can craft plate armor in 100 days (3 × 5 gp = 15 gp per day; 1,500 gp ÷ 15 gp per day = 100 days). The total raw-material cost remains half the market value (750 gp) unless the GM rules otherwise.

### Crafting and lifestyle expenses

While crafting, you can maintain a modest lifestyle without paying the normal 1 gp per day cost. Alternatively, you can maintain a comfortable lifestyle at half the normal cost while crafting.

## Interactions

- Artisan's tool proficiency is required to begin crafting; without it, the character cannot produce the item regardless of access to facilities.
- The GM may rule that rare materials, unique facilities, or exceptional designs alter cost or time requirements.
- For general downtime rules (how days are counted, switching activities, etc.), see [Downtime Activities](/library/rules/hearth-and-station/downtime-activities).
