# Downtime Activities

## Overview

Downtime activities are tasks characters perform between adventures. They require a prescribed number of days of dedicated work to produce their effects; each day counted toward a downtime activity requires at least 8 hours spent on that activity. Days need not be consecutive. The GM may ask what a character does during downtime or declare that time has passed and allow a summary of activities.

## Mechanics

### Downtime Basics

- Each downtime activity requires a certain number of days to complete before a character gains its benefit.
- A day counts if the character spends at least 8 hours on the activity; the days can be nonconsecutive.
- If a character has more days available than an activity requires, the character may continue the same activity for longer or switch to a new downtime activity.
- If the downtime activity desired is not listed in this section, discuss the proposed activity with the GM for adjudication.

### Crafting

Crafting nonmagical goods is a downtime activity available to any character proficient with the relevant artisan's tools. Full rules — output per day, raw material costs, combining efforts, and the interaction with lifestyle expenses — are in [Crafting and Creation](/library/rules/hearth-and-station/crafting-and-creation).

### Lifestyle Expenses

- Between adventures, you choose a particular lifestyle; lifestyles have mechanical effects on how NPCs react and what services are available.
- Living a particular lifestyle has social effects but does not usually change your character's mechanical capabilities beyond those social and cost effects.

### Practicing a Profession

- You can work between adventures, allowing you to maintain a modest lifestyle without having to pay the normal 1 gp per day associated with it while you practice your profession.
- If you are a member of an organization that can provide gainful employment (for example, a temple or a thieves' guild), you may earn enough to support a comfortable lifestyle instead of merely a modest one.
- This professional benefit persists as long as you continue to practice that profession during downtime.

### Recuperating

You can spend downtime recovering from debilitating injuries, disease, or poison.

- After three days of downtime spent recuperating, make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw.
- On a successful save, you can choose one of the following results:
  - End one effect on you that prevents you from regaining hit points.
  - For the next 24 hours, gain advantage on saving throws against one disease or poison currently affecting you.
- If the save fails, you do not gain those benefits; continue to work with the GM on further recovery options.

## Interactions

- GM adjudication: The GM adjudicates access to facilities, special materials, and whether a proposed downtime activity is allowed or requires modification.
- Multiple systems: Downtime crafting interacts with item cost and market-value rules (use market value to determine crafting time and raw-material cost). Effects that prevent a creature from regaining hit points interact directly with recuperation results (recuperation can end a single such effect on a successful save).
- Time passage: When the GM declares that a long period has passed, players may summarize activities rather than track every day; conversely, the GM may require precise accounting when the details matter.

(If a downtime activity not listed here is desired, discuss it with the GM for a ruling.)
