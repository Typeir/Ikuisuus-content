# Mounts and Miles

This section covers transport animals, tack and harness, drawn and waterborne vehicles, feed and stabling costs, and per-mile travel service rates. These tables govern the acquisition, outfitting, and movement costs for any journey where animals or vehicles are involved.

### Per-Mile Travel Costs

| Service                     |          Cost |
| --------------------------- | ------------: |
| Coach / cab (between towns) | 3 cp per mile |
| Messenger                   | 2 cp per mile |
| Road or gate toll           |          1 cp |
| Ship's passage              | 1 sp per mile |

- Coach/cab and messenger rates are charged per mile of travel between settlements.
- Road or gate tolls are paid per crossing at gates or bridges; the listed cost is a typical example.
- Ship's passage is the per-mile embarkation cost for sea travel.
- Travel costs may be added to a hiring contract when services require travel.

## Interactions

- When buying mounts, vehicles, or tack, consult the tables for listed cost and weight. Apply local modifiers for rarity, season, or regional scarcity as the GM sees fit.
- Feed and stabling are recurring daily costs; failing to provide them can result in reduced mount performance or mount fatigue as adjudicated by the GM.
- For drawn vehicles and ships, the listed speeds are representative; actual overland or sea travel rates should be resolved using the campaign's travel rules and navigation mechanics.
- Vehicle costs and weights interact with encumbrance and overland travel rules. A GM should reconcile vehicle carrying capacities and crew requirements with party resources.
- Stabling and feed interact with animal handling, mount fatigue, and long overland journeys.
