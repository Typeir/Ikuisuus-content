# Tools, Trades and Crafts

This page covers trades — the structured bodies of practical knowledge that organise crafting, professional work, and the use of specialised gear across the Lands of Damocles. A trade combines the equipment of a discipline with the trained understanding required to use it. For the rules on producing goods within a trade, see [Crafting and Creation](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/crafting-and-creation).

## Trade Proficiency

A trade represents both the gear of a discipline and the knowledge required to wield it. Proficiency with a trade allows you to add your tier bonus to ability checks made within that discipline. Trade work is not tied to a single ability: the GM determines which ability applies based on the task (for example, a Dexterity check to carve fine detail in Carpentry, or a Strength check to shape iron in Smithing).

Each trade requires its own proficiency. Familiarity with a trade — such as from watching a practitioner work — does not confer proficiency or specialization.

## Trade Specializations

Specializations represent narrow mastery within a trade. Each specialization grants a specific mechanical benefit and may associate with a particular skill.

**Gaining specializations:**

- When you gain **proficiency** with a trade, choose **one specialization** from that trade's list. This choice is permanent.
- When you gain **expertise** with a trade, choose a **second specialization** from that trade's list. This choice is also permanent.
- **Familiarity** with a trade grants no specialization.
- Additional specializations beyond the two above can be gained through downtime training. See [Learning a Trade Specialization](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/downtime-learning-and-training).

Specializations are never lost and cannot be changed after they are chosen.

## Maintaining an Item

Some specializations reference _maintaining_ an item. Unless a specialization states otherwise, maintaining an item works as follows:

Spending 10 minutes during a Repose or Recovery tending to an item counts as maintaining it. Its associated benefit persists until the start of your next Recovery. You can maintain a number of items simultaneously equal to your tier bonus.

## Guidance for GMs

- The GM decides which ability applies to a trade-based task. Treat trades as granting specialised competence in tasks associated with that discipline.
- If a task closely parallels a skill check, use the same ability but allow trade proficiency to apply instead of (or in addition to) skill proficiency only where appropriate.
- Specialization benefits that grant advantage apply whenever the check falls within the specialization's described domain. The GM adjudicates edge cases.
- Do not call for a roll when failure is not meaningful or interesting. Specializations that reference a check assume the GM has decided a check is warranted.
- Crafting: see [Crafting and Creation](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/crafting-and-creation) for rules on producing goods within a trade during downtime.
