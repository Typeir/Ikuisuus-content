# Trades and Tradesmen

This section covers the barter economy of trade goods, the context for wealth and mercantile regulation in the world, the pay rates for hired workers at all skill levels, and the costs and constraints of commissioning spellcasting services.

## Mechanics

### Trade Goods and Barter

Merchants commonly exchange goods without using currency. The following entries show approximate trade equivalencies used for barter and valuation.

- 1 cp = 1 lb. of wheat
- 2 cp = 1 lb. of flour or one chicken
- 1 sp = 1 lb. of iron or 1 sq. yd. of canvas
- 5 sp = 1 lb. of copper or 1 sq. yd. of cotton cloth
- 1 gp = 1 lb. of ginger or one goat
- 2 gp = 1 lb. of cinnamon or pepper, or one sheep
- 3 gp = 1 lb. of cloves or one pig
- 5 gp = 1 lb. of silver or 1 sq. yd. of linen
- 10 gp = 1 sq. yd. of silk or one cow
- 15 gp = 1 lb. of saffron or one ox
- 50 gp = 1 lb. of gold
- 500 gp = 1 lb. of platinum

### Trade and Wealth

- Most wealth in the world is measured in tangible goods: livestock, grain, land, rights to collect taxes, or rights to resources such as a mine or forest.
- Guilds, nobles, and royalty regulate trade and set prices for goods and services in many regions.
- Chartered companies can be granted rights to conduct trade along specific routes or to operate merchant shipping.
- Merchants commonly exchange trade goods without using currency; the barter equivalencies above provide a working reference.

### Hirelings

Hirelings are nonplayer characters hired to perform tasks for adventurers. They fall into broad categories by skill and expected pay. The pay shown is a minimum; expert hirelings or hazardous tasks require higher compensation.

- **Untrained hirelings** — menial workers requiring no particular skill (laborers, porters, maids). Typical pay: **2 sp per day**.
- **Skilled hirelings** — people performing a service requiring proficiency (mercenaries, craftsmen, scribes, carpenters, blacksmiths). Typical pay: **2 gp per day**, though specialists may demand more.
- **Messenger** — paid per mile; typical rate: **2 cp per mile**.

Skilled hirelings include any hired person whose work involves proficiency with a weapon, tool, or specialized skill. Expert hirelings require higher pay than the listed minima.

> Contracts for long-term service often include housing and other perquisites as part of compensation. Contract terms should be negotiated and recorded.

### Spellcasting Services

People who can cast spells do not count as ordinary hirelings. Finding someone willing and able to cast a spell for coin or favor varies by spell level, rarity of the magic, and local availability of spellcasters.

- Common low-level spells (cure wounds, identify) are often available in a city or town. Hiring a caster to perform a 1st- or 2nd-level spell might cost on the order of **10 to 50 gp**, plus the cost of any expensive material components required by the spell.
- Higher-level spells are progressively harder and more expensive to commission. Finding a caster willing to perform a higher-level spell may require travel to a large city, a university, or a prominent temple, and may involve additional costs, favors, or services in exchange.
- A spellcaster may request payment in coin or ask for a service in return — retrieving a rare item, undertaking a dangerous task, or performing a favor tied to the caster's interests.

Spellcasting services are negotiated individually. No fixed rates exist: availability, the caster's reputation, and the spell's level determine price.

## Interactions

- The barter table interacts with merchant encounters, bargaining, and downtime activity rules: goods can be used as currency in regions where guilds permit barter.
- Spellcasting services interact with the material component economy: any expensive material component for a requested spell remains an extra cost the hiring party must provide unless negotiated otherwise.
- Hiring a skilled or expert hireling for a task involving dangerous terrain should account for hazard pay, equipment, and possible loss; these are negotiated and not included in baseline pay.
- Local supply and demand influence all listed costs: remote settlements, wartime conditions, or wealthy courts can raise or lower the practical price of goods, services, and hired labor.
