# Fare and Feasting

This section lists the costs of individual food items, drink, and formal banquets, along with daily meal costs at each lifestyle tier. These prices are included in a character's total lifestyle expenses and apply whenever provisions are purchased separately.

## Mechanics

### Food and Drink

| Item                   |  Cost |
| ---------------------- | ----: |
| Ale (gallon)           | 2 sp  |
| Ale (mug)              | 4 cp  |
| Banquet (per person)   | 10 gp |
| Bread, loaf            |  2 cp |
| Cheese, hunk           |  1 sp |
| Meat, chunk            |  3 sp |
| Wine, common (pitcher) |  2 sp |
| Wine, fine (bottle)    | 10 gp |

- Banquet cost reflects a formal, single meal served to guests; it is a separate expenditure from daily meal costs.

### Meals Per Day

Daily meal costs by lifestyle tier. Included in total lifestyle expenses.

| Lifestyle    | Meals (per day) |
| ------------ | :-------------: |
| Squalid      |      3 cp       |
| Poor         |      6 cp       |
| Modest       |      3 sp       |
| Comfortable  |      5 sp       |
| Wealthy      |      8 sp       |
| Aristocratic |      2 gp       |

## Interactions

- Food and drink item prices are included in a character's total lifestyle expenses; they apply when purchasing provisions individually or when reconciling a character's lifestyle budget.
- Banquet costs apply to formal single meals served to guests and are a separate expenditure from daily meal costs.
- Downtime activities involving hospitality or social maneuvering — hosting dinners, bribing officials with fine wine — use the item prices here as a cost baseline.
