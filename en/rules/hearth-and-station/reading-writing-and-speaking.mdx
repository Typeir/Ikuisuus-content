# Reading Writing and Speaking

In Damocles, communication is key. Whether you're negotiating with a merchant, deciphering ancient texts, or engaging in diplomatic discourse, your ability to read, write, and speak effectively can greatly influence your success.

## Languages

Most inhabitants of Damocles speak **Common**, also known as "Late Empyrean", the lingua franca that facilitates trade and interaction across diverse cultures. However, many regions have their own unique languages and dialects.
Among the languages you might encounter are:

- **Empyrean**: The ancient language of the Empyreans, often used in scholarly texts and rituals. It has a slow, heavy sound with long vowels, dense consonants, and a formal, deliberate rhythm. Empyrean is known for its complex grammar and rich vocabulary, and is divided into several stratas, including:
  - **High Empyrean**: The language spoken by the legendary celestial Empyreans, rare nowadays, and barely understood. Most nobles and scholars have a passing familiarity with it.
  - **Classical Empyrean**: The formal written form used in literature, legal documents, and religious texts, a strata belonging to the late Empire of Thealas.
  - **Late Empyrean**: The common spoken form, which has evolved over time and is the basis for the Common language.
- **Gaean**: The language of the Edaphites, a strong derivation of **Empyrean** with influences from various regional dialects, rough and uneven in sound, it is an eroded and patched up tongue.
- **Hidden Tongues**: A set of interconnected and mutually intelligible languages spoken by the inhabitants of the Hidden Kingdom. While many dialects exist, the folks of the Hidden Kingdom never refer to their languages as separate entities, making studying them all the more difficult. It is a language with clean vowels, very consistent and rhythmic stress, and minimal consonant clusters.
- **Tongue Of Dragons**: An ancient cuneiform script used by terrors and rarely ever found outside their lairs. It is said when debris from beyond the Opaline Belt crashes onto Taiva, the Tongue Of Dragons can be found inscribed on the fragments.
- **Primordial**: The language, or lack thereof, spoken by the Primordial Voids. It lacks a traditional subject structure, instead relying on negation and abstract concepts to convey meaning.
- **Glyphic**: A highly combinatorial language where every symbol is greatly altered by the one before, used primarily by the few Vakis that decide to communicate with the creatures of Damocles.
- **Brumean**: The language of the Brume, an attempt to breach the gap between Gaean and Classical Empyrean, filled with rolling, open vowels, a clear syllabic structure and a bright, direct sound.
- **Borossan**: A highly irregular language consisting of hundreds of loanwords from various other tongues, and a deeply eroded Late Empyrean base. It is known for its clipped, staccato rhythm and heavy use of consonant clusters.
- **Edonian**: The language of the Edonians, deeply influenced by their blade-and-brush culture, its characters are pictorial in nature, often resembling the objects or concepts they represent. It has a fluid, melodic sound with a focus on tonal variations to convey meaning.
- **Aquerontine**: A language spoken in the halls of the Acheron. An oddity among the languages of Damocles, for it is mutually intelligible with Brumean yet it predates it by eons. It strongly resembles an archaic form of Late Empyrean, with guttural consonants and a slow, deliberate cadence.

## Acquiring Languages

Characters begin play with the languages granted by their **race** and **background**. These reflect their upbringing and education. Additional languages may be learned through the downtime [_Learning and Training_](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/learning-and-training) system.

- Your race typically grants **Common (Late Empyrean)** and one regional or ancestral language.
- Your background may grant one or more additional languages — check your background's language entries.
- Languages learned through downtime training follow the standard training rules: 250 days, 1 gp per day, [RR](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/rolling-and-special-rolls) Int DC 20.

Some languages — such as **High Empyrean**, **Primordial**, or **Tongue Of Dragons** — may require special circumstance to learn, such as access to a speaker, a rare archive, or exposure to the source directly. The Game Director adjudicates whether a language can be trained into through standard means.

## Literacy

Speaking a language does not automatically confer literacy in it. Many spoken languages in Damocles have distinct written forms or scripts.

- **Classical Empyrean** uses a highly formal syllabic script requiring years of study. Scholars and clergy are most likely to be literate in it.
- **Edonian** uses pictorial characters that require aesthetic as much as linguistic study.
- **Gaean** is largely oral; written records are rare and non-standardized.

Unless your background specifically grants literacy in a non-Common script, assume your character can read and write only in **Common (Late Empyrean)**. Additional literacy may be acquired through downtime training in the same manner as the spoken language.
