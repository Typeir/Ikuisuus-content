# Character Progression in Damocles

Along their travels through the lands of Damocles and beyond, characters will grow— not just in strength, but in complexity. To live is to change, and to change is to live.

Wielding power in Damocles leaves marks: scars from wielding magicks, forbidden knowledge stolen from ruins, strange skills learned at knife’s edge, or pacts whispered under ruined moons. These changes are not always clean or comfortable, but they are real.

As characters progress, they advance through their **classes**, gain new **features**, and acquire **feats** that represent the lessons, failings, wounds and changes forged during play.

_No one leaves a life of violence and wonder unchanged_.

---

## Leveling Up, Standard Play

- Characters gain **experience points** (XP) per encounter, as determined by the GM and the appropiate calculations if combat occurs. **Milestone leveling** is not contemplated for Damocles.
- On leveling up, characters increase their **vocation level**, gaining new features, spells, hit points, and abilities according to their chosen vocation.

## Mixing

Mixing is vocation levels is common in Damocles, as not every individual can be expressed by a straight path.
Mixing allows characters to gain levels in different vocations.

- A character may choose to take levels in another vocation when they level up, provided they meet the prerequisites.
- **Hit points, features, spells, and abilities stack** according to the mixing rules.
- **Narrative justification is required, at the GM's discretion**.
  - Mixing must reflect the character's actual experiences or development.
  - For example, a fighter becoming a wizard must have studied under someone or discovered relevant knowledge; a wizard becoming a barbarian might reflect a bodily or mental shift from trauma, possession, or revelation.

## Feats and Tier Progression

In Damocles, the **tier bonus** defines mechanical breakpoints and narrative growth alike. To reflect this:

### Additional Feats at Tier Transitions

Whenever a character’s **tier bonus increases**, they gain an **extra feat**. This is in addition to any feats gained through normal ability score improvements (ASIs).

#### Feat Gains by Tier Bonus:

| Tier Bonus | At Level | Feat Gained  |
| ---------- | -------- | ------------ |
| +2         | 4        | 1 extra feat |
| +3         | 7        | 1 extra feat |
| +4         | 10       | 1 extra feat |
| +5         | 13       | 1 extra feat |
| +6         | 16       | 1 extra feat |
| +7         | 19       | 1 extra feat |
| +8         | 22       | 1 extra feat |
| +9         | 25       | 1 extra feat |
| +10        | 28       | 1 extra feat |

Additionally, upon reaching **Level 20**, a character gains an epic boon. At **Level 30**, a character gains a second epic boon in place of a feat.

---

### GM Enforcement

- The GM has final say on whether a feat choice is **plausible and consistent**.
- Players are encouraged to explain how their character gained access to the new feat— through training, discovery, battle, or transformation.
- The choice of feat **must remain consistent with the character’s nature, history, and the world of Damocles**.
- Feats that are **impossible, implausible, or lore-breaking** are not allowed.

## Epic Progression: Beyond Level 20

Most lives end before this point.  
But a select few have shed their fate to **be** more.

**Running games beyond level 20 is extremely demanding.** The power scale shifts dramatically — characters become god-candidates, and the hardships they face or cause must reflect that weight. The GM should not undertake this tier lightly.

Mixing is recommended to survive in the highest levels. To reach level 30 is to become **more** than a mere wizard or warrior — something the world has not seen since the Age of Fates.

## [_Heroic Awakening_](/en/library/rules/creatures-critters-and-characters/heroic-awakening/main) is heavily encouraged for this level of play.
