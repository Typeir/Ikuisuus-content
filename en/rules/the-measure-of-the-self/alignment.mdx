# Alignment

Alignment records a character's moral and ethical orientation as a pair of axes: **Law–Chaos** (relation to order, tradition, and authority) and **Good–Evil** (relation to others' welfare). Together they produce nine positions. Alignment is a character trait, not a constraint — it describes tendency, not compulsion.

In Damocles, alignment carries mechanical weight. Certain items, features, and entities are alignment-locked: they function only for characters of matching alignment, or impose consequences on those who violate their nature.

---

## The Nine Alignments

### Lawful Good (LG)

Acts within structure to protect and uplift others. Honors codes and institutions that serve justice. Will break unjust law only as a last resort.

### Neutral Good (NG)

Does what is good without strong preference for or against structured systems. Cooperates with laws when they help, ignores them when they obstruct.

### Chaotic Good (CG)

Places individual freedom and conscience above order. Acts with kindness and generosity but resists authority, tradition, or hierarchy.

### Lawful Neutral (LN)

Operates by code, tradition, or personal principle without strong commitment to good or evil. The law — whether external or self-imposed — is the anchor.

### True Neutral (N)

Neither strongly ordered nor disordered, neither strongly altruistic nor predatory. Pragmatism, balance, or simple indifference. Many creatures that lack a reflective moral life fall here.

### Chaotic Neutral (CN)

Prizes freedom above all: personal whim, impulse, and self-determination over any external claim. Not cruel by design, but ungovernable.

### Lawful Evil (LE)

Pursues self-interest and power through structure: codes, hierarchies, contracts. Methodical, patient, reliable in the sense that they honor obligations when it serves them.

### Neutral Evil (NE)

Pursues self-interest without the discipline of law or the abandon of chaos. Takes what can be taken. No institutional loyalty, no guiding creed.

### Chaotic Evil (CE)

Driven by destructive impulse: violence, domination, ruin. Resists all restraint, including its own long-term interest.

## Alignment by Nature

Most creatures choose alignment through behavior and disposition. Some beings carry alignment as essence rather than choice — entities sufficiently ancient, abstract, or cosmologically defined that their alignment is constitutive of what they are.

Attempting to change the alignment of such a being through mundane means fails. Their alignment cannot be shifted by charm, persuasion, or re-education; it can only be altered by events of cosmological significance.

---

## Alignment and Mechanics

The following mechanical effects reference alignment:

- **Alignment-locked items** — some weapons, armor, and relics specify an alignment requirement in their properties. A character whose alignment does not match cannot attune to or activate the item, and may suffer consequences for attempted use.
- **Alignment-locked features** — certain class features, oaths, and pacts impose alignment requirements as prerequisites or ongoing conditions.
- **Detect alignment effects** — spells and abilities that detect the ethical nature of creatures reference the alignment track; celestials, fiends, and sufficiently essence-bound entities always register.

---

## Tracking Alignment

Alignment is recorded on the character sheet. The Game Director may shift a character's alignment one step along either axis as a consequence of sustained behavior that contradicts their stated alignment. A single act does not force a shift; a pattern does.
