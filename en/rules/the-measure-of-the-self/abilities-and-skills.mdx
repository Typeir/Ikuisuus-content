# Abilities and Skills

Every creature in Damocles has six ability scores that define its physical and mental capacity. Skills represent narrower domains of expertise derived from those scores.

---

## The Six Ability Scores

Each score is a number representing the magnitude of that capability. A score of 10–11 is the human average. Player characters can reach 20; monsters and exceptional beings can reach 30.

The ability **modifier** is what you actually add to rolls. To derive it without a table: subtract 10 from the score, then divide by 2 rounding down.

$$\text{Modifier} = \left\lfloor \frac{\text{Score} - 10}{2} \right\rfloor$$

| Score | Modifier |
| ----: | :------: |
| 1     | −5       |
| 2–3   | −4       |
| 4–5   | −3       |
| 6–7   | −2       |
| 8–9   | −1       |
| 10–11 | +0       |
| 12–13 | +1       |
| 14–15 | +2       |
| 16–17 | +3       |
| 18–19 | +4       |
| 20–21 | +5       |
| 22–23 | +6       |
| 24–25 | +7       |
| 26–27 | +8       |
| 28–29 | +9       |
| 30    | +10      |

---

### Strength

Bodily power, athletic training, raw force. Covers lifting, pushing, climbing, jumping, and swimming against resistance.

Carrying capacity is your **Strength score × 15** in pounds—high enough that it rarely constrains play.

Attack rolls and damage with melee weapons and thrown weapons use the Strength modifier.

### Dexterity

Agility, reflexes, balance, and fine motor control. Used for ranged attack rolls, armor class (light and medium armor), and initiative.

### Constitution

Endurance, health, and vital resilience. No dedicated skills are tied to it, but the GM may call for a Constitution check when a character attempts to push beyond normal physical limits—holding breath, marching without rest, resisting extreme cold, going without food or water.

Your **Constitution modifier** is added to each Hit Die you roll when determining hit points. If your Constitution modifier changes, your hit point maximum adjusts retroactively as though the new modifier had always been in effect.

### Intelligence

Reasoning, memory, and education. Used as the spellcasting ability for **Wizards**.

### Wisdom

Attunement to the world, perceptiveness, and intuition. Used as the spellcasting ability for **Clerics, Druids, and Rangers**.

### Charisma

Force of personality, confidence, and social presence. Used as the spellcasting ability for **Bards, Sorcerers, and Warlocks**.

---

## Skills

A skill is a focused application of an ability score. When a character is **proficient** in a skill, they add their proficiency bonus to ability checks that use it.

### Strength

- **Athletics** — Climbing hazardous surfaces, jumping unusual distances, swimming against currents, forcing open locked doors, breaking restraints, or any feat of brute exertion.

### Dexterity

- **Acrobatics** — Balance, tumbling, staying upright during treacherous movement, or performing acrobatic stunts.
- **Sleight of Hand** — Pickpocketing, palming objects, concealing items on the body, or fine manual tasks requiring deft fingers.
- **Stealth** — Moving without being detected, hiding, and shadowing targets without alerting them.

### Intelligence

- **Arcana** — Lore about spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, magical traditions, planar cosmology, and their inhabitants.
- **History** — Knowledge of historical events, legendary figures, ancient kingdoms, past disputes, and lost civilizations.
- **Investigation** — Searching for clues, making deductions from evidence, locating hidden objects, or reasoning out how something happened.
- **Nature** — Lore about terrain, flora, fauna, weather, natural cycles, and survival in the wild.
- **Religion** — Knowledge of deities, rites and prayers, religious hierarchies, holy symbols, and the practices of cults.

### Wisdom

- **Animal Handling** — Calming animals, controlling mounts during risky maneuvers, or reading an animal's intentions.
- **Insight** — Reading a creature's true intentions, detecting deception, and gleaning meaning from body language and behavior.
- **Medicine** — Stabilizing dying companions and diagnosing illness.
- **Perception** — Detecting the presence of things—sounds, movement, hidden creatures, concealed objects, or anything your senses might register.
- **Survival** — Tracking, hunting, navigating wilderness, identifying creature signs, predicting weather, and avoiding natural hazards.

### Charisma

- **Deception** — Hiding the truth—through misdirection, ambiguity, impersonation, or outright lies.
- **Intimidation** — Influencing others through threat, hostile presence, or the credible promise of force.
- **Performance** — Entertaining an audience through music, dance, acting, or storytelling.
- **Persuasion** — Influencing through tact, social grace, reasoned argument, or appeals made in good faith.

## Variant: Skills with Different Abilities

The GM may apply a skill to an unusual ability when the situation calls for it. Proficiency applies the same way it would for the normal pairing.

Examples:
- A long-distance swim against dangerous currents might call for **Constitution (Athletics)** rather than Strength—stamina over raw power.
- A barbarian trying to frighten an enemy through sheer physical display might call for **Strength (Intimidation)** rather than Charisma.

When the GM names an unusual pairing, treat proficiency identically to the standard pairing: add the proficiency bonus if you are proficient in the named skill.
