# Ability Checks and Passive Perception

## Overview

This page collects the rules and examples for Passive Wisdom (Perception), Intelligence checks (and their specialties), Constitution checks, and how Constitution affects hit points. It reproduces the mechanical guidance and examples used by the GM to resolve perception, recall, endurance, and related tasks.

## Mechanics

### Passive Wisdom (Perception)

When you hide, there is a chance someone will notice you even if they aren't actively searching. To determine whether such a creature notices you, the GM compares your Dexterity (Stealth) check with that creature's passive Wisdom (Perception) score. A creature's passive Wisdom (Perception) equals:

- 10 + the creature's Wisdom modifier, plus any other bonuses or penalties (for example, proficiency in Perception or situational modifiers).

If the creature has advantage on Perception checks, add 5 to the passive score. For disadvantage, subtract 5.

For example, if a 1st-level character (with a proficiency bonus of +2) has a Wisdom of 15 (a +2 modifier) and proficiency in Perception, he or she has a passive Wisdom (Perception) of 14.

The GM may allow a hidden character to remain unnoticed when approaching a creature that is distracted; in such circumstances the GM might allow you to stay hidden as you approach, granting you advantage on an attack roll before you are seen.

### Intelligence Checks

An Intelligence check comes into play when you need to draw on logic, education, memory, recall, and deductive reasoning. Intelligence checks model the ability to recall facts, recognize patterns, and reason through problems.

Specialized Intelligence checks and typical uses:

- Arcana. An Intelligence (Arcana) check measures your ability to recall lore about spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, magical traditions, the planes of existence, and the inhabitants of those planes.
- History. An Intelligence (History) check measures your ability to recall lore about historical events, legendary people, ancient kingdoms, past disputes, recent wars, and lost civilizations.
- Investigation. When you look around for clues and make deductions based on those clues, you make an Intelligence (Investigation) check. For example, you might deduce the location of a hidden object or determine from the appearance of a wound what kind of weapon dealt it.
- Nature. An Intelligence (Nature) check measures your ability to recall lore about terrain, plants and animals, the weather, and natural cycles.
- Religion. An Intelligence (Religion) check measures your ability to recall lore about deities, rites and prayers, religious hierarchies, holy symbols, and the practices of secret cults.

Other Intelligence checks: The GM might call for an Intelligence check when you try to accomplish tasks that require recall, logic, or specialized knowledge not covered by a specific skill.

### Constitution Checks

Constitution measures health, stamina, and vital force. Constitution checks are uncommon and model attempts to push beyond normal limits or to resist physical hardship. A Constitution check can represent an attempt to accomplish tasks that require endurance or an exertion of physical resilience.

The GM might call for a Constitution check when you try to accomplish tasks like the following:

- Hold your breath.
- March or labor for hours without rest.
- Go without sleep.
- Survive without food or water.
- Quaff an entire stein of ale in one go.

Constitution checks are appropriate when the task involves an immediate physical strain rather than a passive, long-term endurance tracked by other systems.

### Hit Points and Constitution

Your Constitution modifier contributes to your hit points. Typically, you add your Constitution modifier to each Hit Die you roll when determining your hit points.

If your Constitution modifier changes, your hit point maximum changes as though the new modifier had always been in effect. ## Interactions

- Passive Perception interacts directly with Dexterity (Stealth) checks: the GM compares one character's active Stealth roll against a creature's passive Perception.
- Advantage and disadvantage on Perception checks modify the passive score by ±5.
- Intelligence skill specializations (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion) determine which lore or deduction is appropriate for a task and therefore which modifier or proficiency applies.
- Constitution checks and the contribution of Constitution to hit points affect a character's survivability and endurance; changes to Constitution modifier retroactively alter maximum hit points as described above.
