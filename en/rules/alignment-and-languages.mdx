# Alignment and Languages

## Overview

This page explains alignment categories, how alignment relates to creatures, and how languages are chosen and used. It also documents the Inspiration rule as presented in the source text. The sections below reproduce the mechanical guidance and tables from the original SRD material.

## Mechanics

### Alignment

Alignment is a moral choice for many thinking creatures and describes broad tendencies that guide behavior.

- Lawful Neutral (LN): Individuals act in accordance with law, tradition, or personal codes. Many monks and some wizards are lawful neutral.
- Neutral (N): Those who prefer to steer clear of moral questions and don’t take sides, doing what seems best at the time. Lizardfolk, most druids, and many humans are neutral.
- Chaotic Neutral (CN): Creatures who follow their whims, holding personal freedom above all else. Many barbarians and rogues, and some bards, are chaotic neutral.
- Lawful Evil (LE): Creatures that methodically take what they want within codes of tradition, loyalty, or order. Devils, blue dragons, and hobgoblins are lawful evil.
- Neutral Evil (NE): Creatures who do whatever they can get away with, without compassion or qualms. Many drow, some cloud giants, and goblins are neutral evil.
- Chaotic Evil (CE): Creatures who act with arbitrary violence, spurred by greed, hatred, or bloodlust. Demons, red dragons, and orcs are chaotic evil.

Some creatures lack the capacity for rational thought and are unaligned; such creatures (for example, many beasts) are incapable of making moral or ethical choices and act according to instinct.

Alignment is an essential part of the nature of celestials and fiends: a devil is lawful evil in essence rather than merely choosing to be lawful evil.


### Languages

Your character’s race indicates the languages your character can speak by default, and your background might grant access to one or more additional languages of your choice. Note languages on your character sheet.

Choose your languages from the Standard Languages table, or choose one that is common in your campaign. With your GM’s permission, you can instead choose a language from the Exotic Languages table or a secret language such as thieves’ cant or the tongue of druids.

Some languages are families of related dialects. For example, the Primordial language includes the Auran, Aquan, Ignan, and Terran dialects, one for each elemental plane. Creatures that speak different dialects of the same language can communicate with one another.

### Inspiration

Inspiration is a rule the game master can use to reward you for playing your character in a way that's true to his or her personality traits, ideal, bond, and flaw. By using inspiration, a character can draw on a personality trait to gain an edge in social situations (for example, negotiating with a Beggar Prince). Inspiration can also let a character call on a bond (for example, the defense of a home village) to push past the effect of a spell that's been laid on them.

(Original SRD text: Inspiration is awarded by the GM for playing character traits, ideals, bonds, and flaws; it can be spent to reflect those traits in mechanical outcomes.)


## Interactions

- Alignment interacts with creature nature and the cosmology of celestials and fiends: some beings are aligned by essence rather than choice.
- Languages interaction notes: dialects within a language family allow mutual understanding; choosing languages can depend on race, background, campaign common tongues, and GM permission for exotic or secret languages.
- Inspiration interacts with roleplaying and other mechanical effects by providing situational benefit when the GM permits its use; the source examples illustrate social and defensive usages but do not prescribe a precise mechanical effect in the quoted text.
