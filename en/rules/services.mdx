# Services

## Overview

This page lists common services, hireling pay, and guidance for hiring spellcasters. It reproduces the mechanical costs and categories adventurers use when budgeting for travel, lodging, provisions, and hired help.

## Mechanics

### Services and listed costs

| Service | Cost | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Banquet (per person) | 10 gp | Formal, single meal served to guests |
| Bread, loaf | 2 cp | Simple staple food |
| Cheese, hunk | 1 sp | Single serving hunk |
| Meat, chunk | 3 sp | Fresh chunk of meat |
| Wine, common (pitcher) | 2 sp | Table wine, common quality |
| Wine, fine (bottle) | 10 gp | Fine imported bottle |
| Coach / cab (between towns) | 3 cp per mile | Cost for travel by coach between settlements |
| Messenger | 2 cp per mile | Per-mile charge to send messages or small parcels |
| Road or gate toll | 1 cp | Typical toll fee at a gate or bridge |
| Ship's passage | 1 sp per mile | Per-mile embarkation cost for sea travel |

### Inn stay (per day)

- **Squalid:** 7 cp — minimal shelter, poor conditions.
- **Poor:** 1 sp — very basic lodging.
- **Modest:** 5 sp — reasonable room and board.
- **Comfortable:** 8 sp — nicer accommodations and service.
- **Wealthy:** 2 gp — high-quality rooms, good service.
- **Aristocratic:** 4 gp — luxury suites and attendants.

> Note: the pay listed above is a baseline. Local prices may vary by region and season.

### Meals (per day)

- **Squalid:** 3 cp — minimal rations included with the poorest lodgings.
- **Poor:** 6 cp — slightly better meals than squalid.
- **Modest:** 3 sp — daily meals for modest living.
- **Comfortable:** 5 sp — better food and variety.
- **Wealthy:** 8 sp — fine dining daily.
- **Aristocratic:** 2 gp — lavish daily meals with multiple courses.

### Hirelings: categories and pay

Hirelings are nonplayer characters hired to perform tasks for adventurers. They fall into broad categories by skill and expected pay. The pay shown is a minimum; experts or hazardous tasks require higher compensation.

- **Untrained hirelings** — menial workers who require no particular skill (laborers, porters, maids). Typical pay: **2 sp per day**.
- **Common (ordinary) hirelings** — people with ordinary skills used in towns (e.g., general labor). Pay varies by role and task.
- **Skilled hirelings** — people who perform a service requiring proficiency (mercenaries, craftsmen, scribes, carpenters, blacksmiths). Typical pay: **2 gp per day** for skilled mercenary/worker, though specialists may demand more. Skilled hirelings performing expert work often require higher rates.
- **Messenger** — paid per mile; typical: **2 cp per mile**.

Skilled hirelings include any hired person whose work involves proficiency with a weapon, tool, or specialized skill (a mercenary soldier, an artisan, a scribe, a blacksmith, etc.). Expert hirelings require higher pay than listed minima.

Some example service-pay pairs (summary of categories above):

- Untrained: 2 sp per day (menial labor)
- Modest: ~3 sp per day (service-level workers)
- Comfortable: ~5 sp per day
- Wealthy: ~8 sp per day
- Aristocratic: ~2 gp per day

> These values are minimum guidelines. Contracts for long-term service often include housing and other perquisites as part of compensation.

### Spellcasting services

People who can cast spells do not count as ordinary hirelings. Finding someone willing and able to cast a spell for coin or favor varies by spell level, rarity of the magic, and local availability of spellcasters.

- Common low-level spells (examples: cure wounds, identify) are often available in a city or town. Hiring a caster to cast a 1st- or 2nd-level spell might cost on the order of **10 to 50 gp**, plus the cost of any expensive material components required by the spell.
- Higher‑level spells are progressively harder and more expensive to commission. Finding a caster able and willing to cast a higher‑level spell may require travel to a large city, a university, or a prominent temple, and may involve additional costs, favors, or services in exchange.
- A spellcaster who provides a service may request payment in coin or may ask for a service in return (for example, retrieving a rare item, undertaking a dangerous task, or performing a favor tied to the caster's interests).

Spellcasting services are negotiated individually. No fixed national rates exist: availability, the caster's reputation, and the spell's level determine price.

### Examples and guidance

- Hiring a wizard to craft an elaborate chest and miniature replica for use with a secret-chest spell is an example of a specialized service tied to a spell effect; pay and material costs vary and should be negotiated.
- A fighter commissioning a blacksmith to forge a special sword, or a bard paying a tailor to create exquisite clothing for a performance, are ordinary service transactions; the pay shown represents a baseline.
- Mercenary soldiers hired to fight or aid in military ventures are hirelings; their pay should reflect the danger and the required skill.

## Reproduction of procedural notes and constraints

- The pay shown in the above tables and lists is a guideline and a minimum. Some expert hirelings and specialists require more pay depending on rarity of skill or the danger involved.
- Hirelings under long-term contracts often receive housing and lodgings as part of compensation; the contract terms should be negotiated and recorded.
- Travel costs (coach/cab, messenger rates, ship passage) are typically charged per mile and may be added to a hiring contract when services require travel.
- Road or gate tolls are paid per passage and are listed as a separate small cost (typical example: **1 cp** per toll crossing).

## Interactions

- Spellcasting services interact with the magic component economy: any expensive material component for a requested spell remains an extra cost the hiring party must provide unless negotiated otherwise.
- Hiring a skilled or expert hireling for a task that requires travel through dangerous terrain should account for hazard pay, equipment, and possible loss; these are negotiated and not included in baseline pay.
- Local supply and demand influence listed costs: remote settlements, wartime conditions, or wealthy courts can raise or lower the practical price of services.
