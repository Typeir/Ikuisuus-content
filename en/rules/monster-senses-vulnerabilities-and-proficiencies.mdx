# Monster Senses, Vulnerabilities, Resistances, Immunities, and Proficiencies

## Overview

This page collects the mechanical rules for monster senses (darkvision, blindsight, tremorsense, truesight, telepathy), how vulnerabilities, resistances, and immunities function for creatures, language listings, and assumptions about a creature's proficiencies with armor, weapons, and tools. These provisions apply to monster stat blocks and describe how the DM resolves sensing and damage interactions.

## Mechanics

### Vulnerabilities, Resistances, and Immunities

- Some creatures have vulnerability, resistance, or immunity to certain damage types. Apply vulnerability, resistance, or immunity when damage of the specified type is being resolved.
- Particular creatures can be resistant or immune to damage from nonmagical attacks. A "magical attack" for this purpose is an attack delivered by a spell, a magic item, or another magical source.
- Some creatures are immune to certain conditions; apply condition immunity when a condition would be imposed.

### Tremorsense

- A monster with tremorsense can detect and pinpoint the origin of vibrations within a specific radius, provided that the monster and the source of the vibrations are in contact with the same ground or substance.
- Tremorsense cannot be used to detect flying or incorporeal creatures.
- Many burrowing creatures (for example, ankhegs and umber hulks) have this special sense.

### Truesight

- A monster with truesight can see in normal and magical darkness, see invisible creatures and objects, automatically detect visual illusions and succeed on saving throws against them, and perceive the original form of a shapechanger or a creature transformed by magic.
- Additionally, a monster with truesight can see into the Ethereal Plane within the same range as its truesight.

### Senses and Passive Perception

- The Senses entry in a monster's stat block lists its passive Wisdom (Perception) score and any special senses the monster possesses.
- Special senses are described in the stat block below the Senses heading. Use those descriptions to resolve perception checks and sensory capabilities.

### Blindsight

- A monster with blindsight can perceive its surroundings without relying on sight, within a specific radius.
- Creatures without eyes, such as grimlocks and gray oozes, typically have this special sense. Creatures with echolocation or heightened senses (such as bats and true dragons) often have blindsight as well.
- The radius of a creature's blindsight defines the maximum range of that perception.

### Darkvision

- A monster with darkvision can see in the dark within a specific radius.
- The monster can see in dim light within that radius as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light.
- The monster can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
- Many creatures that live underground have this special sense.

### Telepathy

- Telepathy is a magical ability that allows a monster to communicate mentally with another creature within a specified range.
- The contacted creature doesn't need to share a language with the monster to communicate telepathically, but it must be able to understand at least one language.
- A creature without telepathy can receive and respond to telepathic messages but can't initiate or terminate a telepathic conversation.
- A telepathic monster can initiate or terminate telepathic contact without using an action, but while the monster is incapacitated it cannot initiate contact and any current contact is terminated.
- Telepathic contact is broken when the two creatures are no longer within range of one another or if the telepathic monster contacts a different creature within range.
- A telepathic monster can initiate or terminate a telepathic conversation without using an action; incapacitation or range changes end the contact immediately.
- If a creature is within the area of an antimagic field or any location where magic doesn't function, that creature can't send or receive telepathic messages.

### Languages

- The languages a monster can speak are listed in its stat block in alphabetical order.
- Sometimes a monster can understand a language but cannot speak it; this is noted in its entry.
- A dash "—" in the languages line indicates that a creature neither speaks nor understands any language.

### Armor, Weapon, and Tool Proficiencies

- Assume that a creature is proficient with the armor, weapons, and tools it is presented with in its stat block. If you change a creature's equipment, decide whether the creature is proficient with its new equipment.
- Example: a hill giant typically wears hide armor and wields a greatclub. If equipped with chain mail and a greataxe instead, the DM decides whether the giant is proficient with both, one, or neither.
- See the Player's Handbook for rules on using armor or weapons without proficiency; apply those rules when required.

## Interactions

- Damage resistances, vulnerabilities, and immunities interact with other effects that alter damage (spells, magical items, class features). Resolve the base damage, then apply resistances, vulnerabilities, and immunities as instructed by the effect ordering rules.
- Senses interact with conditions and positioning: tremorsense requires shared contact with the ground or substance; truesight ignores darkness and many illusion effects; blindsight bypasses concealment that only affects sight.
- Telepathy is magical and is blocked or suppressed by antimagic effects; it does not, by itself, grant language comprehension beyond requiring the target to understand at least one language.
- When adjudicating monster perception (passive or active), include special senses and apply any applicable immunity or condition modifiers.
