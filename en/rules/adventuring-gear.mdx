## Overview

This page reproduces the weapon tables and selected adventuring-gear rules from the SRD. It lists simple and martial melee and ranged weapons with cost, damage, damage type, weight, and properties, and it reproduces the mechanical descriptions for alchemical items and other gear excerpts present on the source page.

### Improvised and Ranged Use Notes

- When you use an item that is not a weapon to make a melee attack, treat it as an improvised weapon. If the item closely resembles an actual weapon, the DM may decide to treat it as that weapon.
- Several thrown items in the tables specify ranges in the form X/Y (e.g., 20/60). The first number is the normal range; attacks beyond that up to the second number are made with disadvantage.

### Adventuring Gear (selected items and rules reproduced)

#### Acid

- As an action, you can splash the contents of this vial onto a creature within 5 feet of you or throw the vial up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. In either case, make a ranged attack against a creature or object, treating the acid as an improvised weapon. On a hit, the target takes 2d6 acid damage.

- (Mechanic reproduced verbatim: acid is handled as a thrown improvised weapon; damage on hit is 2d6 acid.)

#### Alchemist's Fire

- This sticky, adhesive fluid ignites when exposed to air. As an action, you can throw this flask up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. Make a ranged attack against a creature or object, treating the alchemist's fire as an improvised weapon.

- On a hit, the target takes 1d4 fire damage at the start of each of its turns. A creature can end this damage by using its action to make a DC 10 Dexterity check to extinguish the flames.

#### Antitoxin

- A creature that drinks this vial of liquid gains advantage on saving throws against poison for 1 hour. It confers no benefit to undead or constructs.

#### Arcane Focus

- An arcane focus is a special item—an orb, a crystal, a rod, a specially constructed staff, a wandlike length of wood, or some similar item—designed to channel the power of arcane spells. (Listed here to preserve the item category and its mechanical purpose as a focus for spellcasting.)

## Interactions

- Items thrown as improvised weapons (acid, alchemist's fire) use ranged attack rules for improvised weapons. If an improvised weapon closely resembles a weapon listed in the tables, the DM may allow treating it as that weapon for damage and properties.
- Alchemist's fire causes ongoing fire damage that can be ended by a creature's action through a Dexterity check (DC 10) to extinguish; this interacts with effects that grant resistance to fire or immunity to conditions that cause ongoing damage.
- Antitoxin grants conditional advantage on poison saves for 1 hour; it specifically notes no effect on undead or constructs.
