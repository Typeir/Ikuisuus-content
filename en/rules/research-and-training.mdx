# Research and Training

## Overview

This rule covers downtime activities for learning information (Research) and gaining new practical abilities (Training). Both activities occur during time between adventures and require days of downtime, expenditures, and GM adjudication.

## Mechanics

### Research

Research is an activity undertaken between adventures to gain insight into mysteries encountered during a campaign. Research can include, but is not limited to:

- poring over dusty tomes and crumbling scrolls in a library
- visiting locations that hold relevant information
- buying drinks for locals to pry rumors and gossip from their lips

When you begin your research, the GM determines the following before play proceeds:

1. Whether the information is available at all.
2. How many days of downtime it will take to find the information.
3. Whether there are any restrictions on your research (for example, needing to seek out a specific individual, tome, or location).
4. Whether one or more ability checks are required (see examples below).

The GM might require one or more ability checks to represent the difficulty of locating or extracting information. Example checks the GM might call for include:

- Intelligence (Investigation) to find clues pointing toward the information you seek.
- Charisma (Persuasion) to secure someone's aid in researching or to coax information from a reluctant source.

Once the GM's conditions and any required checks are met, you learn the information if it is available.

Expenses: For each day of research, you must spend 1 gp to cover expenses associated with that research (lodging, copying, bribes, consumables, local fees). This cost is in addition to your normal lifestyle expenses.

### Training

Training represents spending downtime learning a new language or training with a set of tools (tool proficiency). The GM may allow additional training options at their discretion.

Prerequisite: You must find an instructor willing to teach you.

The GM determines:

- How long the training takes (the required number of days).
- Whether one or more ability checks are required to reflect aptitude or to gain the instructor's help.

Defined example duration and cost: A standard training option lasts 250 days and costs 1 gp per day. After you spend the requisite amount of time and money, you learn the new language or gain proficiency with the specified tool.

The training's cost (1 gp per day in the defined example) covers instruction, materials, and other incidental expenses and is paid in addition to any normal lifestyle expenses unless the GM rules otherwise.

## Interactions

- Downtime vs. Adventuring: Time spent researching or training consumes downtime days that are not available for adventuring activities.
- Lifestyle Expenses: Research and training costs are additional to normal lifestyle expenses; track both when calculating downtime costs.
- Ability Checks: Any ability checks called for by the GM during research or training use the standard check rules; failures may require additional time, costs, or a change in approach as the GM adjudicates.
- Availability and Restrictions: If the GM rules the necessary information, instructor, or material is unavailable, research or training cannot produce the desired result until those prerequisites are obtained.
- GM Discretion: The GM adjudicates exact durations, costs, required checks, and any special restrictions (specific persons, tomes, or places) for both research and training.
