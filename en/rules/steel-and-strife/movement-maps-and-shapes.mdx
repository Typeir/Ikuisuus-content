# Movement, Maps, and Shapes

This page covers how physical space is represented at the table: the grid, how to measure distances, how diagonal movement is handled, and the geometry of area shapes used by spells, abilities, and environmental effects.

---

## The Grid

Combat is typically played on a grid where each square represents **5 feet**. A creature's size determines how many squares it occupies (see [Movement](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/movement) for the size table).

- Each square represents a 5-foot cube of volume — relevant for three-dimensional effects such as cylinders and spheres.
- All distances in the rules (movement speed, weapon reach, spell range) are expressed in feet and translate directly to squares.

---

## Measuring Distance

To measure the distance between two points, count the number of squares along the shortest path.

### Diagonal Movement

On a flat grid, moving diagonally covers more real-world distance than moving along a cardinal direction. Two common approaches are used:

- **Flat counting** — every diagonal square costs 1 square (5 ft). Simple and fast; distances are slightly underestimated.
- **Alternating cost** — the first diagonal costs 1 square (5 ft), the second costs 2 squares (10 ft), alternating. More accurate; tracked per turn, not per move.

The GM chooses one method at the start of a campaign and applies it consistently. Distances for spell ranges, movement speeds, and area measurements all follow the same method.

### Vertical Distance

Height is measured in the same 5-foot increments. Flying, falling, and three-dimensional areas use the same grid counting rules as horizontal movement.

---

## Points of Origin

Every area of effect has a **point of origin** — the position from which the effect expands.

- The point of origin is typically a point in space that you select when you use the effect.
- Some effects specify that a creature, object, or location is the origin.
- An effect expands outward from its origin in straight lines. If no unobstructed straight line can reach a location from the origin, that location is outside the area.
- Only **total cover** blocks these lines. Partial cover does not limit an area's extent — it only affects attack rolls and saving throws.

---

## Area Shapes

The following shapes define how areas of effect are laid out on the grid. Spell descriptions, monster abilities, and environmental effects all reference these shapes by name.

### Cone

A cone expands outward from its point of origin in a chosen direction. The width of the cone at any point equals its distance from the origin — so a 30-foot cone is 30 feet wide at its far end.

- The cone's maximum extent is its stated length.
- The point of origin is **not** included unless the effect specifies otherwise.

### Cube

A cube is placed so that one face touches or passes through the chosen point of origin. The size of a cube is the length of each edge.

- The point of origin is **not** included unless the effect specifies otherwise.

### Cylinder

A cylinder is defined by a circular base of a given radius and a height. The center of that circle is the point of origin.

- The effect fills the full height of the cylinder above (or below) the base circle.
- The point of origin **is** included unless the effect specifies otherwise.

### Line

A line extends from its point of origin in a straight path to its stated length. Lines have a stated width that determines how many squares they cover.

- The point of origin is **not** included unless the effect specifies otherwise.

### Sphere

A sphere extends outward from its chosen point of origin to a stated radius in all directions.

- The point of origin **is** included unless the effect specifies otherwise.

---

## Inclusion and Exclusion of Origin

Whether a point of origin falls inside its own area matters for targeting — creatures standing at the origin may be affected or protected depending on the shape.

| Shape    | Origin included by default? |
| -------- | --------------------------- |
| Cone     | No                          |
| Cube     | No                          |
| Line     | No                          |
| Cylinder | Yes                         |
| Sphere   | Yes                         |

When the spell or ability text says otherwise, follow the text.

---

## Interactions

- Area shapes interact with cover rules: total cover blocks lines of effect; half and three-quarters cover do not limit the area but do affect saving throws and attack rolls.
- When a creature or object is the origin of an area, use that creature's or object's full space as the origin point — not just a single corner or edge.
- Diagonal distance rules apply equally to area measurements: a sphere's radius and a cone's length are both counted using the same diagonal method in use for movement.
