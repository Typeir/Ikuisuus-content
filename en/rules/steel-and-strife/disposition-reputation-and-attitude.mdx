# Disposition, Reputation and Attitude

Not every conflict is settled with steel. Every creature capable of wanting something holds a **disposition** toward you — the true inclination of its intent, whether it wishes you well, wishes you gone, or does not care either way. This page defines the track that disposition moves along, how it shifts, and how effects read it.

One mechanic, three names. **Disposition** is the state itself — what a creature actually harbors toward you. **Reputation** is the same track held at social scale, by a crowd, a guild, a city. **Attitude** is another word for disposition. Rules text, spells, and stat blocks may use any of the three; they always refer to this track. It is sometimes called the **behavior chart**, as in the [Parlay](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat#parlay) action.

---

## The Track

Disposition occupies one of three **ranges** — **Hostile**, **Neutral**, or **Friendly** — and within its range, a creature sits at a **depth**: its **disposition modifier**, running from **−50** (a nemesis) through **0** (Neutral) to **+50** (a soulmate). Together, range and depth state precisely what a creature is and is not willing to do:

| Modifier   | Standing                  | The creature will...                                                                    | The creature will not...                                                                                                  |
| ---------- | ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| −50        | A nemesis                 | Risk its life to end you; spend wealth, allies, and years for the chance.               | Parley in good faith, accept your surrender, or believe your word — even when it is true.                                 |
| −45 to −30 | A blood feud              | Bleed to hurt you: attack when the odds allow, fund your enemies, celebrate your ruin.  | Trade, shelter, or treat with you except under compulsion or hard guarantees.                                             |
| −25 to −15 | An earned enemy           | Obstruct you, expose your secrets, refuse you service, speak against you.               | Aid you at any price short of extraordinary leverage.                                                                     |
| −10 to −5  | Slighted, distrustful     | Deal with you if it must — watching your hands, charging you double.                    | Extend trust, credit, hospitality, or the benefit of the doubt.                                                           |
| 0          | Neutral                   | Trade at a fair price, answer harmless questions, observe common courtesy.              | Take risks, bear costs, keep your secrets — or become your friend because you asked. Friendship is earned, not requested. |
| +5 to +10  | A pleasant acquaintance   | Do small favors that cost little: directions, introductions, a warning, a meal.         | Risk its safety, standing, or savings for you.                                                                            |
| +15 to +25 | A proven companion        | Take real risks, lend real coin, keep your secrets, vouch for you.                      | Abandon its own bonds and duties, or die for you.                                                                         |
| +30 to +45 | Sworn bond, chosen family | Bleed to help you: fight beside you, hide you from the law, endure loss on your behalf. | Follow you into what it holds unforgivable.                                                                               |
| +50        | A soulmate                | Risk its life to save you; anything asked in good faith is on the table.                | Survive your betrayal unchanged — see _Through betrayal_.                                                                 |

The **will not** column is not a DC Guide but a concise, robust wall. A Charisma check operates within what a creature's disposition already permits: it can win a fair hearing, a better price, a favor the depth allows. No check, however high, makes a creature cross its will-not line. Only depth moves the line, and depth moves through deeds (see _Shifting Disposition_). The Game Director calls for checks; declaring an intent does not summon a roll.

### The Modifier in Play

The modifier is flat roll math: it stacks with everything, and nothing that rescues a bad roll will rescue fifty years of earned hatred.

- A **Hostile** creature's modifier applies as a **penalty** to any Charisma check that attempts to shift its disposition toward Neutral or Friendly — including [Parlay](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat#parlay) contests.
- A **Friendly** creature's modifier applies as a **bonus** to Charisma checks made to request its aid, within what its depth permits. The bonus never protects the relationship from you — see _Through betrayal_, below.

Deeds move the modifier within a range just as they move creatures between ranges; the Game Director adjudicates, typically in increments of 5.

## Disposition Is the Face Behind the Mask

Disposition measures what a creature **actually harbors** — never its allegiance, its manners, or its team.

- A companion who smiles at your fire while planning to sell you out is **Hostile**. The smile is a mask; the disposition is the truth beneath it.
- A dupe sent against you by an enemy, honestly convinced you are the villain, may bear you no ill will at all — naively manipulated, they might be **Neutral**, or even **Friendly**, while working your ruin.
- The Game Director always knows a creature's true disposition. Players read it the hard way: through behavior, Insight, and consequence.

Effects that detect, target, or trigger on a disposition read the **truth**, not the mask. A ward that strikes those of hostile disposition strikes the traitor in your ranks the moment they step through it — and spares the honest pawn your enemy sent to kill you. Both outcomes are correct. Both are stories.

## Shifting Disposition

Disposition moves in **steps** — one range at a time, never skipping Neutral. Hostile does not become Friendly in a single gesture, and a friend does not become a murderer over one insult — magic and betrayal excepted.

**Through play.** Deeds shift disposition: gifts, aid, insults, threats, debts honored and debts broken. The Game Director adjudicates when an act warrants a step. Shifting a creature a full step within a single scene should demand something proportionate — sparing a life, returning something precious, a truth that reframes everything.

**Through parlay.** The [Parlay](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat#parlay) action is the in-combat lever: a successful contest shifts a Hostile creature one step toward Neutral, where its disposition can change at all.

**Through harm.** Harming a creature or its allies shifts its disposition one step toward Hostile, immediately. Some things cannot be talked around.

**Through betrayal.** Friendship is easier to lose than hatred is to unmake. Wronging a Friendly creature shifts it toward Neutral without ceremony — and a grave enough betrayal falls through Neutral entirely, in a single act. When it does, the depth of the bond becomes the depth of the enmity: sell out a +50 soulmate and you have made a −50 enemy for life.

**Through magic.** Effects may **shift** disposition along the track, **lock** it in a range for a duration, or **read** it. Unless the effect says otherwise, a creature whose disposition was magically altered remembers how it behaved once the effect ends; whether it realizes magic was responsible is stated by the effect. A creature that realizes it was made to feel something usually likes you rather less for it.

**Immovable dispositions.** Some creatures' dispositions cannot change — beings of pure obsession, the mindless, the sworn, constructs executing their purpose. A stat block or the Game Director may declare a disposition fixed. Parlay and magic state whether they can move what refuses to be moved.

## Reputation: the Track at Social Scale

A community — a village, a guild, a garrison, a faith — holds a collective disposition toward a person, a company, or a name. This is **reputation**, and it moves by the same steps and carries the same modifier, driven by deeds _the community knows about_.

- News travels at the speed of story, not the speed of truth. A heroic act nobody witnessed shifts nothing; a crime pinned on you by a liar shifts plenty.
- Individual members may deviate from the collective range — a Friendly gatekeeper in a city that wants you hanged is a door; a Hostile clerk in a town that adores you is a problem.
- Sustained effort can work reputation deliberately: building one is a downtime undertaking like any other (see [Downtime, Learning and Training](/en/library/rules/hearth-and-station/downtime-learning-and-training)).

Väkis, for what it is worth, also hold dispositions — but theirs answer to caprice, not reputation. The taboos of Speaking (never command, never repeat, never lie) move an Idea toward Hostile faster than any injury, and an Idea's disposition, once lost, is not argued back.

## Examples

- **The parley at the ford.** A raider chieftain (Hostile, −10) is losing warriors. The party's face takes the Parlay action mid-battle — the chieftain's −10 weighs on the contest — and wins anyway: the chieftain shifts to Neutral and calls a wary halt. One more success, and an offer worth hearing, could make the raiders Friendly enough to share what they know. Then an archer looses one arrow: one step toward Hostile, immediately, and parlay is off the table until the next turn ends.
- **The trusted knife.** A hireling has been paid handsomely by the party's rival. He carries their packs, laughs at their jokes — and his disposition is Hostile, because his intent is theirs to suffer. A ward that punishes hostile disposition drops him at the threshold, to everyone's surprise but the Game Director's.
- **An enemy for life.** Years ago, the captain abandoned her sworn companion (+35) to save the mission. He survived. He is Hostile at −35 now, and every honeyed word she rolls against him starts thirty-five points in the hole.

## Interactions

- **Alignment**: a creature's [alignment](/en/library/rules/the-measure-of-the-self/alignment) is its moral orientation; its disposition is its inclination toward _you_. The two are independent — a Lawful Good knight can be your bitterest enemy, and a Chaotic Evil cutthroat can be your +25 proven companion. Effects that reference alignment and effects that reference disposition read different things.
- **Parlay**: the standard action for shifting disposition in combat; it moves creatures one step toward Neutral and is disabled by fresh harm. The target's disposition modifier applies as a penalty to the contest. See [Actions in Combat](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat#parlay).
- **Charmed**: the [charmed](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#charmed) condition constrains behavior while it lasts; it does not move the underlying disposition. A creature charmed into acting against its disposition typically shifts a step toward Hostile once the condition ends and it understands what was done to it.
- **Social checks**: the disposition modifier is flat roll math — it stacks with circumstances, features, and magic, and cannot be negated the way rolled advantages can. Effects that grant or deny rerolls and advantages leave it untouched.
- **Effects keyed to disposition**: spells and wards that target "creatures of hostile disposition" (or lock, shift, or read the track) always operate on true disposition, per _Disposition Is the Truth, Not the Mask_ above.
