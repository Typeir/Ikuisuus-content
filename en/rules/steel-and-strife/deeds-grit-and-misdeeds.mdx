# Deeds, Grit, and Misdeeds

Fortune favours the bold, but Damocles favours the stubborn. The Deed economy is a shared resource pool — a tide of defiance that recedes and comes crashing back.

---

## Deeds — The Party Pool

Deeds are a shared resource tracked by the party as a group.

```
party Deeds = lowest Tier Bonus (TB) + number of party members
```

| Party Example     | Pool |
| ----------------- | ---- |
| 4 members at TB 2 | 6    |
| 5 members at TB 6 | 11   |
| 6 members at TB 8 | 14   |

### Gaining Deeds

- **Recovery.** The party Deeds pool fully refreshes at the end of a Recovery.
- **Roleplay.** When the GM deems a moment of play exceptional — compelling portrayal, costly conviction, a sacrifice that defines the character — the party pool gains **+1 Deed**.
- **GM Overspend.** When the GM overspends (see Misdeeds), the party gains a Deed.

### Individual Deeds

Certain features grant a Deed to a specific creature rather than the party pool. An individual Deed is spent and tracked the same way, but belongs to that creature alone. It does not expire — it persists until spent or until the feature that granted it expires or refreshes.

---

## Spending a Deed — The Grit Roll

Spend **1 Deed** (party or individual) to trigger a Grit Roll. The player must **describe how the character grits through**.

### Grit Roll Triggers

| Trigger             | Grit Roll Effect                                                   |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Fail a saving throw | Reroll with **bonus = relevant stat mod + TB**                     |
| Hit by an attack    | Attacker **rerolls with penalty = stat mod + TB**                  |
| Fail a skill check  | Reroll using a **different attribute** + TB if proficient          |
| Fail a death save   | Reroll with **any stat mod + TB**. Describe how you cling to life. |

### Constraints

- A roll cannot be rerolled using the **same attribute**. Fail your best roll, and you are forced into a weaker stat — the spiral tightens.
- Grit Rolls are **not auto-success**.They grant better odds, not certainty.
- Grit Rolls can be declared **before or after** the outcome of the original roll is known.

## Misdeeds — Overshooting the target

Spending more Deeds than the pool has, and you will generate a **Misdeed**.

### Player Overspend

When a player overspends Deeds:

1. The **GM gains 1 Misdeed**.
2. The character **loses 1 Hit Die**. This HD cannot be recovered until all Grievous Wounds on that character are cleared. Lost HD recover at a rate of **1 per Recovery**.
3. The character suffers **[Grievous Wounds](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/grievous-wounds)**: roll the lost Hit Die + CON. The result is added to the character's Grievous Wound pool. Taking Grievous Wounds in this way never causes a character to fall under 1 HP.

> **Example.** A party has spent all of their 8 Deeds. They spend a 9th Deed to reroll a failed Dexterity save. The GM gains 1 Misdeed. The character loses 1 HD (a d10), rolls 7 + 2 CON = 9 Grievous Wounds. Their max HP drops by 9, current HP drops by 9.

### GM Overspend

The GM has their own Deed pool — drawn from monster stat blocks and accumulated Misdeeds. When the GM overspends:

1. The **party gains 1 Deed**.
2. The same Grievous Wound mechanics apply to whatever creature the GM used the Deed for, if applicable.

The GM **cannot** overspend without spending at least one real Deed first. Misdeeds cannot be conjured from nothing.

### What Deeds Do For The GM

A GM can spend Deeds to activate the abilities listed in a creature's stat block under its **Legendary Deeds** entry. These include:

| Deed Type | Timing                                          |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Act       | End of another creature's turn                  |
| Resist    | When the creature fails a saving throw          |
| Stratagem | Declared at end of turn; resolves next turn     |
| Lair      | Start of the round                              |
| Phase     | When HP drops below a threshold (75%, 50%, 25%) |

See [Legendary Deeds](/en/library/rules/creatures-critters-and-characters/legendary-deeds) for the full rules on each type, deed caps, and the one-deed-per-turn limit.

Not all creatures have Legendary Deeds. But don't be fooled — If the DM accrues too many Misdeeds, when the party meets the next Legendary Creature, they are in for a world of hurt.

---

## Misdeed as Narrative

Every Misdeed is a story beat. When the party overspends, the GM has ammunition for escalation. When the GM overspends, the party gains breathing room. Misdeeds are not punishment — they are pacing.
When both pools hit zero, every failed roll is final. Every Deed is a Misdeed waiting to happen.
