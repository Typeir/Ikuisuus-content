# Contextual Conflict

## Overview

Sometimes combat takes place in special circumstances that require additional rules. Common examples are as follows:

- Mounted combat (mounting, dismounting, and controlling mounts) (more information on mounts in [Mounts](/en/library/rules/the-long-road/mounts-and-vehicles)).
- Underwater combat (how immersion affects attacks, ranged weapons, and damage types).
  These rules govern movement costs for mounting and dismounting, how mounts behave when controlled or independent, consequences when a mount is moved or knocked prone, and the mechanical penalties and exceptions that apply while fighting underwater.

## Mechanics

### Mounting and Dismounting

- Once during your move, you can mount a creature that is within 5 feet of you or dismount. Doing so costs an amount of movement equal to half your speed.
  - Example: If your speed is 30 feet, you must spend 15 feet of movement to mount a horse.
  - You cannot mount a creature if you do not have at least that much movement remaining or if your speed is 0.

- If an effect moves your mount against its will while you are on it, you must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw or fall off the mount. If you fail, you land prone in a space within 5 feet of the mount.
  - If you are knocked prone while mounted, you must make the same DC 10 Dexterity saving throw to avoid falling off.

- If your mount is knocked prone, you can use your reaction to dismount it as it falls and land on your feet. Otherwise, you are dismounted and fall prone in a space within 5 feet of the mount.

- Opportunity attacks and targeting while mounted:
  - If the mount provokes an opportunity attack while you are on it, the attacker can choose whether the attack targets you or the mount.

### Controlling a Mount

- While mounted you have two options for how the mount behaves: you can control the mount or allow it to act independently.

- Controlling a mount:
  - You can control a mount only if it has been trained to accept a rider (domesticated horses, donkeys, and similar creatures are assumed to have such training).
  - The initiative of a controlled mount changes to match yours when you mount it.
  - A controlled mount moves as you direct it and has only three action options: Dash, Disengage, and Dodge.
  - A controlled mount can move and act even on the turn that you mount it.

- Independent mounts:
  - An independent mount retains its place in the initiative order.
  - Bearing a rider places no restrictions on the actions the mount can take; it moves and acts as it wishes.
  - An independent mount might flee from combat, rush to attack, or otherwise behave according to its instincts and motivations.

### Underwater Combat

- Environment note: Fighting underwater or in flooded rooms imposes special rules for melee and ranged weapon attacks and grants some resistances to submerged creatures and objects.

- Melee weapon attacks:
  - A creature that does not have a swimming speed (either natural or granted by magic) has disadvantage on melee weapon attack rolls while underwater unless the weapon used is one of the following: dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident.

- Ranged weapon attacks:
  - A ranged weapon attack automatically misses a target beyond the weapon's normal range when made underwater.
  - Even against a target within the weapon's normal range, the attack roll has disadvantage unless the weapon is a crossbow, a net, or a weapon that is thrown like a javelin (for example: javelin, spear, trident, or dart).

- Damage resistances:
  - Creatures and objects that are fully immersed in water have resistance to fire damage.

## Examples and Clarifications

- Mounting example: A character with a 30-foot speed must spend 15 feet of movement to mount a horse. If the mount is then forced to move by an effect and the rider fails the DC 10 Dex save, the rider falls off and lands prone within 5 feet of the mount.

- Controlled versus independent mount example: If a trained warhorse is controlled by its rider, the horse's initiative becomes the rider's initiative and the horse may only take Dash, Disengage, or Dodge actions while under the rider's control. If the warhorse is independent (an intelligent creature or an untrained beast that acts on its own), it keeps its original initiative and may take any action available to it.

## Interactions

- Opportunity attacks and targeting: When a mount provokes an opportunity attack, attackers may choose to target the rider or the mount; this interaction affects how players protect mounts and whether they employ Disengage actions.

- Spell and feature interactions: If a spell or feature grants a creature a swimming speed or modifies weapon properties (for example, making a weapon count as a thrown weapon with no underwater penalty), that effect supersedes the base underwater penalties described above.

- Temporary hit points and mounts: Temporary hit points granted by features follow their stated durations (if any). In general, temporary hit points remain until depleted or until the recipient finishes a long rest, which can affect whether a rider or mount absorbs damage via temporary hit points.
