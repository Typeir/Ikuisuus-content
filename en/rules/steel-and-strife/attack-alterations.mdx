# Attack Alterations

An attack alteration is a special melee attack that replaces one of your attacks when you take the Attack action. Rather than dealing weapon damage, it imposes a condition, forces movement, or establishes physical control over a target. Every creature has access to the core alterations below. Certain class features, feats, and abilities introduce additional attack alterations with their own rules.

---

## Using an Attack Alteration

When you take the Attack action, you may substitute one attack for an attack alteration. If you can make multiple attacks with the Attack action, determine how many attacks can be replaced by alterations based on the specific alteration's rules.
For example, if an alteration can only replace one attack, you can use it once per Attack action, even if you have multiple attacks.

---

## Grapple

You seize a creature and hold it in place. You must have **at least one free hand**.

You can graplle creatures of varying sizes depending on your strength and your size, as described in the following table:

| Your Strength | Maximum Target Size |
| ------------- | ------------------- |
| 0-10          | Your size           |
| 11-15         | One size larger     |
| 16-20         | Two sizes larger    |
| 21+           | Three sizes larger  |

In order for you to grapple a creature, you must first land an attack against it. You can use your unarmed strike or a weapon attack with a weapon with the [bind](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapon-properties) property to initiate the grapple, but you must declare that you are using the attack to grapple before making the attack roll.
If the attack hits, you can attempt to grapple the target as part of the same action.

**Contest:** Strength (Athletics) vs. the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics).

**On a success:** The target gains the **Grappled** condition — its speed becomes 0 and it cannot benefit from any bonus to its speed.

You can release the grapple at any time (no action required).

### Escaping a Grapple

A grappled creature can use its action to escape. It makes a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check contested by the grappler's Strength (Athletics). On a success, the Grappled condition ends.

### Moving a Grappled Creature

When you move, you can drag or carry the grappled creature with you. Your speed is **halved** unless the creature is two or more sizes smaller than you.

## Shove

You push a creature off balance, either to knock it prone or to create distance.

**Contest:** Strength (Athletics) vs. the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics).

**On a success:** Choose one:

- Knock the target **prone**, or
- Push the target **5 feet** straight away from you.

---

## Restrain

You press your advantage on a grappled creature to pin it entirely. You can only attempt to Restrain a creature that is already **Grappled** by you.

**Contest:** Strength (Athletics) vs. the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics).

**On a success:** The target gains the **Restrained** condition in addition to Grappled — its speed is 0, attack rolls against it have advantage, and its own attack rolls and Dexterity saving throws have disadvantage.

Maintaining a Restrain counts as using one hand. If you lose the free hand used to maintain it (by being forced to drop the hold, losing the limb, or taking the Incapacitated condition), the Restrained condition ends, though the Grappled condition may remain depending on circumstances.

### Escaping a Restrain

A restrained creature can use its action to escape. It makes a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check contested by the grappler's Strength (Athletics). On a success, the Restrained condition ends; the Grappled condition also ends unless the grappler succeeds on a Strength (Athletics) check against the same DC to maintain the hold.

## Masterful Blow

A Masterful Blow is a special strike technique tied to a specific weapon type. Rather than replacing an attack, it augments a hit — when you land a weapon attack, you may spend a use to apply the weapon's Masterful Blow effect.

Full rules, save DCs, use limits, and the complete table of Masterful Blow effects are in [Weapon Properties](/en/library/rules/arms-armour-and-burden/weapon-properties#masterful-blows).

---

## Feature-Based Attack Alterations

Some class features, feats, and abilities introduce their own attack replacements or alterations. These function like the core alterations above — they replace one attack in the Attack action — but may have different checks, conditions, or target requirements. Examples include:

- Bladesinger multiattack substitution
- Certain maneuvers that impose conditions on a hit
- Feat-granted special attacks (such as trip, disarm, or redirect)

Each such feature defines its own procedure. Where a feature's rules conflict with the general constraints above, the feature's specific rules take precedence.
