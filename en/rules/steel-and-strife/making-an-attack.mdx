# Making an Attack

## Overview

This page covers the full procedure for resolving attacks: rolling, applying modifiers, determining hits, cover, and the special attack forms — melee, ranged, two-weapon, and opportunity.

---

## Attack Rolls

When you make an attack, roll a d20 and add the appropriate modifiers. If the total equals or exceeds the target's Armor Class (AC), the attack hits. On a hit, roll damage and apply any special effects listed for the attack.

The ability modifier used:

- **Melee weapon attacks** — Strength
- **Ranged weapon attacks** — Dexterity
- **Finesse or thrown weapons** — Strength or Dexterity (your choice)
- **Spell attacks** — the caster's spellcasting ability

Monsters use whatever modifier their stat block provides.

### Modifiers to the Roll

- **Ability modifier**: determined by attack type as above.
- **Proficiency bonus**: added when you are proficient with the weapon or when your class grants spell attack proficiency.
- Other modifiers (cover, advantage/disadvantage, spells, features, environment) apply as the situation warrants.

### Resolving an Attack

1. **Choose a target.** Pick a target within range: a creature, object, or location.
2. **Determine modifiers.** The GM determines cover and whether advantage or disadvantage applies; add ability modifier, proficiency, and any other applicable modifiers.
3. **Roll and resolve.** Roll the attack. On a hit, roll damage or apply the attack's specified effect.

### Rolling a 1 or 20

- **Natural 20** — hits regardless of modifiers or AC; counts as a critical hit, doubling **all** the rolled dice for that attack's damage.
- **Natural 1** — misses regardless of modifiers or AC.

### Unseen Attackers and Targets

- Attacking a target you can't see: disadvantage on the attack roll.
- Attacking a target that can't see you: advantage on the attack roll.
- If the target isn't in the location you targeted, the attack automatically misses.
- Making an attack while hidden reveals your location when the attack hits or misses.

---

## Cover

Obstacles between you and a target can increase the difficulty of hitting it. Cover applies when an attack or effect originates from the opposite side of the obstacle. If a target is behind multiple sources of cover, only the most protective degree applies — they do not stack.

| Degree of cover | Effect                                              |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Half cover      | +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws          |
| Three-quarters  | +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws          |
| Total cover     | Cannot be targeted directly by an attack or spell   |

- **Half cover**: an obstacle blocks at least half the target's body (a low wall, large furniture, a narrow tree trunk, or an intervening creature).
- **Three-quarters cover**: roughly three-quarters of the target is blocked.
- **Total cover**: the target is completely concealed. Area effects that do not require targeting can still affect it.

---

## Melee Attacks

A melee attack targets a creature within your reach. A typical creature has a 5-foot reach; some larger creatures have greater reach as noted in their stat block.

Melee attacks use handheld weapons — swords, axes, warhammers — or natural weapons such as claws, horns, or tentacles. You can also make an **unarmed strike** (a punch, kick, or head-butt):

- Deals bludgeoning damage equal to 1 + your Strength modifier.
- You are always proficient with unarmed strikes.

---

## Ranged Attacks

You can make ranged attacks only against targets within range. Many ranged weapons have **two ranges** — a normal range and a long range:

- Attacking beyond normal range but within long range: **disadvantage** on the attack roll.
- Attacking beyond long range: not possible.

If a ranged attack has a single range, you cannot target beyond it.

### Ranged Attacks in Close Combat

Aiming a ranged attack is more difficult when a foe is adjacent. When you make a ranged attack with a weapon, spell, or similar means, you have **disadvantage** on the attack roll if you are within 5 feet of a hostile creature that can see you and is not incapacitated.

---

## Two-Weapon Fighting

When you take the Attack action and attack with a **light melee weapon** in one hand, you can use a **bonus action** to attack with a different light melee weapon in your other hand.
This secondary weapon is referred to as your **"off-hand weapon"**.

- You do not add your ability modifier to the bonus attack's damage, unless that modifier is negative.
- If either weapon has the thrown property, you may throw it instead of making a melee attack.

---

## Opportunity Attacks

You can make an opportunity attack when a hostile creature you can see **moves out of your reach**. Using your reaction, make one melee attack against the provoking creature. The attack occurs immediately before the creature leaves your reach.

You **do not** provoke an opportunity attack when:

- You take the **Disengage** action.
- You teleport.
- You are moved by an external force (an explosion, a spell's push effect, a fall) without spending your own movement, action, or reaction.

---

## Interactions

- Advantage and disadvantage cancel each other; if you have both, neither applies.
- The thrown property works in both melee and two-weapon fighting contexts: a thrown-property weapon can be thrown instead of used for a melee attack.
- Teleportation and forced movement do not provoke opportunity attacks.
- Spells that require ranged attack rolls follow the same range and disadvantage rules as weapon ranged attacks.
- Monster attack modifiers come from the stat block and override generic character rules where specified.
