# Movement

This page covers all movement in combat: how speed works, breaking up movement around actions, terrain costs, special movement modes, and spatial rules.

---

## Size and Space

A creature's space is the area it effectively controls in combat — not a precise measure of its body. A Medium creature occupying a 5-foot space may not be 5 feet wide; the space represents the room it needs to fight.

| Size       | Space                  |
| ---------- | ---------------------- |
| Tiny       | 2½ by 2½ ft.           |
| Small      | 5 by 5 ft.             |
| Medium     | 5 by 5 ft.             |
| Large      | 10 by 10 ft.           |
| Huge       | 15 by 15 ft.           |
| Gargantuan | 20 by 20 ft. or larger |
| Colossal   | 30 by 30 ft. or larger |
| Titanic    | 40 by 40 ft. or larger |

Larger creatures take up more space and therefore fewer can surround a single target.

---

## Speed and Breaking Up Movement

On your turn, you can move a distance up to your speed. You can break that movement up freely — some before your action, some after.

- Your movement can include walking, jumping, climbing, and swimming in any combination.
- Deduct the distance of each segment from your remaining speed as you move.

### Moving Between Attacks

If you can make multiple attacks with the Attack action (e.g. Extra Attack), you can split your movement between those attacks as well.

_Example: A creature with 25 ft speed and two attacks could move 10 ft, attack, move 15 ft, then attack again._

### Using Different Speeds

If you have more than one speed (walking, flying, swimming), you can switch between them during your move. When you switch, subtract the distance already moved from the new speed; the result is how far you can still go with it.

- If the result is 0 or less, you cannot use the new speed during the current move.

_Example: Flying speed 60 ft, walking speed 30 ft — fly 20 ft, walk 10 ft, then fly up to 30 ft more._

---

## Moving Around Other Creatures

- You can move through a **nonhostile** creature's space freely.
- You can move through a **hostile** creature's space only if it is at least two sizes larger or smaller than you.
- You cannot **end** your move in another creature's space, friend or enemy.
- Leaving a hostile creature's reach during your move **provokes an opportunity attack**.

---

## Difficult Terrain

Every foot of movement in difficult terrain costs **1 extra foot**. Moving 1 foot in difficult terrain uses 2 feet of your speed. This applies to all movement modes unless otherwise stated.

Examples: loose rubble, dense undergrowth, shallow bogs, steep stairs, ice, deep snow, the space of another creature (whether hostile or not).

---

## Climbing, Swimming, and Crawling

Each of these movement modes costs **1 extra foot per foot** of movement (i.e. 2 feet of speed per foot traveled), unless the creature has a dedicated speed for that mode.

- In difficult terrain, this stacks: climbing or swimming in difficult terrain costs **2 extra feet per foot** (3 total).
- At the GM's option, a slippery vertical surface or rough water requires a successful **Strength (Athletics)** check.

---

## Jumping

Your Strength determines how far you can jump. Each foot you clear costs 1 foot of movement.

### Long Jump

- With a running start of at least 10 feet: you clear a number of feet equal to your **Strength score**.
- From a standing start: you clear half that distance.
- At the GM's option, clearing a low obstacle (no taller than a quarter of the jump's distance) requires a DC 10 **Strength (Athletics)** check; failure means you hit the obstacle.

### High Jump

- With a running start of at least 10 feet: you leap **3 + your Strength modifier** feet into the air.
- From a standing start: you leap half that height.
- You can extend your arms while jumping, reaching a total height of the jump height plus 1½ times your height.

### Landing

When you land in difficult terrain after a jump, succeed on a DC 10 **Dexterity (Acrobatics)** check or land prone.

---

## Prone

Combatants sometimes end up prone — knocked down or having thrown themselves down.

- You can **drop prone** without using any of your speed.
- **Standing up** from prone costs movement equal to **half your speed**. If you lack sufficient movement or your speed is 0, you cannot stand.
- While prone, you can only crawl or use magic such as teleportation to move.

### Crawling While Prone

Crawling costs **1 extra foot per foot** of movement (2 feet per foot traveled). Crawling in difficult terrain costs **2 extra feet per foot** (3 total: crawl cost plus difficult terrain cost).

---

## Squeezing

A creature can squeeze through a space large enough for a creature **one size smaller** than itself.

While squeezing:

- Movement costs **1 extra foot per foot** moved.
- Attack rolls **against** the squeezing creature have **advantage**.
- The squeezing creature has **disadvantage** on attack rolls, Dexterity saving throws, and ability checks.

Large or larger creatures must squeeze to move through spaces of **5 feet or less** in width. Medium and smaller creatures may squeeze through unusually narrow gaps at the GM's discretion.

---

## Flying and Falling

Flying creatures enjoy mobility benefits but must deal with falling.

- A flying creature that is **knocked prone**, has its **speed reduced to 0**, or otherwise **loses the ability to move**, falls immediately — unless it can hover or magic is keeping it aloft.
- **Standing up while flying** follows the normal standing-up movement cost rule; if you cannot pay that cost, you remain prone and, if unsupported, continue to fall.

## Object Interaction

## Interactions

- Difficult terrain and hostile creature spaces increase the per-foot movement cost; they stack when both apply.
- Creatures with a climbing or swimming speed do not pay the extra cost for those modes.
- Effects that grant additional movement (Dash, class features, spells) apply the same breaking-up and terrain rules.
- Size and space determine how many creatures can surround a target and whether a hostile space can be passed through.
- Declaring **Steady** replaces your Move action; you cannot declare Steady and also take a Dash action on the same turn.
