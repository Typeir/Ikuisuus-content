# Turns and Initiative

Initiative determines the order of turns during a combat encounter. One round represents about 6 seconds of game time. When combat starts, every participant rolls initiative; the result fixes the order for the encounter unless an effect changes it.

---

## The Order of Combat

1. **Determine surprise.** The GM determines whether anyone involved is surprised.
2. **Establish positions.** The GM places all characters and monsters and determines distances.
3. **Roll initiative.** Everyone rolls initiative (a Dexterity check), determining the order of turns.
4. **Take turns.** Each participant acts in initiative order — move up to your speed and take one action.
5. **Begin the next round.** When everyone has had a turn, the round ends. Repeat from step 4 until combat ends.

Ties: among GM-controlled creatures, the GM decides order. Among tied player characters, the players decide. Between a PC and a monster, the GM may have each roll a d20 (highest goes first).

The initiative order remains fixed from round to round unless an effect explicitly changes it.

---

## Your Turn

On your turn you may:

- **Move** up to your speed.
- **Take one action.**
- **Take one bonus action** (if you have one available).

You choose whether to move first or act first unless an effect dictates otherwise. You can forgo any or all of these; doing nothing is a valid choice.

---

## Surprise

- Surprise occurs when one side does not notice a threat that initiates combat.
- If neither side tries to be stealthy, they automatically notice each other.
- Otherwise, the GM compares Dexterity (Stealth) checks of anyone hiding against the passive Wisdom (Perception) of creatures on the opposing side.
- A **surprised** creature cannot move or take an action on its first turn, and cannot take a reaction until that turn ends.
- Individual members of a group can be surprised even if others in the group are not.

---

## Bonus Actions

- Various class features, spells, and other abilities allow you to take a **bonus action** on your turn.
- You can take a bonus action only when a feature specifically states you can do something as a bonus action.
- You can take only one bonus action per turn. When multiple are available, choose which to use.
- You choose when during your turn to take the bonus action unless its timing is specified by the granting feature.

---

## Reactions

A reaction is an instant response to a trigger. It can occur on your turn or on another creature's turn.

- You can take only **one reaction per round**. Once used, you cannot take another until the start of your next turn.
- If a reaction interrupts another creature's turn, that creature continues its turn right after the reaction resolves.
- The **opportunity attack** is the most common reaction — see [Making an Attack](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/making-an-attack#opportunity-attacks).
- The **Ready** action lets you prepare a reaction to a specified trigger — see [Actions in Combat](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat).

---

## Interactions

- Initiative is fixed per encounter; effects that alter turn order state how and when they modify it.
- Surprise prevents movement, actions, and reactions on the first turn; this interacts with any feature that would otherwise grant a free reaction or movement before a turn begins.
- The GM may make one initiative roll for an entire group of identical creatures, so they all act on the same count.
