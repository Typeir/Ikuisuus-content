# Turns and Initiative

Initiative determines the order of turns during a combat encounter. One round represents about 6 seconds of game time. When combat starts, every participant rolls initiative; the result fixes the order for the encounter unless an effect changes it.

---

## The Order of Combat

1. **Determine surprise.** The GM determines whether anyone involved is surprised.
2. **Establish positions.** The GM places all characters and monsters and determines distances.
3. **Roll initiative.** Everyone rolls initiative (a Dexterity check), determining the order of turns.
4. **Take turns.** Each participant acts in initiative order — move up to your speed, take your actions as you see fit.
5. **Begin the next round.** When everyone has had a turn, the round ends. Repeat from step 4 until combat ends.

Ties: among GM-controlled creatures, the GM decides order. Among tied player characters, the players decide. Between a PC and a monster, the GM may have each roll a d20 (highest goes first).

The initiative order remains fixed from round to round unless an effect explicitly changes it.

---

## Your Turn

The turn operates under the "Two actions, one reaction" principle. It is encapsulated in the movement; movement happens — Or doesn't — while you act.

- **Movement.** Move up to your speed. You can break movement before and after your actions. You may sacrifice your movement to declare **Steady** (see [Actions in Combat](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat#steady)).
- **Two actions.** You have a pool of two actions you can spend them as you see fit. You are limited to one Major Action per turn, unless a feature says otherwise.
- **One Major Action.** You can consume one Major Action from your pool for a Major Action. This action is used for attacking, casting a spell with a casting time of 1 Major Action, or any of the actions listed in [Actions in Combat](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat).
- **One Minor Action.** You can consume one Major Action from your pool for a Minor Action. Used for quicker tasks — drinking a potion, making an off-hand attack, issuing a command to a companion.
- **One Reaction per round.** Used for off-turn responses. You regain your Reaction at the start of your turn.

You can forgo any or all of these whenever you see fit.

---

## Major Actions

The Attack action is a Major Action. So are Dodge, Help, Hide, Cast (For Major Spells), Ready, Dash, Disengage, Search, and Use. These are Actions that require focus and attention.

---

## Minor Actions

A Minor Action handles tasks too **quick** to demand the focus of a Major Action. Drawing a weapon that was stowed, drinking a potion, issuing a short command — these are Minor Actions.

---

## Reactions

A reaction is an instant response to a trigger, taken on your turn or another creature's turn.

- You have **one reaction per round**.
- If a reaction interrupts another creature's turn, that creature resumes its turn after the reaction resolves.
- The **opportunity attack** is the most common reaction — see [Making an Attack](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/making-an-attack#opportunity-attacks).
- The **Ready** action lets you set a trigger and hold a response — see [Actions in Combat](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/actions-in-combat).

---

## Action Degradation

On your turn, you may **convert any of your actions** into an **additional Reaction** for the round. Sacrifice proactive capacity for reactive readiness.
You must declare degradation **during your turn**. You cannot degrade between turns or in response to a trigger.
You may degrade **any number of Actions**, and **both Reactions and Actions** may be degraded into **Interactions**. Degradation lasts until the start of your next turn.

---

## Interactions

You can interact with one object or environmental feature for free during your movement or action — opening an unlocked door, drawing a weapon as part of an attack, picking up a dropped item. A second interaction costs a Major Action, and is considered a Minor Action.

---

## Surprise

- Surprise occurs when one side does not notice a threat that initiates combat.
- If neither side tries to be stealthy, they automatically notice each other.
- Otherwise, the GM compares Dexterity (Stealth) checks of anyone hiding against the passive Wisdom (Perception) of creatures on the opposing side.
- A **surprised** creature cannot move or take actions on its first turn, and cannot take a reaction until that turn ends.
- Individual members of a group can be surprised even if others in the group are not.

---

## Initiative Notes

- Initiative is fixed per encounter; effects that alter turn order state how and when they modify it.
- The GM may make one initiative roll for an entire group of identical creatures, so they all act on the same count.
- Action Degradation is always voluntary — no rule or condition forces a creature to degrade.
