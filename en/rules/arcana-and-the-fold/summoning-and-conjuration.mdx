# Summoning and Conjuration

A conjuring makes nothing. It calls, and something that already exists answers — for a while, on terms.

---

## The Challenge Budget

A conjuring spell carries a **Challenge Budget** equal to **twice the level of the slot spent**. Everything that answers must fit inside it.

| Slot Spent | Challenge Budget |
| ---------- | ---------------- |
| 1st        | CR 2             |
| 2nd        | CR 4             |
| 3rd        | CR 6             |
| 4th        | CR 8             |
| 5th        | CR 10            |
| 6th        | CR 12            |
| 7th        | CR 14            |
| 8th        | CR 16            |
| 9th        | CR 18            |
| 10th       | CR 20            |
| 11th       | CR 22            |
| 12th       | CR 24            |

---

## Spending the Budget

Spend the budget on a single creature whose Challenge Rating matches it, or on a multitude of lesser creatures until it is met.

- Fractional ratings cost their fraction: CR 1/8 costs an eighth, CR 1/4 a quarter, CR 1/2 a half.
- No creature may be called whose Challenge Rating exceeds the budget.
- Whatever is not spent is lost.

> A 5th-level slot carries CR 10: one CR 10, or two CR 5, or four CR 2 and two CR 1, or eighty CR 1/8.

---

## Multitudes

Called in numbers, they arrive already ordered. Tiny and Small answer as a **Swarm**, Medium and larger as a **Formation**, both run under [Field and Formations](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/field-and-formations) — one initiative, one set of actions, one body.

---

## What Answers

Each conjuring names what it can call, and reaches no further.

| Row            | Spell                                                       | Answers                                                           |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Wildlife**   | [Conjure Wildlife](/en/library/spells/conjure-wildlife)     | Living things of tooth, hide, bark, and instinct                  |
| **Väki**       | [Conjure Väki](/en/library/spells/conjure-vaki)             | Idea-fragments wearing a shape — Ice, flame, brine, hollow places |
| **Construct**  | [Conjure Construct](/en/library/spells/conjure-construct)   | Animated matter, whatever it was made from: forged, hewn, or dead |
| **Aberration** | [Conjure Aberration](/en/library/spells/conjure-aberration) | Hiisi, and the obsessions that walk as them                       |
| **Celestial**  | [Conjure Celestial](/en/library/spells/conjure-celestial)   | Agents of the Canvas, procedural and unglad                       |
| **Null**       | [Conjure Null](/en/library/spells/conjure-null)             | Absences — faults where meaning failed, hidden from most          |

A corpse walks by the same principle as a golem: inert matter given motion, differing only in what it was before. The Construct row holds both. Raising the dead specifically is a making rather than a calling — see [Animate Dead](/en/library/spells/animate-dead).

The GM is arbiter of what there is to be called. A thing must exist in the world to be called out of it.

---

## Command

- The called creature is friendly to you and yours.
- It shares your initiative and acts immediately after you.
- It obeys your spoken word at no cost to your action. Uncommanded, it Dodges and keeps out of reach.
- It goes when it drops to 0 hit points, and when the spell ends.
- It brings no legendary deeds, lair deeds, phase deeds, or lair effects, whatever its stat block holds. Those belong to it at home, and it is not at home.

### Loss of Control

If your concentration breaks while a called creature remains, it is not dismissed. It is **loosed**: it stays for the spell's full remaining duration, hostile to whatever stands nearest, and the GM directs it.

---

## Beyond the Budget

These call, but not on this framework:

- [Summon Rotworm](/en/library/spells/summon-rotworm) and [Grasping Vine](/en/library/spells/grasping-vine), which carry their own creature in their own text.
- [Dragonmaw](/en/library/spells/dragonmaw), [Dragonclaw](/en/library/spells/dragonclaw), [Dragonwing](/en/library/spells/dragonwing), [Dragonbreath](/en/library/spells/dragonbreath), and [Dragon Whole](/en/library/spells/dragon-whole) — these change you. Nothing is called.
