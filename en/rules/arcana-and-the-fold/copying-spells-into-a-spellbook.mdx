# Copying Spells into a Spellbook

## Overview

This rule describes the procedures, time, and cost required to copy spells into a spellbook, to replace or transcribe spells between books, and what a wizard's spellbook typically is. It governs how a spell becomes part of a wizard's written repertoire and how prepared spells can be recovered if a book is lost.

## Mechanics

When learning and recording a spell in a spellbook, a wizard must spend time experimenting with the spell and acquire the fine inks and materials necessary to record it. After paying the required time and expense, the spell is treated as part of the wizard's spellbook and can be prepared like the wizard's other spells.

Replacing the book. A wizard can copy a spell from the wizard's own spellbook into another book (for example, to create a backup). Copying a spell from your own book uses the same general procedure as copying a new spell into your spellbook but is faster and easier because you already understand your notation and know how to cast the spell. The cost and time to copy a spell in this case are 1 hour and 10 gp per level of the copied spell.

Transcribing prepared spells after loss. If you lose your spellbook, you can use the same transcription procedure to write the spells you currently have prepared into a new spellbook. Restoring only the spells you have prepared requires that you transcribe those known/prepared spells; filling the remainder of the new spellbook requires finding and copying new spells as normal.

The book's appearance. A wizard's spellbook is a unique compilation of spells with decorative flourishes and margin notes. It might be a plain, functional leather volume received as a gift, a finely bound gilt-edged tome recovered from an ancient library, or a loose collection of notes assembled after loss of a previous spellbook. Many wizards therefore keep backup spellbooks in a secure place.

## Interactions

- Resource interaction: Copying and transcribing spells consumes both time and gp; the explicit rate for copying from your own book is 1 hour and 10 gp per spell level.
- Preparation interaction: Once a spell is transcribed and the time and cost are paid, that spell can be prepared like any other entry in the wizard's spellbook.
- Recovery limits: Only spells currently prepared can be transcribed from memory into a replacement book; recovering the rest of a lost book requires locating and copying additional spells using the normal procedures.
