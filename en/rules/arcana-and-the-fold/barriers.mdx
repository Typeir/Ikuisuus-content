# Barriers

A barrier is a spell that puts something between you and what is coming. They are siblings, not copies — each one has its own rider, its own damage, and its own reason to exist. What follows applies to all of them, so that no barrier has to restate it.

---

## Casting a Barrier

Every barrier can be cast with a **Major Action** or as a **Reaction**. A wall raised after the arrow is loosed is worth more than a wall raised before anyone drew, and a caster who has been holding a slot all fight should be allowed to spend it at the moment it matters.

Casting a barrier as a Reaction does not change anything else about it: same range, same shape, same duration, same concentration.

## Solid and Energy Barriers

Barriers are made of two different kinds of thing, and the difference decides one question: **does an area effect stop at the wall?**

A **solid barrier** is matter — stone, ice, thorn, water, packed sand. It occupies its space and refuses to share it. An area effect that would cross a solid barrier **breaks against it** and does not continue past. A cone of fire aimed through a basalt wall scorches the basalt.

An **energy barrier** is not matter — light, wind, flame, whirling blades. It does exactly what its own text says it does, and nothing more. **Area effects pass straight through it.** A wall of fire blocks nothing; it merely burns whatever walks into it.

## Barriers and Palls

A **barrier** is a wall — something raised in a space, standing between one place and another, which anyone may walk up to and be stopped by.

A **pall** is worn. It hangs on a creature and travels with it: a heavy, dense curtain drawn close rather than a wall stood up. [Thermal Pall](/en/library/spells/thermal-pall), [Archivists' Pall](/en/library/spells/archivists-pall), and [Mental Pall](/en/library/spells/mental-pall) are palls, and nothing on this page applies to them — they have no length, no shape to choose, and no side to be on.

If a spell wards the one who wears it, it is a pall. If it divides a room, it is a barrier.

## Leaving a Barrier Behind

Some solid barriers become ordinary matter when their duration runs out rather than vanishing. A barrier does this **only if you are still within the spell's casting range when the duration ends**. Walk away from your wall and it goes with you.

An abandoned wall is a nonmagical object from that point on. It cannot be dispelled, and it weathers, melts, rots, or is quarried like anything else of its kind.
