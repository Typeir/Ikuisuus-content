# Multiclass Spellcasting

This section governs how spellcasting works for characters with levels in more than one spellcasting class. It explains how to determine available spell slots, how spells known and prepared are handled for each class, how spellcasting ability and foci apply, and how Pact Magic interacts with the Spellcasting feature.

## Preparing and Casting Spells

### Determining Known and Prepared Spells

- If you have the Spellcasting feature from more than one class, you determine what spells you know and can prepare for each class individually, as if you were a single‑classed member of that class.
  - Example: If you are a ranger 4 / wizard 3, you know the ranger spells known for a 4th‑level ranger, and you know and prepare spells as a 3rd‑level wizard (for example, three wizard cantrips and a spellbook containing ten wizard spells, two of which gained when you reached 3rd level can be 2nd‑level spells). If your Intelligence is 16, you can prepare six wizard spells from your spellbook.

- Each spell you know and prepare is associated with one of your classes, and you use the spellcasting ability of that class when you cast the spell.
- A spellcasting focus (such as a holy symbol) can be used only for the spells from the class associated with that focus.

### Spell Slots (Multiclass Spellcaster)

- To determine your available spell slots, add together all your levels in the bard, cleric, druid, sorcerer, and wizard classes, and add half your levels (rounded down) in the paladin and ranger classes. Use this total to determine your spell slots by consulting the Multiclass Spellcaster table below.
- If you have more than one spellcasting class, the table might give you spell slots of a level that is higher than the spells you know or can prepare. You can use those slots, but only to cast the spells you know — using a higher‑level slot can enhance the effect of a lower‑level spell (for example, burning hands) if the spell has an enhanced effect when cast using a higher‑level slot.

### Pact Magic and Spellcasting Interaction

- If you have both the Spellcasting feature and the Pact Magic class feature from the warlock class, you can use the spell slots you gain from the Pact Magic feature to cast spells you know or have prepared from classes with the Spellcasting class feature.
- You can also use the spell slots you gain from the Spellcasting class feature to cast warlock spells you know.

### Examples and Special Notes

- Example (combined usage): A ranger 4 / wizard 3 counts as a 5th‑level character for determining multiclass spell slots; that character would have four 1st‑level slots, three 2nd‑level slots, and two 3rd‑level slots per the table at 5th combined level. However, the character knows ranger spells appropriate to a 4th‑level ranger and knows/prepares wizard spells appropriate to a 3rd‑level wizard; the character cannot learn 3rd‑level ranger spells simply because a slot of that level exists.
- Using a higher‑level slot for a lower‑level spell can enhance the spell's effect if that spell's description provides an enhanced effect when cast at a higher level.

## Interactions

- Spellcasting ability, spellcasting foci, and species/class feature interactions are determined by the class that provides the spell. Do not substitute a different class's spellcasting ability for a spell associated with another class.
- Pact Magic and Spellcasting mix only in the ways described above; pay attention to which spells are associated with which class when applying ability modifiers, spell preparation rules, and foci.
