# Areas of Effect and Schools of Magic

This entry covers the attack and save mechanics for spell effects, and the descriptive schools of magic used to categorize spells.

The geometry of area shapes (cone, cube, cylinder, line, sphere), points of origin, lines of effect, and their interaction with cover are covered in [Movement, Maps, and Shapes](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/movement-maps-and-shapes).

## Spellcasting Mechanics

### Saving Throws

- When a spell allows a saving throw, the spell specifies which ability the target uses for the save and what happens on a success or failure.
- The DC to resist one of your spells equals 8 + your spellcasting ability modifier + your proficiency bonus + any special modifiers imposed by features, items, or other effects.

### Attack Rolls for Spells

- Some spells require the caster to make an attack roll to determine whether the spell effect hits a target. Most spells that require attack rolls are ranged attacks.
- Spell attack rolls follow the general attack procedure in [Making an Attack](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/making-an-attack).
- Your spell attack bonus equals your spellcasting ability modifier + your proficiency bonus, plus any other modifiers that apply.
- You have disadvantage on a ranged attack roll if you are within 5 feet of a hostile creature that can see you and that creature is not incapacitated.

## Schools of Magic (descriptive)

- Academies of magic group spells into eight categories called the schools of magic. Scholars, particularly wizards, apply these categories to spells as a way to describe how they function; the schools have no independent rules of their own, though some other rules and features refer to the schools.

- Abjuration
  - Abjuration spells are protective in nature. They create magical barriers, negate harmful effects, harm trespassers, or banish creatures to other planes of existence.

- Conjuration
  - Conjuration spells involve the transportation of objects and creatures from one location to another. Some conjurations summon creatures or objects to the caster’s side; others teleport the caster or create objects or effects out of nothing.

- Divination
  - Divination spells reveal information: secrets long forgotten, glimpses of the future, locations of hidden things, the truth behind illusions, or visions of distant people or places.

- Enchantment
  - Enchantment spells affect the minds of others, influencing or controlling their behavior. They can make enemies see the caster as a friend, force creatures to take a course of action, or control another creature like a puppet.

- Evocation
  - Evocation spells manipulate magical energy to produce a desired effect. Some evoke blasts of fire or lightning; others channel positive energy to heal wounds.

- Illusion
  - Illusion spells deceive the senses or minds of others. They cause people to see things that are not there, to miss things that are there, to hear phantom noises, or to remember things that never happened. Some illusions create phantom images or otherwise alter perception.

(Nota bene: the source references that there are eight schools; only the schools described in the source are listed here verbatim.)

## Interactions

- Spell saving throw DC and spell attack bonus are used by many other rules and features; calculate them as described above and apply any additional modifiers from class features, magic items, or circumstances.
- Area geometry and cover interactions are covered in [Movement, Maps, and Shapes](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/movement-maps-and-shapes).
