# The Crux of the Cast

Spellcasting in Damocles demands both physical discipline and mental focus. These rules govern the components a caster must supply and the concentration required to sustain ongoing effects.

---

## Spell Components

A spell's components are the physical requirements the caster must satisfy to produce its effect. Each spell lists one or more of the following component types.

### Verbal (V)

A verbal component requires the caster to speak resonant words, intone specific syllables, or otherwise produce sound with their voice. A creature that is silenced or within an area of magical silence cannot cast a spell with a verbal component. A gagged creature cannot cast a spell with a verbal component.

### Somatic (S)

A somatic component requires the caster to perform forceful and precise gestures. The caster must have at least one hand free to perform these gestures. A creature that is restrained or whose hands are bound cannot cast a spell with a somatic component unless a feature or item explicitly bypasses this requirement.

Armour does not interfere with somatic components if the caster is proficient with the armour worn.

### Material (M)

A material component is a physical object or substance consumed or manipulated during casting. The caster must have a free hand to access the component, but it can be the same hand used for somatic components.

A spellcaster may use a **spellcasting focus** or a **component pouch** in place of any material component that does not have a listed gold-piece cost and is not consumed by the spell.

If a material component has a gold-piece cost listed, the caster must possess that specific component. If the component is consumed, the spell consumes it on casting; otherwise the component is retained.

### Component Notation

In spell descriptions:

- **V** denotes a verbal component.
- **S** denotes a somatic component.
- **M** denotes a material component, followed by the component in parentheses. If a cost or consumption applies, it is noted: e.g. _M (a diamond worth 300 gp, consumed)_.

## Concentration

Some spells require the caster to maintain concentration to sustain their effect. A spell that requires concentration is marked with a duration entry that includes "Concentration" (e.g. _Duration: Concentration, up to 1 minute_).

### Concentration Limits

A creature can concentrate on only one spell at a time. Casting a new spell that requires concentration ends concentration on the previous spell immediately. A creature cannot concentrate on two spells simultaneously.

### Breaking Concentration

The following events can break concentration:

- **Taking damage.** When a creature concentrating on a spell takes damage, it must succeed on a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher.
- **Incapacitation or death.** A creature that is incapacitated or killed loses concentration.
- **Environmental disruption.** The DM may call for a Constitution saving throw when the caster is subjected to extreme conditions — a wave crashing over the deck of a ship, an earthquake, or similar events. The DC is set by the DM based on the severity of the disruption.
- **Voluntary end.** The caster can end concentration at any time, requiring no action.

---

## Ritual Casting

Some spells are tagged with the **Ritual** descriptor. A spell with the Ritual tag can be cast without expending a spell slot, provided the caster has the spell prepared or known.

Casting a spell as a ritual adds **10 minutes** to the casting time. The spell is cast at its lowest level and cannot be upcast. All other requirements — components, range, targets — remain unchanged.

Only spells that have the Ritual tag in their description can be cast in this way. The Ritual tag does not grant any other benefits, and a spell cast as a ritual does not count against the caster's limit of one concentration spell if the spell requires concentration.
