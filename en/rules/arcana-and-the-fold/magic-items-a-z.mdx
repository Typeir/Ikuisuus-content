# Magic Items A–Z

## Overview

This page collects alphabetical item entries and the general rules that govern how magic items function, how they are activated, and how their limited resources (charges, consumables, ammunition) behave. It covers command words, action requirements, how spells are cast from items, and several exemplar items (Adamantine Armor; Ammunition; Amulet of Health; Amulet of Proof against Detection and Location; Amulet of the Planes).

## Mechanics

- Activation and actions

  - If an item requires an action to activate, that action is not the same as the Use an Item action. Features that specify the Use an Item action (for example, the rogue’s Fast Hands) therefore cannot be used to activate an item that specifically requires its own action to activate.
  - A command word is a word or phrase that must be spoken for an item to work. A magic item that requires a command word cannot be activated in an area where sound is prevented (for example, inside the area of a silence spell).

- Charges

  - Some magic items have charges that must be expended to activate their properties. The number of charges an item has remaining is revealed when an identify spell is cast on it, and when a creature attunes to it.
  - Certain items use special rules for regaining charges; an item’s description specifies how and when it regains charges (refer to each item entry for its regain schedule and limits).

- Consumables

  - Some items are used up when they are activated. A potion or an elixir must be swallowed, or an oil applied to the body. The writing vanishes from a scroll when it is read. Once used, a consumable item loses its magic and is no longer magical or usable for that effect.

- Ammunition (magic ammunition: +1, +2, or +3)

  - Magic ammunition grants a bonus to attack rolls and damage rolls made with that ammunition. The bonus is determined by the rarity of the ammunition.
  - Typical rarity → bonus mapping shown by item rarity: uncommon (+1), rare (+2), very rare (+3). Use the ammunition’s entry for exact rarity and bonus.
  - Once the ammunition hits a target, that piece of ammunition is no longer magical.

- Spells cast from items

  - Some magic items allow the user to cast a spell from the item. The spell is cast at the lowest possible spell level and does not expend any of the user’s spell slots.
  - Unless an item’s description says otherwise, a spell cast from an item requires no components. The spell uses its normal casting time, range, and duration, and the user of the item must concentrate if the spell requires concentration.
  - Many items (for example, potions) bypass the casting of a spell and simply confer the spell’s effects with their usual duration.
  - When an item requires you to use your own spellcasting ability to cast a spell from the item, and you have more than one spellcasting ability, you choose which of your spellcasting ability modifiers applies to the item. If you do not have any spellcasting ability, treat the modifier for the item as +0; your proficiency bonus does not apply unless an item’s description explicitly says it does.

## Example Items (excerpted entries)

### Adamantine Armor

Armor (medium or heavy, but not hide; uncommon)

This suit of armor is reinforced with adamantine, one of the hardest substances in existence. While you’re wearing it, any critical hit against you becomes a normal hit.

### Ammunition, +1, +2, or +3

Weapon (any ammunition; uncommon (+1), rare (+2), or very rare (+3))

You have a bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this piece of magic ammunition. The bonus is determined by the rarity of the ammunition. Once the ammunition hits a target, the ammunition is no longer magical.

### Amulet of Health

Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement)

Your Constitution score is 19 while you wear this amulet. It has no effect on you if your Constitution is already 19 or higher.

### Amulet of Proof against Detection and Location

Wondrous item, uncommon (requires attunement)

While wearing this amulet, you are hidden from divination magic. You can’t be targeted by such magic or perceived through magical scrying sensors.

### Amulet of the Planes

Wondrous item, very rare (requires attunement)

- While you wear this amulet, you can use an action to name a location that you are familiar with on another plane of existence. Then make a DC 15 Intelligence check.
- On a successful check, you cast the plane shift spell (transporting yourself and any willing creatures you touch) to the chosen location.
- On a failed check, you and each creature and object within 15 feet of you travel to a random destination. Roll a d100 and consult the result:
  1. d100 1–60: You travel to a random location on the plane you named.
  2. d100 61–100: You travel to a randomly determined plane of existence.

(When the item forces a random destination, use the DM’s table for random planar destinations or the item’s specified table if provided in the full entry.)

## Interactions

- Silence and command words: Any item that depends on a spoken command word cannot function in an area where sound is suppressed (for example, within a silence spell). Items that require other activation methods are unaffected by silence.
- Items vs. Use an Item action: Because some item activations are their own actions, class features and abilities that depend on the Use an Item action cannot be used to activate those items unless the item’s description explicitly says otherwise.
- Concentration and item-cast spells: If a spell cast from an item requires concentration, the user must concentrate on it just as when casting the same spell normally. Subsequent damage or effects that would break concentration may end the spell.
- Attunement and identification: The number of charges on an item is visible when an identify spell is cast on the item and is revealed when a creature attunes to it; check an item’s description for rules about regaining charges and any special charge-handling behavior.
