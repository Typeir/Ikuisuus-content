# Black Rot

Nigredo, lord of flies and rot, is the source of a terrible disease known as **Black Rot**.
This illness is caused by the spores of the fungus **Stachybotrys Ventralis**, a strain of fungi-like creatures made of his very essence.
Nigredo spreads these spores through the air and his very touch.

Many servants of Nigredo can use the **Black Rot**, and when afflicted, a character must roll on the table below to determine the specific symptom they suffer.
Multiple symptoms can stack.

| **Roll** | **Effect**                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0~5      | **Black Cough**: Disadvantage on **Constitution saves to maintain concentration**.                                                                                                                                    |
| 6~10     | **Thinned Blood**: Lose **1 Hit Die** (of your largest die type).                                                                                                                                                     |
| 11~15    | **Septic Fever**: During long rests, you **do not regain Hit Dice**.                                                                                                                                                  |
| 16~20    | **Open Lesions**: When you take damage, you **bleed**: take **10 necrotic damage** at the start of your next turn. This damage doubles each subsequent turn. An **action** or **any magical healing** ends the bleed. |
| 21~25    | **Suppurating Nodes**: Disadvantage on **saving throws vs. poison and disease**; **Medicine checks** made to treat you have **disadvantage**.                                                                         |
| 26~30    | **Necrotic Malaise**: Disadvantage on **Strength checks**.                                                                                                                                                            |
| 31~35    | **Rot-Stiff**: **Speed –5 ft.**                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| 36~40    | **Cartilage Dissolution**: Disadvantage on **Dexterity saving throws**.                                                                                                                                               |
| 41~45    | **Muscle Wasting**: **–4 Strength** (score).                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 46~50    | **Rotgut**: **–4 Constitution** (score).                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 51~55    | **Tremor Fever**: **–4 Dexterity** (score).                                                                                                                                                                           |
| 56~60    | **Sluggish Heart**: Disadvantage on **initiative**; you cannot **Dash** unless you first succeed on a **DC 18 Constitution save** that turn.                                                                          |
| 61~65    | **Infected Wounds**: When you spend Hit Dice during a **short rest**, you regain **only half** the HP rolled (round down).                                                                                            |
| 66~70    | **Fungal Paralysis**: You **cannot take reactions**; at the start of each of your turns, roll a d6—on **1–3** you **cannot take the Action** that turn.                                                               |
| 71~75    | **Sepsis**: You are perpetually **poisoned**. At the end of each **long rest**, make a **DC 18 Constitution save**; on a success, this poisoned condition is halted that day.                                         |
| 76~80    | **Osteonecrosis**: You become vulnerable to bludgeoning and thunder damage.                                                                                                                                           |
| 81~85    | **Organ Rot**: Your **maximum hit points are reduced by 4d10** (to a minimum of 1).                                                                                                                                   |
| 86~90    | **Flesh Slough**: You become vulnerable to **piercing or slashing** damage.                                                                                                                                           |
| 91~95    | **Dry Gangrene**: **Magical healing you receive is halved**, and you **cannot regain HP from Hit Dice** during short rests.                                                                                           |
| 96~98    | **Blackened Brain**: You are affected by Feeblemind.                                                                                                                                                                  |
| 99       | **Putrefactive Collapse**: Your maximum hit points are halved. You are perpetually poisoned. Your movement speed is halved.                                                                                           |
| 100      | **Tombsteel Skeleton**: Your bones begin ossifying into **tombsteel**. Immediately take **8d8 necrotic damage**, and gain the [_Tombsteel Skeleton_](/en/library/items/heirlooms/tombsteel-skeleton) sickness.                   |

---

## Curing Black Rot 
Like any sickness of magical nature, it can be removed by the usage of the [_Lesser Restoration_](/en/library/spells/lesser-restoration), [_Greater Restoration_](/en/library/spells/greater-restoration) or [_Remove Curse_](/en/library/spells/remove-curse) spells, or similar magic.
