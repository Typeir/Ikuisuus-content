# Erosion

A creeping affliction born of **Nullbrand’s spite**, Erosion is the unraveling of a creature’s physical and metaphysical cohesion.  
Unlike most ailments, its effects are immediate and cumulative. Every new stack corrodes what little definition remains of body and will.

When a creature gains a **stack of Erosion**, roll **1d4** and **subtract that number** from all of the following:

- **Attack rolls**
- **Armor Class**
- **Saving throws**
- **Ability checks**

These penalties are **permanent** until the Erosion is removed.  
Subsequent stacks add additional penalties. The effects of multiple stacks **stack cumulatively without limit**.  
A creature reduced to 0 or lower Armor Class or unable to roll above 1 on a d20 still counts as having a minimum result of **1**, but all attack rolls against it automatically hit.

| **Stack Count** | **Manifestation**                                                                                                                                                               |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1–2             | **Damaged Vessel**: Small flakes of skin and light trails shed when you move.                                                                                                 |
| 3–4             | **Rusted Essence**: Your shadow vibrates slightly; metallic objects on you rust and lose polish.                                                                                       |
| 5–6             | **Atemporal Drift**: Your voice delays half a second behind your speech; light distorts near you.                                                                               |
| 7–8             | **Decaying Shape**: You leave a faint trail of ash or brine when you walk.                                                                                                       |
| 9–10            | **Crumbling Form**: Your body begins to desaturate. Each dawn, make a **DC 20 Constitution save** or take **8d10 force damage** as you shed fragments of yourself into nothing. |
| 11–15           | **Nullbrand’s Claim**: You can no longer cast spells or maintain concentration. Any spell you attempt fizzles and deals **4d10 force damage** to you instead.                   |
| 16+             | **Unmooring**: You become translucent and lose all sense of touch. If you start your turn with 0 HP, you disintegrate entirely into inert dust and rust.                     |

**Removal.**  
Only the following effects can reduce Erosion:

- [_Greater Restoration_](/en/library/spells/greater-restoration)
- [_Wish_](/en/library/spells/wish)
- [_Remove Curse_](/en/library/spells/remove-curse), cast as a fifth level spell.
- Complete immersion for 24 hours in **unsullied water**, untouched by brine or machine oil.

No other magic can restore what has been unmade.
