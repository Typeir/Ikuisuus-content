# Illnesses

Illnesses in Damocles are not abstractions of poor sanitation or unlucky bites. They are consequences — of proximity to entities, of lingering in Fold-tainted spaces, of contact with substances that should not exist outside the Fold's architecture. Each illness has an origin, and that origin is specific.

The sample diseases of other settings do not apply here. A creature infected by Nigredo is not suffering from sewer plague; it is suffering from **Black Rot**, and the cure, if one exists, requires understanding what Nigredo is.

---

## How Illnesses Work

### Transmission and Infection

How a creature contracts an illness depends entirely on the illness. Most Damocles illnesses are not contagious in the mundane sense — they spread through specific vectors tied to the entity or phenomenon responsible. An illness entry specifies:

- **Source**: which entity, creature, or environment causes it
- **Transmission**: what action or condition triggers infection (bite, proximity, spore inhalation, contact, Fold-exposure)
- **Saving throw**: the Constitution save DC required to resist infection upon exposure

When exposure occurs, the creature makes the specified save. Failure means infection takes hold; success means the creature has resisted for this exposure. Repeated exposures may require repeated saves at the Game Director's discretion.

### Incubation and Symptom Onset

Each illness entry specifies an incubation period — the time before symptoms manifest after infection. Until that period elapses, the creature is infected but shows no effects. The Game Director is not obliged to disclose whether a character is infected during incubation.

### Saving Throws and Progression

Many illnesses worsen over time unless the creature actively fights them. Unless an illness entry specifies otherwise:

- At the end of each long rest, an infected creature makes a Constitution saving throw against the illness's DC.
- **On a failed save**: the creature gains a new symptom roll or the illness advances.
- **On a successful save**: the illness does not advance. Some illnesses specify that successive successes can reduce symptoms — consult the illness entry.

Some illnesses impose permanent effects that cannot be reversed by rest alone. These entries will specify what, if anything, can remove the condition.

### Recovery and Cure

Recovery conditions vary by illness. Some illnesses resolve with enough successful saves. Others require:

- A specific reagent or ritual tied to the entity that caused them
- Removal of the creature from the afflicting environment
- Magical intervention — where healing magic applies, the illness entry states it

The Game Director may adjudicate unusual cures. Illnesses sourced from Fold entities are generally not curable by generic restoration magic; they require understanding the entity's nature.

### Scale and Outbreaks

A single infected creature is a personal crisis. A spreading outbreak is a plot event. The mechanical framework above handles the personal case; outbreak scale, contamination spread, and societal response are narrative decisions for the Game Director. If an area is broadly contaminated (such as a Precursor-saturated mine), creatures may face repeated exposure with every rest taken in that space.
