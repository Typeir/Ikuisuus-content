# Illnesses

Not every affliction is a curse laid by a god. The world of Damocles still holds mundane sicknesses — fevers, infections, plagues — that spread through natural vectors and respond to natural remedies.

These are ordinary illnesses. They follow the logic of contagion, environment, and biology rather than the active attention of a cosmological entity. A character who catches camp fever from a rain-soaked march is sick in the way any soldier might be sick — it is not personal. It is simply the cost of living in a world that does not care whether you survive.

For supernatural afflictions tied to specific entities, see [Curses](/en/library/rules/life-blood-and-ruin/curses).

---

## How Mundane Illnesses Work

### Transmission

Mundane illnesses spread through ordinary vectors: contaminated water, spoiled food, proximity to the sick, infected wounds, exposure to weather. The Game Director determines the specific vector based on the fiction. Unlike curses, mundane illnesses do not require an entity source — they arise from the environment itself.

### Saving Throws and Progression

A mundane illness typically calls for a Constitution saving throw at a DC appropriate to its severity. Progression follows the same structure as curses: failed saves advance the illness, successful saves hold or reduce it.

### Recovery

Mundane illnesses respond to rest, mundane medicine, and magical healing. A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Medicine) check during a Recovery can halt progression. _Lesser Restoration_ removes most mundane illnesses outright.

The Game Director may introduce specific illnesses as the fiction demands, using these guidelines as a baseline. Unlike curses, mundane illnesses do not require understanding an entity to cure — only time, care, and competent medicine.

## Example Mundane Illnesses

| Illness    | Vector                    | DC  | Effect                                                             |
| ---------- | ------------------------- | --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Camp Fever | Exposure, poor sanitation | 12  | Disadvantage on Constitution saves; -1 to all ability checks.      |
| Wound Rot  | Untreated injuries        | 14  | Cannot regain HP during Repose; Medicine check each day or worsen. |
| Bog Lung   | Inhaled swamp vapours     | 13  | Speed halved; cannot Dash.                                         |
| Red Pox    | Contact with infected     | 15  | -2 Constitution; contagious to creatures within 5 feet.            |

The Game Director is encouraged to invent additional mundane illnesses as the story demands. These are not cosmic events — they are the grit of a world that grinds down the unprepared.
