# Madness

## Overview

Madness represents temporary or lasting psychological breaks—short-term, long-term, or indefinite—that impose mechanical penalties and unusual behaviours on a character. Magical effects (for example, certain spells and artifacts), diseases, poisons, and planar phenomena can inflict madness. Some spells (such as contact other plane and symbol) can cause insanity; the madness rules below may be used in place of those spell effects when appropriate.

## Mechanics

### Inflicting Madness

- A madness-inducing effect typically calls for a saving throw (Wisdom or Charisma, as specified by the effect). If the save fails, determine which madness table applies (short-term, long-term, or indefinite) and roll on that table.
- Short-term madness effects are temporary and measured in minutes. Long-term madness effects last longer (hours). Indefinite madness persists until cured.
- Some magic, artifacts, diseases, poisons, and planar effects can cause madness; the text of those effects indicates whether to use the madness tables in place of or in addition to other consequences.

### Resisting Madness

- Resisting a madness-inducing effect usually requires a Wisdom or Charisma saving throw. The specific effect indicates the DC and which ability to use.
- When a madness effect grants a duration, the duration (short-term: minutes, long-term: hours, indefinite: until cured) determines how long the effect persists unless removed by magic or other restorative methods.

### Short-Term Madness (d100)

- Duration: 1d10 minutes. While afflicted by a short-term madness effect, apply the listed behaviour and any mechanical penalties described.

| d100   | Effect                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 01–10  | The character feels compelled to repeat a specific activity over and over, such as washing hands, touching things, praying, or counting coins.                                                                                       |
| 11–20  | The character experiences vivid hallucinations and has disadvantage on ability checks.                                                                                                                                               |
| 21–30  | The character suffers extreme paranoia. The character has disadvantage on Wisdom and Charisma checks.                                                                                                                                |
| 31–40  | The character regards something (usually the source of the madness) with intense revulsion, as if affected by the antipathy effect of the antipathy/sympathy spell.                                                                  |
| 41–45  | The character experiences a powerful delusion. Choose a potion (or similar simple item); the character imagines that he or she is under its effects.                                                                                 |
| 46–55  | The character becomes attached to a “lucky charm,” such as a person or an object, and has disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws while more than 30 feet from it.                                           |
| 56–65  | The character is blinded (25%) or deafened (75%)—roll or adjudicate per DM.                                                                                                                                                          |
| 66–75  | The character experiences uncontrollable tremors or tics, which impose disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws that involve Strength or Dexterity.                                                           |
| 76–85  | The character suffers from partial amnesia. The character knows who he or she is and retains racial traits and class features, but doesn’t recognize other people or remember anything that happened before the madness took effect. |
| 86–90  | Whenever the character takes damage, he or she must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or be affected as though he or she failed a saving throw against the confusion spell. The confusion effect lasts for 1 minute.            |
| 91–95  | The character loses the ability to speak (mute) for the duration.                                                                                                                                                                    |
| 96–100 | The character falls unconscious for the duration; no amount of jostling or minor damage can wake the character while this short-term effect lasts.                                                                                   |

### Long-Term Madness (d100)

- Duration: 1d10 × 10 hours. Long-term madness effects persist for that duration unless cured or removed by magic or other means. Apply the described behaviour and mechanical consequences for the duration.

| d100   | Effect                                                                                                                       |
| ------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 01–20  | The character retreats into his or her mind and becomes paralyzed. The effect ends if the character takes any damage.        |
| 21–30  | The character becomes incapacitated and spends the duration screaming, laughing, or weeping.                                 |
| 31–40  | The character becomes frightened and must use his or her action and movement each round to flee from the source of the fear. |
| 41–50  | The character begins babbling and is incapable of normal speech or spellcasting.                                             |
| 51–60  | The character must use his or her action each round to attack the nearest creature.                                          |
| 61–70  | The character experiences vivid hallucinations and has disadvantage on ability checks.                                       |
| 71–75  | The character does whatever anyone tells him or her to do that isn’t obviously self-destructive.                             |
| 76–80  | The character experiences an overpowering urge to eat something strange, such as dirt, slime, or offal.                      |
| 81–90  | The character is stunned for the duration.                                                                                   |
| 91–100 | The character falls unconscious for the duration.                                                                            |

### Indefinite Madness

- Indefinite madness lasts until cured. A character afflicted with indefinite madness gains a new character flaw from the Indefinite Madness table; that flaw persists until it is cured by appropriate magic or long-term treatment. (Use the listed Indefinite Madness table where the campaign specifies it.)

## Interactions

- The DM may substitute the madness tables for the mechanical effects of spells or artifacts that induce insanity when doing so better serves the tone of the encounter.
- Madness interacts with concentration, spellcasting, and social checks: if an effect prevents speech, the character cannot cast spells with verbal components; if the character is incapacitated or stunned, they cannot take actions, reactions, or move as usual.
- Some madness effects impose disadvantage on checks and saves; these stack with other sources of disadvantage per normal rules (two disadvantages do not worsen the effect beyond single disadvantage). Apply all explicit mechanical modifiers given by the madness entry.

## Curing Madness

- Short-term madness typically ends after its listed duration. Long-term madness ends after its duration or when cured by magic (for example, lesser restoration, greater restoration, or other effects the DM rules appropriate). Indefinite madness requires stronger or longer-term remedies as adjudicated by the DM.
