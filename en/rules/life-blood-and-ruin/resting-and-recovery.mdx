# Rest and Recovery

In Damocles, the world does not heal quickly.

Only **legendary heroes**—the kind chanted about in taverns, sung by trovadeurs, or carved into stone—can sustain **days of battle without rest**.

For ordinary mortals, no matter how skilled, the **body deteriorates** under endless strain.

Muscles tear at microscopic levels. Reflexes dull. The mind frays from overexposure to threat. Even if magic is not used directly, the Fold’s currents leak into everything touched by heirlooms.

For those who wield magicks, the **Folds are dangerous**, and using it strains the body in ways not even a surgeon can repair.

A soldier can fight for a day. A hero might fight for three.  
But without rest, **all warriors will eventually fall to exhaustion**.

---

## Sustenance

Sustenance is the act of **eating and drinking**, or performing a set of basic bodily functions to ensure your survival.
Sustenance is required to maintain your integrity as a living being.
Sustenance can be performed through the different methods of resting.

## Effects

When indulging in Sustenance, a character can consume meals of different qualities, which can be purchased or prepared.
A **Standard Meal** has no particular effects; a **Hearty Meal** removes **1 level of exhaustion**, and a **Lavish Meal** restores a single hit dice of the character's choosing.
Hearty and Lavish meals are considered **"_Fulfilling meals_"**, and will interact with different features.
Creatures whose Sustenance habits are strange, like **Silent Ones** and **Bilupines** will require their meals be accommodated to their needs, or they will gain no benefits from Sustenance.

### Adversities

If a character does not indulge in Sustenance as part of a Respite or Repose at least once every 24 hours, they will incur **1 level of exhaustion**. Every 8 hours without Sustenance after the first level is accrued, they will gain an additional level of exhaustion.

---

## Respite

A **Respite** is any period of **30 minutes** to **1 hour** spent in relative safety, where no intense activity is performed.
This time is usually accompanied by a meal, a beverage or a brief rest.

### Effects

- **Sustenance**:  
  Characters may eat, drink, and tend to wounds during this time; certain features can be used during a Respite.
- **Ability Recharge**:  
  Any abilities, heirlooms, or features that recharge on a **Respite** are refreshed after this period of **30 minutes to 1 hour**.
- **Limitations**:  
  You **cannot** fight, cast spells, or engage in strenuous activity other than preparing meals during this time.

---

## Repose

A **Repose** is any period of **8 hours** spent in relative safety, where no intense activity is performed.  
This allows for basic recovery: catching breath, tending to wounds, adjusting bearings, eating, drinking, and most importantly—Sleeping.

Some heirlooms and abilities refresh after a Repose. Some even refresh at specific intervals in time.  
But wounds do not close fully, and deeper fatigue remains.

### Effects

- **Sustenance**:  
  Characters may eat, drink, and tend to wounds during this time; certain features can be used during a Respite.
- **Hit Dice Recovery**:  
  A character may spend any number of **Hit Dice** to recover hit points. For each die spent, **roll the die and add the character’s Constitution modifier**.
- **Ability Recharge**:  
  Any abilities, heirlooms, or features that recharge on a **Repose** are refreshed after this period of **8 hours**.
- **Limitations**:  
  You **cannot** fight, cast spells, or engage in strenuous activity during this time.
- Remove **1 level of exhaustion**, provided the character has had **Sustenance** as part of a Respite or Repose in the last 24 hours.
  If the character has not had a Repose in the last 24 hours, they will **not** remove any levels of exhaustion.

### Adversities

Resting is a cornerstone of survival, but it is not always possible.
If a character is **unable to find a safe place to rest**, they may be forced to **sleep in the open** or **remain awake**, or risk ambushes, attacks, and other dangers.
When a Repose is not performed for 24 hours, a character will **gain 1 level of exhaustion**. Every 8 hours without a Repose after the first level is accrued, they will gain an additional level of exhaustion.

---

## Recovery

A **Recovery** is **10 uninterrupted days** of low-exertion living.  
This does not mean idleness, but it does mean stepping back from battle, magic, and trauma—For the most part, the vile and beautiful lands of Damocles never offered much rest for the wicked.

### Effects

- **Sustenance**:  
  Characters may eat, drink, and tend to wounds during this time; failure to meet the daily sustenance requirements will interrupt progress in the Recovery.
- **Interrupted Recovery**:  
  If a character is interrupted by strenuous combat, levelled spellcasting, or intense exertion, the Recovery will lose half of its progress, they will always lose a minimum of two days.
  Easy encounters may not incur in a loss of progress, if the character **does not fall unconscious during the encounter**, or **spends recovery related resources**.
  If a character spends resources that are only refreshed on a Recovery, they will lose half of their progress, they will always lose a minimum of two days.
- **Benefits of a Recovery**:
  - Regain **all lost hit points**.
  - Recover **spent Hit Dice** equal to half of your total Hit Dice (minimum 1).
  - Remove **Potion Sickness** effects.
  - Remove all **levels of exhaustion**.
  - Restore **spell slots and vocation features** that refresh on a Recovery.

---

## What Happens During a Recovery

Over the course of a Recovery:

- The body rebuilds tissues damaged by magic overuse.
- The mind stabilizes after exposure to horrors, both of the physical and metaphysical kind.
- Magic reservoirs, if any, regain integrity, though most mortals lack them altogether.
- Exhaustion and potion sickness fade.

---

## Important Notes

- A Recovery must not be **interrupted by no medium, hard or worse encounters, extreme magic usage, or continuous travel** though easier means of travel, such as **a caravan** or a **vehicle** can be considered resting.
- As such, an **easy** encounter might not be much in the way of a Recovery.
- Characters who cannot find rest may survive—but they will degrade. If a character does not have at least a Repose a day, they will incur **two levels of exhaustion** after a progressing Constitution saving throw (DC 15 + 5 per consecutive day awake)
- To ease technicalities, a Recovery also has all of the effects of a **Repose**.

---

## Why This Is Necessary

- A **wizard** who rips the Folds apart to ignite their will is not just casting a spell—they are **shredding the threads of their own body**, channeling alchemical precursor, and forcing seams in reality to align with personal desire.  
  This leaves scars on places no surgeon can reach.

- A **paladin** who smites their enemies is not simply calling on faith. They are **rupturing the fabric of the world with their own marrow**, letting it act as conduit and beacon.  
  This has consequences.

- Even a warrior who swings a Heirloom-blade is dragging fragments of the current through metal and tendon, wearing down unseen reserves.

---

## On Martial Recovery

Most fighters, scouts, and frontline combatants rely on **Repose recovery**, their energy replenished after a good night's sleep.

This reflects the reality of their craft: tending wounds, rewrapping grips, repositioning cracked ribs, stitching tendon back to bone, caring for their armaments...
