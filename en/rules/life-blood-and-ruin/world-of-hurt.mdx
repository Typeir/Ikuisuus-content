# World of Hurt

The world of Damocles is unforgiving. Gravity, water, and the absence of air are as lethal as any blade.

---

## Falling

A creature that falls takes damage upon impact based on the distance fallen. Falling damage is bludgeoning damage.

### Fall Damage

A creature takes **[% 1d6 bludgeoning %] per 10 feet fallen**. This damage is uncapped — there is no maximum.

### Size and Falling Damage

Larger creatures suffer greater impact from falls. The damage die increases by size category above Medium.

| Size       | Damage Die |
| ---------- | ---------- |
| Tiny       | d4         |
| Small      | d4         |
| Medium     | d6         |
| Large      | d8         |
| Huge       | d10        |
| Gargantuan | d12        |
| Colossal   | [% 2d8 %]        |
| Titanic    | [% 2d10 %]       |

A creature that falls into water or another yielding surface may take half damage, at the DM's discretion.

### Landing on a Creature

If a falling creature lands on another creature, the DM may split the falling damage between them. The creature landed on may attempt a Dexterity saving throw (DC 15) to avoid the impact entirely. On a success, the falling creature takes the full damage alone.

### Catching a Falling Creature

If a creature falls past another creature within reach, the creature below may use its reaction to attempt to catch the falling creature. Make a Strength (Athletics) check against a DC equal to 5 + the distance fallen divided by 10 (round down). On a success, the falling creature takes half the normal falling damage and the catcher takes the other half. On a failure, both creatures take the full falling damage.

## Suffocation and Drowning

A creature can hold its breath for a limited time. The DM judges whether a given creature is capable of holding its breath at all — creatures without lungs, creatures that breathe water, or creatures that do not breathe cannot suffocate or drown by mundane means.

### Holding Breath

A creature can hold its breath for a number of minutes equal to **1 + its Constitution modifier (minimum 30 seconds)**.

A creature trained or adapted to underwater environments may hold its breath for longer, at the DM's discretion.

When a creature runs out of breath, it gains the [**Suffocating**](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#suffocating) condition.

### Drowning

A creature that inhales water or another non-breathable substance gains the [**Suffocating**](/en/library/rules/steel-and-strife/conditions#suffocating) condition immediately, regardless of any remaining breath-holding time.
