# Curses

Curses in Damocles are personal. They are not generic hexes or unlucky breaks — they are the active attention of a specific cosmological entity, manifesting as corruption, decay, madness, or transformation in the afflicted. A curse is a relationship: something has noticed you, and it does not wish you well.

Each curse demands that those who would cure it first understand its source. The cure for Black Rot, if one exists, requires understanding Nigredo.

---

## How Curses Work

### Transmission and Affliction

How a creature contracts a curse depends entirely on the entity behind it. Most Damocles curses are not contagious in the mundane sense — they spread through specific vectors tied to the entity or phenomenon responsible. A curse entry specifies:

- **Source**: which entity, creature, or environment causes it
- **Transmission**: what action or condition triggers affliction (bite, proximity, spore inhalation, contact, Fold-exposure)
- **Saving throw**: the Constitution save DC required to resist affliction upon exposure

When exposure occurs, the creature makes the specified save. Failure means the curse takes hold; success means the creature has resisted for this exposure. Repeated exposures may require repeated saves at the Game Director's discretion.

### Incubation and Symptom Onset

Each curse entry specifies an incubation period — the time before symptoms manifest after affliction. Until that period elapses, the creature is cursed but shows no effects. The Game Director is not obliged to disclose whether a character is cursed during incubation.

### Saving Throws and Progression

Many curses worsen over time unless the creature actively fights them. Unless a curse entry specifies otherwise:

- At the end of each Recovery, a cursed creature makes a Constitution saving throw against the curse's DC.
- **On a failed save**: the creature gains a new symptom roll or the curse advances.
- **On a successful save**: the curse does not advance. Some curses specify that successive successes can reduce symptoms — consult the curse entry.

Some curses impose permanent effects that cannot be reversed by rest alone. These entries will specify what, if anything, can remove the condition.

### Recovery and Breaking a Curse

Recovery conditions vary by curse. Some curses resolve with enough successful saves. Others require:

- A specific reagent or ritual tied to the entity that caused them
- Removal of the creature from the afflicting environment
- Magical intervention — where magic applies, the curse entry states it

The Game Director may adjudicate unusual cures. Curses sourced from Fold entities are generally not breakable by generic restoration magic; they require understanding the entity's nature.

### Scale and Outbreaks

A single cursed creature is a personal crisis. A spreading curse is a plot event. The mechanical framework above handles the personal case; outbreak scale, contamination spread, and societal response are narrative decisions for the Game Director. If an area is broadly contaminated (such as a Precursor-saturated mine), creatures may face repeated exposure with every rest taken in that space.
