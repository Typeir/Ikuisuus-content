# Poisons

Poisons are chemical agents that deal poison damage and impose debilitating conditions. Given their insidious and deadly nature, poisons are illegal in most societies but are a favored tool among assassins, drow, and other malign practitioners.

## Types

Poisons come in four delivery types: inhaled, contact, ingested, and injury. Each type has its own delivery rules and typical effects.

## Mechanics

### Ingested

- A creature must swallow an entire dose of ingested poison to suffer its effects. A dose can be delivered in food or a liquid.
- A partial dose can have a reduced effect; the GM may rule that a partial dose grants advantage on the saving throw or deals only half damage on a failed save.

### Inhaled

- Inhaled poisons are powders or gases that take effect when inhaled.
- Blowing the powder or releasing the gas subjects creatures in a 5-foot cube to its effect; the resulting cloud dissipates immediately afterward.
- Holding one’s breath is ineffective against many inhaled poisons, as they affect nasal membranes, tear ducts, and other parts of the body.

### Contact

- Contact poison can be smeared on an object and remains potent until it is touched or washed off.
- A creature that touches contact poison with exposed skin suffers its effects.

### Injury

- Injury poison can be applied to weapons, ammunition, trap components, and other objects that deal piercing or slashing damage; it remains potent until delivered through a wound or washed off.
- A creature that takes piercing or slashing damage from an object coated with the poison is exposed to its effects.

## Interactions

- Many poisons impose the poisoned condition in addition to damage; the poisoned condition interacts normally with other systems that check or remove conditions.
- Several poisons render a creature unconscious while poisoned; such a creature wakes if it takes damage or if another creature takes an action to shake it awake.
- Partial doses reduce effects in ways determined by the GM (examples: advantage on the save, half damage on a failed save).
- Contact and injury poisons remain potent on objects until washed off or delivered through a wound.
- Inhaled poisons affect creatures in an area (5-foot cube) when released and dissipate immediately afterward; holding breath is often ineffective against them.
