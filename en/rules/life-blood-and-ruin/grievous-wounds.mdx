# Grievous Wounds

Not all harm heals with a night's rest. Grievous Wounds represent deep, lasting injury; cracked ribs, torn sinew, arcane lacerations...

---

## What Are Grievous Wounds?

Grievous Wounds are a **separate numerical pool** tracked alongside hit points. Think of it as a negative health bar: damage that has burrowed past the flesh and into the bone, the self, or the will.

- Grievous Wounds are **stacking** — a creature can accumulate any amount, to a maximum of their maximum HP, at which point they die.
- They represent lingering trauma, not open bleeding. A creature with Grievous Wounds is not visibly spurting blood; they are _diminished_.

---

## Taking Grievous Wounds

When an effect inflicts Grievous Wounds, the amount is subtracted from **both maximum HP and current HP** (current HP to a minimum of 1).

> **Example:** A creature with 40 maximum HP and 28 current HP takes 12 Grievous Wounds. Its maximum HP drops to 28, and its current HP drops to 16.

Grievous Wounds do not cause unconsciousness on their own — only the HP loss they create can drop a creature to 0.

---

## Recovering Grievous Wounds

Grievous Wounds do not heal naturally. They do not respond to magic. The only way to reduce them is **spending Hit Dice**.

- During a Rest, a creature may spend Hit Dice to reduce its Grievous Wounds.
- Roll each spent Hit Die as normal. The result is subtracted from the Grievous Wound pool — **not** added to HP.
- These Hit Dice restore no hit points. They only clear the wound debt.

> **Example:** A creature with 18 Grievous Wounds and a Constitution modifier of 0 spends 2 Hit Dice, rolling a 5 and a 4.
> Grievous Wounds drop to 9. No HP is restored.

Once Grievous Wounds reach 0, the creature's maximum HP returns to normal. Lost current HP must still be healed separately.

---

## What Does NOT Work

| Source           | Effect on Grievous Wounds                                              |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Magical healing  | **None.** Cure Wounds, potions, Lay on Hands — none reduce GW.         |
| Natural recovery | **None.** Time alone does not knit these wounds.                       |
| Temporary HP     | **Unaffected.** Temporary HP sits atop the reduced maximum regardless. |

---

## Grievous Wounds and Death

- If Grievous Wounds reduce a creature's maximum HP to 0, the creature dies.
- A creature below 1HP with Grievous Wounds still makes death saving throws normally. The GW pool does not interact with death saves.
- If a creature dies while carrying Grievous Wounds and is revived, the GW pool persists — resurrection does not erase lasting injury.

---

## Sources of Grievous Wounds

Grievous Wounds are inflicted by specific effects, not by ordinary damage:

- **MISDEED overshoot:** Overexerting oneself through DEEDS (see the Grit & Deeds system).
- **Certain monster features:** Abilities that rend soul, shatter bones, or drain vitality.
- **Environmental hazards:** Exposure, corrupted terrain, akashic fallout.
- **GM adjudication:** Any effect the GM deems too severe for simple HP loss.

Ordinary weapon attacks, spells, and falling do **not** inflict Grievous Wounds unless explicitly stated.
