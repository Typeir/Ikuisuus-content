# 1. The Northern Federation of Binturia

Binturia is a northern secular nation defined by its militaristic culture and the distinct heritage of its people, the **Sunborn**.
These beastlike descendants appeared in the aftermath of **Päimär’s** death, shaped by the remains of the fallen sun god.
Their existence became the core of Binturia’s identity; symbols of endurance, transformation, and pride.

The nation itself is a federation of loosely allied states bound by shared defense and trade.
Though the alliance was once among the strongest powers of the north, its dominance ended with the **War of the Mists**, when the **Brume Empire** crushed its armies and seized nearly half its territories.
The defeat marked the beginning of a long decline, but not of submission.

---

## 1.1 Traditions of Binturia

In daily life, Binturians retain a communal, martial ethos.
Training camps and public drills are ordinary sights, and children are encouraged to join from an early age.
Alongside this, the arts—particularly music and poetry—remain deeply respected, forming the cultural backbone of the Binturian “poet-warrior” ideal, where discipline and expression share the same breath.

Binturia’s adherence to tradition has also slowed its technological progress, albeit not to the extremes of nations like **The Holy Brume Empire**.
In choosing to perfect the art of war through discipline rather than machinery, the federation has found itself increasingly vulnerable.
Coastal raids and piracy are now common, and outsiders often speak of Binturia as a dying nation.
Still, its people remain fiercely proud, sustained by the conviction that their endurance will outlast that of their conquerors.

---

## 1.2 Governance

At the head of the federation sits the **High Comairle**, a council made up of the foremost military leaders from each state.
In principle, this body governs through cooperation, but in practice, the northern toll-town of **Gemraedgh** holds decisive authority due to its newly granted veto power.
This shift has effectively made Gemraedgh—and its leader—Binturia’s true center of power.

---

## 1.3 Faith and the Cult of Kuutar

Though the federation has no official religion, the Cult of [Kuutar](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/kuutar) maintains a complex and often contradictory relationship with its rulers.
The cult venerates the fallen moon goddess **[Kuutar](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/kuutar)**, and many among the **Sunborn** view her as a figure of salvation.
Within the cult’s teachings lies the prophecy of **Ludwig, the Moon-Blade**, believed to be a returning messiah who will restore balance.
Certain members of the **Binturian Hinterlands’** upper ranks are rumored to hold allegiance to this faith, though the government neither confirms nor denies it.

---

## 1.4 The Last Eclipse Project

The **Binturian Federation**’s ambitions once reached beyond its current borders.
During its brief occupation of **the Moon’s Gambit**, its forces uncovered ancient records detailing the creation of **moon-touched god-warriors**, among them **Ludwig the Blood Plague**.
Inspired by these discoveries, the federation began a secret project—**Last Eclipse**—intended to recreate such beings.
The experiments met with mixed results, though their continuation remains unacknowledged by any official source.

## 1.6 The Silver Hands

Privately, **Aislinget Lirinargh** of **Gemraedgh**, alongside **Cillianet Chaoim** of **Faolett** and **Sorchargh Chaoim** of **Byrnen**, has entered direct contact with the **Red Queen**, now called **Mother Moon**.
In return for supplying her with information and resources concerning the **Golden Path**, she provides guidance in refining Binturia’s **moon-touched** systems and a steady flow of **Bloodletters** and **Lunar Kystepods** to strengthen their armies.

Aislinget has gone further than his peers.
His own children, **Fionnulet** and **Tadhgen Lirinargh**, were subjected to the moon-touching ritual and now serve as his personal guards within a paramilitary order known as **The Silver Hands**.
