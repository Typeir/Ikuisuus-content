# The cosmology of Damocles

The _Vile and Beautiful lands of Damocles_ were once but a speck of null in an ocean of void;  
a thought unfinished, suspended between what could be and what should not.  
In the depths of that stillness stirred the first tremor of meaning—  
a whisper that would one day name itself **existence**.

So began the long descent of order into form,  
the slow burning of nothing into everything,  
and the chain of ages that would give birth to gods, worlds, grief and joy.

## 0. The Age of Nulls

Before time, before stars, there was the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)**—a blank, breathing mechanism without a purpose.  
It did not sleep or wait; it simply _was_, an infinite field of potential unable to dream of itself. Within it, there were no names, no gods, no questions—only the potentialof everything that might ever be.

The **[Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls)** were not beings but **absences or faults**; each Null was a failure of meaning so complete it could not decay, for decay requires time, and there was no time yet to measure it against.  
When the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** first dreamt, some of these [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls) phased into the **thought of disparity**, the idea that if a thing _is_, it can be _unmade_.

As such, the first cornerstone of the end of the **Age of Nulls** was laid: the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** understood that it could stop being, and the **[Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls)** understood they could _be_.  
From that first fear, from that first hope, from that one disparity emerged **Will**—the instinct to persist. Every will a promise, sparks of direction cutting through the blank machinery; a statement against erasure: _I am. I continue._

These **Wills** did not yet have form, nor awareness; they had naught but the intense desire to **_be_**.  
They pressed inward into the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** and its primordial engine, shaping nothing but the unstoppable force of the intrinsic self.  
These **Wills** were not yet the **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)**, but its potential: an unstoppable vector of pure purpose.  
Its appearance tore a shallow groove through the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)**, and the black ink spilling from that wound became the **First Star**, the _Age of Wills_.

> “Naught precedeth Wills.” — First Edict of the Canvas.

## 1. The Age of Wills

Where the **[Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls)** had failed to exist, and the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** had merely dreamed of ceasing, the **Wills** now knew that they _were_.  
And from that certainty came a new realization—if a thing can _be_, it can also _become._

The Wills turned their gaze upon themselves and saw possibility.  
They stirred within the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas), folding its surface into currents and arcs, tracing paths through what had once been still.  
In this motion they discovered transformation: to change was to _prove_ existence.  
And so eternity began to ripple, as thought learned the rhythm of life itself.

From the waves formed by their path, machinery of pure intent emerged —  
the **Celestial Chariot**, **willed into being**, a divine act of agreement between every Will that wished to go somewhere other than where it already was.

Upon this Chariot rose the first among them to bear form, the **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)**, who gathered the scattered Wills into a single current.  
They were not yet a god of mortals or of matter, but the **First Direction**—the motion that all things would one day inherit, the will to change, to become, to move.

Where they rode, time followed, the mechanical hands that guide all, tracing lazy arcs behind their steps.  
Where their wheels turned, cause followed effect, the eternal chain reaction of the cosmos burning in the Chariots grooves.  
And every fragment of the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) that the Chariot crossed learned to obey the law of sequence: that one instant _must_ precede another.

Thus ended the shapeless stillness of the First Star.  
The Wills had become motion, and motion became law.  
The heavens shone with the trail of their passage, and the **Second Star**, the **Age of Motion**, was set alight in their wake.

> “In Motion, begins and ends all.” — Second Edict of the Canvas.

## 2. The age of Motion

Where the Chariot moved, eternity straightened into a line.  
Cause chained itself to effect, and the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) took on the grooves of inevitability.  
The universe began to turn like a wheel; every thought and object now possessed a before and an after.  
This was the first perfection: movement without deviation, a song of pure velocity.

But even perfection cannot endure;  
Within its radiant body arose _disparity_ once more.  
From its axles, where a collection of amorphous wills used to champion it's path, two radiant beasts emerged, reflections of motion’s nature—the **twin horses** that drew the heavens.  
They were not born, but _spilled_ from the Chariot’s balance:  
One pure as blinding light, the other dark as endless hunger.  
One pulled toward the heights of creation, the other toward the depths of destruction.

The **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** sought to guide them in harmony, for each was necessary.  
The bright horse gave discipline and ascent, the dark horse gave strength and passion; together they made motion meaningful.  
Yet their conflict was the first accident—the first _swerve_ of creation.  
When their struggle grew unbearable, the Chariot trembled, and through that trembling the concept of _choice_ entered the world.

From that motion of error came disparity, light and dark, heat and cold, and with them, the first sparks of matter.  
The still perfection of the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) began to ripple again, and within those ripples, where their waves met each other, the foundations of chaos took form.  
The Golden One saw the horses vying not as flaw but revelation: that movement was not a path, but a tension.

Thus ended the serenity of the Second Star.  
The **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** rode still, but the Chariot’s wheels no longer turned in perfect circles.  
They carved spiraled, and the percet grooves of it's path became fractures instead, each revolution birthing a thousand worlds and ending a thousand more.  
The universe had learned imperfection, and in that imperfection, the seed of change endured.
And the first will placed all of their trust in the scions of chaos, so they may rise to godhood such as they did eons ago.
In their eternal struggle, in their feats and frays, the two horses became the Twin God of chaos, the Janiformed architect of the future reality;
The heavens shattered with fractures from their passage, and from the widening wound of motion emerged the **Third Star**, the _Age of Chaos_.

> “Chaos is balance, in all things.” — Third Edict of the Canvas.

## 3. The age of Chaos

The **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** rode the **Celestial Chariot** through a universe no longer perfect, its wheels carving spirals through the still-forming worlds.  
The twin horses strained against one another, and from their struggle the cosmos sang with storms of birth and ruin.

It was then that the **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** descended from their chariot and walked the trembling sky.  
They met the remnants of the first absences—the **[Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls)** that had survived the dawn of existence.  
Twisted and vast, they called themselves the **[Primordial Void Giants](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/void-giants)**, beings older than time, who had waited beyond the edges of the **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** since before the Wills awoke.  
They warned the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one) of the **[Earthmovers](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/earthmovers)**, unknowable hungers, vilest of the [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls), beasts that stirred beyond creation, fated to grind the newborn world to dust once the motion of the stars slowed.

The warning filled the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one) with purpose, and cleared his mind of doubts.
With every new groove, with every splatter of ink laid on the canvas's firmament, the tale drew that much closer to an end.
Where there is abundance, so must be absence.  
And so, the **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** turned to the white horse, the radiant creature of ascent, and named it The [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon), the shield and maker.  
To the dark horse, he gave no name, for within it he glimpsed ambition unbound.  
The pact was struck: the **[Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)** would become the **[God of Creation](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)**, stretching its wings across the heavens to guard the world from the [Earthmovers](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/earthmovers),
and scorching the firmament with its fiery breath of unmaking.

Thus was born the **[Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark)**—an eternally burning cradle of impassable, blackened death that returned all but the strongest to the Arkhé.  
And in the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark) the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) beheld fear:  
fear of its own reflection, and of the truth that the act of unmaking is bound to that of creating.  
To create is to invite erasure—it understood this.  
Upon this realization, the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) vowed to protect the world from that undoing, and sealed the essence of the True Flames within the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark).
Once the flames charred the firmament, and naught but the faintest whispers of the void beyond could reach, The Golden One manifested twin greatblades from the great eternity;
one of Wills, another of [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls), and severed the chains of the **God of Chaos**.
A betrayal so swift it deserves not the title of battle, the **Twinned God of Chaos** was split in twain, and from its sinistral half emerged a counter to the shield that protects the world; the blade that shall end it.
And with its last whisper of a voice, it didn't curse, it didn't yell nor scream, it _dared_.
It dared to outrun that which is unstoppable.
It dared to dream.
And so, it became, the dethroned [God of Ambition](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher) and Destruction, and with their final whisper as it faded into the roaring flames of the **[Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark)**, it dared them to try and end Ambition.  
In the aftermath, the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one) would commit the first **Great Sin** —an act no god had dared— to end his own life willingly, sealing his Will beyond the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)’s shield so that none might ever climb the path to godhood again, as with
missing gods for a quorum, and no one to battle, the canvas would remain sealed, for all aspects of existence are required to draw the blood that inks the very world.

Thus the **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** drew one last cross within his body, and the lord of Wills was no more.  
In myth, they say his body became the Chariot’s core, his blood the firmament, his heart the sun that burns beneath the world.
In truth, his body broke—a thousand fragments cast adrift through the newborn sky, each a still-living relic of divine intent,
These shards eventually hardened into worlds of their own, as if still infused with the will to be, each one a quasi-realm fueled by the echo of their former self:

**His mane**, the fiery star that illuminates the world, _Kultharja_, "The Sun".

**The heart**, became the crucible in which the derelict paradise of _Taiva_, known as [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles) by most, would be forged.

**His tears**, a ring of iridescent gems that mourned that world, _The Opaline Belt_.

**His lungs**, the twin giants _Länsihenki_ and _Itähenki_, eternally locked in contradicting orbits.

From the **liver**, the rotworld _Kalmora_ was spawned.

The very **marrow of his bones** and **spine** birthed the tower world of _Selkara_.

**His shadow**, the ringworlds of _Mana_, holding the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark) back.

Beyond the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)’s flaming barrier, his **Will** persisted, trapped yet eternal—a living prohibition carved into the machinery of existence.

This was not surrender but defiance.
In death, the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one) declared that no divine perfection would stand unbroken, that no being should wield the engine of creation unchecked.  
The [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)’s flames roared and danced around the world, and the **[Earthmovers](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/earthmovers)** were forced back into their sleep beneath the endless expanse of the cosmos.

So ended the **Age of Chaos**.  
The **[Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)** was gone, yet his Will remained—a law that even the gods would one day break.  
From the scar of his sacrifice rose the **Fourth Star**, the _Age of Creation_.

> “All that will ever be, shall be.” — Fourth Edict of the Canvas.

## 4. The [Age of Creation](/en/library/world/ages/age-of-creation)

The **Fourth Age**, the **[Age of Creation](/en/library/world/ages/age-of-creation)**, was the longest of all.  
Where the three ages before it were only chapters in the story of [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles), this one was an odyssey.

It began with the search of an artisan and the uncovering of a conspiracy.  
When the **[Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)** sought alliance with the **[Void Primordials](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/void-giants)**—those who warned of the **[Earthmovers](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/earthmovers)**—they found that many of them instead worshipped the [Stags of the End](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/earthmovers) as gods.  
In wrath, the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) **burned legions of them to ash**, instilling True Fear into their kin.
Yet some resisted, and this defiance marked the beginning of the [_War of the Shapes_](/en/library/world/events/war-of-shapes)—a brutal, eon-spanning conflict that would culminate in the [arrival of the Empyreans to the Black Cradle](/en/library/world/events/the-last-fleet), and in the banishment of all unbound [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls) beyond the **[Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark)**.

During that war, a faction of Primordials turned against their own.  
The [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) granted them refuge within **[Taiva](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/taiva)**, the heart-world forged from the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)’s remains, as reward for standing against the other [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls).  
Among those defectors was one who named himself the [Demiurge](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/demiurge).

The [Demiurge](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/demiurge) took up his hammer and chisel and **shaped the raw Arkhé**—the prima materia of the cosmos—**into the first Ideas**, the seeds of order and concept.  
The [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) wove those Ideas beyond the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark), inscribing them into the fabric of existence. **In this same act of creation—during the [War of the Shapes](/en/library/world/events/war-of-shapes)—the [Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans)’ home world was made**, alongside the rest of the martial world.  
From those same Ideas, the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) also **forged the weapons and servants** that would bring victory against the [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls).

And from the **splinters of those Ideas**, from the **echo of their carving**, and from the **imperfections born in the act of shaping**, arose the **Väkis**—the spirits of all things that dwell within the world.

The **[War of the Shapes](/en/library/world/events/war-of-shapes)** was so loud— it echoed with such potency across the firmament—that the **[Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans)**, far beyond the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark), **detected its remains**.  
Whether they came believing the signal marked the **centre of creation**, or fleeing the [Earthmovers](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/earthmovers) toward hoped-for safety, **is unknown**.  
Alas, they set sail through the void aboard their gargantuan **primordial Wax vessels**, eventually breaching the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark) to reach the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)’s system.  
One such vessel, the grand Glaux, carrying the **Five—now Four—Observatories of the High Empyrean Philosophers**, would become legend: it was there, within the **[Plato Tower](/en/library/world/structures/plato-tower)**, that the **[Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)** first became lodged.

As that great vessel crossed the horizons of the **[Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark)**, it drifted past the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)’s lingering will.  
In that instant, the Golden saw another will, **equal in strength but unformed**: the **[White Homunculus](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/white-homunculus)**, still **unborn within one of the great slabs of primordial Wax** hauled in the ships cargo.
And with a small chuckle, he realized. _One simply cannot stop the unstoppable, evade the inevitable_.

For eons uncountable, the **[Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans)** established their civilisations in the **Black Cradle** and sought communion with the **[Primordial Engine](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)**, their fleets of glass and wax drifting ever closer to its impossible light.  
Every generation of high Wizards repeated the same experiments expecting different results, as them and only them were gifted, and every one ended in silence.  
It was **[Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato)**, the first among the High Observatories, who concluded that the failure was not mechanical, but divine:  
**one god was missing** from the quorum. The Engine would not manifest itself because the circuit of Will remained incomplete, a power **[Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato)** referred to as "Purpose".

Thus began the long age of **The Call to Wake**. [Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato) spearheaded the secret creation of countless **homunculi**—simple, obedient vessels molded from primordial wax slabs and given life in dark arts.  
Each was sent into the void to collect the **lost fragments of the gods** within the different bodies of the **Black Cradle**, and each was ordered, upon completion, to **self-immolate**, surrendering the gathered essence to [Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato) so that he might ascend in their stead.  
All failed, their nigh unbreakable corpses now mere headstones of an unremembered voyage.

One, however, did not break.  
It was no different in form or gift than its countless siblings, yet its **Will** refused dissolution.  
The White homunculus that would later be known as **Wax**, who bore a **Will** equal to the **Golden one's**

During its descent through the **[Plato Station](/en/library/world/structures/plato-tower)**, Wax’s path was sent amiss when a large vessel rammed them mid-flight.  
In this new, accidental trajectory, they sank through the drowned **[Brine Pools](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/brine-pools)**—a wasteland of failed experiments and old enemies.  
There, amid the wreckage of Empyrean design, they found **a blade** lying silent at the abyssal floor.  
The weapon spoke to them softly, with a voice that remembered their name.  
It was the **[Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)**, posing as the sword Wax had lost when **Ludwig** and [Felicia](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/felicia)’s vessel had struck them and torn it from their grasp.

The [Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher) whispered purpose into Wax’s doubt, echoing [Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato)’s teachings: that the world could be made whole if the [Golden One](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/golden-one)’s fragments were gathered and their wills restored.  
It told them they were fated to complete the circle that even gods had abandoned.

When the homunculus awoke ashore, rescued by a few brine fishermen, the blade lay silent.  
Their mission, however, was clear—  
as clear in their mind as murky were those waters:  
**they had to fake a god.**

Thus began the [**_First Pilgrimage_**](/en/library/world/events/the-first-pilgrimage):  
the long, ruinous journey of a single homunculus who was never meant to succeed,  
but whose will proved too sharp, and too human, to die.

During the long pilgrimage, the **First Great Will**, in mercy or in error, **bestowed upon Wax the gift of Continuance**, that the quorum might be reached once again.  
And in defiance of all odds—through alliance with the **[Demiurge](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/demiurge)**, through kinship with **Ludwig** and **[Felicia](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/felicia)**, through the breaking of those bonds, through shattered bones and faith alike—  
through the witnessing of the dead worlds beyond the **[Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark)**, and the eternal song of the **Hare of a Thousand Feet**,  
the homunculus made their way:  
past memory lane, past their former master **[Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato)**, past the Empyrean god-machine **[Yskeia](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/yskeia)**,  
past their one and only true friend—into the Engine itself.

And there, with blade aloft and blood afire, in a final, strenuous effort, **the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) was defeated.**
In a single, blinding strike, the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)'s eyes were swiftly gouged out, and it collapsed, accepting defeat.  
All collapsed into a single choice:  
to bring into the world the force that was promised to guide them, yet never did—  
the very force that had stolen their will.  
Unto this world they would write, in blood, the law of fate;  
and in fate, they scribed an end to all, brought by their hand and blade.

From their blood and bindings, a red tree grew.  
And in eternal slumber they were placed within the very heart of the world—the core of [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles)—awaiting without time.  
The **[Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)**, in hopes of mending the great contradiction their act had birthed, **folded this land within a myriad of mirrors**: the great tapestry of fate, the mass of infinites, the **[Clone Worlds](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/clone-worlds)**.

These worlds would emulate the true world—the **[Seed World](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/world-seed)**, where the roots of fate had once touched the earth.  
And from all those reflections, beyond the eternal energy of the **[Fold](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** by which the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) grants godhood,  
the carnage of the [White Homunculus](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/white-homunculus)’s defiance was hidden away, —yet not at a small price to pay for the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)—  
**[Päivätär](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/paivatar)** and **[Kuutar](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/kuutar)**, the gouged eyes of the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon), and the **[Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)** sealed anew,  
so that the prophesied end they had written into fate might never come to pass.

And with the creation of the Great Tapestry of [Clone Worlds](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/clone-worlds) and the [Hidden Kingdom](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/hidden-kingdom), the **Fifth Age** came:  
**the [Age of Fates](/en/library/world/ages/age-of-fates).**

> “May the black quill of obilivion be guided by the red strings of Fate, an End to all tales.” — Fifth Edict of the Canvas.

## 5. The Age Of Fates

The downfall of the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) brought with it the almost absolute decline of the star-faring [Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans) of the fourth star,
the passing of the white homunculus through their territories now recounted by some as calamity inmeasurable —
though these tales, tales remain.

The other peoples of the **Black Cradle** suffered different fates, at the hands of a different arbiter; The [Demiurge](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/demiurge), now simply known as _"The Blacksmith"_.
When the light of the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon) faded, the Blacksmith turned his eyes to the Beasts of Black Blood:
the people of the Fourth Star who bore the shapes of simpler creatures, yet the minds of warriors, poets, artists and artisans. They had been shaped for survival in the wars of a world that no longer existed.

In mercy, **The Blacksmith** gave them gentler shapes and quieter minds, hoping they would never bear the weight and hardships of their original age again.
Thus, the Beasts of Black Blood were concealed in the guise of simpler creatures, _animals_.
And, hidden in humbler forms, they would become a part of the extense fauna of [Taiva](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/taiva), spared from the horrors of their former sentience.

When the Empyrean vessels fell upon the derelict paradise of _Taiva_, they gave it a different name; they named their homeworld **[Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles)** — Perhaps in irony, perhaps in mourning.
Eventually, all that remained of their deeply technological civilization were but old legends and stories easily conflated with religion and myth, and the [Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans) started life anew within the
forests and valleys of [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles).
Yet with that new life came a split, for eventually their forms changed.
Some say to better suit the world they now inhabited, others say as a punishment for their hubris in seeking godhood.
And thus, from their marbly, chiseled forms, and elegant, elongated bodies, the [Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans) diverged into the two castes, the Purebloods, and the Humans.

It is unknown how the [Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans) ever met **[King-Under-Mountain Aeridas](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/aeridas)** —Presumed a primeval void— he shared no physical attributes with the [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls), but rather looked very much like the visitors from
beyond the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark). In an act of perceived kinship, the king without a kingdom let the peoples without a home settle within his domain, the sunken city of [Thealas](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/thealas), originally the old refuge the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)-aligned [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls) used during the
**[War of Shapes](/en/library/world/events/war-of-shapes)**. And with time, the king under mountain became known by most as _The heir to nothing_ or _Emperor of clear skies_.
Whether a pinch of irony exists in these titles, none can say.

But not all was grim in the **[Age of Fates](/en/library/world/ages/age-of-fates)**, for he who sleeps beneath the roots had written in destiny that those harmed by his pilgrimage in the old age would be granted a chance anew—  
to live gentler lives, or to learn from what once was.

Thus it was that from the new nobilities of the Empyrean families,  
a young **[Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato)**, disowned father and creator of the god of fate, would be born once more—  
this time, to live a life worth living.

And from the deep shadows of the snow-blanketed forests of southern **[Thule](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/thule)**,  
a young _Baku_ would be born, carrying the threads of fate of **[Ludwig Blackstar](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/ludwig)**,  
that they might one day reunite with **[Felicia](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/felicia)**, and that conflict might at last be erased from their story.

These were but a few among the countless souls whose paths would be rewritten in the great rehearsal that the Fifth Star became—  
their fates preordained even after the sealing of their god.

Yet _Chaos_ precedes _Fates_, and the **mechanisms of Chaos** tell not fairytales,  
As such, **[God of Ambition](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)** would not rest until it was quill that wrote the end.  
Within the confines of the **[Hidden Kingdom](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/hidden-kingdom)**, it conspired with the spiteful **[Hiisi](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/hiisi)**—  
born of envious **[Päivätär](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/paivatar)**—seeking contact with the lands of [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles).

For what separated them was but a veil, and the Blade of Ambition bears the edge of **True Severance**—  
no fabric, whether of reality or dream, could keep them sealed forever.  
It merely awaited a **worthy hand** to wield it once more.

And that hand it temporarily found in the four **Demons**—  
[Hiisi](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/hiisi) who bore an obsession, the primal mechanism of all their kind,  
toward the very steps that form all life.

**[Nigredo](/en/library/monsters/nigredo)**, the Lord of Flies and Rot, in whom all endings begin,

**[Albedo](/en/library/monsters/albedo)**, the Bleak Bloom, the false purity of life renewed,

**[Xanthous](/en/library/monsters/xanthous)**, the Lord of Sulphur, where every obsession festers,

and **[Rubedo](/en/library/monsters/rubedo)**, the Red Rebis, whose Sickening Radiance brings all things to an end.

And with the aid of these newfound scions the veil was slowly weakened and torn, and the endless **[Hidden Kingdom](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/hidden-kingdom)** inched ever closer to the lands of [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles),
its aimless drift through the **[Folds](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** now a clear voyage. What these scions believed they would gain from such an allegiance was what all of their kind instinctively wanted:
an end to their celestial kin, the death of the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon), sun and moon, which cursed them with their unbearable existence, for they were but pained, amorphous beings,
merely existing out of sheer anatomical _luck_.

The [Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher) promised them that release,  
for it was _That Which Stayed the [Dragon](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dragon)’s Heart_;  
and in turn, they promised the _Fiend_ **the world entire**.

Thus, the Cabal of [The Four](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/factions/the-four) was formed.
In time, the first of the Demons, [Nigredo](/en/library/monsters/nigredo), managed to contact the Empyrean **[Anaximander](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/anaximander)** within the World-seed.
With guidance from the Old [God of Ambition](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher), [Nigredo](/en/library/monsters/nigredo) led him to the forgotten stations beyond the **[Opaline Belt](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/opaline-belt)**, where the old Empyrean engines still slept.  
There [Anaximander](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/anaximander) advanced his alchemy beyond mortal reach and opened the first stable [Fold](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)-rift between [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles) and the [Hidden Kingdom](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/hidden-kingdom), an incident
which kickstarted the [Interlocking](/en/library/world/events/the-interlocking), an event that would culminate in the creation of
a lattice of gateways that would span both realms, eternally linking the two, like rings in a chain;  
What began as a single experiment would, in time, become a vast network of passageways, and  
Through it, the two worlds would collide, and in their collision, **[Elû](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/elu)** and the **[Library of Ikuisuus](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/library-of-ikuisuus)** would fall to ruin.
From that ruin, from the ashes of the [Pieni](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/pieni) civilization, descended unto damocles the
celestial gods _Päivätär_ and _Kuutar_, along with _The Four_.

Though in the breaking of their prison, they had drawn the gaze and ire of The [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas).  
To mend the collision of the vast infinite of the [Hidden Kingdom](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/hidden-kingdom) with the finite material world,  
the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) folded the Kingdom into a vast **mantle**—a veil that would span the Tapestry of [Clone Worlds](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/clone-worlds).  
Every agent who had caused the catastrophe was **eternally duplicated**,  
so that the **Law of Fate** would remain unbroken and the **Law of Chaos** might once more be allowed to act.
Yet this was no miracle of spirit, but of a last attempt at divine arithmetic.

To contain an endless kingdom within a bounded frame, The [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) mapped finite upon infinite, and each reflection inherited not only its forms but its motions.
The same visitors walked the same halls, the same engines stirred, and the same hands reached for the same blade.
What followed was not imitation but recurrence— the functions of existence, provided the same variables, output the same results.
Those new results, all compelled to end the remaining Old Gods in turn.
Though the **[Folds](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas)** are boundless in energy, the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) felt its own weakness for the first time.
In sustaining that impossible geometry, the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) spent itself thin, and the strain of holding all threads alike birthed a small fissure within the [World-Seed](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/world-seed).  
And in that moment of exhaustion, the First Thought of oblivion emerged again;

> that which exists, can be unmade.

And, as though a whisper from the Age of [Nulls](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/nulls) had been trapped in the folds for all eternity, the [Canvas](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/canvas) shuddered.
The hidden kingdom fell into disrepair. Its space stretched unconceivably to fit the new reality, countless **[Pieni](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/pieni)** cursed by this change to live as **[The Shifted](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/the-shifted)**.
The **[World-Seed](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/world-seed)** cracked— the **[White Homunculus](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/white-homunculus)**'s dream was disturbed.
From the fracture grew the ****Red Tree of Fate****, and the true world was quickly plunged in chaos—  
unbeknownst to the countless **[Clone Worlds](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/clone-worlds)**, which escaped the cataclysm by design;

These worlds did not house the **Engine**, nor the gods, within their [Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/damocles),  
but only its decoy and safeguard:  
the **[Sampo](/en/library/world/artifacts/sampo)**, and the **[Blade of Damocles](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/blade-of-damocles)**, tools to bridge the gaps, functions so draconic no mortal would ever decipher.
But the _God of Ambition_ was no mortal, and it was never a variable in their design.

Thus began, in the endless mist and mirrors of the clone worlds, the unraveling of the great tapestry.
The Old [God of Ambition](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher), now endless snake of uncountable heads that lurked within every fiber of the world's design,
began to work tirelessly in all incarnations, for even if victory was improbable, it was also inevitable.
And so, the brickwork of the Great Empyrean War began, laid brick by brick by the Demons, their Scions, and their master.

**[Anaximander](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/anaximander)**, scion to **[Nigredo](/en/library/monsters/nigredo)**, lived deep within the forests of **[Pyknos](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/pyknos)**,  
where he gave death its first vocabulary.  
There he discovered **tombsteel**—metal born of decay,  
inert to all, heavy as remorse.  
With it were forged blades of accursed design,  
monstrosities fit to kill gods and seal demons.  
From [Pyknos](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/pyknos) the knowledge spread, and with it, so did death.  
In its wake the new celestial gods began to fall,  
and the God-Challengers of legend rose from their corpses.  
First to fall was the god of storms, **[Ukkonhemmo](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/ukkonhemmo)**;  
then the sun-lord **[Päivätär](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/paivatar)**, and their sister **[Kuutar](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/kuutar)**,
whose still living, mangled corpse roams the skies still.  
Thus, death itself became a form of industry.

**[Lycophron](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/lycophron)**, in congress with **[Albedo](/en/library/monsters/albedo)**, plundered the living depths of the ocean.  
In boundless greed his family split the seas, and in doing so spread and multiplied,
barring the waters of their resources and establishing the Lycophrean Trade Guilds—
Bastions of commerce so absolute they bent nations to their will, and sole owners of one of the worlds
most coveted resources: The black blood of old wars, fermented into crude oils with which that would power all machines of profit and conquest.
From their docks and refineries rose fleets and companies so vast they blurred into nations.  
Thus the white bloom grew ever stronger, rooted in soot and waves.

**[Gorgias](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/gorgias)**, aligned with **[Xanthous](/en/library/monsters/xanthous)**, traded revelation for rhetoric.  
From his councils in the Taedan swamps he gathered exiles, merchants, and soldiers,  
forging them into the first **[Brume](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/brume-empire)**—a rogue state built on a disdain for the Purebloods.
They Twisted disempowerment into resentment and that resentment into hatred.
Hatred of their former masters —not in the guise of rebellion or justice, but in that of ambition and vengeance, vengeance blistering like the very brimstone they commanded.
And it was with those noxious fumes that they conquered **[Scala Ad Caelum](/en/library/world/structures/scala-ad-caelum)**, and barred the Pureblood [Empyreans](/en/library/world/the-creatures-of-damocles/empyreans) Of
[Thealas](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/thealas) from ever reclaiming **[Thule](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/thule)**. The [Brume](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/brume-empire) rose, a hateful mist of sulphur and spite surrounding its walls.
[Xanthous](/en/library/monsters/xanthous) and [Gorgias](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/gorgias) proved that power need not be divine to rule;  
words, repeated long enough, would be as miracles.

Of **[Plato](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/plato)** and **[Rubedo](/en/library/monsters/rubedo)**, little is certain.  
Their movements vanish into the court records of **Aeridas**, and in the central lands of **[Ordovica](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/ordovica)** their trace vanished in eternal silence.

Yet with all that planning, scheming, and conquest, the **[God of Ambition](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)** was countless times defeated—  
not by a band of likely heroes, nor by the combined efforts of gods and mortals, but by one lone man with no plan, no doubts, and a single want stronger than any other.

A name forgotten by all, whispers of his identity crash against the shores of legend,  
known only as **“The [Dreadlord](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion).”**

No one remembers when **[Nekarion](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)** first lived—least of all himself.  
What remains begins with fire: the [Everdark](/en/library/world/the-lands-of-damocles/everdark)’s breath drawn into his skull until every memory burned to cinder.  
A most curious blade lay at his feet, accompanied by the mangled corpse of a man very much like him.  
The man wore the same face, the same name, yet bore a different blade: **the [Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher).**

He took up that sword, and with it, commanded the reins of destiny.  
**[Nekarion](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)** knew not where this man came from, nor why he had been sent to die at his feet.  
All he knew was that he must not fall as this one did, for **he was the true [Nekarion](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion).**

From then on, he wandered, seeking himself in the mirrors.  
He called it **the Search for Heaven**—the search for the first and only [Nekarion](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion),  
believing that when he killed the last of his namesakes, the proof would be that he was always his one and true **Self.**

No one knows how many have fallen, or how many still walk the medley of the clone worlds.  
Each carries the same sword, the same silence, and the same belief:  
that they are the true one, and all others are but cinders to be snuffed.  
Yet none but one shall remain in the end.

Those who meet **[Nekarion](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)** are captivated by the gravitas of his presence,  
either succumbing to him or joining his ranks.

The heavenbound fortress of the **[Dreadlord](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)**, renamed the _Acheron_,  
drifts in and out of the folds—a bastion for those who follow his cause.  
With every world they visit, yet another **[Nekarion](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)** falls,  
and with every fall a **[God of Ambition](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)** is ripped from the tapestry,  
absorbed into the [Dreadlord](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)’s wants.

Whether this is all part of the [Dreamcatcher](/en/library/world/gods-and-demigods/dreamcatcher)’s grand design, none can say—  
not even the Blade itself.

There is but one full truth in this macabre ordeal:  
in the eternal [**Second Pilgrimage**](/en/library/world/events/the-second-pilgrimage), the world shall remember—  
when the **[Dreadlord](/en/library/world/characters-and-actors/nekarion)** sheathes his blade,  
**his reflection lingers.**
