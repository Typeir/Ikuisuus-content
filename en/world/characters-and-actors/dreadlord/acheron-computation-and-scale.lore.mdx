# A True Relation and Computation of the Scale of Acheron

## Of the Surface and Its Design

Know ye, your graces, that Acheron doth comprise approximately **10,050 km²** of effective built surface, the fruit of an urban footprint well-nigh circular in figure, measuring some **16 kilometers** in diameter, raised and superimposed upon **50 complete urban strata**.

To this grandeur is added—as a supplement of no small merit—a further **10%** in the form of elevated parks, lateral terraces, hanging gardens, and open unbuilt lands, reaching thus a total of nearly **11,100 km²** of usable surface, which is to say, some **11,100,000,000 square meters**.

---

## Of the Population

Acheron harboureth within its bosom some **27,000,000 inhabitants**.

The average hope of life is high for these present times, hovering about **70 years**, and the rate of fertility remaineth close to **2.1**, wherefore the population bideth stable, neither overflowing nor withering away.

A small portion of the dwellers is, to all practical effects, immortal:

- **The Empyreans** do usually refuse sustained integration amongst the civil populace.
- **The Sunborn** are not numbered in the rolls of the immortal.
- **The Primordial Voids** remain entirely outside any census or reckoning.
- **The 777** who existed before Nekarion are not considered an active population—not out of malice, but by their own decree.
- **The Homunculi** likewise are not included in the civil count, for to maintain a reckoning of them proveth well-nigh impossible and a most unnecessary impediment to sorcerers.

---

## Of Natural Death

With such numbers, Acheron recordeth approximately **1,050 natural deaths** each day, which amounteth to some **385,000 natural deaths** per year.

---

## Of the Chains and the Cadavers

Acheron findeth itself adorned and sustained by some **10,000 chains**.

All souls, without exception, end their journey within them; there is no distinction of birth, creed, craft, nor rank, for the system is neither selective nor punitive, nor doth it grant any privilege.

A vast majority of deaths proceed from natural, accidental, or internal causes within the system itself. Wars, rituals, and open violences represent but a smaller fraction, albeit one more visible and better remembered.

Throughout some **2,000 years of history**—with a gradual growth of folk during the first 500 years and stability in the 1,500 years following—Acheron hath processed between **400 and 500 million dead** in total.

At any given moment, the chains contain the structural and visible equivalent of some **120 to 160 million cadavers**. Such a thing is possible because:

1.  The chains possess **solid cores** of stone, metal, tombsteel, or other matters, so that they collapse not under their own weight.
2.  The ancient remains are **compacted, ossified, or integrated** as structural mass.
3.  The chains are not an immobile archive, but rather a **continuous and perpetual process**.

> And even Nekarion, should the hour strike, shall at the last hang from them.
