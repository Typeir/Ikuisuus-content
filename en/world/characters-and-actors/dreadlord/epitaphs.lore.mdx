# Epitaphs of the Dreadlord

A collection of epitaphs, inscribed in Aquerontine script, on unwieldy slabs of stone.
Too impractical to be moved, yet inexplicably found in every corner of the Acheron, the Dread Citadel.

> Here lay the path of the Dreadlord, carved in stone as his pilgrimage was in my memory. — Ónice of the Acheron, the Dreadlord’s very shadow.

## Epitaph I — Arrival

When the Second Pilgrim first arrived, our sanctum lay in disarray,  
for bearing the guise of a foul, blackened beast,  
and carrying a blade in mockery of our Savior,  
the Dreadlord arrived,  
forging a path paved with corpses—  
a path whose first stone he laid himself,  
in more ways than one.

</Collapsible>

## Epitaph II — Ambition

Yet the ruins left in his wake sang a tune in unison,  
a tune not of hope,  
not of longing or desire,  
but that of want.  
That of will.  
Ambition.

The interloper subjugated and dissolved our chambers  
and, with iron fist, commanded us—  
at first in fear and rejection,  
but slowly formed trust and devotion,  
for even if his shape were anathema,  
his Soma was the same.

Our Savior.  
Our Second Pilgrim.

</Collapsible>

## Epitaph III — Naming

Within the first aeons of their arrival, the fortress was baptized in black flame.  
Its prior identity excised,  
snuffed like the selves of those who paved the _Path to Heaven_.

In that renewed lucidity,  
our home became the _Acheron_,  
soon known to the naysayers of Taiva as the _Dread Citadel_.

Yet it was no matter,  
for Taiva was merely a stop along our path.  
One of many.

</Collapsible>

## Epitaph IV — Threads

Yet in the very heart of renamed Damocles, a likeness was found,  
and with time, more and more would become heavenbound—  
iron-willed pilgrims,  
following in the footsteps of our lord.

Those pilgrims, too, came to pave the _Path to Heaven_,  
their ambitions eternal,  
their wills unshakable.

With each arrival, a new battle.  
With each victory, a new memento bound to our home.

In time, our fortress drifted effortlessly  
through the vastness beyond the Veil,  
our collection, our Threads,  
a blessed bulwark—  
a deterrent powerful enough to quell a god’s wrath.

A blessing in the shape of a curse.

</Collapsible>

## Epitaph V — Inferno

Self-inflicted were most of the wounds of our master;  
his swordsmanship so heightened that no battle could be of use to him.  
Yet a select few—  
infernos among cinders—  
those who wounded his body and fueled his heart,  
those became his ever-present _Death Guard_.

A hunter of beasts inconceivable.  
A warrior who withheld entropy itself.  
An everlasting wound upon the world.  
And a forgotten, frozen echo.

As tradition ordains,  
as our culture dictates,  
their names, too, were burned away in the _Everdark_.  
One does not need a name to reach Heaven.  
One merely needs themselves,  
whoever that may be.

Thus were the four granted the highest honor:  
they would be our Lord’s shadow,  
and our Lord would be their light.

</Collapsible>

## Epitaph VI — Refusal

The last heart burst,  
the final grape upon the vine undone.  
The corpse-god steeped in carnage,  
a succinct collage of murder and destruction.

In spite of this,  
they did not deride Sire Nekarion.  
No malice was turned upon him,  
no violence sent his way.

At his feet, within an empty, bloodstained Vermelka among the stars,  
the moon grew lucid,  
and with it, in unison, a world entire—  
a motherless family.

They believed they had found a second parent to follow.  
So it seemed.

Yet our lord refused that role.

In eternal wisdom, he spoke.  
He proclaimed:

“Only those strong of will and free of heart may follow.”

“I am no father.  
I am no god.”

“I pray to no one,  
nor will I be prayed to.”

“I will excise prayer from this world.”

“I will take Heaven.”

“It is your choice to share in the bounty.  
It is not mine to deny you.”

Few dared follow.

Those who did became the last four of the Eight Generals:  
there was the _Death Guard_,  
and there were the _Moon Sages_.

</Collapsible>

## Epitaph VII — Recluse

Time and rime again, one would come for our lord,  
in claims of hellfire and ruination.  
With claws, jaws, blades, and bows,  
they challenged the Pilgrim—  
yet no blow, cut, arrow, or strike would find its mark.

At first, they were crushed in moments.  
Yet the Pilgrim grew curious.

Time and time again, they returned.  
Time and time again, the Pilgrim fought them.  
Every time, someone different—  
yet every time, one was the same.

A shepherd from beyond the stars.

The Dreadlord began keeping his mementos to himself,  
in a separate chamber.  
He rarely sought kinship in others,  
yet in this rivalry he reveled.

No matter the path he took,  
no matter the chance he was given,  
the confrontation was inevitable.

That is when the Dreadlord knew.

If there was another true Pilgrim,  
if some other poor dredge of this world,  
_another recluse of these lands,_  
must climb the ladder,  
must reach Heaven—

_it had to be Him._

</Collapsible>
